WHICH, THAT, WHO, WHERE
19
SEE
You can explain or describe things/people/places by using “which, “that,” “who,” and “where.”

“Sake” is a Japanese alcoholic beverage that/which is made from rice.

A vet is a doctor who sees animals.

Owl cafes are restaurants where you can enjoy both drinking beverages and watching owls.

Example

Ken:

I have good news. Rodel Garcia is visiting Japan next month.

Mary:

Who is he?

Ken:

He is a client who has been with us for a long time. I think I’ll invite him to a “fugu-shabu” restaurant.

Mary:

What’s a “fugu-shabu”?

Ken:

It’s thin-sliced blowfish that is boiled in a hotpot. There is a good restaurant near here where I had dinner a few years ago.

TRY
You are role B. Read the conversations with your trainer. You can change the underlined words.
  • A: What’s your favorite place in Japan? B: There is an area called Sumida. It’s a place where you can see the tallest tower in Japan. It’s also a place where you can enjoy some traditional attractions of Tokyo.
  • A: Who is a famous person you like? B: I like Akira Kawakami. He is a journalist who explains difficult news in a simple way on TV.
  • A: What is a company that is well known in your country? B: Wakuten is very well known. It’s a company that provides online shopping and many other services.
Now, switch roles.
▼▼▼
ACT
Your trainer will ask you the questions below. Answer them in complete sentences.
  • What is your favorite Japanese dish? Can you tell me about it? (It’s a dish that...)
  • Tell me about the area your office is in. (It’s an area where...)
  • Tell me about a person who has influenced you greatly. (He/she is a... who...)

レッスンのポイント

「関係詞」を使って文中の人、モノ、場所に関する情報を追加する方法を学びます。

関係詞で文章をつなげる2つのパターン

文章aの目的語＝文章bの主語

(a)Sudoku is a number puzzle + It is very popular in Japan.

= (b)Sudoku is a number puzzle that is very popular in Japan.

※ an application と It は同じものを指すので、 thatのあとはすぐに動詞が続く

文章aの目的語≠文章bの主語

(a)Sudoku is a number puzzle + I use the most often.

= (b)Sudoku is a number puzzle that I use the most often.

※ a number puzzle と I は異なるので、 thatのあと主語が続く

SEE
You can explain or describe things/people/places by using “which, “that,” “who,” and “where.”

"which", "that", "who", "where "を使って、物事/人/場所を説明したり、説明したりすることができます。

“Sake” is a Japanese alcoholic beverage that/which is made from rice.

“お酒” は、お米を原料とした日本のお酒です。

A vet is a doctor who sees animals.

獣医とは、動物を診る医者のことです。

Owl cafes are restaurants where you can enjoy both drinking beverages and watching owls.

フクロウカフェとは、飲み物を飲むこととフクロウを観察することの両方が楽しめるレストランのことです。

Example 例

Ken:

I have good news. Rodel Garcia is visiting Japan next month.

ケン:

良いニュースがあります。ロデル・ガルシアが来月、来日します。

Mary:

Who is he?

メアリー:

彼は誰ですか。

Ken:

He is a client who has been with us for a long time. I think I’ll invite him to a “fugu-shabu” restaurant.

ケン:

昔からお世話になっているお客さんです。 ふぐしゃぶのお店に誘ってみようと思います。

Mary:

What’s a “fugu-shabu”?

メアリー:

ふぐしゃぶって何ですか。

Ken:

It’s thin-sliced blowfish that is boiled in a hotpot. There is a good restaurant near here where I had dinner a few years ago.

ケン:

薄くスライスしたふぐを鍋で煮込んだものです。この近くに数年前に食べた美味しいお店があるんですよ。
