BIZMATES PROGRAM LEVEL 1IRANK CILESSON 9
TALKING ABOUT COMPLAINTS
9
SEE
Read the story aloud.

Jason:

I sometimes get complaints from customers. The complaints are usually about late delivery or quality. I always try to address each complaint as best as I can.

I contact customers right away whenever I receive a complaint. They usually send us an e-mail and provide details of their complaints. Some customers have many requests.

Last week, I received a big complaint from an important customer. The wrong model was sent to the customer because the shipping department made a mistake. I tried to rebuild trust by explaining the situation and the next steps honestly and carefully.

▼▼▼
TRY
To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.
  • How often do you get complaints from customers?
  • What are the complaints usually about?
  • What do you (or does your company) do when there's a complaint?
  • How do you (or does your company) handle complaints?
  • When was the last time you received a big complaint? Who was it from?
  • What do you (or does your company) usually do to rebuild trust with your customers?
▼▼▼
ACT
Make a short presentation about complaints. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.
  • How often you receive complaints from customers
  • Common complaints you receive
  • The steps you take to address complaints
  • The last time you received a big complaint from a customer
  • The reason the customer filed the big complaint
  • The actions you take to rebuild trust with your customers
