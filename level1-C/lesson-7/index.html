TALKING ABOUT YOUR COLLEAGUES
7
SEE
Read the story aloud.

Jason:

There are 25 sales representatives in the computer division at TRITEC. There’s a healthy competition in the division, but we still work closely as a team.

 

My closest colleagues are Sam and Tony. They are the same age as I am, and they are very hard working. We share the same hobbies so we get along really well.

 

My boss’s name is Ms. Chang. She’s been our manager for over four years. Ms. Chang can be very strict sometimes and some of my colleagues don’t like this. However, I like her management style because she’s understanding and fair-minded.

▼▼▼
TRY
To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.
  • How many people are there in your team/division?
  • What can you say about your work relationship with them?
  • Who are your closest colleagues? What are they like?
  • Why do you get along with them so well?
  • Who’s your boss? How long has he/she been leading your team?
  • What is his/her personality like?
  • How do you feel about his/her management style?
▼▼▼
ACT
Make a short presentation about your company. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.
  • The number of people in your team/division
  • Your work relationship with your colleagues
  • Your closest colleagues
  • What you have in common with your closest colleagues
  • Your boss and his/her tenure as your manager
  • Your boss’s personality
  • How you feel about his/her leadership
