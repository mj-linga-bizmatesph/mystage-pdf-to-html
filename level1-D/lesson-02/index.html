TALKING ABOUT MEETINGS
SEE
Read the story aloud.

John:

I attend a lot of meetings here at TC Com. I usually have about 6 meetings each week. Some are just thirty minutes, while some are three hours long.

I often have internal meetings with colleagues and people from other departments. I also have external meetings with vendors and prospective customers.

Yesterday, I had a meeting with the marketing director. The purpose of the meeting was to talk about the next advertising campaign. There were only two of us in the meeting. I was happy because I was able to share my ideas openly.

▼▼▼
TRY
To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.
  • Do you have a lot of meetings? How many meetings do you have each week?
  • How long are your meetings at work?
  • Who do you have internal meetings with?
  • Who do you have external meetings with?
  • When was the last time you had a meeting? Who did you meet with?
  • What was the purpose of that meeting?
  • At your company, can you share ideas openly in meetings?
▼▼▼
ACT
Make a short presentation about meetings. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.
  • The meetings you attend in your company
  • The length of a typical meeting in your company
  • Your counterparts in internal meetings
  • Your counterparts in external meetings
  • Your last meeting and the person you met with
  • How people interact during meetings
