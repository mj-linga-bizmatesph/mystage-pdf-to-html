TALKING ABOUT GOSSIP
3
SEE
Read the story aloud.

John:

My colleagues and I are very close. We often talk about many things during lunch. I’m ashamed to say this, but we usually talk about people we don’t like.

I know much of the gossip isn’t true. I just enjoy getting new information about them.

I heard that my boss, George, may be transferred next year. I’m happy to hear the news because I don’t get along with him so much. My other colleagues are happy, too, but they’re more worried about our next boss, Sandy.

▼▼▼
TRY
To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.
  • When do you and your colleagues usually have time to talk?
  • What do you often talk about?
  • Do some of your colleagues say something bad about other people?
  • Do you believe what they say? How do you feel about it?
  • What was the last gossip you heard in your company?
  • How do you feel about that gossip?
  • How do others feel about it?
▼▼▼
ACT
Make a short presentation about gossip. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.
  • Your usual chitchat time with colleagues
  • The usual topics of gossip
  • How you feel about the rumors in your company
  • The last gossip you heard
  • How you felt about the last gossip you heard
  • How your colleagues felt about that last gossip
