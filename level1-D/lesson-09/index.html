TALKING ABOUT TELECONFERENCING
9
SEE
Read the story aloud.

John:

I have teleconferences with my global project team twice a week. Teleconferencing can be difficult because I can’t see my team members. I can only hear their voices.

I have to make sure I understand them clearly, so I ask them to speak slowly. I sometimes ask questions whenever I don’t understand what they’re saying.

We are having another teleconference next week. I’m preparing some slides so that they will be able to follow what I’m saying. I hope the slides will help them understand my ideas clearly. I’ll send them the slides before the teleconference.

▼▼▼
TRY
To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.
  • How often do you have teleconferences? Who do you have teleconferences with?
  • Is teleconferencing more difficult than having a face-to-face meeting?
  • Do you ask other participants to speak slowly? What do you tell them?
  • What do you do when you can’t understand them?
  • When is your next teleconference? Who will be the participants?
  • Would making slides help the other participants understand your ideas?
▼▼▼
ACT
Make a short presentation about teleconferencing. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.
  • The people you have teleconferences with
  • The difficulties of teleconferencing
  • How you handle difficult situations during teleconferences
  • Your next teleconference
  • How visual aids can help during a teleconference
