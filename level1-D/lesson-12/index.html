BIZMATES PROGRAM LEVEL 1IRANK DILESSON 12
TALKING ABOUT MISCOMMUNICATION
12
SEE
Read the story aloud.

John:

Miscommunication is a common problem in any company. Miscommunication can happen anytime in meetings, e-mail messages, or even normal conversations.

At TC Com, meetings are always recorded and sent out to all the attendees. In addition, employees are told to double-check e-mails before sending them out.

There was some miscommunication between me and my colleagues. I wanted to have a meeting last Wednesday, but some of them thought it was the following Wednesday. I realized my e-mail wasn’t very clear, so I had to reschedule the meeting.

▼▼▼
TRY
To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.
  • Is miscommunication a common problem in your company?
  • Where does miscommunication usually occur in your company?
  • What do you do to avoid miscommunication in meetings?
  • How can you avoid miscommunication in your e-mail messages?
  • Have you experienced miscommunication with someone? Who was it with?
  • What was the problem? How did they understand your message?
  • Who made the mistake? What did you do next?
▼▼▼
ACT
Make a short presentation about miscommunication. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.
  • Sources of miscommunication in your company
  • How you prevent miscommunication during meetings
  • How you prevent miscommunication in e-mails
  • Someone you’ve had some miscommunication with
  • A miscommunication issue between you and your colleagues
