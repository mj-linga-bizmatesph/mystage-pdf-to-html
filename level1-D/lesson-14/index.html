TALKING ABOUT TIME MANAGEMENT
14
SEE
Read the story aloud.

John:

At TC Com, employees are trained to prioritize their work. Employees are supposed to do the most important work first.

However, many employees often prioritize the work that they like. Poor time management like this can result in serious inefficiencies for the company.

I need to improve my time management skills. I often find myself doing a lot of overtime work at the end of the month. I’ll start to keep a To-Do list.

▼▼▼
TRY
To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.
  • Do you get any training on time management in your company?
  • How can employees manage their time well?
  • Do many employees have poor time management skills in your company?
  • What can be the result of poor time management skills?
  • How would you describe your skill level in time management?
  • What areas in time management do you need to work on?
▼▼▼
ACT
Make a short presentation about time management. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.
  • Time management training in your company
  • How your company likes its employees to manage their time
  • People with poor time management in your company
  • Possible results of poor time management
  • Your time management skills
  • Areas in time management you’d like to develop
