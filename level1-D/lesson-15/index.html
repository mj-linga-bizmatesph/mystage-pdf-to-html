TALKING ABOUT TEAMWORK
SEE
Read the story aloud.

John:

Our company values teamwork, so we have many opportunities to work in teams. We have projects, internal competitions, and even sports activities during summer.

My boss asked me to lead a project team. Our goal is to come up with a new marketing campaign. It’s my first time to lead a group, so I’m really nervous.

It’s very difficult to lead the team because two members oppose each other. In team meetings, when one of them expresses an opinion, the other person always disagrees. Disagreeing is OK, but they need to learn to work together. I think I need to help them understand each other better.

▼▼▼
TRY
To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.
  • How much does your company value teamwork?
  • Are there many opportunities to work in teams in your company?
  • Have you ever handled a team before? What is your goal as a team?
  • How did you feel when you first led a team?
  • Is it easy to lead a team? Why do you say so?
  • Does everyone in your team get along very well?
  • What can you or the team leader do to avoid problems?
▼▼▼
ACT
Make a short presentation about teamwork. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.
  • The value of teamwork in your company
  • The opportunities to work in teams in your company
  • Your first time in handling a team (and your objectives)
  • How you felt when you first handled a team
  • How you feel about leading a team now
  • How members of your team get along
  • Remedies to team issues
