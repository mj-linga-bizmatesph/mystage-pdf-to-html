TALKING ABOUT MANAGEMENT
1
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Sandy:

P-Chem has three types of managers. First-line managers are responsible for daily operations. They work with employees on a day-to-day basis.

Middle managers supervise first-line managers. And then, there are top managers who are responsible for the company’s overall performance. They have regular meetings for status updates and decision-making.

I’m a first-line manager in the finance department at P-Chem Corp. I have 10 people in my team who I have a lot of respect for. I listen to my staff members’ concerns. My goal is to hit our targets every month by making sure everyone in the team can work comfortably. I feel it is important for the manager to give the staff mental support by trusting them.

▼▼▼
TRY

To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.

  • How many types of managers are there in your company?
  • What does each type of manager do?
  • What is your or your boss’s management style like?
  • What does the manager do to help people in your team?
  • In your team, what’s the manager’s goal?
  • How do you feel about the manager’s approach to his/her goal?
▼▼▼
ACT

Make a short presentation about management. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.

  • The types of managers in your company
  • The responsibilities of managers
  • Your management style
  • Your boss’s management style
  • The manager’s goal
  • How you feel about the manager’s approach to his/her goal
