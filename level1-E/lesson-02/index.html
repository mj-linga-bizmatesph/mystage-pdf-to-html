TALKING ABOUT PRODUCTIVITY

SEE

Read the story aloud.

Sandy:

My responsibility is to help my staff work productively. I want them to do more work in less time. So, I have made three office rules to achieve this.

First, they must set goals every morning. Second, 2PM-3PM is quiet time. So, they must focus only on their work. And third, they can’t work overtime unless it is urgent. My trick for being productive is to get the hardest task done in the morning.

I’m worried about one of my staff, Tim. He often misses deadlines and has trouble setting daily goals. I really want to help him. However, it seems like he wants no help from me. I want him to become more open-minded, so I give him regular feedback.

▼▼▼
TRY

To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.

  • What are your responsibilities?
  • How do you make sure you complete your day-to-day tasks?
  • Have you or your team set any rules to increase productivity?
  • Do you have any tricks for being productive?
  • Are you worried about anyone in your team?
  • What can you or the manager do to help that person?
▼▼▼
ACT

Make a short presentation about productivity. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.

  • Your responsibilities
  • How you or your team maintain efficiency at work
  • Rules you have in your team to increase productivity
  • Tricks to be more productive
  • Someone in particular that you’re worried about in your team
  • The reasons you’re worried about this person
  • Ways to increase productivity of others
