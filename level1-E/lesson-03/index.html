TALKING ABOUT PERFORMANCE
3
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Sandy:

As a manager, I need to check my staff’s performance. I do this by having one-on-one evaluation meetings with each of them twice a year. I check their progress in the middle of the year, and then I check their goals again at the end of the year and give them feedback.

I ask them these three questions when it’s time to set goals: “Is your goal realistic?” “How will it help our customers or the company?” and “What new skills or knowledge will help you achieve it?”

Tim’s mid-year performance didn’t go well. He failed to achieve some of his goals. I asked him to change his goals a little and change his work style. He promised to work more productively to achieve his goals at the end of the year.

▼▼▼
TRY

To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.

  • How is each employee’s performance evaluated at your company?
  • How often are employees evaluated?
  • What are some important questions to ask when you set goals?
  • Did everyone in your staff perform well last year?
  • What goals did some of them fail to achieve?
  • What advice can you give to help them?
▼▼▼
ACT

Make a short presentation about performance. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.

  • How employees’ performance is evaluated at your company
  • How often evaluations take place
  • Important points to consider
  • How your team performed last year
  • The goals they failed to achieve
  • A piece of advice for people who struggle to achieve their goals
