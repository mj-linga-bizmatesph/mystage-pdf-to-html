BIZMATES PROGRAM LEVEL 1IRANK EILESSON 8
TALKING ABOUT DECISION-MAKING
8
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Sandy:

I’m the only decision-maker in my team. Decisions like scheduling meetings are easy. However, some decisions are more difficult, like deciding who to assign to a project.

When I’m unsure about what to do, I talk to my boss or other colleagues about it. However, I don’t always follow their advice. Sometimes I just go with what I feel is right.

I need to make a difficult decision soon. I’m very unhappy with Jenny’s performance. She often fails to meet expectations. I already talked to her about it. If I see no improvement in the next two weeks, I will have to let her go.

▼▼▼
TRY

To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.

  • Who are the decision-makers in your team?
  • What are some easy decisions? What are some difficult ones?
  • What do you do when you’re unsure about what to do? Who do you talk to?
  • Do you sometimes go with your gut feeling?
  • What’s a difficult decision you need to make soon?
  • Why is this a difficult decision?
  • What do you think your decision will be?
▼▼▼
ACT

Make a short presentation about decision-making. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.

  • The decision-makers in your team
  • The easy and difficult decisions you make
  • The people you talk to when you’re at a dead end
  • Situations you go by your instincts
  • A difficult decision you need to make soon
  • What you think your decision will be
