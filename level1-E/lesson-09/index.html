TALKING ABOUT LEADERSHIP
9
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Sandy:

I’ve been a manager for a year but I need to learn to be a leader more. Some of my colleagues have taken an MBA, so my boss has recommended me to try it, too. But I don’t think you can study to be a good leader.

Maybe what I need to do is work with a great leader. I have met a person like that before. She had a great personality, great listening skills and a very open mind. Talking with her made me feel important because she really listened to me. So when she gave me advice, I listened carefully, too.

I’m so motivated to learn more about leadership. I recently read a book about a famous business leader. According to him, leaders are not born, but they are created through self-study and experience.

▼▼▼
TRY

To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.

  • Are you interested in becoming a leader?
  • How do you think people become leaders?
  • Have you met a good leader before?
  • What makes that person a good leader?
  • Have you done any reading on leadership?
  • Who are the business leaders you respect?
  • What have you learned from them?
▼▼▼
ACT

Make a short presentation about leadership. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.

  • Your idea on how to become a good leader
  • A role model you have met
  • The characteristics of that role model
  • Books on leadership
  • Business leaders you respect
  • What you have learned from those business leaders
