BIZMATES PROGRAM LEVEL 1IRANK EILESSON 13
TALKING ABOUT REPORTING
13
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Sandy:

Reporting information is something that every businessperson has to do. I report to my direct boss and also managers of some other departments because I’m part of a few project teams.

Project members report to leaders on the status of a project. Managers report to executives on business performance. The sales department shares their weekly report with my department.

I need to report the company’s financial performance to my boss. I need to include key financial information, such as sales, costs, and profit. I will also give my opinion and make recommendations in my report.

▼▼▼
TRY

To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.

  • Is reporting information important in your job?
  • Who do you usually report to in your company?
  • Do other departments share information with your department?
  • What details do you include in your report?
  • Do you always include your opinion and recommendation in your report?
▼▼▼
ACT

Make a short presentation about reporting. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.

  • The importance of reporting information in your job
  • The people you usually report to
  • The information shared by other departments
  • The details you include in your report
  • The analysis and recommendation in your reports
