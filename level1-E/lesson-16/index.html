TALKING ABOUT PROCESSES
16
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Sandy:

I’ve set up many processes for my team. There’s a process for writing a report, taking a day off, booking a meeting room, and so on. We need these processes to keep work efficient.

If someone wants to take some time off, he or she needs to fill out a form. The employee needs to write down the reason for taking time off, too. Then, the employee needs to give at least a one-week notice for approval so that I can check the calendar.

Most people think this process is fair, but I’m starting to feel that writing the reason down is unnecessary. My staff work too hard, so I want them to use their paid vacation days more. They may take time off more often if I don’t ask them the reason.

▼▼▼
TRY

To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.

  • Does your team have many processes to follow?
  • What are these processes for?
  • Can you explain one of them?
  • What is the process for taking time off at your company?
  • How do you and your other colleagues feel about this process?
  • Can you think of a way to make some processes better?
▼▼▼
ACT

Make a short presentation about processes. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.

  • Examples of the processes you have at your company
  • The reasons you have these processes
  • The process for taking a day off
  • How you and your colleagues feel about a certain process
  • Your idea on improving processes
