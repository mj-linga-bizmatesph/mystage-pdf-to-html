TALKING ABOUT SPENDING
17
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Sandy:

At P-Chem, when business is good, the company has extra cash. P-Chem gives this extra money to employees through benefits or bonuses.

We sometimes make expensive purchases, like equipment and facilities. Also, P-Chem often re-invests its profits in staff training.

On the other hand, P-Chem needs to cut costs when business is bad. We usually do an audit to see where money is being spent. Then, executives ask department heads to reduce their costs. Right now, my company is eager to cut costs. The most common cost cutting activity is reducing overtime work.

▼▼▼
TRY

To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.

  • What does your company do with extra cash when business is good?
  • Does your company invest in staff training?
  • What does your company do when business is bad?
  • Is your company eager to cut costs now?
  • What’s the most common cost cutting activity in your company?
▼▼▼
ACT

Make a short presentation about spending. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.

  • How your company allocates its extra funds
  • The expensive purchases your company makes
  • What your company does when business is bad
  • The eagerness of your company to cut costs
  • The most common cost cutting activity in your company
