TALKING ABOUT DIVERSITY
18
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Sandy:

When P-Chem first started, there were only a few employees. However, as P-Chem became bigger, more types of people joined the company. Now, P-Chem has a diverse workforce.

We have people of different genders, ages, nationalities, backgrounds, and personalities. It’s sometimes difficult to manage so many different kinds of people.

All managers must receive diversity training. The goal is for us to understand and respect different ways of thinking. We also learn effective ways to communicate with our staff. I’ve learned a lot in these workshops.

▼▼▼
TRY

To prepare for the ACT Part, practice these questions with your trainer.

  • Do you know how your company started?
  • What kind of people started your company?
  • What’s the workforce like in your company now?
  • How diverse is your workforce?
  • Is the company doing a good job managing its workforce?
  • Does your company have any diversity training?
  • What can you learn from this kind of training?
▼▼▼
ACT

Make a short presentation about diversity. Keep these points in mind. Then, answer questions from your trainer.

  • The workforce when your company first started
  • Your company’s current workforce
  • The diversity of your company’s workforce
  • The challenges of managing a diverse workforce
  • Diversity training in your company
  • Things you can learn from diversity training
