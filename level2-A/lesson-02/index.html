Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 2: Explaining where you’re from

Biz Tip

To sound more natural, say, “So” or “Oh” or “And” in your conversation.

Biz Tip

When answering questions, follow three steps:

  • Give your answer
  • Add some details
  • Ask a question
See

Example

Wei:

So, where are you from?

Taro:

I’m from Chigasaki.

Wei:

And where’s that?

Taro:

It’s near Tokyo.

Wei:

I see.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

So, where are you from?

Taro:

Oh, I’m from Chigasaki. It’s a small city near Tokyo. And how about you?

Wei:

Oh, I’m from Nanjing. It’s a big city near Shanghai. So, what’s there to see in Chigasaki?

Taro:

Well, it’s near the beach, so a lot of people come from Tokyo to enjoy marine sports. Do you do any marine sports?

Wei:

No, not really, but I’ve always wanted to try surfing. Do you surf?

Taro:

No, I don’t, but a lot of my friends do. Do you …

Try

Practice using “So”, “Oh”, and “And” in your conversation. Also, be sure to give your answer, add some details, and ask a question to continue the conversation.

Example:

“Where do you live?” →

“Oh, I live in Fujisawa. It’s a small city near Yokohama. And where do you live?“

Try it with these questions.

“What are your hobbies?”

“Where do you work?”

“How was your weekend?”

“Do you have any plans tonight?”

Act

Talk about where you’re from with a business partner and try to continue the conversation by asking questions.
