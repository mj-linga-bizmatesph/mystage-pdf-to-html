Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 3: Using numbers
See

Biz Tip

To give yourself time to think, use these expressions:
Let me see
Let’s see,
That’s a good question.
Let me think.

Biz Tip

Unless necessary, it’s more natural to quote ballpark figures by using words like: About/around
Over/under
Roughly

Example

Wei:

So, how many people are there in your department?

Taro:

(long silence)… I think there are 112 people.

Wei:

Sounds like a huge department. How about the whole company?

Taro:

(long silence)… there are 10,273 employees in total..

Wei:

Wow, that’s a big workforce. I bet your company has a lot of customers too.

Taro:

Yes it does. We have 2,356,704 customers worldwide.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

So, how many people are there in your department?

Taro:

Let’s see, I’d say over a hundred people. That’s about a fifth of the number of employees in the Tokyo office.

Wei:

Sounds like a huge department. How about the whole company?

Taro:

That’s a good question. I’m not sure, but I think it’s somewhere around 10,000 employees.

Wei:

Wow, that’s a big workforce. I bet your company has a lot of customers too.

Taro:

Yes we do. We have roughly 2,000,000 customers worldwide.
