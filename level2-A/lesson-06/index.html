Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 6: Describing your typical day
See

Biz Tip

When you explain your typical day, it’s more meaningful to say why you need to do each task.

Biz Tip

To keep your conversation positive, also explain what you like about your job.

Example

Wei:

What’s it like being a sales representative at MMB?

Taro:

I go to work at nine o’clock every morning. I check my e-mails. I have meetings with two or three clients every day. I listen to their needs and recommend our products. At the end of the week, I report my work to my boss.

Wei:

It sounds like a busy job.

Taro:

Yes, I’m very busy.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

What’s it like being a sales representative at MMB?

Taro:

It’s hard work, but it’s also very exciting.

Wei:

Really? How so?

Taro:

Well, my job is not just to sell electrical wiring. I need to build trust with my clients.

Wei:

Right.

Taro:

So, I get to the office at 9AM to check e-mails from my clients. I respond immediately to questions so our customers don’t have to wait. Then, I usually visit two or three clients a day.

Wei:

So you have a lot of clients?

Taro:

Yes, I do. And each client is different. They have different needs and different requests. So, I have to be flexible and really listen to them. But when I can offer the perfect solution to them, I get a lot of satisfaction.

Wei:

I see you really like your job.

Try

Before you explain your typical day to a business partner answer these questions.

  • What are your main responsibilities?
  • What do you do in the morning?
  • What do you do in the afternoon?
  • Who do you work with?
  • What do you like about your job?
Act

Explain your typical day to a business partner.
