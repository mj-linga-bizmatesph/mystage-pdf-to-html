Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 7: Making a connection

Biz Tip

The first step to making a connection is to find something in common. It could be business or non-business. You can do this by asking questions.

Biz Tip

Another key point in making a connection is to look for ways to help your business partner.

See

Example

Taro:

So, is this your first time to attend this trade show?

Wei:

No, I come to this Tokyo Technology Trade Show every year.

Taro:

Oh, I see. How do you like it?

Wei:

It’s great. I meet so many people each year.

Taro:

That’s good. Well, I hope you enjoy the event.

Wei:

Thanks. It was nice meeting you.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

So, is this your first time to attend this trade show?

Wei:

No, I come to this Tokyo Technology Trade Show every year.

Taro:

Me too. Have you seen anything exciting here so far?

Wei:

Oh yes. I was really impressed with BT’s new web application.

Taro:

Yes, I saw that. It’s very similar to the one we use now.

Wei:

Really? We’re looking for something like that for our company.

Taro:

Well, the application that we’re using now is a different brand, but it’s very easy to use. I can give you the contact information of the salesperson if you’d like.

Wei:

Yes, that would be great. Here’s my contact information, by the way.

Try

Before making a connection with your business partner, answer these questions.

  • Where do you have opportunities to meet new people?
  • What type of people would you like to make connections with?
  • What valuable information could you give these people?
Act

Make a connection with a business partner by finding something in common and looking for ways to help him or her.
