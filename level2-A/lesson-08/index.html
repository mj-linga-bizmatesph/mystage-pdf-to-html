Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 8: Describing company facilities

Biz Tip

It’s a good idea to be knowledgeable of your company’s overseas facilities as this knowledge could lead to potential partners.

Biz Tip

When explaining your company’s facilities, explain the location, size, and function of the facility.

See

Example

Wei:

So, does MMB have offices overseas?

Taro:

Oh yes, we have many offices and factories overseas.

Wei:

Oh really? Do you have a manufacturing plant in China?

Taro:

I think so, but I’m not sure. I’m sure we have a branch office in Shanghai.

Wei:

Oh, what does the Shanghai office do?

Taro:

Oh, I have no idea. I never have contact with them.

Wei:

Oh, I see.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

So, does MMB have offices overseas?

Taro:

Yes, we do. Our main factories are in Japan, but we have a wiring plant in Thailand, too. We also have an R&D center in Singapore. And we have sales offices in China and Malaysia.

Wei:

Oh, what does the office in China do?

Taro:

Well, we have a small sales team in the China office and a couple of engineers to provide our China-based customers with technical support.

Wei:

I see.

Try

Before you describe your company’s facilities to a business partner, answer these questions.

  • How many offices/factories does your company have in Japan and overseas?
  • How big are those facilities and what are their functions?
  • Which is the biggest and the most important?
Act

Describe the facilities of your company to a business partner.
