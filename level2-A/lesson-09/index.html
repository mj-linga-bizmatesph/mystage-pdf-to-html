Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 9: Explaining career opportunities
See

Biz Tip

To build relationships in business, always look for ways to help your business partner.

Biz Tip

The new global currency is information. Be willing to share information you have and to ask for information you want.

Example

Wei:

Are MMB employees outside of Japan all Japanese?

Taro:

I don’t think so. We have many local staff based in overseas locations. I believe they’re hired locally.

Wei:

I have a friend working in sales in Malaysia and he’s interested in changing jobs. Who should he contact to get more information about available positions at MMB Malaysia?

Taro:

Sorry. I don’t know anyone in MMB Malaysia.

Wei:

That’s OK.

Taro:

Maybe he can get more information from their website.

Wei:

Okay, I’ll let him know.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

Are MMB employees outside of Japan all Japanese?

Taro:

I don’t think so. We have many local staff based in overseas locations. I believe they’re hired locally.

Wei:

Oh really? I have a friend working in sales in Malaysia and he’s interested in changing jobs. Who should he contact to get more information about available positions at MMB Malaysia?

Taro:

I don’t know anyone in MMB Malaysia, but I might be able to get more information from our HR department.

Wei:

I don’t want to trouble you.

Taro:

It’s no trouble at all. Let me get back to you. By the way, do you know anyone in China who can help me with…

Try

Before you explain career opportunities to a business partner, answer these questions.

  • What career opportunities are there in your company?
  • What is the process for hiring overseas and locally at your company?
  • Do you know anyone working at an overseas location in your company?
  • Where can you get more information about available positions at overseas locations in your company?
Act

Explain career opportunities at overseas locations to your business partner.
