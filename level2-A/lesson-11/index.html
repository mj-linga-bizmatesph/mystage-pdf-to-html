Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 11: Explaining your company history

Biz Tip

When explaining your company history, give it a personal touch to make it sound more interesting.

Biz Tip

Instead of a one-way speech, ask your business partner questions to maintain his or her interest.

See

Example

Wei:

How did your company get started?

Taro:

Our company was founded in 1927 by Junpei Shinoda. In 1952, his son took over the company. In 1981, we were listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange. In 1998, we merged with Takano Wiring Co. to become MMB Corp.

Wei:

So, MMB has a long history.

Taro:

Yes, that’s right.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

How did your company get started?

Taro:

Our company was founded in 1927 by Junpei Shinoda. Have you heard of Junpei Shinoda?

Wei:

No, I haven’t.

Taro:

He was an engineering genius. He had worked in the US and could speak English very well. Do you know anyone who worked overseas 85 years ago?

Wei:

No, I don’t. That’s amazing.

Taro:

Yes, he patented many new wiring designs and started his own company in Japan. In 1952, his son, Kotaro, took over the company. What do you think his major was in university?

Wei:

I don’t know. Engineering?

Taro:

No, politics. He wanted to become a politician. But, …

Try

Think of some interesting facts about your company history. Use these questions to help you.

  • When and by whom was your company founded?
  • What type of person was the founder?
  • What was the founder’s dream?
  • Did your company go through any mergers or acquisitions?
  • Was your company ever listed on the stock exchange?
  • When and how did the company begin to grow?
Act

Explain your company’s history to a business partner. Give it a personal touch and be sure to ask some questions to keep your business partner interested.
