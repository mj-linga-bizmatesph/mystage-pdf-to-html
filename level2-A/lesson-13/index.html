Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 13: Explaining your customers
See

Biz Tip

When describing who your customers are, start with customer groups.

Biz Tip

It’s also good to give some examples using “like” or “such as”.

Example

Wei:

So, who are your customers?

Taro:

We have many customers. They are manufacturers.

Wei:

Like electronic companies?

Taro:

Yes, but we also sell to car makers.

Wei:

I see.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

So, who are your customers?

Taro:

Well, we have three main customer groups: transportation companies, like airlines and train companies, and electronic companies, such as audio equipment manufacturers, and medical equipment companies.

Wei:

So, your company has a wide customer base. Which customer group is the most important?

Taro:

All three are really important. Transportation companies make up over half of our sales, but the profit margin is much lower than sales from electronic companies and medical equipment companies.
