Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 15: Describing your company’s performance

Biz Tip

When explaining your company’s performance, give some information about the industry as a whole instead of just your company.

Biz Tip

Focus on the non-financial as well as financial performance.

See

Example

Wei:

How’s business at MMB?

Taro:

Not very good. It’s a tough situation for us now.

Wei:

I’m sorry to hear that. Why is that?

Taro:

Car sales in Japan are low and we have many foreign competitors now.

Wei:

I see. I hope things get better.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

How’s business at MMB?

Taro:

Financially, it’s been very tough for the whole industry. Car sales are down, so demand for wiring is down. But we have some good news too.

Wei:

That’s great. Like what?

Taro:

Well, this year we’ve already registered 20 new patents and we received a global award from FT Trade Magazine. So, our brand awareness is going up. We also opened a new factory in Thailand last month.

Wei:

So, it’s been a good year so far.

Taro:

Yes, I think so.

Try

Before you explain your company’s performance to a business partner, answer these questions.

  • How is the business performance of your industry as a whole?
  • How are your company’s sales and profit compared to company targets?
  • Has your company improved any processes or systems?
  • Has your company opened new facilities?
  • Has your company started any new projects?
Act

Explain your company’s performance to a business partner.
