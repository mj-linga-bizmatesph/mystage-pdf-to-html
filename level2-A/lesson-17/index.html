Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 17: Describing your corporate culture

Biz Tip

Every company has its own unique culture, so avoid saying it is typical.

Biz Tip

Many factors affect corporate culture, including the industry, the leader, the corporate structure, the history, the rewards system, the people, and globalization.

See

Example

Wei:

What’s the corporate culture like at MMB?

Taro:

We are a typical Japanese company.

Wei:

Do you mean everyone works hard and is loyal to the company?

Taro:

Yes, and we must listen to our boss.

Wei:

I didn’t know all Japanese companies were like that.

Taro:

I think most Japanese companies are.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

What’s the corporate culture like at MMB?

Taro:

Our employees are product-minded, so we care about quality and perfection. We have many rules and we are very careful not to make mistakes. We have a process for everything.

Wei:

It sounds like a very strict culture.

Taro:

It is, but I think it’s necessary to make top quality products. Another unique characteristic is that we are like a family business. Everyone has met the president and most people stay with MMB for their entire career.

Wei:

That sounds like a wonderful place to work.

Try

Before you explain your corporate culture to a business partner answer these questions.

  • What is considered to be most important in your company?
  • What type of employees are successful in your company?
  • What are some things that employees must never do in your company?
  • What historical events are important to your company?
  • What are your company’s long-term goals?
Act

Explain your company’s corporate culture to a business partner.
