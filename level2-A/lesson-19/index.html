Level 2 - Rank A - Lesson 19: Describing your company’s future

Biz Tip

When explaining the company’s future, use SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats) to help organize your thoughts.

Biz Tip

To sound positive, explain the weaknesses and then the strengths, same with threats and opportunities.

See

Example

Wei:

So, how does the future look for MMB?

Taro:

I hope we have a good future.

Wei:

I mean what are some of the key internal factors that affect your business?

Taro:

I’m sorry I don’t understand.

Wei:

Well, what are your company’s strengths and weaknesses?

Taro:

To be honest, I’ve never really thought of that before.

Bizmates Style

Wei:

So, how does the future look for MMB?

Taro:

We have many challenges ahead, but I’m confident that MMB has a bright future.

Wei:

That’s good to hear.

Taro:

Our prices may be a little higher than our competitors, but we have excellent technology and a strong brand image.

Wei:

I see.

Taro:

Although, more foreign competitors are entering the Japanese market, we are not worried because technology is getting more advanced, so companies need more specialized wiring that only we can make.

Wei:

Then, the future looks good for MMB.

Try

Before you explain your company’s future to a business partner answer these questions.

  • Strengths: What are your company’s unique selling points?
  • Weaknesses: What areas could your company improve in?
  • Opportunities: What changes, such as technology, government policy, and social trends, can positively affect your company’s business?
  • Threats: What changes, such as technology, government policy, and social trends, can negatively affect your company’s business?
Act

Explain your company’s future to a business partner.
