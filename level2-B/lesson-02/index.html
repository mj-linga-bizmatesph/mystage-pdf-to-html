Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 2: Running into an old contact
See

Biz Tip

Meeting an old contact is an excellent opportunity to develop the business relationship.

Biz Tip

So make the effort to remember your contact and to continue the relationship.

Example

(Taro has just arrived at his company.)

Bill:

Hi, have we met before? You look really familiar.

Taro:

No, I don’t remember.

Bill:

Maybe we met at a trade show or a networking party.

Taro:

I don’t know.

Bill:

Did you go to the Osaka Technology Awards Ceremony last year?

Taro:

No, I didn’t.

Bill:

Oh OK. Anyway, I’m here on assignment in the IT department. Maybe I’ll see you around.

Taro:

OK, see you.

Bizmates Style

(Taro has just arrived at his company.)

Bill:

Hi, have we met before? You look really familiar.

Taro:

Oh hi. I’m sorry I can’t remember. Do you work here?

Bill:

No, I’m just here on assignment in the IT department.

Taro:

I see. What company are you with?

Bill:

I’m an IT consultant with Techno Consulting.

Taro:

Oh I see. Maybe we exchanged business cards at the Tokyo Technology trade show last October.

Bill:

Yes, I was there. I remember now. You work in sales right?

Taro:

Yes, that’s right. I’m sorry I can’t remember your name.

Bill:

That’s OK. I’m Bill Chatterton. Let me give you my card again.

Taro:

Yes, and let me give you mine. I’m Taro Sato. Just let me know if I can help you with anything while you’re here.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • When was the last time you’ve met an old contact?
  • How long have you not seen him/her?
  • What did you talk about?
Act

Imagine you’ve run into an old contact you can’t really remember. Make effort to remember the person and build the relationship.
