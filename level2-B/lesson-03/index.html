Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 3: Inviting your colleague out

Biz Tip

A sudden invitation by someone you don’t know very well could be intimidating.

Biz Tip

When inviting someone out, it’s polite to ask if he or she is free and to give them some background information first.

See

Example

Taro:

Hi Li. Do you want to go for lunch?

Li:

Ah …, well, …., ah …, who’s going?

Taro:

Probably Kenji and Yuko.

Li:

Ah …, where are you going?

Taro:

We haven’t decided yet. Maybe to the Italian place across the street.

Li:

Ah …, when are you going?

Taro:

Maybe in about 15 minutes or so.

Li:

Ah, … OK, sure.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Hi Li. Have you already had lunch?

Li:

No, not yet.

Taro:

Well, Kenji, Yuko, and I are planning to go to that Italian place across the street. Would you like to join us?

Li:

Sure. What time are you leaving?

Taro:

In about 15 minutes or so.

Li:

Sounds good.

Try

Practice inviting a colleague out to the following events:

  • A sports event
  • A round of golf
  • Drinking
Act

Think of an event you’d like to invite your colleague out to.
