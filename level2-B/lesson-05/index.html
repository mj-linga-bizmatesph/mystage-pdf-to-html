Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 5: Telling time

Biz Tip

We don’t usually use the 24-hour clock to read time in English. Instead, we use AM/PM or in the morning, afternoon, evening, or at night.

Biz Tip

You can also use the following expressions when referring to another time zone: Ahead/behind by x hours A day ahead, minus x hours A day behind, plus x hours etc.

Example

Li:

So, what time is our flight to New York on Tuesday (JST), Taro?

Taro:

We need to be at the airport by 19:30.

Li:

You mean 9:30?

Taro:

No, 7:30 in the evening.

Li:

OK, what time do we arrive in New York?

Taro:

The flight’s about 12 hours. So, we’ll arrive at around 7:30AM (JST).

Li:

And what time would that be in New York?

Taro:

Let’s see. It should be 6:30PM in New York (EDT).

Li:

But our meeting is at 3PM.

Taro:

No, 6:30PM on Tuesday.

Li:

Really? I’m confused.

Bizmates Style

Li:

So, what time is our flight to New York on Tuesday (JST), Taro?

Li:

OK, what time do we arrive in New York?

Taro:

The flight’s about 12 hours. So, we’ll arrive at around 7:30AM (JST).

Li:

And what time would that be in New York?

Taro:

Let’s see. Japan is ahead by 13 hours. So, New York is a day behind, minus 1 hour. And just change AM to PM.

Li:

So, we’ll arrive at 6:30 in the evening on Tuesday (EDT).

Taro:

That’s right.
