Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 7: Giving advice

Biz Tip

Make sure you have sufficient background information before you make suggestions.

Biz Tip

When giving advice, explain how you feel and then make a suggestion as a question. “I’m thinking…” “Is it possible to…”

See

Example

Taro:

Have you finished your presentation slides?

Li:

Yeah, can you take a look at them?

Taro:

Sure. Oh, you should make the font size bigger.

Li:

Well, it’s size 24. And it’s not a very big audience.

Taro:

Well, it’s too small. Trust me. Also, you shouldn’t have so many colors. It’s very confusing.

Li:

Well, the colors are supposed to make it clearer.

Taro:

Do you want my feedback or not?

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Have you finished your presentation slides?

Li:

Yeah, can you take a look at them?

Taro:

Sure. How many people are going to be in the audience?

Li:

About 20-30.

Taro:

I’m thinking that some people in the back might find it difficult to read some of the slides. Do you have enough space to make the font size a bit bigger?

Li:

Yeah, I can make it a bit bigger.

Taro:

Do these colors you used have a special meaning?

Li:

Yes, each color represents a different product.

Taro:

I see. I’m thinking some people might not understand that. Maybe you could add a legend at the bottom?

Li:

That’s a good idea. Thanks.

Try

Before giving your colleague some advice, answer these questions.

  • What’s a common mistake that new employees make in your company?
  • What kind of advice do you give them and how do they react?
  • What could be the positive outcome of your advice?
Act

Give your colleague some advice.
