Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 8: Complaining to your colleague

Biz Tip

It sounds very direct in English when you say, “Please do …” or “Please don’t.” It’s better to phrase it as a question.

Biz Tip

When making a request like this, show that you understand your colleague’s situation, explain your situation, and then make your request.

See

Example

Taro:

Li, please don’t speak so loudly over the phone. I can’t concentrate on my work when you’re talking so loudly.

Li:

Sorry, I was speaking to a customer.

Taro:

Yes, but many people are complaining about it.

Li:

Who’s complaining?

Taro:

Everyone.

Li:

Well, I’m just trying to do my work.

Taro:

Me too.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Li, I know you’re always busy with so many business calls from customers, but I really need to concentrate on my work to finish my project. Could I ask you to speak a little more quietly when you’re at your desk?

Li:

I’m sorry. I didn’t realize I was speaking so loudly.

Taro:

It’s not your fault. It’s just that our desks are so close together.

Li:

OK, I’ll be a little more careful next time.

Taro:

Thanks a lot, Li. I really appreciate it.

Try

Before asking your colleague to stop doing something, answer these questions.

  • What are some things that your colleagues do that you find annoying?
  • What would you say to a colleague who never refills the paper in the copy machine?
  • What would you say to a colleague who makes inappropriate jokes at the office?
  • What would you say to a colleague who is always late with his/her assignments?
Act

Complain to your colleague.
