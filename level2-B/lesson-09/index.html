Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 9: Calming someone down
See

Biz Tip

Telling someone to calm down often has the opposite effect.

Biz Tip

To calm someone down, it’s best to just listen to them in private. Once you completely understand their situation, offer a suggestion if appropriate.

Example

Li:

I hate my boss!

Taro:

What’s wrong with you?

Li:

Don just rejected my request for time off. It’s so unfair.

Taro:

Well, you should have made your request earlier.

Li:

I couldn’t because I was too busy working on the sales campaign. I should complain to our director.

Taro:

Calm down, Li. It‘s not the end of the world.

Li:

Calm down? How can I calm down?

Bizmates Style

Li:

I hate my boss!

Taro:

Why, Li? What’s the matter? Come on, let’s talk in private.

(Li and Taro go to a private room.)

Taro:

OK, what’s the problem?

Li:

Don just rejected my request for time off. It’s so unfair.

Taro:

Why did he do that?

Li:

He said that someone else had already blocked the same time off.

Taro:

Oh, that’s too bad. Where were you planning to go?

Li:

I was hoping to visit my parents in China next month for Chinese New Year.

Taro:

I see. Well, let’s try to think of some possible solutions. Could you change the dates of your trip a little?

Li:

Maybe. I have to check with my travel agent.

Try

Before calming your colleague down, answer these questions.

  • What are some reasons your colleagues get really upset about work or the company?
  • How do you usually try to calm them down?
  • What solutions could you come up with for them?
Act

Calm down your colleague.
