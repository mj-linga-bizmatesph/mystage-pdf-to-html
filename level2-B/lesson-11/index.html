Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 11: Setting up a meeting

Biz Tip

When scheduling a meeting, always give the other person necessary information, such as the purpose, the agenda, and attendees.

Biz Tip

When scheduling a meeting, give the other person a choice of dates and times.

See

Example

Taro:

Li, I’d like to have a meeting with you tomorrow.

Li:

To talk about what?

Taro:

I’d like to talk about our new sales campaign.

Li:

Well, I’m pretty busy tomorrow.

Taro:

When are you free?

Li:

Well, how urgent is this?

Taro:

Very urgent.

Li:

OK, how about Wednesday afternoon?

Taro:

OK, I’m free at 3PM.

Li:

3PM is fine, but how long do we need?

Taro:

Maybe 1 hour.

Li:

OK, what’s the agenda?

Taro:

Agenda? I want to talk about the new sales campaign.

Li:

Taro, I think we need to change how to schedule meetings.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Li, how’s your schedule looking this week?

Li:

I’m pretty busy today and tomorrow, but I’m free the rest of the week.

Taro:

OK, could Mr. Kato and I have about an hour to talk about the new sales campaign either Wednesday afternoon or Thursday morning?

Li:

Sure, how about on Wednesday from 3:00 to 4:00?

Taro:

That’s perfect. Here’s the draft agenda. Please let me know if you’d like to add anything.

Li:

OK, I’ll let you know by Tuesday.
