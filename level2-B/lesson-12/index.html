Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 12: Expressing your opinion

Biz Tip

Instead of using negative words, like ineffective or poor, try to say it in a neutral way.

Biz Tip

When expressing your opinion, be sure to give reasons for your opinion and evidence to support your reasons.

See

Example

Li:

So, how’s our new sales campaign going?

Taro:

I think it’s ineffective.

Li:

What do you mean?

Taro:

The results of the campaign are very poor.

Li:

It’s still too early to say that.

Taro:

But it’s also very expensive.

Li:

Well, we need to spend money to make money.

Taro:

Yes, but …

Li:

Taro, maybe we should wait a little longer before we make any changes.

Bizmates Style

Li:

So, how’s our new sales campaign going?

Taro:

We’re not getting the results we expected.

Li:

What do you mean?

Taro:

Well, we’re not getting enough new customers. Our target was 100 last month, but we only got 60.

Li:

I see.

Taro:

It’s also costing us a lot of money. We’ve already gone over our budget by 10%.

Li:

That’s a big problem. We need to make some changes.

Try

Practice expressing your opinion and give reasons and evidence to support it.

  • Is it better to work for a big corporation or a small family business?
  • Is it better to stay with the same company for a long time or to change jobs often?
  • Is it better to work overseas or in Japan?
Act

Express your opinion on a work-related topic to a colleague and support it with reasons and evidence.
