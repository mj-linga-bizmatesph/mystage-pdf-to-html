Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 14: Agreeing and disagreeing

Biz Tip

Here are some common ways to agree: That’s a great idea. I totally agree. I’m right with you. I’m all for that.

Biz Tip

When you disagree with someone, show respect by using some of these phrases: That’s a good point, but … I see what you mean, but … I see where you’re coming from, but…

Example

Li:

OK, so let’s get some feedback from real clients.

Taro:

Yes, I approve of your idea.

Li:

In my opinion, we should first talk to our long-term clients.

Taro:

No, I disagree with you.

Li:

We can find out why our loyal customers have used our products for so many years.

Taro:

No, that’s not good.

Li:

What is your problem!

Bizmates Style

Li:

OK, so let’s get some feedback from real clients.

Taro:

I totally agree.

Li:

In my opinion, we should first talk to our long-term clients.

Taro:

That’s a good idea, but I think it’s our new customers who can really tell us why they selected us this time.

Li:

But we can find out why they have used our products for so many years.

Taro:

I see where you’re coming from, but don’t you think our prospective customers would be more interested in why our new customers chose us over our current competitors?

Li:

That’s true.

Try

Practice agreeing and disagreeing on the following topics.

  • One person thinks education is more important than experience to be successful; the other thinks experience is more important.
  • One person thinks corporate taxes should be higher; the other thinks corporate taxes should be lower.
Act

Imagine your company is planning to make English its official corporate language. Discuss this with your colleague. Be sure to show agreement and disagreement.
