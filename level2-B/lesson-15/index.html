Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 15: Confirming information

Biz Tip

When expecting the answer to be YES,
Example: (I think he likes sushi) You like sushi, don’t you? When expecting the answer to be NO, Example: (I don’t think he likes sushi) You don’t like sushi, do you?

Biz Tip

Note that we answer tag questions the same way we answer a standard question. EXAMPLE:

  • Are you Jun? - Yes, I am.
  • You are Jun, aren’t you? - Yes, I am.
  • You aren’t Jun, are you? - Yes, I am.

Example

Taro:

I want to confirm some points about the sales campaign.

Li:

Yes, go ahead.

Taro:

Will we ask our salespeople to speak to our clients?

Li:

Yes, that’s the plan.

Taro:

Will they speak to clients from different industries?

Li:

Yes, that’s right.

Taro:

Will they speak to our loyal clients?

Li:

No, it was your suggestion to speak to new clients.

Taro:

OK, I just wanted to check. Thank you.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

I want to confirm some points about the sales campaign.

Li:

Yes, go ahead.

Taro:

We’ll ask our salespeople to speak to our clients, right?

Li:

Yes, that’s the plan.

Taro:

And they’ll speak to clients from different industries, won’t they?

Li:

Yes, that’s right.

Taro:

They won’t speak to our loyal clients, will they?

Li:

No, they won’t.

Taro:

OK, I just wanted to check. Thank you.

Try

Practice changing these standard questions into tag questions.

  • Are you from Tokyo?
  • Do you play golf?
  • Did you work yesterday?
  • Will you go to bed early tonight?
  • Have you already had dinner today?
Act

Confirm information with your colleague using tag questions.
