Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 16: Asking for a favor

Biz Tip

When asking for a favor, it’s better to explain your problem before making your request.

Biz Tip

When asking for a favor, check to see if your colleague is available, explain your problem, and then make your request.

See

Example

Taro:

Li, I have a favor to ask you.

Li:

OK, what is it?

Taro:

Could you explain our sales campaign to Mr. Kondo from marketing at 3:00 PM today?

Li:

Today? Why?

Taro:

Well, I’m not feeling very well. I’d like to go home.

Li:

Oh, I see. I wish you would have told me a little earlier.

Taro:

Sorry.

Li:

OK, I can do it this time.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Li, do you have anything scheduled from 3:00 PM today?

Li:

No, I’m free. Why?

Taro:

Well, I’m not feeling very well, so I’d like to go home.

Li:

Yeah, you don’t look too well.

Taro:

But I have a meeting with Mr. Kondo to explain our sales campaign. If it’s not too much trouble, could you cover for me?

Li:

Sure, Taro. I’m familiar with the details. I can do it for you.

Taro:

Thank you so much. I owe you one.

Try

Before making a request to your colleague, answer these questions.

  • What sort of favors do you sometimes ask your colleagues to do for you?
  • How do you deal with the situation when they say no?
  • What favors do your colleagues ask you to do for them?
  • In what situations do you sometimes have to say no?
  • Have you ever returned a favor to someone? What did you do?
Act

Ask your colleague for a favor.
