Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 17: Sharing resources

Biz Tip

Your colleagues can be your best resource. Be willing to help and they’ll be willing to help you back

Biz Tip

It’s polite to show understanding of their situation and then share your resource.

See

Example

Li:

Taro, can you help me with my Japanese?

Taro:

Again?

Li:

I’m sorry. I’m e-mailing our Japanese clients to ask if we can get some feedback from them, but I don’t know how to say it.

Taro:

I’m quite busy now.

Li:

Can you help me with it?

Taro:

OK, OK, I can check it before you send it.

Li:

OK, much appreciated.

Bizmates Style

Li:

Taro, can you help me with my Japanese?

Taro:

Of course, Li. How can I help you?

Li:

I’m e-mailing our Japanese clients to ask if we can get some feedback from them, but I don’t know how to say it.

Taro:

That is a difficult task, even for me. We usually don’t ask our clients to do something for us, so we need to be very careful how we ask them.

Li:

Can you help me with it?

Taro:

Sure, I have some Japanese templates that I’ve created for myself. I think I have one for your situation too. Let me e-mail it to you and we can make some minor changes to it together.

Li:

Great, thank you so much, Taro.

Try

Before sharing resources with your colleague, answer these questions.

  • Do you always share resources with your colleagues?
  • What type of resources do you have that may be useful to your colleagues?
  • In what situations do you share resources like time, experience, skills, etc?
Act

Share some of your resources with a colleague.
