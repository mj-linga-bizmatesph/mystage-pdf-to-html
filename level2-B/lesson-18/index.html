Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 18: Expressing sympathy
See

Biz Tip

Avoid saying “I’m sure he’ll be fine,” especially when you don’t know what the future holds.

Biz Tip

When expressing sympathy, avoid asking for details. Express sympathy and offer help when appropriate.

Example

Taro:

Li, I heard your father’s sick. Is he OK?

Li:

No, he’s not. It’s quite serious.

Taro:

Oh, don’t worry. I’m sure he’ll be fine.

Li:

Actually, the doctor says he’s in pretty bad shape.

Taro:

Really? What happened to him? Is it that serious?

Li:

Sorry, Taro. I’d rather not talk about it.

Taro:

Cheer up. We’re here for you.

Li:

OK. Thanks.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Li, I’m really sorry to hear about your father. I know it’s a hard time for you. If there’s anything I can do, please let me know.

Li:

Thanks a lot, Taro. I’m planning to take a couple of weeks off to go see my father. So, I might need to ask you to cover for me while I’m away.

Taro:

Of course, Li. I’ll do whatever I can. Just let me know how else I can help.

Li:

I really appreciate it.

Try

Before expressing sympathy, answer these questions.

  • What are some situations where you may need to express sympathy to a colleague?
  • What would you say to a colleague who failed a promotion test?
  • What would you say to a colleague who was blamed for a mistake he/she didn’t do?
  • What would you say to a colleague who lost a big contract?
Act

Express sympathy to your colleague.
