Level 2 - Rank B - Lesson 19: Helping someone manage stress
See

Biz Tip

Avoid telling someone that they look stressed out as it could be taken as an insult.

Biz Tip

If you are really willing to help, ask what specific tasks your colleague needs to do, and offer to do the ones you can.

Example

Taro:

Li, you look stressed out.

Li:

It’s just that I have so many things to do before I go back to China next week.

Taro:

What can I do to help you?

Li:

It’s OK, Taro. I can do it.

Taro:

Are you sure?

Li:

Yeah, I’m fine. Thanks.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

You’ve been working late every day. Is everything all right?

Li:

It’s just that I have so many things to do before I go back to China next week.

Taro:

Maybe I can help you. What do you need to do before you leave?

Li:

Well, I have to prepare a lot of things for the upcoming seminar.

Taro:

What exactly do you have to prepare?

Li:

I have to contact several clients, write a report for Don, meet with the sales reps, and set up the seminar room.

Taro:

What is the report for Don about?

Li:

It’s a list of attendees and background of their companies.

Taro:

OK, I can write that report and I’ll set up the seminar room. Do you want it theater style or classroom style?

Li:

Ah, theater style would be great. Thanks a lot, Taro.

Taro:

No problem, Li.

Try

Explain what you can do to help a colleague in the following stressful situations:

  • Tons of paperwork.
  • A Project way behind schedule.
  • A report that needs to be submitted this afternoon.
  • Lack of focus because of other noisy colleagues.
  • A difficult problem that needs to be solved right away.
Act

Help your colleague manage stress.
