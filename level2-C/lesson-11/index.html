Level 2 - Rank C - Lesson 11: Setting up an appointment
See

Biz Tip

Create and specify a need for meeting before offering to visit someone. This rule applies to anyone you make an appointment with.

Biz Tip

When making an appointment, instead of saying “I can visit your office,” say, “I’d be happy to visit your office.”

Example

(The phone suddenly rings at Taro’s office.)

Taro:

MMB Corp., Taro Sato speaking. May I help you?

Kyong:

Hello, this is Kyong Lee with Korean Auto Systems. Ms. Wei Chan recommended I contact you about specialized electrical wiring.

Taro:

Yes, we make wires.

Kyong:

OK, can you send me a catalog and a pricelist?

Taro:

I can visit your office.

Kyong:

It’s not necessary.

Bizmates Style

(The phone suddenly rings at Taro’s office.)

Taro:

MMB Corp., Taro Sato speaking. May I help you?

Kyong:

Hello, this is Kyong Lee with Korean Auto Systems. Ms. Wei Chan recommended I contact you about specialized electrical wiring.

Taro:

Yes, Ms. Chan mentioned that you may be calling. How can I help you?

Kyong:

Can you send me a catalog and a pricelist?

Taro:

Yes, of course. We have a wide range of products. Do you know the specifications you need?

Kyong:

Yes, we’re looking for customized wires, which are environmentally friendly.

Taro:

I see. Our catalog only has our standard products; however, I’d be happy to visit your office to explain the specs and pricing of our customizable wires.

Kyong:

Oh OK, can you come to our office tomorrow?

Try

Before answering a phone call from your business partner, answer these questions.

  • In what situations do you usually set up an appointment over the phone?
  • What do you say to ask for the other person’s availability?
  • What do you say to suggest visiting your customer’s office?
Act

Answer a phone call from a business partner and set up an appointment.
