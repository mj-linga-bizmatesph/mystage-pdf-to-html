Level 2 - Rank C - Lesson 16: Writing a follow-up e-mail

Biz Tip

A standard “thank you” e-mail is OK, but try to make your e-mail a little more personal.

Biz Tip

Be sure to confirm the key points you decided and the action steps you promised.

See

Example

Subject:Confirming our meeting

Dear Mr. Lee,

Thank you for your time today.

I’ve attached our specifications for our Halogen-free wires.

Please let me know if you have any questions.

Taro Sato

Bizmates Style

Subject:Thank you for your time – July 14, 2012

Dear Mr. Lee,

It was a pleasure meeting you at Korean Auto Systems today. I was very impressed with your company’s new car model and hope that we can somehow be a part of it.

Here are the specifications of our Halogen-free wires. I’ll be sure to send you an estimate by July 16.

Please let me know if there’s any other information you need from us.

Best regards,Taro Sato

Try

Before writing an e-mail to your business partner, answer these questions.

  • Who did you have an external meeting with recently?
  • What did you talk about in the meeting?
  • Did you come up with any next steps in the meeting?
Act

Write a follow-up e-mail to someone to thank them for their time. Remember to thank your customer in the opening, state the next steps in the body, and leave a professional comment in the closing.
