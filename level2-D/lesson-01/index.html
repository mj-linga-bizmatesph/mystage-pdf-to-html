Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 1: Explaining your workweek
See

Biz Tip

Instead of telling your boss you are busy, say you have a productive week as it sounds more positive.

Biz Tip

Rather than a day-by-day explanation, place your tasks into understandable groups.

  • Important tasks
  • Ongoing projects
  • Routine work

Example

(Taro is meeting with his boss.)

Don:

Taro, can you give me a report on your tasks for this week?

Taro:

I’m very busy.

Don:

Taro, I didn’t ask you if you were busy. I asked for a report for this week.

Taro:

Sorry. Let’s see, this morning I have to make a report for the Marketing Department. After that, I will prepare a proposal for an important client. Next I will visit another client to introduce our products. After I come back, I will call our Singapore office.

Don:

Taro, I only need a summary.

Taro:

Sorry, Don.

Bizmates Style

(Taro is meeting with his boss.)

Don:

Taro, can you give me a report on your tasks for this week?

Taro:

Sure, this week is going to be very productive.

Don:

Great. Tell me about it.

Taro:

My most important task will be to visit HT Motors on Wednesday to close a major contract. It’ll be the biggest contract for me this year.

Don:

Good luck.

Taro:

Thanks. Another key task will be to complete my report for the Marketing Department.

Don:

Good.

Taro:

I’ll also be spending a lot of time on the GS project with the team in China. And finally, I’ll have all my routine tasks of…

Try

Before explaining your workweek to your boss, answer these questions.

  • What are three important things you’ll be working on next week?
  • What deadlines do you have next week?
  • What ongoing projects are you working on next week?
Act

Explain your workweek to your boss.
