Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 2: Giving your boss an update

Biz Tip

When explaining a problem to your boss, avoid saying it’s someone’s fault. Instead, state the facts clearly.

Biz Tip

A simple process for explaining a problem is to state the problem, explain your actions, and describe the results.

See

Example

Don:

OK, could you give me an update on the customer complaint?

Taro:

Of course. It’s not my fault. The shipping department made a mistake again. I can’t trust those people.

Don:

Taro, I’d like to know what the complaint was about, what action you took, and what the customer said.

Taro:

OK.

Bizmates Style

Don:

OK, could you give me an update on the customer complaint?

Taro:

The complaint is from our new client, Korean Auto Systems. We sent them the wrong order.

Don:

I see.

Taro:

So, I immediately called the shipping department, and had them deliver the correct order the next day. I went to the client’s office on that day to apologize and to explain the problem.

Don:

And how was the client?

Taro:

Of course they were upset at first, but they appreciated our quick response. And they seemed happy to receive a 10% discount on their next order.

Try

Before giving your boss an update, answer these questions.

  • What problems have you faced at work recently?
  • What actions did you take to solve them?
  • What was the result of your actions?
Act

Give your boss an update on something related to your work.
