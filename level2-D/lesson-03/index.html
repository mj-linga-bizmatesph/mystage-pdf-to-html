Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 3: Asking for approval

Biz Tip

When you call on someone at work, it’s polite to ask if he/she has a minute first.

Biz Tip

When asking for approval, give appropriate background information.
Also , instead of saying,
“You have to …”
“You must …”
“You need to …”
make it into a question.

See

Example

Taro:

Don, I have a request to make.

Don:

Taro, I’m a bit tied up right now. Could it wait?

Taro:

Actually, it’s a little urgent.

Don:

OK, what is it?

Taro:

You have to approve my business trip to China.

Don:

Have to? It doesn’t sound like a request. It sounds more like a demand.

Taro:

But I can’t go to China unless you approve it.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Don, could you let me know when you have a minute?

Don:

Sure, Taro. Give me 5 minutes to send this e-mail and then we can talk.

Taro:

Take your time.

(5 minutes later.)

Don:

Sorry to keep you waiting, Taro. What is it?

Taro:

I’m visiting our Shanghai sales office to make a presentation on our sales strategy. I’ll be there from August 7th to the 9th. Could I get your approval for my business trip to China?

Don:

Yes, I remember. Of course, I’ll approve that. But, could you give me a report after you get back?

Taro:

Yes, of course.

Try

Before asking your boss for approval, answer these questions.

  • What do you need to get approval for from your boss?
  • Does your boss always approve your requests?
  • What could you do to help you get approval from your boss?
Act

Get approval for something related to your work from your boss.
