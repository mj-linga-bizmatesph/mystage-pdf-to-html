Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 4: Saying no to your boss
See

Biz Tip

Avoid saying,
“No, I can’t.”
instead say,
“I’d love to but …” or
“I’d like to help, but …”

Biz Tip

When you say no to your boss, try coming up with an alternative solution.

Example

Don:

Taro, do you have a minute?

Taro:

Sure.

Don:

I promised Mr. Kan in finance to give him an update on projected sales for this month, but I have an important meeting scheduled at the same time. Could you meet with Mr. Kan for me next week?

Taro:

No, I can’t. I have to go to Shanghai.

Don:

OK, I guess I’ll have to reschedule my meeting with Mr. Kan.

Taro:

Sorry.

Bizmates Style

Don:

Taro, do you have a minute?

Taro:

Sure.

Don:

I promised Mr. Kan in finance to give him an update on projected sales for this month, but I have an important meeting scheduled at the same time. Could you meet with Mr. Kan for me next week?

Taro:

I’d really like to help, but I have to go to Shanghai next week. I’d be happy to meet with him before I leave or when I get back.

Don:

Mr. Kan is busy this week.

Taro:

I see. In that case, I can write up a short report and e-mail it to him if you’d like.

Don:

Actually, that would be a big help. Thank you.

Try

Before saying no to your boss, answer these questions.

  • What does your boss sometimes ask you to do?
  • What are some reasons you can’t do it?
  • What alternative solutions could you come up with for those situations?
Act

Say “no” to your boss when he/she asks you to do something. Be sure to give a good reason and try to propose an alternative solution.
