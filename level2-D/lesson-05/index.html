Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 5: Reporting after a business trip

Biz Tip

It’s a good idea to have your report written before you report to your boss.

Biz Tip

When reporting after a business trip, be sure to highlight the achievements to show that the company’s money had not been wasted.

See

Example

Taro:

Don, I’m back from Shanghai.

Don:

Welcome back. How was it?

Taro:

So-so. It was just work.

Don:

OK. So, could you give me a report on it?

Taro:

Yeah, I can talk now.

Don:

OK, please tell me about it, but I’d like it written up, too.

Taro:

OK, I’ll do that later. It was a bit of a waste of time. I presented our sales strategy and some people asked me some questions. And then, we went out for dinner.

Don:

It doesn’t sound like it was a very productive trip.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Don, thank you for approving my trip to Shanghai. It was really productive. By the way, I brought back some sweets for everyone.

Don:

Thanks for that. So, tell me about it.

Taro:

Sure. I made my presentation three times. First, to all the salespeople, then, one-on-one to the sales manager, and finally to some of their vendors.

Don:

How was their reaction?

Taro:

Overall, they seemed impressed with our plan. Some asked good questions, which I couldn’t answer. So, I’m putting together an FAQ sheet now. But the best thing is that I really connected with the local salespeople. I’m going to keep in touch with them.

Don:

Yes, please do that.

Taro:

I’ve also written up a report for you. I’ve put it on your desk.

Don:

Thanks for your quick work.

Try

Before giving your boss a report on your business trip, answer these questions.

  • Where did you go on your last business trip?
  • What was the purpose of the trip?
  • What did you accomplish during the trip?
  • What did you learn from your trip?
Act

Give your boss a report on your last business trip.
