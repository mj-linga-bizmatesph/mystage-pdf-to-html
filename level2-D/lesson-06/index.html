Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 6: Apologizing for something

Biz Tip

When you apologize, don’t make excuses. Take responsibility for your actions.

Biz Tip

Apologize, explain why you did it, explain why it was bad, and what you’ll do in the future.

See

Example

Don:

Taro, didn’t I ask you to take Kenji with you on your sales call to HT Motors?

Taro:

Yeah, but Kenji’s not ready to visit clients yet.

Don:

That’s not the point. I asked you to do something and you didn’t do it.

Taro:

Well, Kenji needs to develop his skills faster.

Don:

You’re his mentor. You need to train him.

Taro:

Yes, I will train him, but he needs more time.

Don:

Taro, I’m very disappointed with your actions.

Bizmates Style

Don:

Taro, didn’t I ask you to take Kenji with you on your sales call to HT Motors?

Taro:

Don, yes you did. And I’m sorry I didn’t follow through with your request. When I had a meeting with Kenji before the sales call to explain the contract, he seemed really nervous. So, I determined that he wasn’t ready to go.

Don:

You should have told me.

Taro:

I know. You specifically asked me to do something, and I didn’t do it. That was wrong of me. So, I’m going to do some training with Kenji tomorrow so that he’ll be ready for the next visit.

Don:

Taro, I trust your judgment, but I want you to let me know what’s going on.

Taro:

I understand. I promise I won’t do that again.

Try

Before apologizing to your boss, answer these questions.

  • What have you done to upset your boss in the past?
  • Why did you do those things?
  • What will you do in the future?
Act

Apologize to your boss for something that you did.
