Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 7: Asking for support

Biz Tip

It’s OK to say that you’re having trouble with something, but avoid saying that you can’t do something.

Biz Tip

When asking for support, don’t blame others. Think about what you can do to solve the problem.

See

Example

Taro:

Don, I have a problem.

Don:

What is it?

Taro:

I can’t train Kenji. He doesn’t have the skills to be a good salesperson.

Don:

But that’s your job. You need to train him.

Taro:

Yeah, but he won’t listen to me. When I tell him where he needs to improve, he makes excuses.

Don:

OK, maybe I should talk to Kenji.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Don, could I get your advice on something when you have a minute?

Don:

Yes, of course. What is it?

Taro:

Well, I’m having a little trouble training Kenji. I know Kenji’s trying hard, but he still has problems explaining our products clearly.

Don:

That’s a really difficult skill.

Taro:

I know. But now, when I give him some advice, he gets very defensive. I don’t know what I’m doing wrong. I really want to help him, but I don’t know how.

Don:

Well, this is your first time training someone, so it’s natural to have some difficulties. Let’s look at some ways to more effectively give feedback.

Taro:

I would appreciate that.

Try

Before asking your boss for support, answer these questions.

  • What kind of support do you ask your boss for?
  • What kind of support does your boss give you?
  • Tell me about the last time you got support from your boss.
Act

Ask your boss for support on something related to your work.
