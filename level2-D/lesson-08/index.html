Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 8: Giving your boss the facts
See

Biz Tip

When a serious issue like harassment occurs, it’s important to report all the facts, including dates, times, and exactly what was actually said and done.

Biz Tip

When reporting facts, refrain from getting emotional as it’ll often have a negative effect.

Example

Don:

Taro, I have some disturbing news. Kenji has filed a power harassment claim against you.

Taro:

What? Are you serious? That’s crazy!

Don:

I need to know the facts for human resources.

Taro:

Facts? The fact is he’s a bad salesperson, and he’s blaming me.

Don:

Calm down. Did you tell him that he would get fired if he didn’t make a sale soon?

Taro:

No. Not really.

Don:

Did you or did you not tell him that he would get fired?

Taro:

I told him that we would evaluate his performance at the end , and …

Bizmates Style

Don:

Taro, I have some disturbing news. Kenji has filed a power harassment claim against you.

Taro:

Really? May I ask what exactly the claim is?

Don:

Yes, he says that you told him that he would get fired if he didn’t make a sale soon.

Taro:

I see. Don, I did not say such a thing. Yesterday, I had a meeting with Kenji at 1PM to go over his one-month performance. I told him that his skills were getting better, but we needed to see results. He asked me if he would get fired if he didn’t make a sale soon. And I said we would evaluate his performance at the end of September.

Don:

So, you didn’t tell him that he would get fired?

Taro:

Absolutely not.

Try

Before giving your boss the facts, answer these questions.

  • What are some possible complaints that may be filed against a co-worker in Japan?
  • Has a similar complaint ever been filed in your office?
  • What would the process be to deal with a complaint like this?
  • What facts would be needed?
Act

Imagine that your colleague has filed a complaint against you. Explain to your boss the facts without getting emotional.
