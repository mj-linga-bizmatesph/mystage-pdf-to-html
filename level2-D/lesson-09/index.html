Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 9: Disagreeing with your boss

Biz Tip

Before you disagree with your boss, try to understand your boss’ point of view first.

Biz Tip

It’s OK to disagree with your boss, but you need to have a logical reason.

See

Example

Don:

Taro, it’s the end of Kenji’s development period, and we’ve decided to let him go.

Taro:

I think you’re making a mistake.

Don:

Why would you say that? He filed a power harassment claim against you.

Taro:

Yes, but he’s trying very hard. You should give him another chance.

Don:

I’m sorry, Taro, but I’ve made my decision.

Bizmates Style

Don:

Taro, it’s the end of Kenji’s development period, and we’ve decided to let him go.

Taro:

I see. May I ask why?

Don:

Well, for several reasons. His sales performance is poor, he’s been late, and you gave him a poor evaluation.

Taro:

Didn’t he miss his target by only 15%? Last quarter was bad for all the salespeople.

Don:

Yes, that’s true. But he lacks professionalism.

Taro:

Well, he’s still young. And he had a proper reason for being late.

Don:

I know, but even you gave him a bad evaluation.

Taro:

Kenji still has many areas to improve, but I mentioned in my evaluation that he has potential. So, if the main reason is because of my evaluation, I think there is a misunderstanding.

Don:

OK, let me talk this over with Mr. Kato again.

Try

Before disagreeing with your boss, answer these questions.

  • Have you ever disagreed with your boss?
  • What were your reasons for disagreeing with your boss? And what were his/her reasons?
  • Did your boss change his/her mind?
Act

Disagree with your boss about something related to your job.
