Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 11: Raising a concern

Biz Tip

Instead of saying you have a complaint, use the word concern.

Biz Tip

When you raise a concern, show that you are still positive and want to find a solution together.

See

Example

Taro:

Don, I have a complaint.

Don:

A complaint? OK, about what?

Taro:

I think my sales target is unrealistic.

Don:

Why do you say that?

Taro:

I’ve worked really hard this year, but I’m still 30% behind my target. It’s impossible to reach.

Don:

But we set your target together at the beginning of the year. You committed to it, right?

Taro:

Yes, but I’ve lost two big contracts which were out of my control. It’s not fair.

Don:

Before you give up, let’s look at other things you can do to increase sales.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Don, I’m sorry to bother you, but could I talk to you about something that I’m a little concerned about?

Don:

Sure, Taro. What is it?

Taro:

Well, I’ve managed to get several new contracts this year, but I’ve lost two big contracts which were out of my control. And because of that, I’m still 30% behind my target.

Don:

I know this year has been tough for you.

Taro:

I’ve committed to my sales target, so I want to do everything that I can to reach it before requesting a revision.

Don:

You’ve got the right attitude, Taro. Let’s talk about some things we can do to help you reach your goals. If it’s still looking unrealistic, we can request lowering them a little.

Taro:

Thanks for listening to my concern, Don. It means a lot to me.

Try

Before raising a concern with your boss, answer these questions.

  • What concerns have you had about your job in the past?
  • Have you ever raised them with your boss?
  • What solutions could you come up with to deal with your concerns?
Act

Raise a concern with your boss.
