Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 12: Accepting constructive criticism

Biz Tip

When your boss gives constructive criticism, the last thing he wants to hear is an excuse.

Biz Tip

Try to really understand your boss’ feedback, and show appreciation for it whether you accept it or not.

See

Example

Don:

Taro, I reviewed your proposal for HT Motors.

Taro:

Yes, what did you think?

Don:

I really liked the layout you used, but you have too much information on it. I think the customers are going to find it confusing.

Taro:

No, HT Motors always wants details.

Don:

But you mentioned the same information on some of the pages. Couldn’t you streamline some parts?

Taro:

No, that’s my style. The customer can understand it.

Don:

I’m just trying to help you, Taro.

Bizmates Style

Don:

Taro, I reviewed your proposal for HT Motors.

Taro:

Yes, what did you think?

Don:

I really liked the layout you used, but you have too much information on it. I think the customers are going to find it confusing.

Taro:

Yeah, do you think it’s too technical?

Don:

No, it’s not too technical, but you have the same information on some of the pages.

Taro:

Really? I didn’t realize that. Is there anything else?

Don:

I’m thinking maybe you should remove page 3. It’s too much information at this stage.

Taro:

OK, thanks for your advice.

Try

Before receiving constructive criticism from your boss, answer these questions.

  • What does your boss give you feedback on?
  • How does your boss usually convey his/her feedback? (constructively, negatively, confusing, etc.)
  • How do you feel about your boss’ feedback?
Act

Receive constructive criticism on a work-related topic from your boss.
