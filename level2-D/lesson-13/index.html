Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 13: Reporting good news

Biz Tip

It’s always a pleasure to report good news, but make sure you recognize everyone involved.

Biz Tip

Show you are focused on the merits to the company rather than an immediate win for yourself.

See

Example

Taro:

Don, great news!

Don:

What is it?

Taro:

HT Motors renewed their contract.

Don:

Excellent. Is it big?

Taro:

Yes, it’s 20% bigger than last year. I knew my approach was right.

Don:

Well, congratulations.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Don, I have some great news! HT Motors renewed their contract.

Don:

Excellent. Is it big?

Taro:

Yes, it’s 20% bigger than last year. Thank you so much for your support. I couldn’t have done it without you and Hiro.

Don:

Taro, don’t be so modest. You deserve the recognition.

Taro:

Thank you for saying so. I think this could be a very good case study, which we could share with the other salespeople. It can help us in getting other contracts.

Don:

That’s a great idea.

Try

Before reporting good news to your boss, answer these questions.

  • What are some examples of good news you report to your boss about?
  • Who deserves to be recognized in those situations?
Act

Report some good news to your boss. Be sure to recognize everyone involved.
