Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 14: Requesting for time off

Biz Tip

When requesting time off, show that this is an appropriate time for you to take time off.

Biz Tip

Reassure your boss that the office will be OK without you during that time.

See

Example

Taro:

Don, I need to take some time off. This project is just so stressful.

Don:

Well, who’s going to take over the project while you’re away?

Taro:

I only need a few days. Everything will be fine.

Don:

Taro, you’re managing this project. Don’t you think that’s irresponsible to be taking leave at this time?

Taro:

So, I can’t take a day off until this project is over?

Don:

I’m saying that you need to take responsibility as project manager.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Don, we’ve completed the first stage of our project and things have finally slowed down.

Don:

Well, I’m glad to hear that.

Taro:

Would it be possible to take a short leave before stage two of the project begins?

Don:

Well, who’s going to take over the project while you’re away?

Taro:

We’re in between stages, so it’s a slow time for everyone. However, I’ve asked Yasu to fill in for me, just in case. I’ll also leave my cell phone number so that I can be reached anytime.

Don:

OK, as long as the project can function without you.

Try

Before requesting for time off from your boss, answer these questions.

  • What is the process for requesting for time off at your company?
  • Has your boss ever rejected your request?
Act

Request for some time off from your boss.
