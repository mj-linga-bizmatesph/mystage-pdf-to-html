Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 15: Offering help
See

Biz Tip

When your boss is in trouble, that’s an excellent time to gain trust. So, do what you can to help.

Biz Tip

Be sure that you understand the details and are able to do the task before offering help.

Example

Taro:

Don, you look stressed out.

Don:

I am. I need to prepare this document for our Director by noon today, but I don’t know how to use this new program.

Taro:

OK, I can do it for you.

Don:

Great. I just need to transfer this data into the new program.

Taro:

No problem.

(An hour later.)

Taro:

Don, I don’t know how to create the layout.

Don:

What? My meeting with the Director is in 10 minutes!

Taro:

Sorry.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Don, is everything OK?

Don:

Not really. I need to prepare this document for our Director by noon today, but I don’t know how to use this new program.

Taro:

What do you need to do?

Don:

I just need to transfer this data into the new program.

Taro:

What about the layout?

Don:

Well, I want the categories to come up at the top and the dates to be along the left side.

Taro:

Do you have a template for this?

Don:

Oh maybe one of the managers has one.

Taro:

OK, if you can get a template, I should be able to transfer the data.

Don:

Great, let me ask some of the other managers.

Try

Before offering your boss some help, answer these questions.

  • What sort of trouble or inconveniences does your boss have?
  • What could you do to help your boss in these situations?
  • When was the last time you weren’t able to help your boss?
Act

Offer your boss some help.
