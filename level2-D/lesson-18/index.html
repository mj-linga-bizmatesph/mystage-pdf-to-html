Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 18: Confirming a rumor

Biz Tip

The best advice is to ignore rumors. However, if it’s something that really concerns you, confirm the rumor in private.

Biz Tip

Show understanding of the situation and appreciation for being open with you.

See

Example

Taro:

Don, someone said that you’re leaving the company. Is that true?

Don:

Taro, could we go somewhere private?

Taro:

Sure. So, is it true?

Don:

Who did you hear this from?

Taro:

I promised not to tell.

Don:

Well, it’s still not official, but just between you and me, it’s true. I’ve decided to move on to something new.

Taro:

That’s too bad. We’re going to miss you.

Don:

Thanks for saying so, but please keep this quiet.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Don, could I speak to you in private?

Don:

Sure, Taro. What’s on your mind?

Taro:

I heard this rumor from some of the guys. I usually ignore rumors, but it’s really bothering me.

Don:

What is it?

Taro:

Well, I heard that you may be leaving the company.

Don:

Yes, it’s true. It’s something that I have been thinking about for a long time. Then, a very good opportunity came up. So, I took it. This is still all unofficial.

Taro:

I understand, Don. I promise I won’t say anything. And I appreciate you being honest with me.

Don:

I’m sorry that you had to find out this way.

Try

Before confirming a rumor with your boss or business partner, answer these questions.

  • What sort of rumors do you hear in your company?
  • What do you usually do when you hear them?
  • What can you say to reassure the other person that you can be trusted?
Act

Confirm a rumor with your boss or colleague.
