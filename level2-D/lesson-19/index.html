Level 2 - Rank D - Lesson 19: Saying goodbye

Biz Tip

A goodbye is never really final. You never know when you’ll work with them again. So, try to keep in touch with important connections.

Biz Tip

It’s often difficult to know what to say to someone who is leaving the company. One common approach is to recall an event you shared, show appreciation and exchange contact information.

See

Example

(Today is Don’s last day of work.)

Taro:

Don, it was great working with you.

Don:

Thanks, Taro. I really enjoyed working with you, too.

Taro:

So, take care of yourself, and I hope I see you again someday.

Don:

Yeah, I hope so too.

Bizmates Style

(Today is Don’s last day of work.)

Taro:

Don, I can’t believe you’re really leaving.

Don:

I know. We had some good times together.

Taro:

Do you remember that time I missed our regional sales meeting because I had overslept?

Don:

How could I forget? I called your cell phone three times. And when you finally answered the phone, I knew right away that you were still in bed.

Taro:

I’m surprised you didn’t fire me for that.

Don:

I knew you had the potential to be a great sales rep. And I was right.

Taro:

Thanks for everything, Don. You’re the best boss I’ve ever had. I really mean it. Let me give you my personal e-mail address and phone number. Please contact me if you’re near here. I’d really like to keep in touch with you.

Don:

Of course. I’d really like that.

Try

Before saying goodbye to your boss or business partner, answer these questions.

  • Have you experienced people close to you leave your company?
  • What did you say to them?
  • Did you exchange contact information?
Act

Say goodbye to your boss or colleague who is leaving the company.
