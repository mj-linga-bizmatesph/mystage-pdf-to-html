Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 3: Explaining your expectations

Biz Tip

When explaining your expectations, start with an overview before going into details.

Biz Tip

To get commitment from the other person, encourage two-way communication.

See

Example

Taro:

During your time in the Japan office, I will be your boss.

David:

OK. I’m happy to hear that.

Taro:

For the first month, you will observe my work to understand our internal processes.

David:

Sure.

Taro:

Starting next month, we will visit clients together, so you can understand the needs of our customers.

David:

That’s fine.

Taro:

By the first quarter, I want you to have your own clients.

David:

Really? I’d better study Japanese.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

How are you doing so far?

David:

Just great. Everyone’s been so nice.

Taro:

I’m glad to hear that. Your boss in Shanghai, Mr. Wong, asked me to support you. So, my role is not to tell you what to do. I’m just here to help you achieve your goals for this assignment.

David:

I appreciate that.

Taro:

What would you like to achieve during your 1-year stay here?

David:

Well, I’d like to better understand our company’s direction, not just the sales department. Also, I’d like to learn some best practices from the Japanese sales reps.

Taro:

Good. I agree. What about connections?

David:

Oh yes, those are very important. I want to make…

Try

Before explaining your expectations to your trainee, answer these questions.

  • If a foreign staff were transferred to your department, what overall goals would he/she have?
  • What should the employee learn first?
  • What could you teach the employee?
Act

Go over the expectations of your department with a trainee.
