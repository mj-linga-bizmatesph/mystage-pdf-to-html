Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 4: Giving a verbal warning

Biz Tip

You can be clear about what you want done while still being polite.

Biz Tip

Office rules vary in some countries. So, it’s important to explain the reasoning behind the rule.

Example

Taro:

David, do you know cool biz ended last month?

David:

Oh yes, I know. I don’t have any appointments today, so I took my tie off.

Taro:

Oh, I see. But it’s good to have a tie, just in case.
Sometimes we get sudden appointments.

David:

No problem. I always have an extra tie at the office. I just hate neckties.

Taro:

Ah, but...

Bizmates Style

Taro:

David, do you know that cool biz ended last month?

David:

Oh yes, I know. I don’t have any appointments today, so I took my tie off.

Taro:

I see. During summer, it becomes acceptable in Japanese society to not wear one, but in other seasons, many customers expect salespeople to have ties.

David:

But I don’t have any client visits today.

Taro:

I know, but neither do the people in other departments. It wouldn’t be fair for only some of us to not wear ties. You understand that, right?

David:

Yes, I do.

Try

Before giving your trainee a verbal warning, answer these questions.

  • What are some rules that employees sometimes break?
  • Why do they break these rules?
  • What is the reasoning behind these rules?
Act

Give your trainee a verbal warning.
