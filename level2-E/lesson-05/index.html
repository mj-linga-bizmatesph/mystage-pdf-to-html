Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 5: Training someone

Biz Tip

When training someone, rather than saying,
“I will show you …”, say “We’re going to …”

Biz Tip

One effective method of training is to ask for the trainee’s knowledge and experience first.

See

Example

Taro:

Today, I will show you how to put a proposal together. First, you need to know the customer’s needs. So, you need to put that here.

David:

Really? We do it differently in our office.

Taro:

This is not your office. Please forget about what you learned in your office.

David:

OK.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Today, we’re going to look at putting proposals together. Could you tell me how you put proposals together in China?

David:

Yes, we always start with an introduction of our company. Many people in China don’t know MMB, so we explain the company history and introduce our product line. Then, we summarize the customer’s needs, explain our solution, and end with pricing.

Taro:

OK, that’s very good. We try to make the client’s needs the main message.

David:

Not our solution?

Taro:

No, not our solution. If we can show the client that we truly understand their needs, they will read our solution. If they don’t think we understand, they won’t read our proposal.

David:

I see.

Try

Before training your trainee, answer these questions.

  • What are some skills or tasks that new employees need to learn in your office?
  • What are the steps to doing these tasks?
  • What are some common mistakes that trainees make when doing these tasks?
Act

Train your trainee on something related to his/her work.
