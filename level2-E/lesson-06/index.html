Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 6: Giving feedback

Biz Tip

When giving constructive criticism, always start and end with positive feedback.

Biz Tip

When giving constructive criticism, be specific with your suggestions.

See

Example

Taro:

Have you finished your sample proposal?

David:

Yes, here it is.

Taro:

First, the font size is too small.

David:

OK.

Taro:

This is our old logo. Please change this.

David:

OK.

Taro:

The executive summary is too long. I don’t want to read it.

David:

Is there anything you like about it?

Taro:

Oh yes, the other parts are OK.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Have you finished your sample proposal?

David:

Yes, here it is.

Taro:

Wow, that was quick. Good job. I really like the body of your proposal. It’s short and has a very good storyline. I’m sure customers will be impressed with your solution.

David:

Thanks for saying so. Is there anything I should change?

Taro:

Well, I noticed some minor things. First, I suggest that you make the font size bigger; use at least size 24. Also, this logo is our old one. Let me give you our new one. And the executive summary could be a little shorter. Let’s keep it to a maximum of 120 words.

David:

OK, those are good points.

Taro:

But overall, I like the layout. Well done.

Try

Before giving your trainee feedback, answer these questions.

  • What are some positive points you can say to a trainee about his/her work?
  • What are some negative points you can point out to a trainee about his/her work?
  • What specific suggestions will you tell him/her?
Act

Give your trainee some feedback on a task related to his/her work.
