Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 7: Giving instructions
See

Biz Tip

When giving instructions, organize the steps by using words like

“first, next, finally.”

Biz Tip

After giving instructions, have the trainee summarize them to you.

Example

Taro:

David, I want you to do something. We’re conducting a customer survey.

David:

OK.

Taro:

I want you to put together a survey, but make sure you get some input from the sales reps. Then, hold an orientation with the reps to explain how to distribute it. And analyze the results and make suggestions for improvement. Do you understand?

David:

Yes, I think so.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

David, I have a very important assignment I’d like you to take on. We’re conducting a customer survey.

David:

OK.

Taro:

First, I want you to meet with a few of our sales reps to find out what questions they want in the survey. Then, I want you to put together the survey. Is that clear?

David:

Yes, I think so.

Taro:

Once you have the survey, hold an orientation with the reps to explain how to distribute it. Finally, analyze the survey results and make suggestions for improvement. Do you understand?

David:

Yes, no problem.

Taro:

Could you tell me what you’re going to do?

David:

So, first I’m going to have a meeting with a few sales reps to get their input on survey questions. Oh, how many…

Try

Before giving instructions to your trainee, answer these questions.

  • What’s a simple task that you would delegate to a trainee?
  • What instructions would you give the trainee?
Act

Give instructions to your trainee.
