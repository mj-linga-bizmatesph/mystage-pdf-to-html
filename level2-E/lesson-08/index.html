Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 8: Dealing with conflict

Biz Tip

Dealing with conflict between people is a very sensitive issue. Rather than accusing someone, ask what happened.

Biz Tip

When dealing with conflict, it’s more effective to elicit a course of action from the other person than to tell them what they should do.

See

Example

Taro:

Why did you tell Taka that his idea was silly?

David:

I didn’t mean to offend him. It was just a joke.

Taro:

That is completely unprofessional. Please apologize to Taka.

David:

But I …

Taro:

I don’t want this kind of behavior in our office.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

I heard that Taka was a little upset after your meeting with him.
Could you tell me what happened?

David:

Yeah, before our meeting, we were all joking and having fun. And then, during our meeting, when Taka made a comment, I jokingly said his idea was silly. And Taka suddenly turned red and stopped participating in the meeting.

Taro:

Did you apologize to Taka?

David:

I tried, but after the meeting, he quickly left the room.

Taro:

What are you going to do?

David:

I think I should apologize to him. But I wanted to give him some time to cool down.

Taro:

I know you were just kidding. But remember, he is a senior sales rep, so he deserves some respect.

David:

I know. I’ll talk to him.

Try

Before dealing with conflict between your trainee and colleague, answer these questions.

  • Have you experienced conflict with someone at work?
  • Do you know of any conflict between people in your office?
  • What’s the reason for the conflict?
Act

Deal with conflict between your trainee and another colleague.
