Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 11: Assigning a new project

Biz Tip

When assigning a project, it may be necessary to get buy-in from the employee.

Biz Tip

Try to understand your employee’s needs and be flexible to meet his/her needs as well.

See

Example

Taro:

Tony, I’d like you to take on a new project.

Tony:

Taro, I just finished a big project. I need a break. Can’t you assign someone else?

Taro:

No, I want you to do it. It’s a global project and I need someone who can speak English well.

Tony:

Well, I don’t think it’s fair.

Taro:

I know, but I have no choice.

Tony:

All right.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Tony, congratulations on completing that last project.

Tony:

Thanks. I’m glad it’s finally over. I need a break.

Taro:

Yes, please rest up. But I have a global project starting soon.

Tony:

Can you find someone else for this project?

Taro:

This is a very important project. The project team will consist of people from the China and Malaysia offices. So, I need someone I can trust and who will communicate well with the other regions. And I think you’re the best person for this job.

Tony:

When is it going to start?

Taro:

Well, the first meeting is next Wednesday. Why don’t you take a couple of days off before the project starts?

Tony:

Thanks Taro. That helps a lot.

Try

Before assigning a new project to your staff, answer these questions.

  • What are some projects that are going on in your company?
  • What type of people does your company need for these projects?
  • What could you offer to do for your staff to make taking on a project more attractive?
Act

Assign a new project to your staff.
