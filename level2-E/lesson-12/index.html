Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 12: Asking for a progress report

Biz Tip

Avoid making suggestions until you have a clear understanding of the project status.

Biz Tip

When getting a progress report, be sure to follow a logical order, such as

  • the objective
  • work plan
  • schedule and budget
  • issues
  • action steps
See

Example

Taro:

Tony, I’d like to know the status of your global project.

Tony:

OK. We’re having all kinds of problems.

Taro:

What do you mean?

Tony:

Well, everyone understands that the goal of the project is to create a central database of our clients. But because our markets are so different, the information we have on our clients is completely different in each region.

Taro:

Of course it’s different. That’s why you need to discuss this with the other regions.

Tony:

Yeah, but no one is willing to be flexible. I feel like we’re not making any progress.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Tony, could I get a status report on the global project?

Tony:

OK. We’re having all kinds of problems.

Taro:

Before you tell me about the problems, can you explain the project objective, your work plan, and the schedule? After that, we can talk about issues and actions to take.

Tony:

OK, the goal of the project is to create a central customer database, so that all regions can get necessary information quickly to approach customers.

Taro:

OK, and your work plan?

Tony:

Yes, so our first step is to determine the categories in the database. But, we can’t seem to reach an agreement.

Taro:

Let’s talk about the work plan first.

Tony:

OK, the next step is to collect the information…

Try

Before getting a progress report from your staff, answer these questions.

  • What is an important project currently going on at your company?
  • What are the objective and work plan for this project?
  • What issues might arise in this project?
Act

Get a progress report from your staff.
