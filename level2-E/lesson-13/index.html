Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 13: Motivating your staff

Biz Tip

Avoid using negative expressions, like “give up” and “escape” as they are demotivating.

Biz Tip

Motivate your staff by showing that you understand their situation, giving them recognition, and letting them know about future opportunities.

See

Example

Tony:

Taro, I’m really having a hard time working with the people from the other regions. Could I please be taken off this project?

Taro:

Absolutely not. Don’t give up.

Tony:

I’m not giving up. I just want this project to succeed, and I don’t think I’m the right person for this job.

Taro:

Tony, this is your responsibility. Please don’t try to escape from it.

Tony:

But I didn’t want to do this project in the first place.

Bizmates Style

Tony:

Taro, I’m really having a hard time working with the people from the other regions. Could I please be taken off this project?

Taro:

OK, let’s talk about it first. What’s the matter?

Tony:

Well, the guys from the other regions aren’t being flexible. They keep saying that all the information in their system is important.

Taro:

I know working with the other regions is difficult. Everyone has their own ideas. But you’ve succeeded in every project you’ve taken on. I know you can do this too. If you can show our executives that you can handle a global project like this, I know they will consider you for a promotion next year.

Tony:

Thank you for believing in me. I’ll do my best.

Try

Before motivating your staff, answer these questions.

  • How is the overall motivation of employees in your office?
  • What are some reasons to get demotivated in your job?
  • What could you say to someone who is demotivated?
Act

Motivate a staff who is asking to be taken off a project.
