Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 14: Rejecting a request
See

Biz Tip

Before rejecting a request, make sure you fully understand what the request is and why he/she is making it.

Biz Tip

Sometimes it’s necessary to reject a request, but make sure you have a logical reason for rejecting it and the other person understands why you are rejecting the request.

Example

Tony:

Taro, I’d like to make a request.

Taro:

Yes, what is it?

Tony:

We’re now in the final stage of our project, and I’d like to take a business trip to Shanghai and Malaysia to meet with my project members face-to-face.

Taro:

I’m sorry, but we don’t have the budget for that.

Tony:

Well, I’ve checked the airfares and they’re not very expensive.

Taro:

Tony, I’m sorry but my answer is no.

Tony:

Fine.

Bizmates Style

Tony:

We’re now in the final stage of our project, and I’d like to request for a business trip to Shanghai and Malaysia to meet with my project members face-to-face.

Taro:

OK, and why is it necessary to meet face-to-face?

Tony:

Well, we need to make some important decisions together, and I thought it would be best to do it face-to-face.

Taro:

OK, but up to now, you’ve made many important decisions via teleconferencing. Can’t you use the videoconferencing system?

Tony:

Yes, but it would be nice for all of us to meet and congratulate each other, too. It would be a nice way to end the project.

Taro:

Yes, it would, but I can’t approve it just for that reason. We have a limited budget and we need to use it wisely. Is this trip absolutely necessary for the success of the project?

Tony:

No, it’s not. I understand your point.

Try

Before rejecting your staff’s request, answer these questions.

  • What kind of requests are usually rejected at your company?
  • What are the reasons for rejecting them?
Act

Reject a request for an overseas business trip from your staff.
