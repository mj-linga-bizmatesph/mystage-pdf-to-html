Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 15: Reviewing a project

Biz Tip

Always start with praise when reviewing a project. Show appreciation for the employee’s hard work.

Biz Tip

When reviewing a project, be sure to go over the positive results of the project, and the lessons learned to be applied to future projects.

See

Example

Taro:

Now that you have completed your project, I’d like to have a review. Let’s look at where you need to improve.

Tony:

OK, but I thought the project went quite well.

Taro:

Yes, but you had some trouble, right?

Tony:

Sure, but there’s always some trouble in every project.

Taro:

Well, I want you to learn from your mistakes so you can become a better employee.

Tony:

OK.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Congratulations on another successful project, Tony. I know this project was especially difficult with global project members.

Tony:

Thanks, Taro. I really enjoyed doing it. Thanks for the opportunity.

Taro:

Don’t mention it. So, let’s review the project, and talk about what went well and what you learned.

Tony:

That’s a good idea.

Taro:

So, what can you say about your achievements?

Tony:

Well, we were able to meet a very tight deadline and stay within the budget. Also, as this was my first time to work with global team members, I was able to …

Try

Before reviewing a project with your staff, answer these questions.

  • What’s a typical project in your department?
  • What are some successful results of this project?
  • What are some examples of lessons learned in this project?
Act

Hold a project review with your staff.
