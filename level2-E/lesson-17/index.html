Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 17: Handling resistance to change

Biz Tip

When handling resistance, try to understand the employee’s concerns, express your point of view, and try to come up with a plan to deal with the concerns.

Biz Tip

One main reason that employees resist change is because they were not involved in the process. So, get them involved.

See

Example

Mike:

Taro, can I be honest with you? A few of the sales reps including me are a little worried about the new customer database.

Taro:

Why are you resisting change?

Mike:

We’re not resisting; we just think that the new system is missing some key information about our customers.

Taro:

Of course it’s not a perfect system, but we need to change in order to become a better company. I’m sorry, but this is a company decision.

Bizmates Style

Mike:

Taro, can I be honest with you? A few of the sales reps including me are a little worried about the new customer database.

Taro:

OK, what’s bothering you?

Mike:

We just think that the new system is missing some key information that we need about our customers.

Taro:

What do you mean?

Mike:

Well, the system doesn’t have enough information. Without this information, we would still need to contact our overseas offices. So, the system hasn’t improved efficiency at all.

Taro:

Well, we decided to keep version 1 as simple as possible, but we can add more information in the future.

Mike:

But many people are very negative about the system.

Taro:

Can you help me get the others to see the merits of the system?

Mike:

Try

Before handling resistance to change from your staff, answer these questions.

  • What are some reasons for resistance to change in your company?
  • What can you do to help people see the positive side of the changes in your company?
