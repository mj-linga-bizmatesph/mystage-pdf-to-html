Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 18: Giving a final warning

Biz Tip

When giving a warning, make sure the other person understands how his/her actions have caused trouble to the company.

Biz Tip

When giving a final warning, remind the employee of all past incidents related to the issue, what specific actions you’d like the employee to take, and the penalty for not carrying them out.

Example

Taro:

Leo, why haven’t you started using the new database?

Leo:

The system offers us no merit. I’m not going to waste my time using a system like that.

Taro:

Leo, you have no choice. This is your last chance.

Leo:

Are you going to fire me for not using this silly system?

Taro:

That’s right. So, use the system.

Bizmates Style

Taro:

Leo, I’ve noticed that you haven’t started using the new system.

Leo:

It offers us no merit. I’m not going to waste my time using a system like that.

Taro:

I understand you don’t like the system. That’s OK. But when you don’t use the system, it causes trouble for everyone. They can’t do their work and that’s not OK.

Leo:

Well, can’t they ask me for the information like before?

Taro:

Leo, this is the third time I had to speak to you about this. The last two times were on September 24 and October 4. I’m giving you your final warning.

Leo:

Are you going to fire me for not using this silly system?

Taro:

If you do not update all your new customer information into the system by October 27, I will report it to HR and they will take disciplinary action. Is that clear?

Try

Before giving your staff a final warning, answer these questions.

  • In what situations would an employee be given a final warning in your company?
  • Can you describe a situation when a colleague received a final warning?
Act

Give your staff a final warning for insubordination.
