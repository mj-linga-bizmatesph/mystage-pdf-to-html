Level 2 - Rank E - Lesson 19: Accepting an apology

Biz Tip

When a staff member apologizes to you, don’t go on and on about the details. The goal is to change the employee’s behavior.

Biz Tip

When accepting an apology, listen to them, thank them, show them you understand how they feel, and tell them that their support is important to you.

See

Example

Leo:

Taro, could I speak to you in private for a minute?

Taro:

OK, Leo. What is it now?

Leo:

I just want to say that I’m really sorry for my unprofessional behavior last week.

Taro:

Yes, you were very unprofessional.

Leo:

I know. But I’ve thought about it, and I realized I was wrong.

Taro:

Why were you wrong?

Leo:

Well, you asked me to do something and I didn’t do it.

Taro:

That’s right. It caused trouble to many people.

Leo:

I know. I’m really sorry.

Taro:

OK, I accept your apology. Just don’t let it happen again.

Bizmates Style

Leo:

Taro, could I speak to you in private for a minute?

Taro:

Sure, Leo. Is everything OK?

Leo:

Yeah, I just want to say that I’m really sorry for my unprofessional behavior last week.

Taro:

I appreciate you telling me this.

Leo:

I thought about it, and I realized that I was wrong.

Taro:

I know the new database isn’t perfect. But, I really need the support from my senior staff like you.

Leo:

I know. I’ll do my best to support you.

Taro:

Have you input your customer information?

Leo:

Yes, I have.

Taro:

Excellent. I’m really glad we had this talk.

Leo:

Me, too.

Try

Before accepting an apology from your staff, answer these questions.

  • What are some reasons for an employee to apologize to the boss in your company?
  • How would the employee feel in that situation?
  • What would you say if you were the boss in that situation?
Act

Accept an apology from your staff.
