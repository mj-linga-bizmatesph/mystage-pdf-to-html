Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 2: Applying time management tools

Biz Tip

The most basic tools, such as keeping a To-Do List and keeping your files organized , will be your biggest aid in managing time.

Biz Tip

People with good time management skills don’t multitask , they manage multiple assignments while doing one task at a time.

See

Yasu:

Frank, I’ve been keeping an Activity Log for a week now, and I’m beginning to see how I’m wasting time on tasks that don’t contribute to results.

Frank:

Exactly. Now, your next step is to change this behavior.

Yasu:

How do I do that?

Frank:

First, you can prepare a To-Do list.

Yasu:

OK, that’s easy. It’s just a list of things I need to do, right?

Frank:

Well, that’s only the first step. Next, you need to prioritize based on two factors: impact and time.

Yasu:

I see. So I should focus on the tasks that have the biggest impact and take the least amount of time. Got it. What else?

Frank:

Well, I noticed that when I ask you for a file, it takes you a long time to find it. Maybe you need to better organize your files.

Yasu:

Yeah, I know that’s a weakness of mine.

Frank:

OK, here are some basic rules for filing. Have a folder each for ongoing work and completed work. That’ll clear your desktop immediately. Also keep related documents together, and keep file titles consistent. It’ll be easier to find files with your search feature on your PC. And finally, save just what you need.

Yasu:

That makes sense. This will make it easier to multitask.

Frank:

One more thing, don’t multitask. People with good time management skills don’t multitask. They might manage several projects or assignments, but they complete each task one at a time.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What time management tool do you often use at work?
  • What are some important tasks that only require little effort?
  • What are some less important tasks, but require a lot of effort?
  • Which time management tool is the most applicable to your job?
