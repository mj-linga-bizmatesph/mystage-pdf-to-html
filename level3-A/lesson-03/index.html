Biz Tip

The source of stress and how we deal with it differ from person to person.

Biz Tip

The first step to managing stress is to understand where your stress is coming from and how you react to it.

Yasu:

Frank, I feel so stressed out with this project. I don’t know how much pressure I can take. I’m really not sure what to do.

Frank:

Well Yasu, stress management is a skill. And as you learned with time management, there are some tools and habits that can help you develop this skill.

Yasu:

So, what do I need to do?

Frank:

The first step is to understand what’s causing you stress and how you are dealing with it. It might not be from one source. Is it the tight deadlines? Is it trying to please your boss? Is it a particular project member?

Yasu:

I think it’s all those things.

Frank:

Well, it helps to know what it is and how you are reacting to it.

Yasu:

And how do I figure that out?

Frank:

One thing you can do is to keep a Stress Diary.

Yasu:

A Stress Diary? That sounds so childish.

Frank:

Sure, but it’s similar to the Activity Log that you kept to develop your time management skills. It just means recording cases of stress that you have.

Yasu:

You mean I record the details when I’m stressed.

Frank:

That’s right. Record the stressful event, your level of stress, and your reaction. After a while, you’ll start to better understand what the biggest source of stress is for you.

Yasu:

OK, I’ll try that. Thanks.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What do you think is the biggest source of stress in your work?
  • How do you personally manage stress?
  • Would you consider keeping a stress diary? Why or why not?
