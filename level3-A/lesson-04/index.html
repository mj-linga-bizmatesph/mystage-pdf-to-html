Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 4: Applying stress management tools

Biz Tip

Three main approaches to managing stress include:
  • Taking action to solve the source of stress
  • Being mentally fit to deal with stress
  • Being physically fit to deal with stress

Biz Tip

Don’t be afraid to seek
professional help for
serious stress issues.

Yasu:

Frank, the Stress Diary you suggested is helping a lot. I’m beginning to see patterns in my behavior.

Frank:

Excellent. Understanding yourself is the first step. Now you can pick up some tools and habits to help you manage stress.

Yasu:

Like what?

Frank:

Well, once the source of your stress is clear, it’s easier to take action. If it’s tight deadlines, you can develop your time management skills. If it’s another member, you can talk to the person to resolve any issues.

Yasu:

What if it’s my personality? You know, I’m a negative thinker.

Frank:

Well, we can change that too.

Yasu:

Really? How?

Frank:

Well, mental stress often comes from irrational thinking. Let’s look at it together. What are you negative about?

Yasu:

Sometimes I feel I don’t have the skills for the job. Or I won’t get the results I want. Or I’ll have problems out of my control.

Frank:

If you’re continuously developing your skills for your job, it’s irrational to be stressed out for not having the skills. If you are doing everything you can to achieve results, it’s irrational to worry about not getting results. If you have back-up plans, it’s irrational to worry about things out of your control.

Yasu:

I guess you’re right. I am doing all those things.

Frank:

And don’t forget to have a healthy lifestyle. Sleep, eat, and exercise. Enjoy your personal life as well as your work life.

Yasu:

Thanks for the tips, Frank.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How often do you experience mental stress?
  • What actions do you take to stay positive?
  • How do you keep yourself fit?
Act

Role play

Explain how the three approaches to managing stress are relevant to your work and life.
