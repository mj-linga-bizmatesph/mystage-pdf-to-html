Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 5: Understanding project management

Biz Tip

A project manager manages three important resources of TIME, MONEY, and PEOPLE of a project.

Biz Tip

These three resources need to revolve around
the SCOPE of the project, which includes the specific requirements that define successful completion of the project.

See

Yasu:

Frank, I can’t manage this project. Please help me.

Frank:

OK, let’s first talk about your role as project manager.

Yasu:

Sure, I’m like a facilitator, right?

Frank:

You’re a lot more than that. You have complete responsibility for the success of the project. The company is allocating time, money, and people for you to achieve a specific goal.

Yasu:

That’s a lot of pressure.

Frank:

Yes, it is. And in the most basic sense, you manage the time, money, and people to ensure the goal is achieved.

Yasu:

I see. So I basically need to carefully manage these three resources.

Frank:

There’s more. The three resources of time, money, and people need to be directly linked to the scope of the project.

Yasu:

What do you mean by scope?

Frank:

The scope is the specific requirements that you are expected to achieve in order for the project to be called a success.

Yasu:

You mean the goal of the project, right?

Frank:

They’re a little different. Let’s say your project goal is to set up a meeting room. Suddenly, your boss changes the scope from 50 people to 500. So, the goal is the same, but the scope has changed.

Yasu:

I think I get it now. Thanks Frank.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Describe the scope of a project that you were involved in at work.
  • What resources were allocated to this project in terms of time, money, and people?
  • What was the result of this project?
Act

Role play

Imagine you’ve been assigned to lead a job-related project. Describe the scope of the project and the resources needed in terms of time, money, and people.
