Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 7: Understanding team management

Biz Tip

Team development follows five basic stages:

Forming: members are polite and respectful to others
Storming: conflict between members occurs
Norming: members begin to understand one another
Performing: members collaborate and create synergy
Adjourning: members separate after the project
ends

Biz Tip

It’s up to the project manager to lead the team through each stage

See

Yasu:

Frank, I’m having trouble with my team. I think the team is falling apart.

Frank:

What do you mean?

Yasu:

When we first started the project, everyone was polite and respectful. However, now two of the members are always disagreeing with each other. Another member keeps challenging me. I think he’s trying to take over the project.

Frank:

It sounds like a natural progression of your team to me.

Yasu:

Natural progression? We’re making no progress now.

Frank:

Let’s talk about team management, Yasu. You see, there are five basic stages a team goes through as it develops.

Yasu:

What are these stages?

Frank:

Well, the first stage is forming. That’s when all the members were polite and respectful.

Yasu:

Right.

Frank:

Well, as members get to know each other, they become more direct and less careful with their actions. They notice things that they like and dislike about others, and conflict occurs.

Yasu:

Yes, that’s what’s happening now.

Frank:

Don’t worry. This is a natural progression of the team. Soon they’ll get to the norming stage where they understand and accept others for who they are. Then, they’ll move to the performing stage. This is when the team performs at its best.

Yasu:

What’s the last stage?

Frank:

It’s adjourning. This is when the project ends and the team disbands.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Is your team going through the five basic stages of development?
  • Which stage is your team going through right now?
  • Which stage could be the most difficult for a project manager?
Act

Role play

Think of a project you worked on. It could be back in school or at work. Explain whether your project team went through the five stages of team development.
