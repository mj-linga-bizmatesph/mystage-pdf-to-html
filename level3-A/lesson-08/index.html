Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 8: Applying team management tools

Biz Tip

The most common source of conflict on a project team is lack of clarity. The project manager needs to take the lead in deciding the rules, processes, and responsibilities.

Biz Tip

The project manager needs to make sure everyone is on the same page.

See

Yasu:

OK, I now understand that my team is just going through a difficult stage. But is there anything I can do?

Frank:

Of course. And as Project Manager, you should do something. A common source of conflict is lack of clarity. What are the rules?

Yasu:

Rules? We all work for the same company. We all know what the rules are.

Frank:

Yes, but you should have your own rules for the project team. Who has responsibility for what? What format is expected? What’s the process? All these things need to be clarified.

Yasu:

I see. Now that you mention it, I think that’s a big source of the problem. But what if the conflict is personal? Two of the members just hate each other and the others have given up on getting them to work together.

Frank:

Well, it’s helpful for all members to understand the 5 stages of the team development process from forming to adjourning. So, explain to them that the team is just going through the storming stage.

Yasu:

That’s a good point. But will they really get along after that?

Frank:

Maybe not, but it’ll give the other team members hope and understanding. It takes time to build up teamwork. Another thing you can do is a team-building activity.

Yasu:

You mean like taking a daytrip somewhere outside the city?

Frank:

That could be expensive. It could be as simple as having a team lunch or going out for drinks after work.

Yasu:

I see. You’ve given me a lot of hope. I’ll try that. Thanks.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What can you do to make communication clear within your team?
  • What sort of rules would you implement? Why?
  • What sort of team-building activity are you planning for your team?
Act

Role play

If you were to lead a team, what common rules would you implement? Explain them to your trainer.
