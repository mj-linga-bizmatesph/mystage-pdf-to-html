Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 9: Understanding career management

Biz Tip

A personal vision not only gives you direction in your life and career, but it gives you a measure to evaluate your life and career.

Biz Tip

Just like with any business plan, your career strategy starts with an analysis. Use SWOT to analyze your Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.

See

Frank:

Congratulations, Yasu. I heard your project was a great success.

Yasu:

Thanks. I’d like to take on another project soon. I wish the company would give me more chances to develop and grow.

Frank:

Yes, this project was a good opportunity for you. However, your development and growth aren’t controlled by the company. That’s ultimately controlled by you.

Yasu:

But I can’t assign myself a project or promote myself to a new position. That’s controlled by the company.

Frank:

Yes, maybe so. But the truth is it’s not luck that makes people successful. Successful people create those opportunities themselves. They manage their own careers.

Yasu:

And how do I do that?

Frank:

I would start by analyzing myself.

Yasu:

You mean like my strengths and weaknesses?

Frank:

Before that, think about your values and motivators. What do you want to achieve in your career? What is success to you?

Yasu:

Those are tough questions.

Frank:

Of course. Most of us never think about it. However, those who achieve great success have a personal vision. And they understand their strengths and weaknesses . They look for opportunities and prepare for threats .

Yasu:

So they do their own personal SWOT analysis?

Frank:

Yes, that’s right. And they take action. They work on their weaknesses and build on their strengths

Yasu:

Now I’m beginning to understand career management.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What values and motivators drive you in achieving your career goals?
  • Have you identified your weaknesses and possible threats in your career?
  • What actions are you taking now to turn them around?
Act

Role play

Share your personal career vision. Conduct a personal SWOT analysis.
