Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 11: Explaining meishi koukan

Biz Tip

People don’t want to be told to do as the Romans do without proper explanation.

Biz Tip

When you explain Japanese culture, explain what to do and why you do it.

See

Example

Makoto:

Frank, before we visit our VIP client today, let me show you how we exchange business cards in Japan.

Frank:

How you exchange business cards? What do you mean?

Makoto:

When you receive the business card from your client, take it with both hands, look it over for a few seconds, and then put it on your cardholder on the table.

Frank:

That’s a lot of work for a first meeting.

Makoto:

Well, that’s the Japanese way.

Bizmates style

Makoto:

Frank, before we visit our VIP client today, let me show you how we exchange business cards in Japan.

Frank:

How you exchange business cards? What do you mean?

Makoto:

Well, meeting someone for the first time is a very important event to the Japanese. So, to show respect to the other person, we follow a customary ritual called the meishi koukan or the exchanging of business cards.

Frank:

OK, how does it work?

Makoto:

When you receive the business card from your client, accept it with both hands as you bow to show that you are grateful for this opportunity. Look it over carefully to show you are interested in learning more about the person. Lastly, place it on your cardholder on the table to show respect to the other person.

Frank:

Wow, that’s deep. Let me practice that with you a couple of times.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What was wrong with Makoto’s approach in the first dialogue?
  • In the second dialogue, what did Makoto say about first meetings before explaining the proper way to exchange business cards?
  • In the second dialogue, what did Makoto say after explaining each step?
  • Is this how you exchange business cards?
Act

Role play

Explain how to exchange business cards in Japan. Then, explain what to do when you leave a client’s office.
