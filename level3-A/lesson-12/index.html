Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 12: Building relationships

Biz Tip

When explaining customs, be careful not to give an explanation that’s too general.

Biz Tip

Sometimes it’s important to show that a particular custom is complex and requires patience and understanding.

See

Example

Frank:

Now I know how to exchange business cards. Let’s go sell.

Makoto:

OK, but as this is our first meeting with this client, please don’t push our products too much.

Frank:

So what are we going to do?

Makoto:

We just listen and be very very polite.

Frank:

So you mean we should just kiss up to them.

Makoto:

Yes, that’s right. That’s the Japanese way.

Bizmates style

Frank:

Now I know how to exchange business cards. Let’s go sell.

Makoto:

OK, but there’s one thing I need to go over with you.

Frank:

OK, I’m listening.

Makoto:

Japanese clients look very carefully at who they do business with. They will not do business with someone they don’t trust.

Frank:

So, we need to do some trust building.

Makoto:

Exactly. The purpose of this first visit is to establish trust with our new clients, to let them know that we are flexible and willing to do what we can to meet their needs. The purpose isn’t to make the sale. That’ll be for a different time.

Frank:

OK, I can do a bit of apple-polishing.

Makoto:

It’s a little more than apple-polishing. We need to show sincerity and dedication and not just flattery.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How do you think Frank felt about Makoto’s advice in the first dialogue?
  • In the second dialogue, what did Makoto say about Japanese clients?
  • In the second dialogue, what did Makoto say about the purpose of this first visit?
  • Is there really a difference between apple-polishing and establishing trust like Makoto explained?
Act

Role play

Explain the importance of establishing relationships in Japanese business.
