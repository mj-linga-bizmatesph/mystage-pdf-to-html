Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 13: Explaining tatemaeand hon-ne

Biz Tip

Just explaining a concept could lead to more frustration. So be sure to explain how to properly deal with it.

Biz Tip

Give the other person reassurance by explaining specific actions you can take.

See

Example

Frank:

Well, the client seemed really interested in our product.

Makoto:

I don’t think so. They were just expressing tatemae. It means just being polite.

Frank:

So, are you saying that the customer was just showing tatemae?

Makoto:

Yes, that’s right. Their true feelings are called hon-ne. They won’t tell us that.

Frank:

Well, that is really frustrating.

Bizmates style

Frank:

Well, the client seemed really interested in our product.

Makoto:

I’m not sure about that. You see, in Japan, we have two important concepts, tatemae and hon-ne. Tatemae is acting in a way that will please the other, which is what we do in public. Whereas hon-ne is our true feelings, which we only show to people we completely trust. And that’s why it’s so important to build trust with the client.

Frank:

So, are you saying that the customer was just showing tatemae?

Makoto:

I think so. We won’t know for sure until we can build the relationship to the point that they trust us. But don’t worry. We’ll visit them a few more times and little by little, we’ll be able to see their true feelings.

Frank:

OK, I want to know their hon-ne.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How would you feel if you were Frank in the first dialogue?
  • According to Makoto, what is “tatemae”?
  • According to Makoto, what is “hon-ne”?
  • According to Makoto, what’s the best thing to do to find out whether someone is expressing tatemae or hon-ne?
Act

Role play

Explain what tatemae and hon-ne are and try to give some examples in your workplace.
