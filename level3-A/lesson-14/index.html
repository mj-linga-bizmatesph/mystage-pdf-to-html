Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 14: Explaining the customer is always right

Biz Tip

Although the idea of “the customer is always right” is a common concept in many parts of the world, the Japanese place much greater importance on it.

Biz Tip

To explain the importance of some customs, it may be effective to explain the consequences of not following them.

See

Example

Frank:

I can’t believe that customer is making such a demanding request. We’re not going to actually meet their demands, are we?

Makoto:

Of course we are. The customer is always right.

Frank:

I disagree. Sometimes we need to tell them that they are wrong.

Makoto:

That’s not the Japanese way.

Bizmates style

Frank:

I can’t believe that customer is making such a demanding request. We’re not going to actually meet their demands, are we?

Makoto:

Well, in Japanese business, we have a very clear hierarchy, and the customer is at the top of this hierarchy. So, we need to doeverything we can to meet their needs.

Frank:

I understand that. I’ve attended customer service workshops in the US, and we should do what we can within reason. But this client’s request is unreasonable.

Makoto:

Let’s look at it this way: there are many other wiring companies in Japan that would be willing to go the extra mile to meet this client’s request. If we say no to this client, they can very easily find another wiring company. That’s why we need to meet this client’s request.

Frank:

Well, I guess I can understand from that perspective.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Why did Frank react negatively to what Makoto said in the first dialogue?
  • What did Makoto say about Japanese businesses in the second dialogue?
  • According to Makoto, why do they need to meet the client’s request?
  • How far is your company willing to go to meet your clients’ needs?
Act

Role play

Explain the importance of the customer being always right and give some examples in your company to support it.
