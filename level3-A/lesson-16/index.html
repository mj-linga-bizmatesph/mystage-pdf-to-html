Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 16: Explaining nominication

Biz Tip

It’s important to respect that some people strongly feel that their private time should be separate from their work.

Biz Tip

Always highlight the merits of following the business custom to the other person.

See

Example

Frank:

Makoto, I’m going to pass on the drinking party tonight. I’ve got better things to do than go drinking with a bunch of senior managers on a Friday night.

Makoto:

Frank, you have to go. This drinking party is kind of a continuation of our business meeting. It’s your duty.

Frank:

Well, I don’t get paid after 6:30PM.

Makoto:

Please don’t be so selfish.

Bizmates style

Frank:

Makoto, I’m going to pass on the drinking party tonight. I’ve got better things to do than go drinking with a bunch of senior managers on a Friday night.

Makoto:

The real purpose of this drinking party is for the senior managers to get to know us better. In fact, we call it “communication through drinking” or nominication. We feel that business moves much more smoothly when we work with people we know. In a formal business setting like our meeting today, it’s really difficult to break the ice, so this drinking event is achance for us to build a trusting relationship with them. Once we do that, it’ll be much easier to be open and honest with them.

Frank:

OK, I hope we go to karaoke, too.

Makoto:

I’m sure we will.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How do you think Frank felt about the conversation in the first dialogue?
  • In the second dialogue, what did Makoto say was the purpose of the drinking party?
  • In the second dialogue, how did Makoto show that Frank would benefit from attending the drinking party?
  • Is this the business custom in your company as well?
Act

Role play

Explain the importance of nominication in Japanese business culture. Then, explain what actually happens at a drinking event in Japan.
