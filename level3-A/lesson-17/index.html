Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 17: Explaining Japanese corporate structure
See

Biz Tip

Slow decision making is often a source of frustration for foreigners doing business in Japan.

Biz Tip

To get the other person to understand the Japanese decision-making process, explain why it takes so long and what the benefits of the process are.

Example

Frank:

Makoto, have the senior managers gotten back to us with their decision?

Makoto:

No, not yet. It’ll still take time.

Frank:

It takes so much time for decisions to be made.

Makoto:

Many people have to approve it before it goes to the executive level.

Frank:

It just seems like a waste of time.

Makoto:

Yes, I understand.

Bizmates style

Frank:

Makoto, have the senior managers gotten back to us with their decision?

Makoto:

No, not yet. It’ll still take time.

Frank:

It takes so much time for decisions to be made here.

Makoto:

Well, we have a hierarchical corporate structure that’s based on consensus and cooperation.
So, instead of the president making all the key decisions, the directors are involved in the decision-making process, and each of these directors has gathered input from their managers who have also heard from first-line employees. So, everyone is involved directly or indirectly in making decisions.

Frank:

That’s involvement by a lot of people.

Makoto:

Yes, but the benefit is that once a decision is made, everyone is 100% committed to carrying it out.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Do you think Frank completely understood why the decision-making process is slow in the first dialogue? Why?
  • In the second dialogue, what did Makoto say was the Japanese corporate structure based on?
  • In the second dialogue, what did Makoto say was the benefit of the Japanese corporate structure?
  • How quickly are decisions made in your company?
