Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 18: Explaining “wa”

Biz Tip

For cultures that encourage open and active communication, “wa” is a difficult concept to understand.

Biz Tip

After explaining the “wa” concept, give specific directions on what to do and explain why it’s more effective to follow those directions.

See

Example

Frank:

Accounting messed up my travel expenses again! They underpaid me last month, too. That’s it. I’m going to report this to the director.

Makoto:

That’s not necessary. You should be more patient.

Frank:

Patient? We’re talking about my salary.

Makoto:

That’s not the Japanese way.

Bizmates style

Frank:

Accounting messed up my travel expenses again! They underpaid me last month, too. That’s it. I’m going to report this to the director.

Makoto:

Don’t worry. They’ll fix it, but is it necessary to go to the director?

Frank:

Yes, I don’t want them to do it again.

Makoto:

OK, but there’s a proper way to do this. You see, we have something called “wa” or harmony. We avoid having confrontations and placing blame on individuals. It’s deeply rooted in all Japanese.

Frank:

So, I can’t complain?

Makoto:

You can, but if you are not careful, you may be accused of ruining the harmony. So, the proper way to handle this is to communicate your concern without offending anyone.

Frank:

How do I do that?

Makoto:

Ask them to confirm your travel expenses. They’ll find the mistake and apologize for it. Then, politely mention that the same thing happened last month. And I’m sure they will take appropriate action and you can still maintain a good relationship with them.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What do you think Frank thought of Makoto's advice in the first dialogue?
  • According to Makoto, what is “wa”?
  • According to Makoto, what does Frank need to be careful of?
  • According to Makoto, what’s the proper way to handle this issue?
Act

Role play

Explain the importance of “wa” and try to give some examples in your workplace.
