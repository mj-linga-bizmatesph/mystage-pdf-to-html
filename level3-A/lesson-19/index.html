Level 3 - Rank A - Lesson 19: Explaining company loyalty
See

Biz Tip

The term “workaholic” is often viewed negatively. So, instead, give a logical answer to explain why the Japanese have so much loyalty to the company.

Biz Tip

Customs evolve over time, so mention how the younger generation’s views may differ from older generations.

Example

Frank:

I can’t understand why everyone in Japan works so late and hard.

Makoto:

The Japanese are workaholics.

Frank:

But I mean for many Japanese, their company is their life.

Makoto:

That’s the Japanese way.

Bizmates style

Frank:

I can’t understand why everyone in Japan works so late and so hard.

Makoto:

Well, not everyone is like that, but the Japanese, in general, tend to be very loyal to their company.

Frank:

But I mean for many Japanese, the company is their life.

Makoto:

Although it’s changing with the younger generation, you’re right. You see, the company you work for has a lot of influence on how you are viewed by society. Your company name is important when you apply for a loan, when you rent an apartment, and even when your child applies to a private school.

Frank:

That is so strange.

Makoto:

Maybe so, but I think that’s a big reason we are so loyal to our company. If the company performs well, all its employees will benefit in one way or another.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What do you think is Frank’s impression about the Japanese being workaholics?
  • According to Makoto, why are the Japanese so loyal to their company?
  • According to Makoto, how can the company name influence its employees’ lives?
  • What does your company do to keep its employees loyal?
Act

Role play

Explain why the Japanese are loyal to their company and give some examples on the influence that your company name has had on your life.
