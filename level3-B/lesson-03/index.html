Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 3: Finding the facts
See

Biz Tip

Fact-finding isn’t just about looking for data. It’s also about researching and understanding the problem as fully as possible.

Biz Tip

By identifying and understanding the other factors behind the problem, you can have a better view of the situation.

Example

Jerry:

The next step in the cycle is to do fact-finding.

Tomo:

Sure. So I should get some data and look at the figures carefully, right?

Jerry:

Yes, figures will allow you to confirm whether something really is a problem or not. But that’s not enough to identify the problem.

Tomo:

Why not?

Jerry:

Fact-finding isn’t just about looking at data. You need to consider all the other factors involved.

Tomo:

Like what?

Jerry:

Well, look at the problem from other perspectives. Speak to people. What do your team members think? What do your customers or end users think?

Tomo:

OK, I can get some feedback from them.

Jerry:

There’s more. How do your competitors deal with this? What ideas have been tried in the past? Could the processes, systems, and technologies you are using be changed?

Tomo:

Wow, that’s a lot of research.

Jerry:

Of course. Properly doing fact-finding will save you a lot of time later on. And the most important thing you need to figure out is whether fixing this problem is really worth the effort you are putting in.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What’s a simple problem you often face at the office?
  • What facts did you take into consideration in order to try to solve this?
  • What other factors do you think might have contributed to this issue?
Act

Role play

Think of a problem you have at work and try to identify the contributing factors by answering some of the questions below:

  • What data can you analyze to confirm whether this really is a problem?
  • How do your colleagues, customers, superiors view this problem?
  • How do your competitors deal with this problem?
  • Could the processes, systems, technologies you are using be changed?
  • Will solving this issue really be worth the effort?
