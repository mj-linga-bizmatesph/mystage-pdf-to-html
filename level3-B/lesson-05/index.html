Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 5: Finding Ideas
See

Biz Tip

The goal of brainstorming is to generate as many ideas as possible. Focus on quantity not quality of ideas.

Biz Tip

Set ground rules when brainstorming to encourage participation, such as

  • Don’t criticize
  • Build on ideas of others
  • The more ideas the better

Example

Tomo:

So the 4 th stage of the problem-solving cycle is brainstorming, right?

Jerry:

That’s right. So, what’s your usual brainstorming session like with your team?

Tomo:

Well, we usually spend an hour or two to think of different ideas. We try to come up with 2 to 3 good ideas in a session.

Jerry:

OK, but the purpose of brainstorming is to produce as many ideas as possible during the session, not to produce as many good ideas as possible.

Tomo:

Really?

Jerry:

Yes, there’s a separate time to evaluate the ideas. Focus on quantity, not quality.

Tomo:

So, you mean it’s OK to come up with crazy and impractical ideas?

Jerry:

The crazier the better. These ideas can give others a different perspective on the problem.

Tomo:

I see. So, I should change the way I do brainstorming from now on.

Jerry:

I suggest that you set some rules. Tell everyone that the more ideas the better. So build on the ideas of others instead of criticizing them.

Tomo:

That makes sense. Thanks.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • When did you last have a brainstorming session with your team?
  • What problem did you talk about?
  • What solutions did you come up with?
Act

Role play

Discuss the importance of brainstorming and how your next brainstorming sessions can be maximized.
