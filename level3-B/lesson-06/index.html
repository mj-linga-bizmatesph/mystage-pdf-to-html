Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 6: Selecting a solution

Biz Tip

You should analyze the risks involved before selecting the best solution. Risk analysis can be done by:

  • Identifying threats
  • Calculating the risks

Biz Tip

Once risks have been identified, the next step is to manage them: This can be done by:

  • Avoiding the risk
  • Sharing the risk
  • Controlling the risk; or
  • Accepting the risk
See

Example

Jerry:

At this point, you should have a number of possible solutions at your disposal. What do you think the next step is?

Tomo:

According to the process, it’s time to evaluate them and select the best solution.

Jerry:

That’s right. In this stage of the process, you should analyze the risks involved by identifying and calculating them.

Tomo:

How do I do that?

Jerry:

Ask yourself questions like, “Is this a cost-effective solution?” or “How much impact will this solution have on the people concerned?”

Tomo:

I see. So after we’ve identified the risks, how do we manage them?

Jerry:

Good question. First, you can try to avoid the risk if it’s too high. Second, you may also try to share the risk with other people. But this would mean sharing potential gain as well.

Tomo:

Can we also control the risk by way of reducing its impact?

Jerry:

Sure. You can test your solution on a small scale to see how it works.

Tomo:

I see. But what if we can’t do anything about the risk?

Jerry:

Then your last option is to accept the risk. This option is OK if the potential gain is worth taking the risk.

Tomo:

But we still need to have a contingency plan, don’t we?

Jerry:

Exactly.

Try

Identify and manage the risks involved in the following solutions:

  • Promote a skilled but inexperienced worker.
  • Transfer some employees to another department.
  • Using your car instead of taking the train everyday.
Act

Role play

Discuss the solution you came up with to a work-related problem. Describe the risks involved and how they can be managed.
