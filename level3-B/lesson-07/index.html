Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 7: Planning

Biz Tip

Use a simple action plan for small-scale solutions by:

  • Identifying tasks
  • Delegating tasks
  • Considering SCHEMES

Biz Tip

For large-scale solutions, it’s best to structure your plan in different phases:

  • Strategy
  • Preparation
  • Design
  • Testing
  • Training
  • Support
  • Closing

Example

Jerry:

Once you’ve decided on your solution, it’s time to plan for its implementation. At this stage, you need to consider how much effort is needed for this project.

Tomo:

What do you mean?

Jerry:

For small-scale solutions like buying a new whiteboard for your team, a simple action plan works best.

Tomo:

Right. So we just have to know what the tasks are and delegate them to the right people.

Jerry:

Exactly. You may need to also consider some of these on this list: Space, Cash, Helpers/people, Equipment, Materials, Expertise and Systems. Otherwise known as “SCHEMES.”

Tomo:

I see. What if the solution requires a lot of effort to implement? For example, implementing a new process to increase team performance.

Jerry:

Then it’s best to structure your plan in different phases.

Tomo:

So what are these phases?

Jerry:

First you need to strategize, prepare and design your plan.

Tomo:

I see. Then we need to test it and train the people concerned, right?

Jerry:

Exactly, but you also need to provide support. Once you’re sure that all these stages have been met successfully…

Tomo:

Then I guess it’s time to bring the plan to a close, correct?

Jerry:

Precisely.

Try

Discuss how you implement solutions:

  • What things do you consider before implementing any solution in your company?
  • How do you execute simple solutions within your team?
  • How do you manage complex solutions within your department or company?
Act

Role play

Think of a simple solution to a small problem at work. Use SCHEMES to determine what you would need to do to implement this solution.
