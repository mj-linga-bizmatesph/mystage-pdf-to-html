Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 8: Selling your idea
See

Biz Tip

When presenting your solutions or ideas, make sure you:

  • Identify the problem
  • Explain your actions
  • Highlight the results

Biz Tip

See objections as opportunities by

  • Showing gratitude
  • Showing understanding
  • Addressing the concern
  • Backing it up with evidence

Example

Jerry:

OK, so now you have your implementation plan. What’s next?

Tomo:

I need to get approval.

Jerry:

That’s right. So how would you get approval?

Tomo:

I need to present my idea.

Jerry:

OK, but don’t forget that your goal isn’t just to explain your idea, it’s to get approval. In other words, you need to sell your idea.

Tomo:

And how do I do that?

Jerry:

Well, they need to know that there is a real problem to solve. Then explain your idea and implementation plan, and finally explain the benefits of implementing your plan.

Tomo:

I see. Problems, Actions, and Results. Got it. But what if I get objections or concerns?

Jerry:

An objection is an opportunity. It’s their way of telling you what’s important to them.

Tomo:

Right. So how do I deal with that?

Jerry:

Well, I would thank them for bringing it up. And then show that I really understand their concern. Then, I can address their concern and have data to back it up.

Tomo:

So what if they say my idea is too expensive?

Jerry:

Here’s what I would say. Thank you for being honest with your concern. I totally understand that we have a tight budget especially this time of year. However, my idea will actually save the company money over a 3-year span as you can see from this chart.

Tomo:

I get it now.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • When was the last time you presented an idea to your boss?
  • What was it about?
  • What objections did you encounter?
  • How did you deal with them?
