Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 11: Understanding decision-making

Biz Tip

Good decision-making is how quickly you can gather information and analyze it in order to make a decision.

Biz Tip

The first steps in the decision-making process are to decide how to decide and develop an open mind as you go along.

Example

Brad:

So how do you find your new position in marketing?

Shinji:

Pretty tough, to be honest. I have so much pressure to make quick decisions, but I need more time to think.

Brad:

I know how you feel, but time is a luxury. You see, decision-making is about speed. And to make good decisions quickly, you need to have a process.

Shinji:

What do you mean?

Brad:

Well, let me show you a simple 4-step process called OODA Loop, developed by Col. John Boyd. This will help you react quickly to a situation and come up with a decision.. Here let me show you:

Shinji:

It looks pretty simple.

Brad:

It is but it takes a lot of practice to master each step. It’s about how quickly you are able to get all the information you need and accurately analyze this information. Then, you can make good decisions and follow through with your actions.

Shinji:

OK, so I just need to make quick decisions.

Brad:

No, that’s not it. You need to become faster at collecting the right information and analyzing it. Without it, you can’t make a good decision.

Shinji:

I see. So time is important not for decision-making but for collecting and analyzing data for decision-making.

Brad:

Exactly. But before you begin this process, you need to decide on how to decide and develop an open mind.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Does your company follow a certain decision-making process?
  • In what situations do you need to make quick decisions at work?
