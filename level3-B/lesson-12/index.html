Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 12: Deciding how to decide

Biz Tip

Decide on the decision-making process you need to take. It can either be:

  • Deciding on your own
  • Deciding with your team

Biz Tip

Consider these factors when deciding how to decide:

  • Quality of the decision
  • Group commitment
  • Time constraints
See

Example

Brad:

So, first you need to decide on how decisions will be made.

Shinji:

You mean, whether I should decide on my own or with my team?

Brad:

That’s right. You need to consider certain factors in order to determine the best approach. First, you need to think about the quality of your decision.

Shinji:

I see. I need to consider how important it is to come up with a good decision.

Brad:

Right. So, when a higher quality of decision is needed, the more people should be involved in the decision-making process.

Shinji:

I understand. What about involving my staff?

Brad:

Of course, you need to consider that too. The more commitment you need from them, the more you need to have them participate in the process.

Shinji:

OK, got it. What about time constraints?

Brad:

Time is also a factor. When you have more time on your hands, the more luxury you have in involving your staff.

Shinji:

I see. So, by considering all these factors, I’ll be able to decide how to decide.

Try

Explain the best decision-making approach on the following issues:

  • Cost cutting in your department
  • Venue for team building
  • Your child’s birthday celebration
Act

Role play

Discuss the last time you made an important decision and an unimportant decision. Describe how the three factors affected the way you made your decision.
