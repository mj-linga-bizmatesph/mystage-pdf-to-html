Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 13: Developing an open mind

Biz Tip

Whether as an individual or as a group, thinking objectively is key to making good decisions, especially in business.

Biz Tip

Avoid any psychological bias like:

  • Any information that confirms a personal belief
  • Jumping to conclusions
  • Overconfidence in your skills or experience
  • Etc.
See

Example

Brad:

OK, let’s imagine that you’ve already decided on the best decision-making approach. Next, you need to develop an open mind.

Shinji:

Right. However, I noticed we all sometimes jump to conclusions and deem something as a bad idea.

Brad:

Yes, we all have a tendency to think illogically and subjectively. It’s called psychological bias and it often leads to poor decisions.

Shinji:

I see. So psychological bias can prevent us from thinking objectively.

Brad:

Exactly. One example is making a decision based on information that supports your belief.

Shinji:

What do you mean?

Brad:

Well, let’s say that you’re a firm believer in the power of old-fashioned newspaper marketing and you saw an article that 90% of businesses became successful because of it.

Shinji:

Oh, I get it. So there’s a tendency that I would decide to stick with this strategy alone because the article confirms what I believe in.

Brad:

Precisely. Another example is making a huge investment, in spite of the risks involved because your past investments were successful.

Shinji:

Right. I might conclude that the next one would end up successfully as well, right?

Brad:

That’s correct. So, it is important to think openly and objectively before using the OODA Loop.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Do you or your team have a tendency to think subjectively? Give some examples.
  • Have you made a decision based on psychological bias? Describe what happened.
  • What other examples of psychological bias can you think of?
Act

Role play

Discuss the importance of thinking objectively when making decisions.
