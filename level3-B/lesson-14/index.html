Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 14: Observing the situation

Biz Tip

Good decision makers need to be aware of the issues that directly and indirectly affect them now and that may affect them in the future.

Biz Tip

The ability to make sense of all this information is another skill decision makers need.

See

Example

Shinji:

So, the first step in the loop is to observe the situation, right?

Brad:

Yes, this is where you gather information to help get a better view of the situation.

Shinji:

But I do that. I keep a close eye on all our financial figures and read all the memos from the president.

Brad:

Knowing what’s happening within your company is important, but you need to be aware of what’s happening outside as well.

Shinji:

Sure, I research our competitors and our industry too.

Brad:

That’s excellent. But you can take it even a step further. Good decision makers know what’s happening in politics and economics. They’re aware of social trends and new technology. To get this information, not only do you need to read a lot but also meet a lot of people.

Shinji:

That’s a lot of information.

Brad:

You’re right. There’s just too much information and most of the time this information is incomplete or not completely true.

Shinji:

So what can I do?

Brad:

That’s another skill. Good decision makers look for patterns in the information they get. They see how all this information is linked and determine what’s important and what’s not.

Shinji:

So it’s not just about how much information I get, but how well I can make sense of it.

Brad:

That’s right, Shinji. Think about how this information directly and indirectly affects you, not just now, but in the future.

Try

Discuss the type of information that you need to be aware of in your job.

  • Issues that directly affect you now
  • Issues that indirectly affect you now
  • Issues that may directly affect you in the future
  • Issues that may indirectly affect you in the future
Act

Role play

Discuss some decisions that you or your company made and how the information around you affected your decisions.
