Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 15: Orienting with reality

Biz Tip

To orient is to objectively interpret the situation. This is a crucial stage because this is where your decision starts to take shape.

Biz Tip

The way we interpret situations are often influenced by:

  • Cultural background
  • Your upbringing
  • Ability to analyze and synthesize
  • Previous experience
  • New information
See

Example

Brad:

OK, Shinji, let’s look at the 2nd stage, Orienting.

Shinji:

So, this is the part where I analyze all the information, right?

Brad:

Right. This is perhaps the most important step but most difficult.

Shinji:

Difficult? Why?

Brad:

Well, the hardest part is getting over our stereotypes and biases. We all tend to live in our own world and perceive things based on our own values and experiences.

Shinji:

I see. So, I need to think objectively and orient my decision with reality.

Brad:

Exactly.

Shinji:

And how do I do that?

Brad:

You need to develop your sense of objectivity. For example, didn’t you go to that new restaurant across the street?

Shinji:

Yes, I did. The service is terrible. Don’t go there.

Brad:

You see. Based on your one visit, you’ve already decided that it’s a bad restaurant. That’s not being very objective.

Shinji:

So, I can’t say it’s a bad restaurant?

Brad:

Sure, you can. But my point is that good decision makers need to be aware that the information they get might have been influenced by factors such as cultural background, your upbringing, a previous experience, and so on.

Shinji:

I see. So, I can’t just accept the information I get at face value.

Brad:

That’s right. It’s up to you to put it into perspective with reality.

Try

Describe how your decisions are influenced in the following situations.

  • Buying a new phone
  • Changing careers
  • Hiring an inexperienced individual
Act

Role play

Analyze your own biases. How do you think your perception is being influenced by these 5 factors: cultural background, your upbringing, ability to analyze and synthesize, previous experience, new information
