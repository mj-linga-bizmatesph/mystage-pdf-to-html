Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 16: Finalizing your decision

Biz Tip

Essentially, your decision is really just your best guess. There’s always a risk when making a decision. Your job is to do proper data collection and analysis to minimize that risk.

Biz Tip

For complex situations, try to view your decision from different angles, such as:

  • Your priorities
  • Stakeholders
  • Short-term and long-term effects
See

Example

Brad:

Now that you’ve gathered enough information and interpreted the situation, it’s time to finalize your decision.

Shinji:

Sounds easy enough since my decision has already taken shape during the previous stage.

Brad:

That’s true.

Shinji:

But what if I still have doubts? Isn’t that a risk?

Brad:

Well, you might need to do more research. But keep in mind that your final decision is really just your best guess. There’s always a risk when making a decision. Your job is to do proper data collection and analysis to minimize that risk.

Shinji:

I see. How about complex decisions?

Brad:

For complex decisions, it’s good to look at it from different angles.

Shinji:

What do you mean?

Brad:

Ask yourself “Is my decision in line with my priorities?” “Have the effects on all stakeholders been considered?” “Have I thought of the short-term and long-term effects?”

Shinji:

I see.

Brad:

Remember, the more you look at your decision from different perspectives, the better your decision will be.

Try

View these decisions from different angles before finalizing them.

  • Moving to a new apartment
  • Filing a complaint against a colleague
  • Raising a concern to your boss.
Act

Role play

Explain the importance of looking at decisions from different angles in your job.
