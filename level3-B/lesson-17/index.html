Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 17: Acting on your decision

Biz Tip

To act, in the OODA Loop, not only means to implement your decision but also to monitor its results.

Biz Tip

The OODA Loop is a continuous learning cycle of observing, orienting, deciding, and acting. Don’t stop after implementation.

See

Example

Brad:

So, in the previous stage, we finalized our decision.

Shinji:

I suppose it’s time for me to act on my decision.

Brad:

That’s right. So be sure to act on it.

Shinji:

What do you mean by that?

Brad:

Well, a lot of people and organizations use the OODA Loop, but some of them fall short because they fail to act on their decisions.

Shinji:

I see. So they just stop at finalizing their decisions.

Brad:

Yes. You see, the OODA Loop is more of a learning process. Every time we take action, we learn a little more of the situation. Without taking action, no new information comes in and the learning stops.

Shinji:

So, I guess taking action is kind of like testing the market.

Brad:

That’s right. When companies wait too long on taking action, they very quickly fall behind the competition. The market leaders are the ones who take action, gather new information, and make necessary adjustments based on this new information.

Shinji:

I understand. Then I really need to act on my decision, monitor the results, and collect new information.

Brad:

Exactly. I think you’re ready to apply the OODA Loop with your team.

Shinji:

Thank you so much for all your help.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What decisions at work are you planning to implement soon?
  • What could be stopping you from implementing it?
  • What new information could you learn from it?
