Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 18: Repeating the process

Biz Tip

As soon as you implement your decision, go back to observing and gathering data.

Biz Tip

By repeating the process, you gain more valuable information and fine-tune your approach.

See

Example

Shinji:

Hi, Brad! Do you have a minute?

Brad:

Sure, Shinji. What’s wrong?

Shinji:

Well, my team and I went through the process you taught me and came up with a plan and implemented it. But it’s not getting the results we expected.

Brad:

I see. So, what are you going to do?

Shinji:

Well, we’re trying to come up with a new idea.

Brad:

Wait a second. Do you remember I said that the OODA Loop is a continuous learning cycle?

Shinji:

Oh, right!

Brad:

As soon as you implement your decision, you need to immediately go back to the “observe” stage. Do you remember what that is?

Shinji:

Yes, I need to be aware of the issues that affect us directly and indirectly, not just now but also in the future.

Brad:

That’s right. So now you have new information from your actions. So, analyze it and orient yourself with reality. It’s OK if your assumptions weren’t accurate the first time as long as you continue to observe, orient, decide, and take action again.

Shinji:

I get it now. So I need to keep going through the process as I make adjustments to our plan. Thanks.

Try

Identify the consequences and the areas affected by these decisions.

  • What work-related decisions have you made lately?
  • What new things did you learn from it?
  • Do you think this new information can help you in a similar decision next time? How so?
Act

Role play

Discuss a situation where you learned something valuable from a past decision and how it has helped you.
