Level 3 - Rank B - Lesson 19: Giving advice on decision-making

Biz Tip

Determine if a decision should be made by an individual or a group.

Biz Tip

Developing an open mind and thinking objectively are key factors to making good decisions – regardless of the process you use.

See

Example

Brad:

Congratulations on your promotion!

Shinji:

Thank you, Brad. I couldn’t have done it without your help.

Brad:

It’s been a pleasure, Shinji. Anyway, a new project manager is coming in next week and I need you to meet with him.

Shinji:

I see. I look forward to meeting him then.

Brad:

Excellent. I also need you to tell him a thing or two about decision-making.

Shinji:

Sure. I don’t think I can forget what you taught me about the OODA Loop, Brad.

Brad:

Great. But, what do we need to do even before using that process?

Shinji:

Oh right! We need to decide on how to decide.

Brad:

Exactly. Whether the decision should be done by a group or by one person. What else?

Shinji:

We need to develop an open mind and avoid any psychological bias.

Brad:

Right. From there you can start using the process I taught you. What are the four stages of the cycle again?

Shinji:

Observe the situation. Orient your decision with reality. Finalize your decision, and act on it.

Brad:

Well done, Shinji.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What could be the most important part of the decision-making process?
  • How important is having a solid decision-making process?
  • What other advice would you give on decision-making?
Act

Role play

Give your colleague some advice on decision-making.
