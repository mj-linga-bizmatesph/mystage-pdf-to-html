Level 3 - Rank C - Lesson 11: Understanding your social networking goals

Biz Tip

Ask yourself what your real purpose is for making connections. Without a good reason for making connections, social networking may just be a waste of time.

Biz Tip

Think not only about what you can gain from making connections, but what you can offer.

See

Yasu:

Frank, how did you make so many business connections?

Frank:

Well, I’ve invested a lot of time in social networking.

Yasu:

Really? Well, I want to make more connections, too.

Frank:

What’s your purpose for making connections?

Yasu:

Purpose? I have no purpose. It’s just business.

Frank:

Unless you have a clear purpose, trying to make connections may be a waste of time. Plus, it’s extremely difficult. By knowing what you want to achieve, networking will become much more meaningful.

Yasu:

That makes sense. So, what’s your purpose?

Frank:

Well, we already have a lot of opportunities to meet people in our industry. So, my purpose is to meet people outside of this industry.

Yasu:

But why? You can’t sell electrical wiring to those people.

Frank:

I don’t make connections to make a sale. I mean if I get a new client from my connections, that’s a bonus but it’s certainly not my motive.

Yasu:

Then, what is your motive?

Frank:

Well, I think everyone has some unique business experience or expertise that they could share. For example, if I needed a good lawyer or an accountant, one of my contacts may be able to recommend one. At the same time, I feel I have something that I can share with others, too.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What did Frank recommend Yasu to do before he started networking?
  • What is Frank’s purpose for networking?
  • Do you make an effort to make connections in business?
  • Are you interested in meeting people in the same business field or outside your business field?
Act

Role play

Discuss how you understood social networking based on the lesson.
