Level 3 - Rank C - Lesson 12: Making opportunities to network

Biz Tip

Networking isn’t something that you do only at events. It should be a way of life.

Biz Tip

The first step in making connections is to make a friend.

See

Yasu:

So, where do I go to make connections?

Frank:

OK, you have a purpose now, right?

Yasu:

Yes, I’d like to meet people outside of my industry. I want to hear about unique experiences that people have had and hopefully I could share my stories too.

Frank:

OK, so where are you going to meet these people?

Yasu:

I don’t know.

Frank:

You can make connections anywhere, at the bus stop, on a plane, or overseas on your next business trip.

Yasu:

What about events?

Frank:

Sure, you can do that, too. I suggest that you go to events that spark your interest.

Yasu:

My interest? But I want to do this for business.

Frank:

Of course, but you need to start off as friends. And it’s much easier to become friends with people you have something in common with.

Yasu:

I see.

Frank:

Well, I’m going to this wine-tasting event. Would you like to join me?

Yasu:

Sure, I like wine.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Where did Frank say Yasu could make connections?
  • Why did Frank recommend going to events that spark Yasu’s interest?
  • What kind of events have you been to?
  • Did you meet anyone at these events?
Act

Role play

Give your colleague some advice on places where he or she can make connections.
