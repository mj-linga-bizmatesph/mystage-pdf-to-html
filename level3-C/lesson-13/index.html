Level 3 - Rank C - Lesson 13: Starting a conversation
See

Biz Tip

Avoid introducing yourself with your name and company. Names aren’t exchanged until you get to really know the other person.

Biz Tip

A natural way to start a conversation is to comment on the venue or the occasion. And continue the conversation by asking questions.

Example

(Yasu is at a wine-tasting event.)

Yasu:

Hello. My name is Yasu Takeda. It’s nice to meet you.

John:

Ah… Hi, there. I’m John.

Yasu:

I work for MMB Corp. It is an electrical wiring company.

John:

Oh, good.

Yasu:

What company do you work for?

John:

Oh, I’m a consultant. Excuse me. My wife is waiting for me. Nice meeting you.

Bizmates Style

(Yasu is at a wine-tasting event.)

Yasu:

Hi, have you tried this Chardonnay? It’s fantastic.

John:

Yes, it’s one of my favorites.

Yasu:

It’s really fruity, isn’t it?

John:

Yes, it is. It comes from a local winery in Adelaide, Australia. They’re well known for their Chardonnay.

Yasu:

Wow, you really know your wine. How often do you come to these events?

John:

I’m a regular here. I try to attend at least once a month. I just love these events. You meet a lot of interesting people and you get to drink all the wine you want.

Yasu:

That sounds great. Can you recommend a good dry Chardonnay from Australia?

John:

Sure, there’s a great selection over on that table. Come on, I’ll show you.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How did Yasu start the conversation in the second dialogue?
  • How did Yasu continue the conversation?
  • What other ways can you think of to start a conversation?
Act

Role play

Imagine you’re at a similar event. Start a conversation with someone you don’t know.
