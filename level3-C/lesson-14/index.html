Level 3 - Rank C - Lesson 14: Asking open-ended questions
See

Biz Tip

Closed questions, such as “Do you …?” or “Are you …?” will only generate short answers. Ask open-ended questions to get the other person to speak more.

Biz Tip

People like to talk about themselves, so focus your attention on them.

Example

(Yasu is making small talk with someone he’s just met at the event.)

Yasu:

Have you tried Belgian wine?

John:

No, I haven’t. Have you?

Yasu:

No, I haven’t. Do you like French wine?

John:

No, not really.

Yasu:

I see.

John:

Excuse me. There’s my wife. I’d better go back to her.

Bizmates Style

(Yasu is making small talk with someone he’s just met at the event.)

Yasu:

So, what types of wine have you tried?

John:

I’ve tried all kinds of wine- French, Australian, South African, Canadian, and many more.

Yasu:

How are they different?

John:

You see, we usually group wine into six categories: whites, reds, rose, sparkling, desserts, and fortified wines.

Yasu:

I didn’t know there were so many. What do you usually drink?

John:

Well, that depends on what I’m having with the wine. But in general, I’m more of a red wine drinker. What about you?

Yasu:

To be honest, I don’t drink very much. But my wife and I sometimes drink white wine at dinner.

John:

That’s good. Oh, there’s my wife. Let me introduce you to her. I’m John by the way.

Yasu:

I’m Yasu.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What type of questions did Yasu ask in the first dialogue?
  • What type of questions did Yasu ask in the second dialogue?
  • If you were Yasu, what other questions would you ask John?
Act

Role play

Imagine you’re at a similar event. Ask some open-ended questions to continue the conversation.
