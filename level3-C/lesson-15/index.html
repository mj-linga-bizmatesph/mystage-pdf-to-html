Level 3 - Rank C - Lesson 15: Articulating what you do

Biz Tip

Once you’ve gotten acquainted and begin to talk about your job and company, keep it simple so that someone who isn’t in your industry can follow.

Biz Tip

Avoid using technical language or company jargon.

See

Example

(Yasu is making small talk with someone he’s just met at the event.)

John:

So Yasu, what do you do for a living?

Yasu:

I’m an electrical engineer in QA, so I oversee mechanisms and controls following ISO regulations. I work for MMB, which is a manufacturer of wiring harnesses and modularized electrical systems, such as small relay modules.

John:

Oh… That’s interesting.

Bizmates Style

(Yasu is making small talk with someone he’s just met at the event.)

John:

So Yasu, what do you do for a living?

Yasu:

I work for this company called MMB Corp. We produce electrical wiring for cars and electronics.

John:

That’s interesting. So, maybe my car uses your wires.

Yasu:

Maybe. There are a lot of electrical wiring companies.

John:

And what do you do there?

Yasu:

I’m an engineer in the quality assurance department. So, I test samples of electrical wires to make sure that they meet our quality requirements before they are sent to the client. So, what do you do, John?

John:

I teach at a university.

Yasu:

What subjects do you teach?

John:

I teach numerous business subjects, like finance and HR.

Yasu:

I see.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How well do you think John understood Yasu’s job in the first dialogue?
  • How well do you think John understood Yasu’s job in the second dialogue?
  • What do you think are the merits of explaining what you do in a simple way?
Act

Role play

Imagine you’re at a similar event. Describe your company and your job in a way that someone unfamiliar with your industry would be able to follow.
