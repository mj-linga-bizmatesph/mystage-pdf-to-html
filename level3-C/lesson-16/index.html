Level 3 - Rank C - Lesson 16: Showing how you can help

Biz Tip

Always look for opportunities to show how you can help the other person.

Biz Tip

The more helpful you are, the more willing the other person will be to keep in contact with you.

See

Example

(Yasu is making small talk with someone he’s just met at the event.)

Yasu:

Do you discuss Japanese companies in your classes?

John:

Oh yeah. We use Japan a lot in our case studies.

Yasu:

Oh really? Which Japanese companies?

John:

All the major manufacturing companies, but we have very little on the small and medium-sized companies. I wish we had more though.

Yasu:

I understand. There’s very little information on small Japanese companies. That’s too bad.

John:

Right.

Bizmates Style

(Yasu is making small talk with someone he’s just met at the event.)

Yasu:

Do you discuss Japanese companies in your classes?

John:

Oh yeah. We use Japan a lot in our case studies.

Yasu:

Oh really? Which Japanese companies?

John:

All the major manufacturing companies, but we have very little on the small and medium-sized companies. I wish we had more though.

Yasu:

Oh, well I know someone who works for an organization that supports small to medium-sized manufacturing companies in Japan. I’m sure he can give you information on them. I’d be happy to introduce him to you.

John:

Actually, that would be really nice. I don’t have any immediate needs, but I could really use some contacts in that area. Let me give you my e-mail address.

Yasu:

Great. Let me give you mine.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How did Yasu offer to help John?
  • If you met John at this event, what sort of help would you be able to offer?
  • Have you ever offered to help someone you just met?
Act

Role play

Imagine you’re at a similar event. Find out about the other person and see how you can offer to help.
