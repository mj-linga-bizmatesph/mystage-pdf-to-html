Level 3 - Rank C - Lesson 17: Following up on a contact
See

Biz Tip

Remember polite and formal are two different things. A formal e-mail for a casual situation may sound impersonal.

Biz Tip

It’s common courtesy to send a follow-up e-mail within 3 days of meeting the other person.

Example

Subject:Wine-tasting event – July 23

Dear Mr. John Miller,

It was a pleasure meeting you and your wife, Karen, at the wine-tasting event on July 23.

As I mentioned, I may be able to provide you with some valuable information on small to medium-sized companies in various fields of manufacturing

I look forward to establishing a long and close relationship with you.

Best regards,Yasu Takeda

Bizmates Style

Subject:Wine-tasting event – July 23

Hi John,

It was really nice meeting you and Karen at the wine-tasting event yesterday.

Thanks to you, I’ve started to take a keen interest in wine tasting. So, you might see more of me at these events. Anyway, I’ll keep an eye out for any interesting business information for you.

Take care and hope to see you again soon.

Regards,Yasu

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How would you feel if you were John and received the first e-mail?
  • How would you feel if you were John and received the second e-mail?
  • What is the difference between the two e-mails?
Act

Role play

Write a follow-up e-mail to someone you’ve just met.
