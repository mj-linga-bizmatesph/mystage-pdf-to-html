Level 3 - Rank C - Lesson 18: Keeping in touch

Biz Tip

Keeping in touch by e-mail is fine, but it’s more natural to have a reason for the e-mail.

Biz Tip

Sending useful information to someone is a natural way to keep in touch. It shows that you actually remember what the other person is interested in.

Example

Subject:Greetings from Yasu

Dear Mr. John Miller,

Do you remember me? I am Yasu Takeda. We met at the wine-tasting event on July 23.

I hope that you are doing well.

I look forward to seeing you again in the future.

Best regards, Yasu Takeda

Bizmates Style

Subject:Greetings from Yasu

Hi John,

Hope all’s well with you and Karen.

I just happened to come across this article on the emergence of small Japanese manufacturers. It’s a Japanese newspaper, but they had a special English edition this time. Anyway, I’ve attached a scanned copy of the article for you.

I hope you like it. Talk to you soon.

Regards, Yasu

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Would you feel a personal connection if you were John in the first 
    e-mail?
  • What was Yasu’s reason for writing in the first e-mail? The 2 nd e-mail?
  • What makes the second e-mail more personal? Why?
Act

Role play

Write an e-mail to someone you met at an event in order to keep in touch.
