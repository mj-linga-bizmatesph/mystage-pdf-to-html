Level 3 - Rank C - Lesson 19: Understanding networking

Biz Tip

Social networking is about helping people. Don’t expect something in return.

Biz Tip

Social networking is not about how well you can talk, it’s more about how well you can listen.

See

Frank:

So how was the wine-tasting event?

Yasu:

Excellent. I met someone there and we’ve been exchanging e-mails since then. Your techniques were really useful.

Frank:

Really? Like what?

Yasu:

Well, I started the conversation by commenting on the venue. And I asked a lot of open-ended questions to get him to speak more. Also, when we started talking about work, I avoided technical language and kept it simple.

Frank:

So it seems like your views on networking have changed a lot.

Yasu:

Yes, I’ve realized it’s a lot of work to meet new people, make some kind of connection, and then keep in touch.

Frank:

Exactly. So, unless you really enjoy doing this, it’s not easy to keep up.

Yasu:

But it was really satisfying being able to offer some help. Information that’s meaningless to you could be very valuable to others.

Frank:

That’s right. Each of us has his or her own unique experiences and expertise to share. By being a good listener, you’ll be able to find out your contacts’ needs and suggest an attractive solution.

Yasu:

So, social networking isn’t just for people who like to talk.

Frank:

No way, it’s for people who are willing to listen.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How have Yasu’s thoughts on social networking changed from before?
  • Do you agree that social networking is about helping people and not about getting help?
  • What other tips on social networking can you think of?
