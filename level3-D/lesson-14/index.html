Level 3 - Rank D - Lesson 14: Taking responsibility

Biz Tip

One way of being assertive is to accept responsibility for your actions.

Biz Tip

Don’t feel that you need to explain the reasons for your decision. Sometimes it’s enough just to accept responsibility.

See

Example

Cindy:

Who chose this restaurant for the welcome party?

Yasu:

Well, Frank said that the restaurant had good food and their prices were reasonable.

Cindy:

Yeah, but it’s so far. Why couldn’t we choose a closer restaurant?

Yasu:

Well, the restaurant is close to where Willy lives. So, Frank thought it would be fair to choose a place that was convenient for Willy since it is his welcome party.

Cindy:

Yeah, but I wanted to go to that other restaurant across the street.

Yasu:

Yeah, I think that’s a great place, but we have no choice but to follow the others.

Bizmates style

Cindy:

Who chose this restaurant for the welcome party?

Yasu:

I did.

Cindy:

But it’s so far. Why couldn’t we choose a closer restaurant?

Yasu:

Well, I collected feedback from everyone and decided that this restaurant was the best choice.

Cindy:

Yeah, but I wanted to go to that other restaurant across the street.

Yasu:

Cindy, this time it was my responsibility to coordinate the party. I’d be happy for you to take over the role next time..

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Did Yasu take responsibility for choosing the restaurant in the first dialogue?
  • Who did Yasu blame in the first dialogue?
  • Why didn’t Yasu mention that Frank suggested the restaurant in the second dialogue?
Act

Role play

If you were Yasu, how would you respond to Cindy’s attitude?
