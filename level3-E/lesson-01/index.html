Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 1: Understanding persuasive communication

Biz Tip

Persuasive communication is NOT about wowing your audience with bells and whistles.

Biz Tip

Persuasive communication is knowing your audience’s needs, framing your ideas to meet their needs, supporting with evidence, and connecting with your listeners.

See

Rick:

Yasu, how’s your proposal for the executive directors coming along?

Yasu:

I’m really nervous about it. I wish I could be more persuasive like you.

Rick:

You’ll be fine, Yasu. You are persuasive.

Yasu:

No, I’m not. I lack confidence and charisma.

Rick:

Yasu, persuasive communication isn’t measured by the amount of confidence or charisma one has. It’s measured by the responses you get from people. You convinced me with your proposal because you did your research, you explained it clearly and logically, and you communicated it persuasively.

Yasu:

But I know you. So, it was easy.

Rick:

That’s true. Persuading people you don’t know very well is much more challenging, but the basic approach is the same.

Yasu:

What’s the approach?

Rick:

First, you need to know what the executives want. They also need to know who you are and why they should listen to you.

Yasu:

What about all the techniques you use?

Rick:

We’ll talk about that too, but don’t expect revolutionary tricks that work like magic because that’s not persuasive communication. It’s really about understanding your listeners’ needs, framing your proposal to meet their needs, supporting your idea with data, and connecting with them.

Yasu:

You make it sound so easy.
