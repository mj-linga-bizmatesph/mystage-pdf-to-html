Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 2: Understanding their needs

Biz Tip

The first step in persuasive communication is to know the needs of the listener.

Biz Tip

Your message needs to change depending on who you are presenting to.

See

Yasu:

So, the first thing I need to do is understand what the executive directors want, right?

Rick:

Yes. So place yourself in their shoes. What are they most interested in?

Yasu:

Well, they want the company to be profitable, gain market share, and expand our product line.

Rick:

Of course, but let’s look a little more carefully at some of the factors that the executives are concerned about.

Yasu:

Well, our profitability has gone down due to price cuts brought on by foreign competition. We’ve lost 3% of our market share over the last four years. And we haven’t released any new products for the last two years.

Rick:

And what is your proposal going to tell them?

Yasu:

I’m going to tell them that I have an idea for a revolutionary business model that will allow us to increase our profitability, gain market share, and offer a unique product nonexistent in the market.

Rick:

Great. But why should they listen to you?

Yasu:

I don’t know. They probably won’t listen to me.

Rick:

If you can show you have credibility, they will listen. We can talk about that next time.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What’s the first step in persuasive communication?
  • According to Yasu, what are the executive directors’ needs?
  • What is Yasu going to propose to them?
  • According to Rick, what does Yasu need to do first to get the directors to listen to him?
Act

Role play

If you were making a proposal to a colleague, your boss, or an executive, what would their needs be?
