Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 3: Building credibility

Biz Tip

Real credibility doesn’t happen overnight. It takes time to establish trust and to prove yourself.

Biz Tip

To build credibility, you can follow this process:

  • Thank the audience
  • Introduce yourself
  • Explain your background
  • Explain some unique and relevant experience

Example

Yasu:

First, I’d like to thank the executive directors for this opportunity to present my idea. My name is Yasu Takeda and I work in the overseas sales department. I’d like to present a new business model that has the potential to significantly …

Bizmates style

Yasu:

First, I’d like to thank the executive directors for this opportunity to present my idea. Before I begin, please allow me to briefly introduce myself. My name is Yasu Takeda and I work in the overseas sales department. Before joining MMB, I worked for MEDTEC, one of our biggest clients, where I was able to see firsthand the range of products offered by our competitors. What I saw were different sales approaches by different wiring companies for the same product. Some were packaged differently and had nicer brochures, but essentially, these companies were selling the same thing. And most of the time, price was the determining factor for our decision.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • Do you think the executive directors would be more interested in listening to the rest of Yasu’s presentation after the first opening or the second opening?
  • How did Yasu build some credibility in the second opening?
  • What types of proposals do you make in your job?
  • How can you show your credibility when you make your proposal?
Act

Role play

If you were making a proposal to a colleague, your boss, or an executive, how would you build credibility?
