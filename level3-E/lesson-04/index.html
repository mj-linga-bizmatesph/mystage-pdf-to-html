Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 4: Framing

Biz Tip

Framing is identifying what it is the audience wants and showing that your proposal will address these issues.

Biz Tip

Having accurate information of the audience’s needs is essential to properly frame your proposal.

See

Example

Yasu:

This new business model will not only improve customer satisfaction,　it will also improve employee satisfaction.

We have a lot of talented employees whose skills are underutilized.

In my proposal, I will highlight the merits to our employees, our customers, and our company.

Bizmates style

Yasu:

So what are the major issues that we are facing at MMB Corp?

Certainly it’s no secret that over the last three years, our profitability has gone down more than 5%, our market share down by 3%, and not a single product release.

This is not unique to MMB Corp. but a trend seen in many domestic companies in our industry. In my proposal, I would like to introduce a new business model that will bring our profitability back to our peak years, enter a new untapped market, and allow us to release several new products each year.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What was the focus of Yasu’s proposal in the first example?
  • What was the focus of Yasu’s proposal in the second example?
  • Which example do you think the executives would be more interested in? Why?
Act

Role play

If you were making a proposal to a colleague, your boss, or an executive, how would you frame it?
