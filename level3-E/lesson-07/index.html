Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 7: Using logic

Biz Tip

One simple way to help form your logic is to ask yourself “Why” a few times.

Biz Tip

Another simple way to help form your logic is to use “If” statements.

See

Example

Yasu:

So, this is my proposal for a systematic customization model.

It will increase our profitability and meet our clients’ needs.

If we implement this system by October, we could gain market share, too.

Bizmates style

Yasu:

My proposal for a systematic customization model is based on a simple fact: it makes business sense.

It makes business sense because it’ll increase our profitability. It’ll increase our profitability because our clients would be willing to pay more. They would be willing to pay more because they can offer customized products at a premium price to end-users.

If we implement this system by October of this year, we would be able to make it in time for the winter bonus season.
If we make it in time for the bonus season, we could make special offers to our clients.
If we do that, we would gain market share.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • How clear was the logic in Yasu’s first speech? In his second speech?
  • What was the logic used in Yasu’s second speech?
  • Try using the “Why” and “If” techniques with the following general statements:
    • I should do more exercise.
    • We need to enter the Chinese market.
    • I need to improve my English.
Act

Role play

If you were making a proposal to a colleague, your boss, or an executive, how would you form your logic to support your ideas?
