Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 8: Connecting with the listener
See

Biz Tip

Persuasion is not just about logic and data. Sometimes, decisions are based on feelings and emotions.

Biz Tip

Connect with the listener by being honest, passionate, and likeable.

Example

Yasu:

In summary, the systematic customization model will help us improve profitability, gain market share, and add innovation to our product line. I hope you see the merits of my proposal and seriously consider it. That’s all for my presentation. Now, I’d be happy to answer your questions.

Bizmates style

Yasu:

Today, we looked at how the systematic customization model can give us the innovation that we have been missing so that we can increase profitability and regain our market share. While I was coming up with this idea, I had a chance to share my thoughts with my colleagues, some managers, and a few directors. The response was very positive. The excitement and energy I felt from them really motivated me to make this proposal. I’m sure that there are other factors that need to be considered at the executive level that I’m not aware of. However, I do know that many people at MMB are ready to take action.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • If you were an executive listening to Yasu’s first speech, how would you feel? In the second speech?
  • Is it common practice in Japanese business to try to connect with the audience?
  • Have you ever attended a presentation where the presenter connected with the audience?
Act

Role play

If you were making a proposal to a colleague, your boss, or an executive, how would you connect to the listener?
