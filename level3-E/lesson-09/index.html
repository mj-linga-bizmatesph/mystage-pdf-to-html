Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 9: Giving advice on persuasive communication

Biz Tip

Persuasive communication is not something you do in a single presentation. It’s a long-term process.

Biz Tip

Persuasive communication is knowing your audience’s needs, framing your ideas to meet their needs, supporting with evidence, and connecting with your listeners.

See

Willy:

Yasu, I heard that the executives were really impressed with your proposal. Well done.

Yasu:

Well, I got a lot of help from Rick. He helped me learn the art of persuasive communication.

Willy:

Yeah, I’d like to learn how to wow an audience in a presentation, too..

Yasu:

One thing that I learned about persuasive communication is that it’s not something you do to wow an audience in a one-time presentation.

Willy:

Really? What is it then?

Yasu:

It’s an entire process that requires time and commitment. It’s about making the effort to really know your listeners, framing your ideas to meet their needs, supporting your ideas with facts, evidence, and examples, and most importantly connecting with them.

Willy:

And how do you do all that?

Yasu:

Well, there are some techniques you can use. I’d be happy to share them with you.

Willy:

Wow. You’ve already persuaded me.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • What was Willy’s understanding of persuasive communication?
  • What was Yasu’s definition of persuasive communication?
  • Has your view changed at all about persuasive communication compared to before you started this section?
Act

Role play

Give advice to a colleague who wants to know about persuasive communication.
