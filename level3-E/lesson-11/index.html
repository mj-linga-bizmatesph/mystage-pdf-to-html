Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 11: Understanding leadership

Biz Tip

A manager controls, plans, and organizes while a leader motivates and inspires.

Biz Tip

Leadership is a skill that is not only reserved for managers. It’s essential for people in all levels of an organization.

See

Rick:

Yasu, congratulations on your promotion to assistant manager.

Yasu:

Thanks Rick. It’s my first time to take on a managerial role. So, I’m a little worried about it.

Rick:

It’s certainly not an easy transition from worker to manager. Suddenly, you deal with pressure from people above you and below.

Yasu:

I don’t know if I can handle all this pressure. I’m not really the type of person to lead people.

Rick:

Well, most of us aren’t born leaders. Leadership is a skill which can be learned.

Yasu:

How can I become a good leader?

Rick:

Well, who would you consider to be a good leader?

Yasu:

Our current president, Mr. Yamamoto, has great leadership skills.

Rick:

Yes, he does. What makes him a great leader?

Yasu:

Well, he has a clear vision for a start.

Rick:

Yes, that’s very important. Anything else?

Yasu:

Yeah, he has integrity, humility, and responsibility.

Rick:

You’re right. He also takes quick action and communicates well.

Yasu:

Thanks for this talk, Rick. I feel much more motivated. I’m going to make some major changes. I don’t want to be a boring manager; I want to be a great leader.

Rick:

Hold on a second, Yasu. Don’t forget that you are a manager, and your job is to manage the operation of your team. With leadership skills, you can instill motivation and change, but your job is to manage the daily operation. That’s important, too.

Yasu:

I see. I need to be a good manager with leadership skills.

Rick:

Exactly.
