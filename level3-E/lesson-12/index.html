Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 12: Explaining your vision
See

Biz Tip

Your vision needs to highlight “What’s in it for me?” or “WIFM” to get everyone on board.

Biz Tip

By getting involvement from your team when creating your vision, they will more likely show commitment.

Example

Yasu:

Hi, everyone. The reason I called this meeting is because I am your new boss, and I would like to explain my vision. This is important because it is what I expect from you. Our company target for this year is to increase sales by 8%. However, I want all of you to aim for 10%. I want to be able to say that my team achieved the highest sales figures in our company.

Bizmates style

Yasu:

Hi, everyone. I’ve already had individual meetings with all of you wherein I got some great ideas on how to make our team better. I’ve gone ahead and molded these ideas to make a draft vision for our team. So, I’d like to share this and get your feedback. Here it goes:
“To create a tight-knit team that willingly shares information and willingly offers support to our members so that we can all surpass our targets and be recognized and rewarded for our performance.” So, what this means is I want all of us to be known as the best, not individually, but as a team. If we can do that, then I know that all of us will be rewarded and recognized not only for being high achievers but also team players. This is just a draft I put together based on your ideas, but what do you think?

Try

Answer these questions.

  • If you were on the receiving end of Yasu’s first vision, how would you feel? How about the second vision?
  • How did Yasu involve his staff in creating the second vision?
  • What are the “WIFM” points in the second vision?
  • What is the vision of your team, department, company?
Act

Role play

If you had to create a vision for your team, what would it be?
