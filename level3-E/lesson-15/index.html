Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 15: Showing integrity

Biz Tip

Think of integrity as doing the right thing even when no one is watching.

Biz Tip

By showing a lack of integrity to your team, you not only encourage unethical behavior, but your staff will less likely trust you.
See

Example

Yasu:

Jack, I think there’s a mistake in the unit price of this sales contract. The unit price is 20% higher than usual.

Jack:

It’s no mistake. This client hasn’t done any research on our competitors, so they have no idea what the market price is.

Yasu:

Really? Are you sure they’re not going to find out later?

Jack:

Well, it’s their fault for not doing any research. Besides, they’ve already signed the contract.

Yasu:

OK, but keep this quiet. I don’t want any of our senior managers to find out.

Jack:

No problem. I’ll keep quiet.

Bizmates style

Yasu:

Jack, I think there’s a mistake in the unit price of this sales contract. The unit price is 20% higher than usual.

Jack:

It’s no mistake. This client hasn’t done any research on our competitors, so they have no idea what the market price is.

Yasu:

Jack, that’s unacceptable. Please revise the contract.

Jack:

But it’s their fault for not doing any research. Besides, they’ve already signed the contract.

Yasu:

Please revise the contract and tell the client that you had made a mistake.

Jack:

How about our vision to achieve our targets, to be the best?

Yasu:

I’m sorry if you misunderstood the vision, Jack. But next time if you are unsure about your decision, I want you to ask me.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • If you were Jack, how would you feel in the first dialogue with Yasu? How about in the second dialogue?
  • How did Yasu fail to show integrity in the first dialogue?
  • How did Yasu show integrity in the second dialogue?
  • What are some examples in your workplace where integrity might sometimes be challenged?
Act

Role play

If you found out that your staff had done something unethical, how would you react?
