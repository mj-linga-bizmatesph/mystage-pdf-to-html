Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 16: Taking responsibility

Biz Tip

Blaming others is a defense mechanism that leaders do not use. True leaders take responsibility for their team.

Biz Tip

Taking responsibility as a leader is more than just accepting the blame. It means taking complete ownership.
See

Example

HR:

Yasu, I heard a rumor that someone from your team charged an outrageous price for our products.

Yasu:

Yeah, it was Jack. I told him it wasn’t a good idea.

HR:

Not a good idea? He put the company at risk. If the client were to find out, this could lead to a major complaint. This client would never do business with us again. Our brand image would be damaged.

Yasu:

I know. Jack only thinks about his sales targets. He really needs some training in ethics.

HR:

Yasu, I’m really concerned about this. I’m going to have to report this to the executives.

Bizmates style

HR:

Yasu, I heard a rumor that someone from your team charged an outrageous price for our products.

Yasu:

Yes, that’s right. The employee spoke to me about it, and I had him immediately revise the proposal for the client. We apologized to the client saying that it was a mistake.

HR:

OK, so the client got a revised contract?

Yasu:

Yes, that’s correct. I’m terribly sorry about that.

HR:

Who’s the employee?

Yasu:

It was Jack, but please understand that I take full responsibility for this. Our team vision wasn’t clearly communicated and Jack thought he was doing the right thing for the team. I have spoken to him about this, and he clearly understands that it was wrong and he will never do that again.

HR:

OK, I trust your judgment, Yasu

Try

Answer these questions.

  • If you were the HR manager in the first dialogue, what would you think of Yasu as a leader? How about in the second dialogue?
  • Why didn’t Yasu reveal Jack’s name at first in the second dialogue?
  • Could you think of some examples of people taking responsibility in your workplace?
Act

Role play

How would you respond to the HR manager in the above situation?
