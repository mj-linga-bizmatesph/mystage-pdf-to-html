Level 3 - Rank E - Lesson 18: Taking decisive action

Biz Tip

Sometimes, leaders need to take quick action and communicate it to their staff. As long as the leader has built up trust, others will follow.

Biz Tip

It’s important to clearly explain the situation, explain your actions, and explain your reasons for your actions.
See

Example

Yasu:

OK everyone. We have two months remaining, and we are now in second place in the rankings, behind by 0.4%. This is good, but we can do better. Please do your best to sell.

Jack:

Isn’t there any action we can take?

Yasu:

I want you to continue doing what you have been doing, but do more of it.

Bizmates style

Yasu:

Listen up, everyone. We have two months remaining, and we are now in second place in the rankings, behind by 0.4%. We don’t have much time. We need to take action.

Lin:

What are we going to do?

Yasu:

I am going to cancel our weekly meetings to give you more time to focus on sales. I’ll also go on sales calls.

Jack:

But what about our vision and supporting each other?

Yasu:

You all know our vision by heart. I don’t want you to forget it. You have all done an outstanding job working towards our vision, and that is why we are here now. However, we do not have time, and we need to take action. Our priority for the next two months is to achieve our targets.

Sang:

Yasu, you can count on us. We won’t let you down.

Try

Answer these questions.

  • If you were a member of Yasu’s team, how would you feel in the first meeting? How about in the second meeting?
  • How did Yasu get his staff to understand why he made this decision?
  • What are some examples in your company where decisive action was taken?
Act

Role play

Imagine that you were Yasu holding this meeting. How would you communicate your decision to your team?
