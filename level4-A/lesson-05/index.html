SEE

Read the article and take note of the highlighted phrases.

How openly one expresses their emotions bespeaks their culture and background. Though some may have been brought up to share their thoughts and emotions freely, others may behave differently. Our third dimension of culture compares and contrasts these two ways of handling emotions
Neutral:
In neutral cultures, self-control and the inhibition of emotions is thought to be considerate and professional. For example, New Zealanders tend to communicate in soft tones with minimal gestures and often joke about their neighboring Australians’ overly-friendly nature.
Affective:
People from affective cultures have been encouraged from an early age to express what is on their minds using gestures, tonal shifts, and facial reactions. For example, in Spain, people are considered very touchy-feely and will often kiss, hug, and may even interrupt someone speaking as a sign of interest.
We all experience emotions, yet one’s perception and expression of these feelings may result in misunderstandings. By refraining from making judgements based on our counterpart’s expression of emotions or lack thereof, we can ensure that our relationships remain healthy and productive.
