Although time is a valuable commodity shared by all cultures and societies, different cultures have come to think of time as either a rigid construct, or something with varying degrees of flexibility. The sixth dimension of culture takes a deeper look at two distinct perspectives on time.
Sequential Time:
For sequential-time cultures, time is money. Tightly organized schedules are expected to be followed in order to maximize efficiency. In addition, more focus is given to short-term goals. For example, meetings in the U.K. are well-structured with a clear purpose and a strict agenda.
Synchronous Time:
In synchronous-time cultures, people behave more flexibly when it comes to time. Although goals are clearly established, scheduling is far looser, allowing for people to work on multiple projects. As a result, countries like Mexico place greater emphasis on long-term planning than on short-term goals.
Although logic may tell us that sequential-time cultures would be more productive, recent studies have shown that a synchronous approach has proven to be more effective for complex projects. Therefore, striking a balance between the two may be the best route to securing success.
