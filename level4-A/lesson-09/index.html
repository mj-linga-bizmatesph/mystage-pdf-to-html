Since the dawn of man, we have asked ourselves: are we at the mercy of nature or are we the masters of our own fates? This question that has shaped many cultures and defined millions of people is central to the final dimension of culture: inner direction versus outer direction.
Inner Direction:
People in inner-directed cultures seek to control their environment and believe that everyone is accountable for their own situations regardless of external factors. For example, Western companies invest exorbitant amounts of money to understand and control the laws of nature, creating numerous inventions and patents.
Outer Direction:
People in outer-directed cultures believe that there is no controlling nature and fate. Only by adapting one’s approach to fit the environment and by working together can we find peace and prosperity. For example, Eastern countries are more adept at taking existing technology to create more refined and popular products.
Recognizing what we can and cannot control enables us to more effectively position ourselves in a manner that allows us to find success. Sometimes, we need to learn how to roll with the punches — regardless of whether we find ourselves in a fateful position or a man-made predicament.
