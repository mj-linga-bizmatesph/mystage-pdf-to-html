bout – short period of time dedicated to a certain activity

SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Yuji, a senior director, has recently been transferred to the Philippines to manage an overseas subsidiary. Although things went smoothly at first, Yuji’s relationship with the local manager, Rupert, began to sour after bouts of miscommunication and cultural misunderstandings.

Rupert

I can’t believe Yuji yelled at me in front of the others. So what if Mia missed her monthly target? What’s that got to do with me? I’m not a slave to be yelled at. I come from an important family, got my MBA in the US, and cut my teeth for years at this company. To be honest, I’m not sure what Yuji wants. He needs to really rethink his approach.

Yuji

Rupert needs to take more responsibility for his team. We need to think long-term and start focusing on more than just meeting our monthly targets. Winning the battle doesn’t mean winning the war. Rupert, and everyone around him, needs to be reminded of this. We’re all in this together, so there’s no point in beating around the bush.

Although Rupert was enjoying his work, he is now thinking of leaving the company and taking a lucrative package from a competing firm where he feels he will get the respect he deserves. Yuji was confused as to why things were so difficult with Rupert; and why, despite his best efforts, they were unable to make progress.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe how the Japanese and the Filipino regard time? (Sequential/Synchronous)
  • What do you think is more important? Short-term or long-term goals?
  • What is the importance of personal achievements in your culture? How about in the Philippines? (Ascription/ Achievement)
  • Have you ever been reprimanded by someone in public? How did/would you feel? How do you think people in the Philippines would react to this?
