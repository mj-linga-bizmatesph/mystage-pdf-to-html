SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Greg, the chief negotiator for an American IT company, has been working hard to set up a joint venture with Haruko, the liaison and translator for a large manufacturing company. After weeks of hard work, they meet with Haruko’s board of directors to present their work. Sadly, things didn’t go as planned.

Greg

What?! They’re thinking about pulling out?! I thought the deal was in the bag! When I presented the details of the contract, everyone seemed like they were on board. We’ve been at this for months now. If we don’t set firm deadlines, a few more months could pass before anything happens! This is business and time is money. What happened?

Haruko

Yes, even though you presented everything we discussed, the board felt that such a long contract was a sign of how little your company actually trusts us. Plus, giving them a deadline added insult to injury. We Japanese are not accustomed to this style of business. In Japan, it takes much more than a detailed contract and a handshake...

Greg’s approach, though well-intended, has jeopardized Wire’s joint venture with Ito Manufacturing. Haruko is attempting to calm the executives, but the board is seriously considering withdrawing from the deal. Greg must now urgently make reparations, for the success of the partnership hangs by a thread.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe business contracts and their importance, according to Japanese and to American culture? (Universalist/Particularist)
  • What advice would you give before meeting with more traditional executives in your country?
  • Do you think that it’s sometimes necessary to be a little pushy to get things done? Why?
  • In your experience, what kind of cultural differences have caused business problems?
