SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Klaus Weber, a German national, arrived in Tokyo to head his firm’s Sales Department. Although everyone in the team is anxious, Takumi Sato, a junior employee, can’t wait for the chance to speak English again after leaving his American university. Klaus, however, didn’t share Takumi’s enthusiasm.

Takumi

I studied abroad in the US, so I feel I’m familiar with foreign cultures. However, when I greeted Klaus, he seemed sarcastic and unimpressed. To break the ice, I asked him about his family and life at home, but he basically brushed me off and asked about my targets this year. When I presented my three-year vision, he simply said it was illogical. I’m at a loss.

Klaus

I understand that Takumi is trying to be open and welcome, but I’m just not comfortable talking so openly about my private life. This is something that I feel doesn’t belong in the workplace. We’re professionals. On that note, I’m not sure where Takumi has been focusing his energy. Three-year plans just don’t make sense when our yearly target is behind.

Although Takumi’s experience and approach may have worked with other cultures, Klaus’ own unique cultural sensibilities have made him difficult to approach and connect with. In global business and cultural etiquette, one size certainly doesn’t fit all.
