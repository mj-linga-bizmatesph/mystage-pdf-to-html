start off on the wrong foot – to begin something (like a relationship) badly
fall in line – to conform or fit in with others or established rules

pedigree – one’s ancestry, lineage, or history
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Ken Tamai, the young but talented new general manager of his firm’s sales office in Shanghai, is eager to prove himself at his new post. Ken was selected for the position due to his TOEIC score and past achievements; however, his inexperience and approach started him off on the wrong foot with his local staff.

Ken

Yes, I may be young, but I studied at the best university in Japan and have an excellent track record. My job is to ensure things run smoothly, like they do at HQ in Tokyo. This means rearranging the desks to facilitate better communication with my subordinates and being frank when need be. They’re not very cooperative but they’ll soon fall in line.

Lei

Ken is ambitious, but he is also very young. That being said, some of our older employees are uncomfortable with his management style, especially being reprimanded in front of others. His process is slowing things down and it’s just not suitable for our working style. Those of us who prefer to do things our way are already starting to ignore him.

With such a large gap in cultural practice and understanding, Ken has a tough challenge ahead of him. Ken’s success in his native country may not speak for itself in his new role; bridging cultural communication and work-style gaps requires more than just a strong pedigree — it requires adaptability.
