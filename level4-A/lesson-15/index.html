WORDS & PHRASES
stick to – to adhere to or to follow (something)
loosen up – to relax and enjoy oneself
go off on a tangent – to steer away from the main subject by talking about an unrelated topic
impersonal – not influenced by or showing emotion or feelings
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Yoshi Mori, a senior manager, is having a teleconference with Luiz Mendez, his chief counterpart from a medium-sized Brazilian plastics manufacturer. Although Yoshi prepared a detailed agenda and wanted to stick to a strict timeline, Luiz’s team remained uncooperative despite Yoshi’s best efforts.

Luiz

My team and I find Yoshi and his team very difficult to read, no matter how hard we try to get them to loosen up. We find it hard to do business with those whom we can’t connect with on a more personal level. We are passionate people and don’t care much for strict formality. We want to know whom we’re doing business with.

Yoshi

From our perspective, Luiz’s team isn’t very professional. They are always late, going off on tangents, wasting time chit-chatting, and seem unwilling to talk about important business matters. I need to report to my executives and meet my deadlines. If things stay as they are, I don’t think it’ll work. I might have to be more direct with them.

“It’s not personal, it’s business,” is a well-known expression that emphasizes the impersonal nature of business dealings. Yet, for many like Luiz, the opposite is true. Business can be very personal, and by understanding each culture’s approach, achieving success may not be as challenging as initially believed to be.
