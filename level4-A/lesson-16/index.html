SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Sarah Evans, a management trainee from the UK, is at Tokyo headquarters on a two-year training program. Ambitious, meticulous, and organized, Sarah set three-month targets for her two-year stay. Yet, after five months of training, her expectations and the reality of the situation couldn’t be further apart.

Sarah

It’s been 5 months, and I haven’t learned or done anything new. I came here wanting to spend time in each of the main departments, so why am I still stuck here? My colleagues are nice and often invite me out for drinks after work, but I’m usually not in the mood. After a full day at work, I just want to go home. I’m here to learn new skills, not drink.

Mr. Saito

As the manager of the department, I wish Sarah would be more involved. Yes, she is very capable, but I’ve received complaints that she isn’t easy to communicate with and often refuses invitations from her fellow colleagues. She wants to learn many things but should focus on building closer relationships to better understand our processes and values.

Sarah failed to realize that single-mindedly pursuing her ambition came at the expense of learning valuable cultural experience. Before she knew it, she only ended up confused and demotivated. However, it is not too late for her to achieve her objectives if she aligns her goals with her peers to gain mutual understanding and support.

Answer these questions.

  • Do you feel that closer personal relationships benefit working relationships? Why or why not?
