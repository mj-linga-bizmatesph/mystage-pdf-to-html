SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Makoto Deguchi, a sales rep in the Overseas Sales Division of his company, drafted a major contract proposal for a leading French car manufacturer. When Makoto pitched his five-year proposal to the French purchasing manager, Daniel Lacroix, the response was far from what he had hoped for.

Daniel

We are absolutely interested in a long-lasting partnership with Makoto’s company, so I do not see the logic in tying us to a formal, multi-year contract. If anything, the contract seems to emphasize Makoto’s distrust and lack of confidence in our partnership. If his company’s product quality is up to snuff, they have nothing to worry about.

Makoto

This is confusing. My five-year proposal is very favorable for Daniel’s company. I included everything — from product training, logistics and inventory management, to a 15% discount for large orders. I wanted to create a long-lasting, win-win partnership, but Daniel is still unwilling to sign the contract. His actions, requests, and words don’t match.

Makoto’s efforts seemed in vain and his hopes for signing Daniel’s company became less and less likely. Though both parties have similar goals, distinct cultural approaches have negatively impacted the relationship of the parties involved and may ultimately jeopardize the growth of both companies.
