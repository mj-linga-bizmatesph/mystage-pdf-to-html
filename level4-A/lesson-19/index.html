(AM/PM) sharp – exactly at that time, not a minute earlier or later
accountability – responsibility or liability for something
contingency - backup plan for a possible problem or situation
pressing issue – a serious or important problem
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Jun Aikawa, an employee of the Procurement Department, scheduled an emergency telephone meeting at 8PM sharp with Raj Sharma, his contact in Mumbai, India, to discuss yet another delayed delivery of key parts. Raj calls 23 minutes late, with no apology, action plan, or sense of urgency — infuriating Jun.

Jun

This is the second delay we’ve had in three months. With the way things are going, we may not be able to meet our production quota. We need those parts. I can’t understand Raj’s business style. I have little use and time for small talk, excuses, and half-measures. We need a capable supplier with good accountability and a solid business continuity plan.

Raj

Jun wants contingency plans and solutions for everything. Unfortunately, there are just some things we just can’t predict or control. The labor strike that caused the delay was unavoidable. The only thing we can do is remain flexible and handle the problems as they come. Getting angry doesn’t help the issue. Jun should try to understand this.

While another delayed delivery of key parts is indeed a problem, Jun and Raj’s inability to see eye to eye poses a more serious threat. As business often depends on the successful collaboration between people and organizations, Jun and Raj may have to tackle the more pressing issue between them.
