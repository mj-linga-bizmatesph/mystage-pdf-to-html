Organizational or corporate culture can be simply defined as the way a company thinks and acts. The ins and outs of one’s own corporate culture are rarely explicitly defined and often implied, but understanding them, as well as those of others, will be invaluable to the success of one’s career.
Imagine yourself joining a foreign company for the first time, merging with a large conglomerate, or spearheading a joint venture with a young start-up. Whatever the case may be, in order to ensure a smooth transition, one must be aware of certain key differences in culture and how to best navigate around potential corporate clashes, shorten transition periods, and facilitate effective resolutions.
In order to get to the heart of organizational culture and its impacts, one must examine it from several perspectives. The next set of lessons explores the eight dimensions of organizational culture as described by Geert Hofstede, a leading expert on cultural studies.
