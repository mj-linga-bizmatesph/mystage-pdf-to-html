Before getting into Hofstede’s eight dimensions of organizational culture, it is important to understand some of the forces that impact, define, and mould each company’s unique identity.
  • The industry: Being in a bank versus an IT company affects everything, from attire to work style.
  • The leader: A president’s core values influence the direction of the company in numerous ways.
  • The structure: The company’s setup dictates how employees behave and perform their tasks.
  • The history: A company’s successes and failures have lasting impacts on its culture and strategy.
  • The rewards: A company’s reward system communicates what is important and what isn’t.
  • The people: A diverse workplace will have a very different atmosphere from one that isn’t.
  • The global impact: Global companies must adjust their strategies to suit a more global audience.
By understanding the above, companies may be able to more clearly pinpoint certain strengths, weaknesses, and strategies in order to better align company objectives with corporate culture.
