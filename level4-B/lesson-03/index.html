SEE

Read the article and take note of the highlighted phrases.

Some of us were taught that winning isn’t everything; it’s how hard we try that counts. However, in the business world, some companies place much more weight on results than the process. The first dimension of organizational culture explores this further.
Goal-oriented:
In these companies, employees are expected to get results. For some, this style may be very motivating because it allows for a larger degree of flexibility with minimal supervision. However, this approach may lead to a win-at-any-cost mentality that encourages foul play and short-sightedness.
Process-oriented:
In these companies, the journey is more important than the destination; they believe that an efficient, well-defined process eventually gets results. Progress is more easily measured and work is more consistent. However, employees locked into strict processes may suffer from a lack of drive and innovation.
These two are extremes, and most companies find a balance in between. Some tasks and projects may thrive in goal-oriented environments, while others from a more process-oriented approach. Knowing which to apply in certain situations ultimately contributes to a company’s identity and success.
