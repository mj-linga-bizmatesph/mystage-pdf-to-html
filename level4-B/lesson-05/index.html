Rules, regulations, and codes of conduct are things we’ve all been brought up to respect and follow. Our third dimension of culture recognizes that the application and adherence to certain rules and regulations vary from company to company, thus creating a unique environment.
Easy-going Discipline:
In easy-going companies, rules are loose and employees are given lots of leeway in how they manage their time and work. They believe that strict guidelines serve only to limit people’s creativity. However, the resulting uncertainty and need to improvise may also result in work inconsistency and inefficiency.
Strict discipline:
In strict companies, employees are guided by rules, supervised closely, and given clear directions. They believe that these enhance consistency, efficiency, and control among the staff. On the other hand, this may also limit their employees’ ability to think on their feet and adapt to changing conditions.
Degrees of discipline vary from industry to industry. However, an important thing to remember is that great corporate culture successfully aligns corporate vision, mission, and values, with employee growth and development. The result is an environment that fosters both excellence and profitability.
