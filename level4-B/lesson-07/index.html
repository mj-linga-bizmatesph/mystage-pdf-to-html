In a world full of different ethnicities and cultures, segregation is a challenge that many face. So too, in business. Though some companies are more welcoming than others, fitting in isn’t always easy. The fifth dimension of culture explores how easily companies accept new staff into the workplace.
Open Systems:
In open companies, newcomers are quickly welcomed. Staff and managers show a great deal of patience to newcomers, who quickly feel at ease. However, rookies may see themselves as being on equal footing with everyone else, which could lead to confusion or disrespectful behaviour.
Closed Systems:
In closed companies, newcomers are viewed with suspicion. Breaking the ice is tough and newcomers need to prove themselves. Surviving this difficult transition period can boost performance, but the lack of acceptance, camaraderie, and access to information may result in animosity and distrust.
Oftentimes, new employees are seen as overly soft, dependent, and lacking in real-world experience. Some might think that an open approach would be ideal, but others claim that a closed approach would more effectively put new hires in their place and better prepare them for what lies ahead.
