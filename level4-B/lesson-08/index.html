Most companies swear by the saying, “People are your greatest asset,” only to lay off chunks of their workforce when business goes down. The sixth dimension of organizational culture differentiates between companies that value staff welfare more than productivity, and those that prioritize results over their workforce.
Employee-oriented:
In this culture, companies believe that satisfied employees lead to higher productivity. Employees are seen as individuals, and managers take great care in looking after their staff. However, employees could easily fall into mediocrity as managers try to do what they can to protect their subordinates — even poor performers.
Work-oriented:
In this culture, employees are expected to perform well — and face the consequences if they do not. The office environment is far more impersonal but order is maintained more easily. Unfortunately, this has a way of cultivating distrust among colleagues and deepening the divide between managers and staff.
Although employee welfare and company productivity may sometimes appear to be at conflict with each other, these two things are not mutually exclusive. A company cannot survive by neglecting one or the other, and success is more easily attainable through the careful consideration of both sides.
