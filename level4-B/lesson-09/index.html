How employees view their leaders and how strongly they identify with corporate vision and philosophy are quintessential to the culture of the company. Our final two dimensions of organizational culture, acceptance of leadership and identification, explore these ideas more deeply.
High Versus Low Acceptance of Leadership:
In high-acceptance companies, employees are very receptive to management mandates. They aspire to climb the ladder and respect their leaders greatly. On the other hand, low-acceptance companies tend to have strict regulations, shaky morale, and a high-pressure workplace. As a result, employees typically form unions to express their frustration and create change.
Strong Versus Weak Company Identification:
In companies with strong identification, employees embrace corporate philosophies, see themselves as one with the company, and have a strong sense of ownership. On the flip side, in companies with weak identification, employees may be less loyal to the company and are more driven by short-term, monetary gains.
It is said that people don’t leave jobs; they leave managers. By adopting effective leadership styles that synergize corporate philosophies with business goals and employee motivations, companies can ensure that positivity, efficiency, and loyalty reverberate throughout the business.
