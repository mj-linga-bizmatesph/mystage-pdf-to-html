THE NEWBIE
11
WORDS & PHRASES
take (something) too far – to go beyond the limits or boundaries of (something)
smooth sailing – easy progress, to proceed without any difficulties
learn the ropes – learn or understand the basics of something
shaken – to feel shocked, disturbed, and upset
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

When Tim arrived on his first day of work, Randy, his manager, had explained that everyone is like family, and that he shouldn’t hesitate to ask for assistance. Tim’s colleagues have been very warm and helpful, but Tim seems to have taken asking for help too far, frustrating some of his coworkers.

Randy

It’s been three months since Tim has joined, and it hasn’t exactly been smooth sailing. He’s always asking others for help, which is fine, but he never helps them in return. His excuse is always, “I don’t know how to do that yet.” His casual and upfront approach also comes across as being a bit rude sometimes. I’d better talk to him about his attitude.

Tim

I just talked with Randy and he told me that people are concerned about my work and behavior. I thought I could rely on the others to help me out while I learn the ropes! Why are they complaining? How can I help when I’m still so new? Randy said to treat everyone in the company like family and that’s what I’ve been doing. So what’s wrong?

Tim was shaken. At first, he felt right at home, but after his talk with Randy, he began to feel more and more uncomfortable with his duties and colleagues. He felt pressured to perform and wasn’t sure how to interact with the others. What once was a promising start had become something else entirely.
