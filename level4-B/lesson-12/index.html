WORDS & PHRASES
oblivious – to be unaware about something
breakneck speed – dangerous and usually uncontrollable speed
flying by the seat of our pants – to go by feel or instinct without any formal guidelines
endeavor – an attempt to achieve a target or goal
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

James, a lead programmer at a major IT company, was looking for new work. One day, an old friend e-mailed him to ask if he wanted to join her small IT start-up. James, confident in his abilities and wanting to try something new, jumped at the chance — oblivious to the challenges that lay ahead.

James

I knew working for a venture company would be challenging, but I didn’t expect this. Things are moving at such breakneck speeds that I’m finding it hard to keep up. There’s no standardization, tight deadlines, lots of overtime, and on top of that, I’m handling much more than just IT. Sales, customer service — everything. I can’t take much more…

Talia

We’re trying to create something from nothing. This takes time, dedication, blood, sweat, and tears. In big companies, it’s easy to delegate. They have money, people, systems, and procedures for everything. But here, we’re flying by the seat of our pants. James has been in a traditional company his whole life. It’s no wonder things are hard for him.

Changes in procedures, environment, people, and culture have made adapting to Talia’s company a tiring endeavour for James. Although there are many opportunities for professional and personal growth at his post, all the stress he is experiencing makes him wonder if he made the right choice. with the others. What once was a promising start had become something else entirely.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Is Talia’s new venture company more goal- or process-oriented?
  • Would you describe your country’s workers as being more goal- or process-oriented?
  • How would you describe your country’s entrepreneurial spirit? Are young people interested in starting their own businesses? Why or why not?
  • If you had your own business, what do you imagine the culture to be like?

ACT

Role play.

Imagine you are James. Talia’s business needs more staff as it is growing very rapidly. You are meeting with your ex-coworkers who are interested in joining. Tell them about the work, the culture, and the pros and cons of working for a venture.
