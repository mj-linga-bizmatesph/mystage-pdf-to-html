WORDS & PHRASES
reinvigorate – to give new energy and strength
shake things up – to change things in a new way to improve the current situation
uphill battle – a difficult challenge
apathetic – lacking or having no concern, interest, or enthusiasm
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Kim, the new president of Ace Pharma, had a tough road ahead of her. Sales was steadily decreasing, morale was low, and product development was in a rut. With Bob, head of HR, Kim was hoping to reinvigorate the company. However, the problems may be more deeply rooted than Kim realized.

Kim

The problems we’re facing lies in the DNA of Ace’s corporate culture. We need to reframe our approach and our values. The staff have gotten used to mediocrity and we’ve been relying on our past successes for too long. We hope things will improve because of our history and product quality, but hoping is not enough. It’s time to start shaking things up.

Bob

I feel people are generally pretty happy. We treat everyone like family and our products have always been better than the rest. Until recently, we have never had to worry about sales. It’s great of Kim to hand out new vision cards, organize customer-focused seminars, and give speeches, but changing decades of culture is an uphill battle.

Six months passed and despite Kim’s best efforts, the employees of Ace Pharma remained apathetic to her campaign. Most of them had lost their vision cards, neglected customer focus, and had no sense of urgency. In an effort to demonstrate the seriousness of their situation, Kim began to consider layoffs.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Is Ace Pharma’s organizational culture more internally or externally driven?
  • What do you consider the best way to move and inspire employees into action?
  • How has your company’s previous successes and history created the culture it has today?
