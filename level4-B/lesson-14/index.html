WORDS & PHRASES
consolidate – to combine or unite different parts into a whole
tall order – a very difficult task
fall apart – to break down or to become disorganized and unsuccessful
hang in the balance – to be in a dangerous state or condition
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Jennifer, an IT consultant, is heading a project for a large insurance company to consolidate the IT processes of each region. The project requires her and her team to meet with the managers from each area to better understand their needs and business. Unfortunately, things are not going well.

Jennifer

Even though we’re experts, we’ve never faced such a tough job. Their regional managers are very resistant and we’re not getting the cooperation and information we need. The company has been so lax on rules that each region has developed and settled into their own processes over the years. Standardizing everything is a tall order, given the situation.

Won

Jennifer’s team is very impersonal and it’s hard to work with people we can’t connect with. They understand very little about our business yet the consultants are uninterested in anything other than their own areas of expertise. My region has developed its own process and I think it works well. The other regions feel the same. Why do we need this?

The atmosphere is tense and the project is falling apart. The regional managers have filed an official complaint to top management stating they had lost all confidence in Jennifer and her team’s ability to develop a workable system. The project hangs in the balance and Jennifer is at a loss.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe the insurance company’s culture? (Strict or Easy-going)
  • How would you describe Jennifer’s team’s approach? (Professional or Local)
  • How do business people typically build trust with their business counterparts in your country?
  • Would you say your company is flexible in the way it conducts business with other companies? Why or why not?

ACT

Role play.

Imagine you are Jennifer. What would you do to improve the current situation and reestablish good relations with your clients?
