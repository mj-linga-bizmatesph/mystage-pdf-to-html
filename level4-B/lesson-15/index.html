THE MERGER

WORDS & PHRASES
go south – to fail or decrease in condition, value
to strip away – to remove something with force
fashionista – someone who is very passionate and knowledgeable about fashion
cog – a small part of a large machine
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Matt Swift, the founder of 1UP, a successful fashion-forward brand for teens, recently merged with Globe, a national apparel company with a great distribution network that has been struggling in the youth market. Although it seemed to be a perfect match, it didn’t take long for things to go south.

Matt

I started 1UP with very clear values: freedom, creativity, and passion. We became the best because it was never about money or business – it was about fashion. I thought Globe understood that, but I was wrong. Our values have been stripped away and now we are told to focus on cutting costs at the expense of quality, and increasing quantity at the expense of integrity.

William

I was brought in to make sure 1UP gets in line with Globe’s standards. This is a business, and it’s my job to make sure 1UP doesn’t become a liability and hurt our bottom lines. They’re not seeing the big picture. I might not be a fashionista but I know numbers. They need to understand that sometimes sacrifices have to be made in order to grow.

With this shift, many of the top designers that Matt hired personally are on the verge of leaving the company. They loved 1UP, Matt, and fashion — but Globe represented none of this. To them, what once was a free-spirited, edgy fashion line has become yet another cog in a large corporate machine.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe the level of identification that 1UP employees have? (High/strong or Low/weak)
  • What are the differences between 1UP and Globe’s culture? (Strict/ Easy-going, Employee-/ Work-oriented)
  • What is important to consider before merging with or acquiring another company?
  • Based on your company’s culture and direction, what company would you consider to be a good joint venture? Would it be a partnership, merger, or acquisition?
