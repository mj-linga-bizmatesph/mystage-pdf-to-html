THE INSIDER
17
WORDS & PHRASES
lavishly – in rich, luxurious, or elaborate manner
jumping on bandwagons – to join a new trend because everyone else is doing it
have the gall to – have the arrogance, or be arrogant enough to do (something)
chest-thumping – the act of arrogant boasting or aggressive self-assertion
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

TeraCom is on the verge of bankruptcy. Its previous presidents spent lavishly, invested foolishly, and lost sight of TeraCom’s core business. Wes Blair, the managing director cum president, has been tasked to turn things around. Unfortunately, not everyone is convinced that Wes is right for the job.

Wes

We need to find ourselves. Decades of overspending have put us in a dangerous financial state. We need to take a long, serious look at cost reduction and offload anything unrelated to our core business. We lost ourselves by jumping on bandwagons, pursuing unnecessary acquisitions, and ignoring real issues. It’s time I bring us back to basics.

Lana

Wes has worked closely with every president before him. He’s been there for every bad decision and every misguided acquisition that’s put us in this situation. As an insider, he should’ve had this revelation years ago. Now that things are really bad, he has the gall to tell us we’ve been doing things wrong? He’s been part of the problem the whole time.

Unsurprisingly, many staff members and executives reacted negatively to Wes’ chest-thumping. Many felt that he was hypocritical and lacked accountability. Though Wes has every intention of righting the company, he has a long way to go in gaining the support he needs from the other executives and staff.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Would you describe TeraCom’s staff’s acceptance of Wes’ leadership as high or low?
  • Does TeraCom have an internally driven or externally driven culture? Why?
  • What are the pros of promoting from within the company?
  • What are the cons of promoting from within the company?

ACT

Role play.

Imagine you are attending a board meeting to discuss whom to appoint as the next company President. On one hand, you have Wes Blair, a 30-year veteran of the company with extensive internal knowledge. On the other hand, you have an outsider with equal experience, but he/ she is unfamiliar with the company’s history and culture. Which of the two would you recommend for the position?
