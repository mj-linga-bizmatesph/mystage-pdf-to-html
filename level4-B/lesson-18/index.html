WORDS & PHRASES
to catch (someone) off guard – to surprise someone in a negative way
to run a tight ship – to manage a team or group in a very strict manner
meticulous – showing extreme amounts of care or focus on minor details
drill sergeant – a typically strict non-commissioned officer who trains soldiers in military exercises
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Dennis, a 35-year-old manager at a small, family-owned restaurant for 12 years, left his job to become a management trainee at Eats, Inc. — one of the largest restaurant chains in the country. He was excited to start his OJT with Frank, his new manager. However, what happened next caught Dennis off guard.

Frank

I run a tight ship that’s in line with corporate policies, procedures, and standards. This isn’t a local café where people do what they want, when they want, how they want. I expect everyone to do exactly as they’ve been taught, no exceptions. Know the process, do the work, embrace the philosophy, and we’ll get along fine.

Dennis

I thought I signed up for restaurant management, not the army. Are you serious? We have to recite the company vision every morning? No wonder the turnover rate is so high here. I understand the need to standardize but this level of meticulousness is insane. “Wash carrots like this,” “greet customers like that—” the staff might as well be machines!

Initially, Dennis was impressed by the training, facilities, and professionalism. However, the overly-rigid culture of the company made him question his decision to join Eats. At his previous job, he handled everything from hiring to cooking to inventory; here, he was just a glorified drill sergeant.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe Eats, Inc. and Dennis’ previous company cultures? (Goal-/ Process-oriented)
  • Would you say there is a high level of identification at Eats, Inc? Why or why not?
  • Would you feel comfortable working for a company like Eats, Inc?
  • Are there many companies like this in your country? What kind of impact does this kind of culture have on employees?

ACT

Role play.

Imagine you are Dennis. Your friend is thinking about joining Eats, Inc. Find out what kind of working environment your friend feels comfortable in and then give him/her your advice.
