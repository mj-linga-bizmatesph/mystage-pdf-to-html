WORDS & PHRASES
at the forefront – to be at the lead or most important position
dumbstruck – to be shocked to the point of being speechless
be on one’s high horse – to arrogantly believe oneself to be superior to others
sign a petition – to sign a formal request to appeal to authority about something
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Ann, a consultant and MBA holder, was recruited to Watts, a medium-sized electrical parts supplier, as project manager. Although Watts prided itself on being a family business with local sensibilities, the hopes of taking the business national put Ann at the forefront, much to the dismay of the local staff.

Ann

We’re going to have to make big changes if we want to go national. This means implementing a clear chain of command, delegating key responsibilities, cutting low performers, and setting KPIs. Unfortunately, people aren’t cooperating. The team is exceeding budget, missing targets, and making decisions without me. I’m dumbstruck!

Raj

I don’t think Ann gets how things go around here. The concepts, theories, and systems she’s introduced are confusing everyone. On top of that, she just sits there on her high horse giving orders without putting in any work herself. That’s not how we do things around here. We all pitch in, take care of each other, and focus on teamwork.

Raj approached Ann to inform her that the team is having trouble with her particular style of management and has signed a petition to have her replaced as project manager. Ann was shocked at their inability to see the bigger picture — her job was to make sure things got done, not baby the staff.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe Watts’ organizational culture? (Local/ Professional)
  • How would you describe Watts’ culture towards employees? (Employee-/ Work-oriented)
  • How effective do you think MBA theories are when implementing them in real business situations?
  • How would you persuade someone to change their working style to adapt to new objectives?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine you are president of Watts. You received the petition from Raj, but are unwilling to replace Ann as project manager. Convince Raj in whatever way you feel is best to ensure the team’s cooperation.
