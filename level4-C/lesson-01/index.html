The question, “What do you do?” is something one is often asked when meeting people for the first time. Although we are more than our professions suggest, it is undeniable that our occupation and its culture have significant impacts on our identity and how we are perceived by others.
Every organization has its own occupational culture, which is defined as the patterns of thought and behaviour shared by members of the same profession. Occupational culture can be further categorized into three subcultures:
  • Operators – Those who are directly involved with their company’s products and services
  • Engineers – Those who are directly involved with technology, finance, data, and information
  • Executives – Those that oversee the company and its major processes
By observing the jargon, morals, outlooks, practices, and traditions that people with similar occupations share, one may develop a deeper understanding of certain business cultures, gain unique professional insights, and discover new avenues for success.
