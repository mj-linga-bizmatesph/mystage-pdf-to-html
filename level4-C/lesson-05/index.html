As the saying goes, “It’s not what you know, but who you know”. In some jobs, this expression more or less sums up the hiring process. In other words, no matter how qualified you are for a certain position, you may not even get the opportunity to apply for it unless you know the right people.

Closed Occupations:

In closed occupations, the chances of landing the job are incredibly slim unless you have the right connections and recommendations. The job is typically very exclusive, highly sought-after, and only for those lucky enough to have access to elite circles. Certain executive positions may exemplify this.

Open Occupations:

Most jobs are open occupations as they are offered to the most qualified candidates. Competency assessments, background checks, and interviews allow companies to screen for the person best fit for the job regardless of their connections. The post is open to the public and anyone can apply.
Although closed occupational culture is far less prevalent in hiring, we can never underestimate the value of having a wide network. As the business world and its professionals become increasingly connected, knowing the right people can often lead to once-in-a-lifetime employment opportunities.
