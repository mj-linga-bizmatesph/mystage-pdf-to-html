Read the article and take note of the highlighted phrases.

In some jobs, employees may be required to collaborate with many people in order to be successful and happy. That being said, certain other occupations require little to no collaboration with others. This disparity in the office environment forms very different cultures within occupations.

Social Occupations:

In these jobs, the office is more than just a space to work. It’s a place where people come to share ideas, find inspiration, support each other, and build relationships. Collaboration, team work, and communication are all highly regarded and the environment has a huge impact on job satisfaction.

Private Occupations:

In these jobs, there is little need for continual support and collaboration. Defined by high degrees of flexibility and the ability to work from anywhere means private occupations place high amounts of value on freedom, determination, and self-expression.
The rise in flex time, telecommuting, and home-based start-ups have given people more opportunities to experience the lifestyle of a private occupation. Despite the conveniences that come with private occupations, some have found it difficult to cope with the lack of social interaction.
