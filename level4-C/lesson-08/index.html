Almost every industry, company, and occupation has its own peak and non-peak season. However, in some jobs, these peaks and valleys are so extreme that during the busy season, some people live completely different lives. On the other hand, certain other occupations offer much more stability.

Constant Occupations:

These occupations have ongoing daily duties that keep their employees busy. However, every now and then, these occupations need to put in additional overtime hours when things get hairy. Yet, on the whole, constant occupational culture allows for a fairly balanced lifestyle.

Seasonal Occupations:

For seasonal occupations, work schedules are much more extreme. During the on-season, some may be away from home for months at a time or working daily 16-hour shifts, resulting in enormous physical and mental stress. On the other hand, when things are slow, they are basically without work.
Missing loved ones’ birthdays, recitals, and graduations may seem like a high price to pay for people in seasonal occupations. However, seasonal job holders often have a better work-life balance and tend to value the time with their family during the off-season more than constant job holders.
