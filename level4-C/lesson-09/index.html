Social relationships, especially at the office, can be a complicated matter. Without understanding the numerous personalities, motivations, and occupational cultures that occupy the office space, seeing eye to eye on certain issues and working together can be a herculean affair. However, one can contribute to a healthier workplace environment by considering the following tips:
  • Be responsible and accountable: Do what you say and fulfill your professional obligations.
  • Show appreciation: A simple “thank you,” “good job,” or smile can do wonders.
  • Observe: Watch others, see how they behave, know their triggers, and consider why.
  • Ask questions: Some have said intelligence comes from asking the right questions.
  • Initiate: Whether it’s introducing yourself to new staff or making small talk — act, don’t react.
  • Clarify: Assumptions have led to many business failures and misunderstandings. Don’t guess.
  • Be sincere: For better or worse, be yourself. No one wants to have to second-guess intentions.
  • Do little acts of kindness: Get someone coffee, hold the door open; a little kindness goes a long way.
Most can agree that having happy working relationships in the workplace is the cornerstone of any successful business. By applying some of the tips above, we can all work towards greater professional harmony, understanding, and growth, regardless of occupation, group orientation, or position.
