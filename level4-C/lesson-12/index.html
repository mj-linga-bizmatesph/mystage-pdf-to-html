WORDS &
PHRASES
get a feel for (something) – to become familiar with (something)
give it your all – to do your best and give 100%
expendable – seen as something of little value that can be used and easily replaced
indifferent – unconcerned, unworried, or uncaring about something
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Rina was assigned to oversee operations and improve the situation at her company’s overseas factory. Turnover and quality have been constant issues, so in order to get a feel for things at the factory, Rina sat down with Mark, the previous manager, and Rob, one of the current staff.

Mark

Where I come from, a job is a job. No matter what you do, you have to give it your all! The culture here is different from what I’m used to. We should be proud of our work, but I had seen little in terms of loyalty and work ethics. Everyone should’ve followed orders and respected the chain of command but that wasn’t the case. It was frustrating.

Rob

“Do this. Do that.” We’re just army ants to Mark. There isn’t much keeping us here except for the pay. Anyone can do what we do, so I feel totally expendable. Is it a surprise that I don’t feel any sort of attachment to the work, the people, or the company? That being said, If there are better opportunities somewhere else, I’m gone.

Working relationships can be hard to manage, especially when there are such large differences in motivation and culture. Even though Mark was very loyal and dedicated to his work, the local staff had remained indifferent. Rina will have to take a different approach if the situation is to improve.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe Mark’s and Rob’s identification with their work? (High/Low)
  • Is occupational culture among executives and operators consistently different? Why or why not?
  • Is a high salary typically enough to keep staff satisfied? Why or why not?
  • What do you find most satisfying about your job?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine you are Rina. After hearing Mark’s and Rob’s viewpoints, you need to report your findings back to your boss. Once you’ve finished reporting the situation, brainstorm some ideas with your boss to resolve the issue.
