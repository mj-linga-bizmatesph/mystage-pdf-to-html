WORDS &
PHRASES
lose (one’s) cool – to lose (one’s) temper or to get very angry
be sick of (something) – to be tired or frustrated with (something)
scour through (something) – to examine or search through (something) carefully
tension – an uncomfortable straining, or anxious atmosphere/mental state
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Cam is part of the Customer Support Team at a company specializing in developing office software. Recently, there have been large spikes in the amount of complaints after the release of a major patch from the Engineering Department. Although Cam doesn’t like to complain, this time he lost his cool.

Cam

Dealing with angry customers is torture. They yell, scream, and often forget that we’re people, too. The sad part is, there’s nothing we can do about it. We’re constantly cleaning up other people’s mistakes and get very little recognition for what we do. I want to help by giving suggestions to Engineering but they don’t take me seriously. I’m sick of it.

Ned

As programmers, we need to write and scour through thousands of lines of code. So, it’s expected that we encounter some bugs at launch. It’s not like we put the bugs in there on purpose! What we do is very complex and Cam’s “suggestions” don’t help. We know what the issues are. Dealing with clients is his job, coding is mine — let’s leave it at that.

Tensions began to rise soon after the launch, the CS Team and the Engineering Department began communicating less and less. Cam, in particular, started to feel more and more like an undervalued punching bag. He had accepted his fate and gave up on trying to improve the situation.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you classify Cam’s work? (Constant/Seasonal)
  • How would you describe the type of knowledge required to do Ned’s job? How does this affect his ability to communicate with Cam? (General/Esoteric)
  • How has the high emotional demand of Cam’s work impacted his morale?
  • What are some tips for communicating with people who have high degrees of esoteric knowledge?
