A CONDESCENDING REMARK
14
WORDS &
PHRASES
rave reviews – highly positive reviews
despicable – something awful that deserves hatred, anger, or contempt
zilch – nothing, zero
mar – to make something less enjoyable, good, or successful
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

The first smart contact lenses, manufactured by SmarT, were finally approved, and factories were working non-stop to meet deadlines. After a wildly successful launch, rave reviews, and huge profits, SmarT’s CEO gave Johan, the talented inventor, a large bonus, while the factory workers, to their disappointment, received nothing.

Johan

They’re just blue-collars. All they do is follow simple instructions, push some buttons, and pull some levers. Robots can do that. My development team and I are the real reason why this project was such a smashing success. So, why do they deserve more? They got their overtime pay and now they want more? That’s just being greedy. It’s despicable.

Paul

Just blue-collars?! Just being greedy?! Despicable?! Is that how we’re viewed? If it weren’t for us, SmarT would have no product. Zilch. We deserve more respect. What’s more, we demand a formal apology and a 10% increase in wages. After all, the company can afford it now. If not, we’re striking. We’ll see how valuable we are then.

Johan’s comments infuriated the factory staff and SmarT’s successful product launch was marred by controversy. No longer was the media focused on the global success of the world’s first smart contact lens — the news was now about the condescending remarks of its technological visionary.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you define Johan’s work? (Modern/Traditional)
  • Why doesn’t Johan seem to respect Paul’s profession?
  • How are unions viewed in your company and in your country?
  • What do you think is a fair way of distributing bonuses? Should it be based on position? Performance? Tenure?
