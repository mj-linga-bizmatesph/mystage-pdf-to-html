WORDS &
PHRASES
father figure – a wise, gentle, and respected older man that also provides emotional support like a real father would
come out the other end – to get through something (usually a hard or challenging experience)
to hemorrhage – to lose large or great amounts of blood or financial assets
a man of the people – a person who identifies and empathizes with ordinary people and is liked in return
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

X-Motors, a family-owned business that builds custom cars, is a wonderful place to work. Everyone is friendly, especially Cesar, one of X-Motors’ pioneer employees. He is a role model and father figure to many of the staff. Although business has been slow, morale remained high thanks to Cesar’s efforts.

Cesar

I think of everyone here like family. They’re all like my sons and daughters. Especially Hiro, the President’s son. I watched him grow and taught him everything there is to know about cars. Now, he’s managing our shop like a pro. I know business has been slow, but we’ll get through it. As long as we stick together, we’ll come out the other end okay.

Hiro

Everyone is working hard and keeping their spirits up, but we’re hemorrhaging money. Badly. Father is telling me to start reducing costs by laying off our senior staff. Some of these people, like Cesar, have been with us for more than 35 years. I tried talking Father out of it, but he said I shouldn’t let emotions get in the way of good business decisions.

Hiro’s father has always been a tough business man who was always better with numbers than with people. Hiro, however, is a man of the people and couldn’t be more different. The future of X-Motors and the careers of loyal staff like Cesar hang in the balance, and Hiro is stuck in the middle.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe Cesar’s approach to his occupation? (Social/Private)
  • How much emotional demand does Hiro’s role as shop manager require? (High/Low)
  • What are your thoughts on Hiro’s father’s approach to business?
  • What are some other options to reduce costs? At what point should companies start considering layoffs? Can you think of any examples where this has happened?

ACT

Role play.

You are Hiro. Meet with Cesar, inform him of the situation, and tell him your decision.
