SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Profits fell another 15% at Surge, a mid-sized semiconductor manufacturer. Morale is low, and the executives are wondering where to place the blame. An emergency meeting is called and what started off as a typical meeting soon became a heated exchange between Marketing and Sales.

Bonne

Marketing sets up the potential customers and Sales is supposed to knock them down. We’ve been working hard to promote our brand but the salespeople are failing to capitalize on our efforts. They’re more worried about personal quotas and bonuses than simply closing the deal. It’s the busy season so they’d better start sharing leads, helping each other, and communicating more.

Anna

Marketing keeps accusing us of not working as a team, but all they do is spend company money. If they care so much about sharing, why not hand over some of their budget? We’re stretched thin and we need more resources. Bonne has no idea how tough sales is. We’re the ones dealing with people face to face. We don’t get to hide in the office.

As each side entrenches themselves deeper and deeper into their positions, the infighting became worse and worse. Pride, stubbornness, and blame shifting has damaged the relationship between the two departments. A solution must be found quickly if the situation is to recover.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe Anna’s work in terms of emotional demand? (High/Low)
  • With regard to sales in your industry, is there a busy season and a slow season?
  • In your company, which departments do you think are more likely to have conflicts with others? Why?
  • What do you think is the best way to resolve these inter-departmental conflicts?
