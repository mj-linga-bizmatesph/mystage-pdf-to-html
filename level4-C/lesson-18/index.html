WORDS &
PHRASES
press ahead – to continue doing something despite challenges or setbacks
get (something) off the ground – to start (something) successfully
domestic dispute – a verbal or physical fight involving people living in the same home
sour – filled with anger, disappointment, or resentment
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Jo, Nina, and Lee had always dreamed of becoming lawyers and opening a practice together. Despite his efforts, Jo was unable to pass the bar exam while the other two pressed ahead and started a small firm. Jo, wanting to help, focused his efforts on PR and soon landed a big case for his friends.

Nina

It has been really hard getting our practice off the ground so we really appreciate Jo’s work in getting us such a big case. Unfortunately, Jo’s attitude has changed recently. Maybe he’s overcompensating for not passing the bar, but he’s been bossing us around like children. Lee and I are the lawyers. He has no right to tell us what to do.

Jo

Nina and Lee haven’t been getting much work. If it wasn’t for me, they’d still be settling domestic disputes and traffic cases. It’s because of my contacts and me that we landed such a big opportunity. I might not have passed the bar yet, but I know I’m a good leader and I know what needs to be done. Why are they suddenly acting so cold towards me?

At this point, their friendship began to sour and their dreams of working together started to crumble. Jo felt like he was constantly looked down on while Nina and Lee began distancing themselves more and more. With everyone’s reputation at risk, the three had better find a way to move forward.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe Jo, Nina, and Lee’s occupational identification? (High/Low)
  • How would you describe being the owner of a startup? (Social/Private)
  • What do Jo, Nina, and Lee need to do in order to make things work?
  • Would you ever want to start a business with your close friends?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

You are Lee. You’ve been stuck in the middle between Nina and Jo’s constant arguing and little progress is being made. Consider both sides and come up with a plan that both sides can agree to.
