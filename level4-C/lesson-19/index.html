WORDS & PHRASES
make (one’s) own way in the world – to depend only on (oneself) to accomplish something
have an eye for (something) – being able to appreciate, understand, and make good judgments about (something)
make headway – to make progress
disenchanted – feel disappointed by someone or something previously respected or admired
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Yumi was born into a powerful business family. After returning from her studies in the UK, she was expected to join the family business. However, soon after entering her father’s company, she realized she wanted to make her own way in the world. She quit her job and joined a young design firm.

Yumi

I don’t want to be known for my family’s name, but for who I am and what I accomplish. That’s why I joined this “young” company. Who knew it’d be so frustrating? Clients have no eye for art at all, and their criticisms are baseless. There’s no more freedom in art than there is at my father’s company! I don’t need more people trying to control me.

Ken

As a designer myself, I can say that we are a proud group of people. However, as a business person, it’s essential that we respect our client’s opinions. Yumi needs to learn how to accept criticism and remove her pride from the equation. Design is a collaborative process. She’d better learn this quickly if she wants to make any headway here.

Coming from an affluent but confining background has left Yumi with a strong desire for self-expression. Although she has the talent and drive to be successful, the image of being a designer and the true nature of the work left her disenchanted, frustrated, and on the verge of giving up.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Based on Yumi’s background, which occupational culture would suit her best? (Social/Private)
  • Based on Ken’s reaction, how would he describe a successful designer’s occupational culture? (Social/Private)
  • How would you describe graphic design and art-based occupations? (Modern/Traditional)
  • How has your upbringing influenced your job choices and outlook on certain occupational cultures?
