Gender differences and culture have been hot topics for both men and women for countless centuries. In attempts to determine what makes men so different from women, many studies have been conducted to pinpoint our physical and emotional differences through studies of human evolution, genetics, and DNA.
Although the results have provided great insights on the origins of gender-specific behaviours, modern-day men and women are a far cry from their hunting and gathering ancestors. There may have been a time when there were clearer differences between gender roles and culture, but in today’s society, it may no longer be appropriate to say that men are poor at multi-tasking because they were once hunters and women can’t read maps because their DNA doesn’t allow for it.
In this rank, we will seek to understand modern-day gender values, perspectives, and what it means to be a man and woman in the 21st century. Although anything from national to occupational culture and personal experiences may influence these perspectives, the important thing to note is that we, as men and women, are always changing – and keeping up with the times is essential for success.
