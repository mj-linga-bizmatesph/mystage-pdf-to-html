Not so long ago, one’s role in society and the values associated with being man or woman were inseparable. However, as society evolved, people began to break from traditional gender roles and started to develop their own personal values – not as men or women, but as individuals.
Steven (42) Canadian – President of overseas subsidiary – Who are you and what do you value?
 Who I am and what I value change depending on the role I’m playing. For example, at home, I want to be a loving husband and a caring father. At work, I hope I’m viewed as a communicative, charismatic, and trustworthy leader. In the end, it’s all about taking care of the people I care about.
Chika (37) Japanese – Customer service leader – Who are you and what do you value?
 I want people to view me as a spunky and bright person. To be honest I’m really happy with who I am now. Before, I was unsure of myself because I was trying to be something I’m not, but now I feel confident and independent. This is very important. I value my personal growth very much.
As people, businesses, and nations around the world continue to explore and push the boundaries of traditional gender-specific roles and values, the notions of “be a man” and “act like a lady” are becoming less and less relevant, and may one day vanish entirely.
