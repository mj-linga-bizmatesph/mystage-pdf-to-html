Our worldview is determined by our past experiences, environment, and social interactions. Although science has proven that men and women physically view the world differently, how we process that information ultimately determines our unique perspectives as men and women.
Ava (30) – Sri Lankan – Designer – How has your gender impacted your worldview?
I think I’ve got a lot of choices with where I want to live my life. As a woman though, I feel there’s a lot of pressure. Marriage, family, and my career - all come with certain expectations that I have to contend with. It’s a bit unfair, but it gives me a certain fire that makes me want to rise above it.
Justin (32) Canadian – Product Developer – How has your gender impacted your worldview?
It was, for a lack of a better word, empowering to be brought up as a man. I was told that the world is my oyster. So, I went out there and lived life on my terms. I’ve got a daughter of my own now, and I’m going to tell her the same. The world is something to be challenged and changed for the better.
Although gender perceptions are evolving, many still tag female and male perspectives as being two distinct points of view. How we evolve from here will depend on how much we continue to allow external pressures impact the way we perceive ourselves, the world, and our gender.
