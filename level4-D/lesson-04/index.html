Physical differences may be the easiest to discern when comparing men and women. However, in modern, white-collar societies where physicality no longer directly determines survival, occupation, or status, these differences, however apparent, are becoming less and less important.
Alex (33) – Chilean – Accountant – How important is physicality for men in modern society?
 I look at those fitness models in magazines and on TV and I wish I had an 8-pack. But I’m an accountant, so whatever. However, interestingly enough, my wife tells me she likes my belly. She can put her head on it and it makes her feel safe. So I guess I have something those models don’t have!
Mari (30) – Japanese – Sales – How important is physicality for women in modern society?
 I’m a tough girl. I’ve never felt like I couldn’t do something physically. I can do anything a man can do. Nowadays, there are female soldiers, fire fighters, and athletes. Some say men are still stronger, but mental toughness plays a big part - and mental strength trumps physical strength every time.
Even though certain physical traits may still impact one’s abilities or status to some degree, much of society has begun to look to other things as signs of success and power. And although men and women may always be physically different, strength - in a modern context - has little to do with muscle.
