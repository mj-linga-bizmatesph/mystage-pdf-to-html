Studies of the brain have revealed some interesting results on gender-specific tendencies. However, as fascinating as the research is, an MRI analysis of over 1,400 people has found that most brains are a combination of both “male” and “female” traits. As a result, we may be more similar than we think.
Wayne (24) – British – Grad Student– Would you say your way of thinking is typically male?
 If we’re talking about typical male thinking patterns and behavior, I’d say that I fit the mould to some degree. For example, I like sports, beer with red meat, and looking at girls. On the other hand, I’m also very passive; I like cooking and cleaning, and cry when I watch romantic comedies.
Emily (35) – Singaporean – Nurse – Would you say your way of thinking is typically female?
 Scientific research shows differences in grey matter, neural connections, and brain size, but external experiences have significant impacts on our psychology. I act a certain way because of the way I was treated. If you’re treated like a girl, you start acting like one. I am what the world made me.
Although psychological differences between genders may make for more interesting water cooler chat, contrary to popular belief, our similarities vastly outweigh our differences. Though some may be content believing that men are men and women are women, the mind is infinitely more complex.
