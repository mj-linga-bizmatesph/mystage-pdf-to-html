Emotions run deep in all people. And although they are received, processed, and expressed differently according to gender, emotions are what make us human. That being said, the more interesting question is: are you in control of your emotions, or are they in control of you?
Christine (36) – Irish – Lifestyle Coach – How emotional are you?
 My work often requires me to be emotionally in tune with the people around me. I teach people to embrace their emotions and not to be afraid of them. Feelings never lie. Gender doesn’t play much of a role, in my opinion. Instead, it’s about accepting yourself and knowing when and how much to feel.
Hector (40) Spanish – Teacher – How emotional are you?
 I am very emotional. Most people in my country are. It doesn’t matter if they are men or women. It’s nothing to be ashamed of. Happiness, sadness, rage, peace, all of these things add spice to life. We feel and so we are alive. I believe this is true for men, women, children, animals, and all living things.
In business, some believe that emotions are best left at the door. Yet, so much of our motivation, creativity, and relationships are built around our ability to feel. So, instead of using emotions as a way of comparing genders, one might see emotion as a way to unite people towards a common cause.
