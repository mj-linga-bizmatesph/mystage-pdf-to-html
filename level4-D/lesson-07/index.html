It has been said that success and happiness are dependent on the quality of one’s relationships. Yet how we build and maintain these relationships are often complex affairs - made even more difficult by unpredictable and intricate gender dynamics.
Megan (27) – Canadian – Flight Attendant – Do you find it easier to work with men or women?
 There are much more women than men in my line of work. Some are easy to work with and some aren’t. No matter where you go, there are office politics… but I noticed when men aren’t around, women are a lot more themselves - for better or worse. Work is hard, no matter who you work with.
Andrew (50) – Australian – PR Manager– Do you find it easier to work with men or women?
 There’s been a lot of great progress made these past years in terms of gender equality at the office. That being said, I feel like I need to walk on eggshells with some of the female staff. If I’m too casual or too tough, it could be misinterpreted as being something else. This has really added to my stress.
Although gender and office politics may impact the way certain individuals behave, it is important to remember that the success of any relationship, professional or otherwise, is built upon the tenets of mutual respect, understanding, and open communication.
