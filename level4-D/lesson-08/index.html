Humans are social creatures. As a result, our ability to communicate effectively has tremendous impact on how we are viewed and how we view others. However, gaps in communication between people and genders still occur despite our best intentions and modern sensibilities.
Jim (43) - Welsh – Designer: Describe typical male communication styles versus your own:
 I guess people would say that men aren’t good at emoting. That we don’t talk about our feelings and we keep to ourselves. I can see why some would say that. I’m not much for idle chit-chat, but I wouldn’t say that I don’t express my feelings. It really depends on who I’m talking to and why.
Mira (22) – Korean - Dancer: Describe typical female communication styles versus your own:
 I’d say that people expect women to be more talkative and personal. I agree - we communicate to build relationships. So, when I share my feelings, I want the other person to understand how I feel and form a deeper bond. I’m not always looking for answers. I think women intuitively get that.
Although the way people communicate may be influenced by various factors, the simple desire to be understood is apparent across all nationalities, cultures, and genders. That being said, the things we don’t say are often just as important as the things we do.
