WORDS &
PHRASES
to lend a hand – to offer or give assistance
to spell something out – to give all the little details
personable – to be pleasant and well-spoken
to be at a loss - to be confused and uncertain
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Chris, the president of a successful start-up just hired Emi, an executive assistant to lend a hand with his administrative tasks. Although things got off to a good start, Chris’ communication style became more and more difficult for Emi to understand, and soon began interfering with her duties.

Chris

If I have to spell everything out for her, she’s not doing her job. According to her resume, she’s got a lot of experience working for other executives so she should know what needs to be done. If I have to hold her hand and explain every little thing, I might as well just do it myself. I just want her to get things done, not chit-chat with me.

Emi

Chris is really difficult to understand. I really don’t know what he wants. When I submit my work, he glances it over, gives it back to me, and tells me to redo it without any feedback. It’s like he expects me to read his mind. Maybe it’s a guy thing, but Chris needs to learn how to communicate what he wants more clearly and be more personable.

As the situation continued to devolve, Emi began to wonder if she had made a mistake by agreeing to work for Chris. Even though she tried hard to understand him better and develop a closer relationship by engaging him in frequent small talk and conversation, Chris remained unreceptive. Emi is at a loss.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe Chris’ communication style and expectations?
  • Why is Emi’s approach of using small talk and conversation ineffective with Chris?
  • Would you agree that men are more task-focused and women are more relationship-focused?
  • What do Chris and Emi need to do to ensure the success of their working relationship?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play

  • You are Chris. Emi hasn’t been completing her tasks to your satisfaction. Speak with Emi to find out what the problem is.
  • You are Emi. Chris’ lack of effective communication has been causing you to struggle with your tasks. Speak with Chris to address the issue.
