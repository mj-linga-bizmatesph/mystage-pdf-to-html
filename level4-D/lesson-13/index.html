EMOTIONAL ROLLERCOASTER
13
WORDS &
PHRASES
to clash – to conflict or disagree, especially in opinions or interests
protocol – official process or procedure for something
to heed - to pay attention to or take notice of
to be at each other’s throats – to quarrel, fight, or argue
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Edward and Tammy have always been very passionate about their work. Tammy always expresses her feelings very openly whereas Edward has always been more inexpressive and stubborn. When Tammy and Edward found themselves on the same team, their wildly different personalities clashed.

Tammy

Look – you can’t just do whatever you want! We have procedures and protocols. Also, I’d appreciate it if you stopped telling me that I’m overreacting! I’m not! I’m just stating the obvious. If you knew how to do your job, we wouldn’t be having this problem! So, stop telling me that I’m emotional and start worrying about yourself!

Edward

Tammy, you need to settle down and stop overreacting to everything I’m saying. All I want to do is simply streamline our processes. You’re wasting so much of your time on these meaningless tasks when you could be doing something useful. I’ve had years of experience doing what I do, so it would be wise for you to heed good advice.

With Tammy and Edward constantly at each other’s throats, it was hard for the team to get anything productive accomplished. Although their passion for their work was a positive, Tammy’s emotional outbursts and Edward’s cold approach to business and office relationships weren’t helping matters.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Is it more difficult to deal with overly-emotional or unemotional colleagues?
  • Do you think it’s appropriate to assume that women are emotional and men are unexpressive?
  • Have you ever reacted emotionally to a situation in your office?
  • What do you think Tammy and Edward need to do in order to improve their working relationship?
