SEE

Read and analyze the story.

It was time for yearly performance evaluations at Vita, a medium-sized IT company. Sadly, the company had been facing some issues with the way the evaluations were being handled. Some of the men felt trepidation giving direct feedback to women causing confusion and distrust among the staff.

Pete

I’m always on edge when I’m evaluating the women in the office. It’s not about communication issues, but some women have been coming down on us for discrimination and harassment. I’m trying to be fair, but if I’m too soft, I’m the bad guy; if I’m too hard, I’m the bad guy. I can’t help but feel some are using their gender as an advantage.

Carrie

This is unfair. No matter what we do, we always end up with the short end of the stick. If we’re too passive, we’re either ignored or brushed off. On the other hand, if we take a stronger stance and position, we’re suddenly seen as angry, unreasonable, and selfish feminists that “use our gender as an advantage”, even if that’s not our intention.

Although the evaluations were designed to be as fair as possible, recent rise in complaints regarding unfair evaluations from women put a strain on the credibility of the system. If the situation continues unchecked, the gender dynamics among the staff could spell big trouble for the company.
