LENDING AN EAR
18
WORDS &
PHRASES
to offload (something) - to relieve oneself from problems or worries by talking to someone
draining – something that is physically or mentally tiring
to beat around the bush - to avoid saying or doing something directly
to be torn about (something) – to be filled with uncertainty about two or more choices
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Ryan, the new member of the sales team at Horizon Tech, hasn’t been fitting in very well with the team. He is aggressive and refuses to take any advice. Diana, Ryan’s Mentor at Horizon Tech, is feeling frustrated. She talks with her two colleagues about the issue, hoping to offload some stress.

Mana

I can totally understand how you feel. It must be really draining, trying to build a close working relationship with someone like that. I wonder what his goal is for joining the team if his attitude is like that! The last thing I’d want is to be stressed out by my colleagues when I go to the office everyday. Do you think things are going to improve?

Kaz

Well, you should tell him to get his act together and be a team player. I had a similar situation once where this one guy just wouldn’t cooperate. So, I sat him down and told him very plainly and clearly that his attitude needed to improve, or else he’d risk losing the position. You should do the same. No use in beating around the bush.

Although Diana appreciated Kaz’s input, she didn’t really feel better about things. Mana, on the other hand, had always been a great listener and seemed to really get where Diana was coming from. Diana knows what she needs to do but was torn about the approach she wants to take.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you describe Mana’s approach to Diana’s problem?
  • How would you describe Kaz’s approach to the problem?
  • Are moral support and advice two distinct things or are they one and the same?
  • In this situation, which of the two did Diana need more and why?

ACT

Role play

You are Diana’s boss. Based on what you know about Diana, consider the best approach and speak with her to find out how things are going.
