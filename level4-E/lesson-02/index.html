Read the article and take note of the highlighted phrases.

The end of World War II saw a large rise in marriages, drops in average marriage age, and an increase in the number of births, resulting in a “baby boom” across industrialized nations. This boosted the global economy and resulted in an era of prosperity, change, and the belief that anything was possible.

Characteristics:

Defined by strong work ethic, baby boomers are very committed to their careers and have worked hard for achievements and titles. As a result, they can be quite competitive and hierarchical. The booming economy also allowed for the pursuit of self-actualization and expression unlike previous generations.

Landmark Events:

Civil Rights Movement (1960s): People like Martin Luther King inspired many to fight for social change. Cuban Missile Crisis (1962): The world survived the threat of nuclear war and mutual destruction. Moon Landing (1969): The world watched in awe as Neil Armstrong took “one small step for man,…”

Defining Technology:

Television: Although invented in 1923, TVs didn’t have significant market penetration or cultural impact until 1946 when 55.7% of American households had one. By 1962, that number had soared to 90%.
Experience will always be a valuable asset. And even though some baby boomers are starting to retire, those that are still in the workforce continue to be important contributors to the global economy, the development of new talent, and the success of their respective companies.
