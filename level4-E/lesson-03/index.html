Also known as the “baby bust,” this generation’s birth rate was much lower than previous generations. In America, Gen X saw the rise of latchkey children, high divorce rates, and a pop culture angst that reflected an uncertain economy, dangerous diseases, and a lack of trust in the government.

Characteristics:

Gen Xers are a highly educated generation and are classified as being realistic, independent, and more socially progressive than their baby boomer parents. On the other hand, the challenges of growing up in an era of change and uncertainty left many Gen Xers hardened, skeptical, and cynical.

Landmark Events:

Fall of the Berlin Wall (1989): Communism fell and America was left as the only global super power.The HIV Epidemic (1980s): Killing more than 14,000 people by the end of the 80s, the world panicked.The Dot-Com Bubble (1990s): After some years of prosperity, the bubble burst — leaving many jobless.

Defining Technology:

Personal Computer: By the mid-70s, microprocessors were becoming less expensive and by the 80s, off-the-shelf PCs had arrived - giving rise to today’s tech giants like Apple and Microsoft.
Gen X has seen its fair share of difficulties. The 1987 stock market crash and the Dot-Com bust in 2000 put many on the ropes just as they were establishing their careers. However, despite these setbacks, Xers continue to be innovators, fighters, and a major driving force in the modern world.
