Comprised of youngsters in their mid-twenties to thirties, millennials have overtaken other generations to become the dominant workforce in the US today. In order to best utilize the next wave of human talent, baby boomer and Gen X bosses had best understand what makes millennials tick.

Characteristics:

Desiring flexibility, recognition, and a deep sense of purpose, millennials thrive in collaborative, fast paced, and open environments. Life balance, corporate transparency, and equality are all very important and millennials would rather quit than stick it out if things aren’t going to plan.

Landmark Events:

World Trade Center (2001): Terrorism took front and center and the world was changed forever.
Barack Obama (2008): The first African-American President set a new standard for equality.
Gay Marriage (2015): After a decades-long battle, the US Supreme Court legalized same-sex marriages.

Defining Technology:

Smartphone/SNS: Thanks to the proliferation of smartphone technology, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube impacted the lives of millennials across the developed world and defined a generation.
Many millennials from developed nations grew up wanting for very little. Doting parents wanting nothing but the best for their children created a generation that is viewed as being spoiled and impatient. This certainly applies to some; but for other millennials, nothing could be further from the truth.
