As we continue to highlight the challenges that occur when multiple generations inhabit the same organization, it is important to remember that opportunities for growth are often born of conflict.

INPUT & COLLABORATION

For millennials, cross-generational idea-sharing and collaboration allow for a deeper sense of growth, contribution, and belonging. On the other hand, others may see this as an affront to their position and prefer to take a “just do it” approach for the sake of efficiency or pride — leaving millennials frustrated and disengaged. However, by focusing on more structured collaborative processes alongside detailed objectives and deadlines, employees may be able to get the best of both worlds.

TECHNOLOGY & ADAPTABILITY

Unfamiliarity and lack of confidence with new technology often results in an unwillingness to change. This puts young and old generations at odds and results in an environment stuck in the middle between tried-and-true business practices and uncharted advancements. By focusing on creating a culture of constant improvement and two-way mentoring, everyone can get on the same page which allows for the pursuit of exciting new prospects while minimizing risk and discomfort for all involved.
It is said that acknowledging that problems exist is the most important step in creating solutions. By recognizing the issues that plague cross-generational workplaces on a daily basis, we are one step closer to creating an ideal work environment.
