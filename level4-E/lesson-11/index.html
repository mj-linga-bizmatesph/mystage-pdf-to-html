WORDS & PHRASES
trouble is brewing – problems are forming
bust one’s chops – to work very hard
pay one’s dues – to fulfill certain obligations or requirements before progressing
keep (something) in check – to monitor, control, or limit something
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Trouble was brewing between Chris, an ambitious up-and-coming engineer, and Grant, his direct supervisor ten years his senior. Although Chris enjoyed his work and the company, working under Grant wasn’t easy. Grant had always seen Chris’ age as a weakness and wasn’t afraid to express his doubts.

Chris

I really don’t appreciate the way Grant keeps looking down on me. I’ve been busting my chops but haven’t been recognized at all for my efforts. I’m not sure how much more of this I want to take. I know I’ve only been here for little over a year, but I think it’s time I move on to something else. I don’t feel much purpose and Grant is so hard to deal with.

Grant

I’ve been in this business for a long time even before Chris joined. It’s not that I don’t appreciate his talents or abilities, but he’s got to learn his place. Kids these days want to get ahead without first paying their dues. Being successful requires more than just a fancy degree and upbringing. It takes determination. It’s a life lesson that Chris needs to learn.

Before long, Chris submitted his resignation citing management as being the main reason for his dissatisfaction with his job. With young, skilled labour being more and more difficult to acquire, something must be done to ensure that young talent remains and turnover rates are kept in check.
