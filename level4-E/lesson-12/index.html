WORDS & PHRASES
prerogative – a right or privilege
sluggish – slow and unresponsive
sane – to be clear-headed, rational, and mentally balanced
hinder – to block or obstruct
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Claire punches out at exactly 6PM everyday, regardless of how busy things are. She feels it is within her prerogative, while some of her more senior colleagues find it slightly offensive and selfish of her. Claire has been feeling more and more pressure from her colleagues and a divide is starting to form.

Ben

The way I see it, Claire should feel a stronger sense of responsibility and pride towards her work. After all, this job is what puts food on the table for my family and has given me the life I have. Our business has grown because of hard work and sacrifice. Now that business is sluggish, we need to pull together and work even harder as a team.

Claire

Why are all the seniors giving me those icy stares every time I leave the office? I’ve got a life outside of this office and I intend not to let my work interfere with that. It’s the only way I keep sane. I’ve seen people devote their entire lives to their work only to wind up miserable. I’m not saying I don’t care about my work, I just know the value of balance.

Ben confronted Claire about her behaviour and the two got into a heated debate. Claire felt that Ben had overstepped his bounds and had no right to confront her about the way she managed her time. Ben on the other hand felt that her behaviour is damaging morale and hindering the business.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would Ben’s generation define the concept of work-life balance?
  • How would Claire’s generation define the concept of work-life balance?
  • Was it right of Ben to confront Claire about her time management?
  • What should be done to ensure that both Ben and Claire see eye to eye?
