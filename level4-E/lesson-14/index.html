WORDS & PHRASES
bulk of (something) – the majority of something
wildly unnecessary – completely or totally unneeded
solidarity – a feeling of togetherness, closeness, or friendship
solely – only, not involving anything or anyone else
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

As a way to further reduce costs and optimize operations, Mabel, the young head of customer support, proposed shifting the bulk of the customer support team from office-based to an all-remote work scheme. Some welcomed the idea, while the older, more senior CS staff opposed the proposal.

Mabel

Overhead costs are increasing by the year and it seems wildly unnecessary for us to be spending money on large, expensive facilities to house our CS team. Modern technology allows our staff to do the work from home and I think it’s a win-win situation for everyone. We can keep management on-site, but everyone else can operate from home.

Carmen

I come to work everyday not just because of the job but because of the relationships I have with my colleagues. Sure, e-mails, instant messages, and video conferences are efficient ways of communicating, but nothing beats good old face-to-face conversation. I don’t want to lose that solidarity with everyone. I just don’t think I’ll be happy.

Many of the senior employees and older CS managers had difficulty imagining the process and were uncomfortable communicating with their staff in a solely virtual work environment. Mabel really believed in the idea, but the lack of support meant that she would have to rethink her proposal.
