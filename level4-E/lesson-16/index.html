THE MIDDLEMAN
16
WORDS & PHRASES
young gun – a young person that is assertive and confident
mediate – to moderate or assist in reaching an agreement
unfeasible – unrealistic or not possible
new blood – new members to a group or team
SEE

Read and analyze the story.

Recently, things have been difficult for Harry, a project manager at an advertising firm. He usually enjoys his work, but now he often finds himself caught in the middle of the senior members of his team and the up-and-coming young guns who are eager to shake things up and make an impression.

Harry

My team is very diverse. I have a good mixture of ages and nationalities, but a lot of the younger guys just don’t know how to communicate with our senior members in a way that they understand. The same goes for the older staff too. In the end, it falls on me to mediate for everyone and it’s starting to really slow things down and eat up my time.

Paris

I’ve got a lot of good ideas that I want to move on, but every time we sit down at meetings, it’s as if some of the older guys simply don’t understand the words coming out of my mouth. They either brush me off, tell me it’s too risky, unfeasible, or just not the right timing. Why are they so resistant? It’s like, dude… come on! Really?

Harry’s experience and age put him right in the middle of a growing divide. He’s old enough to relate to his senior staff and young enough to understand where some of the new blood is coming from. Unfortunately, being the only person in the position has given him a lot of difficulty unifying his team.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What are some additional issues Gen X managers face at your workplace?
  • Would you agree that older staff want stability and younger staff want change and innovation?
  • What generation do you most relate with outside your own?
  • What can Harry do to improve his situation?
