SEE

Read the article.

The late Anita Roddick founded The Body Shop in her humble kitchen. Having no budget for fancy packaging, she used recycled bottles and labeled her products manually. There was no question about Anita’s love for The Body Shop, which she built with her bare hands — literally and figuratively. Apart from being an entrepreneur, Anita was also a staunch supporter of the environment and animals, and this is what The Body Shop has been known for.
L’Oréal had been open about its interest in Anita’s eco-friendly store. So it came as a shock to everyone when news about L’Oréal acquiring The Body Shop came about. Being an advocate of animal rights, Anita was the last person you’d expect to sell her company to L’Oreal – a company that had been known for their use of animals for product development. It just didn’t make any sense, but there was something about The Body Shop that L’Oreal wanted to embrace.
Anita’s company was driven by ethics rather than profit. It never occurred to her that these principles would resonate with L’Oreal and influence the way they would do business. She sold her company for 625 million pounds and gave her millions to charity, leaving her daughters with no inheritance. In hindsight, we must give credit to Anita for taking herself out of the equation just to see her business make a bigger impact.
