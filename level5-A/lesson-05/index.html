Read the article.

Having lived in Bangladesh, Dr. Muhammad Yunus witnessed the plight of families living below the poverty line. Armed with the understanding of economic principles, he saw how capitalist banks had preyed upon these families which made the situation worse. He could’ve just ignored it and focused on getting rich himself, but Muhammad Yunus, unlike other people, opted to take the road less traveled — to help alleviate the lives of poverty-stricken families in his home country.
Out of desperation to augment their family income, Bangladeshi women find themselves buried in debt because of ridiculously high interest rates. Muhammad went around the neighborhood and was shocked to find out that the total debt they had to settle was merely 27 dollars. So, from his own pocket, he settled their accounts. However, he knew that paying their debt was just the tip of the iceberg. He had to get into the root of the problem—poverty.
He started out by convincing the banks to approve women’s loans using his name as guarantor. Surprisingly, Bangladeshi women turned out to be good borrowers. This inspired him to establish the Grameen Bank, an institution which grants loans to women who wish to engage in business. In a culture where the empowering of women is unheard of, Muhammad was labeled a heretic. But he doesn’t mind. He has better things to do other than to make his public image look good.
