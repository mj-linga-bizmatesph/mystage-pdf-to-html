Renaissance, a period in history that gave rise to the rebirth of art, science, religion, humanism and self-awareness. This period marked the dawn of man’s quest for knowledge and gave birth to polymaths, men whose expertise spanned a wide range of fields. But out of all the famous masters of this era, no other figure had made such an impact as Leonardo da Vinci – a man whose genius and influence still echoes until today.
Leonardo da Vinci was one of the greatest polymaths to have ever lived. He is primarily known as the great artist who painted the Mona Lisa, but it wasn’t the Arts alone that caught his interest. Leonardo was described as a man with “unquenchable curiosity” – the kind of curiosity that laid out the blueprints of modern knowledge. He carefully presented his observations in detail with his drawings, possibly as reference for future generations – all 13,000 pages of them.
His achievements paved the way for modern art, engineering and anatomy. He may not have been a business tycoon but he was definitely a visionary; a quality that successful business leaders share. His success can be measured not by his net worth, but by the amount of contributions that man is currently benefitting from today. His quest for knowledge has encouraged us to open our eyes and further explore the world around us. This is his legacy and this might have been his vision.
