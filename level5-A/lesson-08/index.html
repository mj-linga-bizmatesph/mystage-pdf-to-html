SEE

Read the article.

Music. The language of the soul. No other medium could perhaps communicate emotions and ideas better than a well-written melodious song. We often remember a musical piece through its melody, but we seldom stop to think to really digest the meaning behind it. There are many musicians who can create wonderful melodies, but only a few can back it up with impactful lyrics. Bob Marley was one of these music geniuses.
Reggae – although not the most popular of melodies out there – is something that Bob Marley was good at. He used this music style to communicate his ideology for peace and unity. Not only did he vividly paint the struggles of his fellow Jamaicans living an oppressed and impoverished life, he also captured the spiritual fervor that remained their source of strength. He made the world aware of the plight of his country and boldly penned his emotions towards love, faith, peace and revolution.
Bob Marley was a man whose songwriting could be so controversial that a group of men tried to kill him days before a concert. However, his audacity inspired others to openly express their feelings in hopes of bringing about change around them according to their own ideals through non-violent means. It is through this legacy of Bob Marley that we are able to listen to music that comments on the wrongs and injustices around us and how the world could be improved through the eyes of a musician.
