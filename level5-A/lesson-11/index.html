QUOTE

I strongly believe that those of us, who are privileged to have wealth, should contribute significantly to try and create a better world for the millions who are far less privileged.

~ Azim Premji

business tycoon / philanthropist

SEE

Read the article.

Dubbed as the “Bill Gates of Asia,” Azim Premji has undoubtedly exceeded his late father’s expectations. From a medium-sized peanut oil company, he turned their family business into a billion-dollar technological mammoth. He’s now touted as one of the richest men in the world. According to Forbes, Azim’s personal wealth is, at the very least, worth 12 billion dollars. But even so, Azim doesn’t feel the need nor desire to live a life of extreme luxury and decadence.
Despite being ranked as the 41st wealthiest man in the world, Azim has no problems driving an average car. That’s quite a feat for someone who could buy the most expensive luxury car in cold cash. And it doesn’t stop there. Azim travels in economy class when he could afford to buy a private jet for himself. One more thing—it’s highly unlikely for you to bump into Azim in five-star hotels. Whenever Azim goes on a business trip, he spends the night in decent three-star hotels.
Apart from industry and profit, Bill Gates and Azim Premji have one more thing in common—both delight in giving. In fact, Azim is the first Indian billionaire to heed Bill and Melinda Gates’ call for the richest people in the world to participate in the Giving Pledge initiative. Azim has good reasons for living a life far simpler than what he could actually afford. Instead of basking in luxury, Azim prefers to give billions away to charity.
