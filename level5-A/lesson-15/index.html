SEE

Read the article.

Leonardo Sarao was the man who revolutionized the Philippine mass transportation industry. He modified the surplus war jeeps that Americans left after the Second World War into a new means of transportation. What started out as a garage project soon became a small automotive business in 1953. Eventually, these brightly-colored refashioned vehicles would go on to become a national icon affectionately known as: the jeepney.
After the Second World War more and more entrepreneurs began converting American surplus war jeeps into jeepneys. Unfortunately, many were considered too ineffective and dangerous for public use. But seeing huge potential in this endeavor, Sarao got a small loan and began applying his talents to create a safer, more efficient, and economical prototype. Eventually, Sarao’s hard work paid off and his jeepneys soon became the most popular mode of transportation in Manila.
From the ashes of war, Leonardo Sarao used his creativity and resourcefulness to address issues that his country was facing at the time. His jeepneys helped kickstart the rehabilitation of the capital by mass-transporting people, goods and building materials as well as providing a source of income to families affected by the war. His values gave birth to a concept so iconic, that it would forever be recognized as uniquely Filipino by the rest of the world.
