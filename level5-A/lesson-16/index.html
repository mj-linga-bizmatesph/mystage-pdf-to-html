SEE

Read the article.

Sacrificing one’s life for a fellow human being is utterly remarkable. But what do you make of someone who is ready to put his/her life on the line to save a far less animated living thing? Trees, for instance. Vandana Shiva of India saved trees by hugging them. She was a part of the so-called tree hugging movement when she was only a teenager. Having a farmer for a mother profoundly influenced Vandana’s take on things.
Vandana became a physicist. She obtained her doctorate certificate from the University of Western Ontario, Canada. But she went to school not only for her own sake. Vandana had an underlying agenda— she wanted to learn everything about chemical technology so she could become the voice of underrepresented farmers. With that in mind and equipped with knowledge and experience, she established the Research Foundation for Science, Technology and Ecology in 1982.
In case you haven’t noticed, fast-food restaurants are not as common in India as in other Asian countries. Any idea why? If you’re guessing Vandana has anything to do with it, you’re right. As a physicist, she knew how food additives could harm the body. Vandana may be old now, but she’s far from wobbly in her cause. She’ll remain standing firm on her beliefs like the trees she hugged way back when she was younger for as long as she has life left in her.
