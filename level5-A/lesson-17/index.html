SEE

Read the article.

The idea of low-cost traveling in Asia was unheard of around 20 years ago. Now, the average person can simply fly around Asia almost anytime. In 2001, the airline industry changed forever thanks to a childhood dream. Tony Fernandes, the boy who once dreamt of starting his own airline company realized his dream and now has around a hundred planes in his fleet. He bought AirAsia, a once debt-stricken company, and turned the business around by pioneering low-cost air travel in Asia.
AirAsia would have never taken off with excellent marketing alone. The company mostly owes its success to Tony’s hands-on management approach or MBWA (Management By Walking Around). He walks around and gets his hands dirty – from checking in on his cabin crew to assisting his ground crew load luggage onto the plane. Where most people of the same position shun manual labor, Tony embraced it willingly in order to see what works well and what doesn’t.
Tony believes that he is bound to make big mistakes if he were just to sit in his office and look at financial reports. He needs to know exactly what is going on and he does this so well. Still, his purpose behind it isn’t just to improve efficiency but to break down the wall that divides employers and employees. By working with people and experiencing their jobs firsthand, Tony was able to understand his employees’ wants, needs, and feelings.
