SEE

Read the article.

Man’s creativity is only limited by the current reality he is living in. The technology, the funds, the society, etc. can potentially stunt the growth of our most innovative ideas. But what if your own company would willingly fund your concepts and help you realize them, would you grab the opportunity? Adobe Incorporated did just that. The software company took one of their core values to the next level. Enter the “Kickbox” program.
The Kickbox is just a box of goodies… with a thousand dollar prepaid card inside. Anyone in the company can request for it, no questions asked. The idea behind the program is to encourage employees to turn their business ideas into reality. It doesn’t matter who you are, what your position is, or which department you belong to. If you have a great idea that you think would contribute to Adobe’s success, grab a Kickbox and start cracking.
Other companies might view this as something new and innovative, while others might see it as something controversial and absurd. Whatever the case, Adobe found a way to foster man’s creativity even further. This core value has manifested itself in their products for many years, and this has encouraged graphic artists, programmers, and editors to push their limits. From their staff to their customers, Adobe has the word “creativity” flowing through its veins.
