Read the article.

Courier services have become an integral part of businesses today. The idea of having an express delivery system decades ago was unheard of, but such was the vision of a university student Frederick W. Smith who came up with the idea to set up a reliable overnight courier service as an assignment for his university class. Sadly, his professor thought the idea wasn’t feasible and ended up giving him a grade of C for effort. But, that didn’t stop the young Fred Smith.
After college, Smith enlisted for the Marine Corps and deliberately studied military procurement and delivery procedures. He fused military efficiency with methods taken from the banking and telecommunications industries, and started his own company – FedEx. Smith set up a hub network similar to bank clearing houses and developed an airfreight system that could accommodate time-sensitive shipments such as medicines, electronics and relief goods.
Today, FedEx is the world’s leading express delivery provider, delivering 7.5 million packages every day to over 200 countries. Just imagine the world without FedEx. How could medicines, computer parts, European fragrances, Hawaiian flowers and vital documents reach us? His professor might not have foreseen this need, but today’s businesses depend on courier services such as FedEx – an idea pioneered by an undergraduate student more than 40 years ago.
