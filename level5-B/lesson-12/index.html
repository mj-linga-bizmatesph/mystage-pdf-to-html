In the 90’s, the California Milk Processing Board (CMPB) was concerned with the long-term decline of milk sales. People knew its nutritional value but sales volume kept falling anyway. Jeff Manning and Jeff Goodby found the answer. They realized that milk was up against a variety of sodas and fruit juices that came in all kinds of colors and flavors and all sizes of packaging; whereas milk was white, it came in gallons, and in a plain carton. In short, milk was just plain boring.  
Enter the ‘Got Milk?’ campaign. It had a simple but powerful message to combat milk’s weakness – yes milk wasn’t exciting, but what would you do without it? Breakfast wouldn’t be the same without milk; without milk, what would you drink to wash down cookies, cakes and peanut butter sandwiches? The first ads that rolled out showed unexpected, funny situations of people running out of milk and the desperation it caused. The ads were so creative that ‘Got Milk?’ earned its place in popular culture.
Soon, the entire United States’ milk industry wanted to jump on the ‘Got Milk?’ bandwagon. In 1997, the iconic milk mustache campaign had celebrities across the nation volunteering to be part of the movement sporting milk mustaches on billboards, magazines and other print ads. From celebrities and athletes to even fictional icons like Batman and Superman, they all became part of the campaign. Manning and Goodby figured out milk’s weakness and used it to come up with a very creative ad.
