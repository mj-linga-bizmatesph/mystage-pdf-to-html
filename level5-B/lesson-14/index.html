SEE

Read the article.

Henry Ford, the man behind one of America’s automotive giants, made the automobile available to the middle class. When he introduced his first mass-produced car, the Model T, he invested in excessive publicity. He wanted to make sure that every newspaper advertised his inexpensive 4-wheeled innovation. The venture paid off and, in a short span of time, the production and sales of the Model T went through the roof. But Ford also had his share of bad decisions.
 Ford wanted to expand his business on a global scale. He established “Fordlandia”, a huge factory in the Amazon that would manufacture rubber tires for his cars. Sadly, things didn’t go as planned. He crossed the line when the local workers were forced to live the “American way” and were forbidden to consume tobacco and alcohol even in their own homes. The workers soon revolted and refused to work, and Ford ended up shutting down the rubber plantation without profit.
It’s safe to assume that Henry Ford learned from this costly mistake, otherwise his company would have been forever relegated to the bottom of automotive history. Had he not forced his own culture on the locals in Brazil, Fordlandia might have been a huge success as well. It just goes to show that when companies decide to expand globally, business leaders should take cultural diversity into account. This is one lesson that multinational companies could learn from.
