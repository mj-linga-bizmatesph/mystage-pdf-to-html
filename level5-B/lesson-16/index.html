Since the 1980s, the world has witnessed the evolution of the Windows operating system. From the old DOS-based environment to the tiled apps interface, Windows has gone through a lot of changes. Oddly enough, Microsoft seemed to have skipped a version – Windows 8 was followed by Windows 10. Accident? Poor counting skills? Hardly. Microsoft claims they jumped to Windows 10 because it’s 2 versions better than its predecessor and may very well be the final version Microsoft will ever release.
Many tech-savvy enthusiasts believe that Windows 10 was launched prematurely. They are correct as the first Windows 10 version was ugly, unstable and bug-infested. Apparently there was a method to this madness. Microsoft intended to launch Windows 10 while still in development. To rack up revenues from sales? Not exactly. In fact, one could upgrade to Windows 10 for free back then. The company did this because it finally learned how to listen to its customers.
Microsoft wanted Windows 10 to be the perfect operating system, both for PC and mobile devices. The company realized that the best person who could truly say this was its user – not the programmer. Unlike before, Windows’ users are now part of the OS development process. The marketing strategy worked as planned. Windows 10 released updates regularly based on feedback from its customers and certainly improved since its first iteration.
