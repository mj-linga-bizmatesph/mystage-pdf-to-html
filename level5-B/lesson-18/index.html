SEE

Read the article.

He never thought of becoming the youngest Filipino billionaire. Edgar “Injap” Sia is the young entrepreneur who commercialized a traditional Filipino dish – the Chicken Inasal. He capitalized on the two things that the locals are fond of: chicken barbecue and rice – lots and lots of rice. Sia immediately saw the business potential behind it and started his business with a 250 square meter restaurant.
Sia’s business gained nationwide success and is now known as Mang Inasal (Mr. Barbecue). What made his business model so successful is that his fast food restaurant chain serves traditional Filipino comfort dishes and unlimited rice – an idea taken from unlimited calls and text messages in the telecommunications industry in the Philippines. Pretty soon, Mang Inasal caught the attention of Jollibee Foods Corp., the country’s leading fast food chain. Sia sold his company for 3 billion PHP.
However, Sia’s success story didn’t stop there. He noticed that most third class cities in the country have big schools, big hospitals, big ports, etc. but never a shopping mall – something that Filipinos are also very fond of. This is his next business venture and with years ahead of him, Sia could write more chapters in his success story. It seems being on the lookout for opportunities to grow and try something new could pay off, even if you decide to drop out of college.
