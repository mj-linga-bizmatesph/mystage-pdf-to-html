SEE

Read the article.

According to the old adage, “knowing the enemy is half the battle won”. The best embodiment of this saying can be read from the book, The Art of War by a Chinese general named Sun Tzu who lived sometime around 500 BC. He laid out 13 chapters showing the science behind winning and losing. According to his book, factors such as planning, leadership, management, execution and effective use of resources all come into play.
In summary, the Art of War is all about coming up with a good strategy. From laying plans and timing the execution to studying the landscape and being flexible with one’s strategies, Sun Tzu wrote a treasure trove of wisdom for winning. At its core, the Art of War is all about knowing who you are and your competitors. By fully understanding the strengths and weaknesses of all the players in the industry, business leaders can devise a great strategy and execute it perfectly.
Although Sun Tzu’s teachings were written down thousands of years ago, the basic principles are still applicable today. Armed forces from different civilizations have learned from it, so it’s not surprising that these ideologies can also be applied in business. Although it may be incomparable to the horrors of actual warfare, the philosophies behind the book when applied to business can be a decisive factor in drawing the line between success and failure.
