Audrey Hepburn, who was famous for her beauty and grace was greatly known for her iconic roles. Her style was regarded as a classic over the years, and although many sought to mimic her elegance, she remained in a league of her own. She had roles in both Broadway and film, but perhaps she is most fondly remembered as Holly Golightly in Breakfast at Tiffany's and as runaway Princess Anne in Roman Holiday. Her fans adored her as she continued to shine in the '50s and '60s.
Audrey's father was a Nazi sympathizer and when her parents separated, she fled with her mother to Holland where she continued to study dancing. The following year, the Germans invaded and she witnessed the abuse and executions of the Jews. Audrey became a messenger for rebel groups and danced ballet in secret shows to raise money for the resistance against the Germans. Since food was scarce, she had to eat tulips, which resulted to her having severe health problems.
Holland was later liberated, but her malnutrition made her unable to do ballet, so she shifted to acting. After many auditions and minor roles, she was able to perform as a lead actress and her acting skills coupled with her beauty made the world fall in love with her. Audrey was one of the few actors who won a Tony, an Emmy and an Oscar. She later became a UN Ambassador to help war victims around the world. Audrey Hepburn was an epitome of being beautiful inside and out.
