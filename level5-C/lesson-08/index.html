SEE

Read the article.

It was after the Second World War and the auto industry suffered greatly along with many others in Japan, but Soichiro Honda took this as an opportunity to establish a technical research facility in Hamamatsu, Japan in 1946. Two years later, Honda Motor Company, Ltd was born and later branched out to Los Angeles California and eventually became one of the top ten largest automobile manufacturers around the globe.
Honda created an indispensable and efficient mode of transportation: the motorized bicycle; and this innovation made the company the world's largest motorbike maker during the 1970's. Pushing his limits, he further developed his 2-stroke engine to venture into car manufacturing. His first models didn't do so well in the American market, but it all changed when he manufactured fuel-efficient engines with lower emissions. This led to the creation of the first Honda Civic.
In spite of his success, Honda did not stop pursuing his passion. He continued to advance in the industry by competing with luxury car makers in the US and Europe thus, he also manufactured a luxury line, introduced under the name Acura. Honda became the very first Japanese car maker to get it this far. Presently, Honda continues to embody the heart and passion of Soichiro—tirelessly innovating and improving their cars to shape the auto industry in Japan and the world.
