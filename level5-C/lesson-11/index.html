SEE

Read the article.

Not all heroes wear capes. In fact, some of them bring around a “kariton” or a makeshift pushcart to teach kids on the streets basic reading and writing. Efren Peñaflorida started the Dynamic Teen Company to lure kids away from vices and pique their interest in social work. A brilliant student himself, he was able to study through scholarship grants, but continued reaching out to the poor with books, pens, tables, and chairs, and teaching in unusual locations like cemeteries and trash dumps.
Growing up in the slums, Efren was surrounded by bullies and gangs. They were either beating him up or recruiting him to join them, but instead of feeling down, he sought alternative activities to help those who were prone to juvenile delinquency. When Efren turned 16, he started his advocacy of bringing around his “pushcart classroom.” As he worked, he and his friends encountered a lot of difficulties but his mentor continued to encourage them, thus pushing them forward.
Later on, Efren's work was filmed and he gained publicity for what he did. In 2009, he won CNN Hero of the Year and continued receiving other awards such as the Order of the Lakandula by then Philippine president, Gloria Arroyo, Bayaning Pilipino (Philippine Hero) by Gawad Geny Lopez and being featured in many youth organizations. Efren was grateful for the honor, but he believed that what he did was not extraordinary, but simply a crucial step to making his dream of change a reality.
