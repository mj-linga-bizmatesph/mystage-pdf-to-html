SEE

Read the article.

Hayao Miyazaki, world acclaimed animator and master story teller, has made a name for himself with his trademark style in art and in his depiction of life lessons in his films. As a man of unique style, he uses unconventional ways to produce his work—he creates the characters and story line as he goes along with the creation process. One recurring theme in his films is the conflict between progress of the world and the order of things in nature.
Born to a family with an aeronautics business, Hayao learned to love flying. They moved to Utsunomiya City, where he became more attached to nature and forests. His fascination with children's stories was seen when he joined the children's literature society while studying, and when he pursued animation despite studying economics. He joined Toei Animation and quickly climbed the ranks as he displayed his creative side through innovative ideas and leading projects in the studio.
Later, Hayao, together with Isao Takahata, launched Studio Ghibli, and gave the world classics such as Princess Mononoke, Spirited Away, and My Neighbor Totoro to name a few. Studio Ghibli used the traditional method of animation: most of the frames were hand drawn before being put together into fluid movement. While the rest of the world was quick to adapt to the use of CGI, Hayao only allowed minor CGI additions to his work. His story and his process made Miyazaki's work praise-worthy.
