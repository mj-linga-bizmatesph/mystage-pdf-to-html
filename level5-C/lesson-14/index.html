SEE

Read the article.

Mainly known as a real estate developer and once the richest man in Asia, Li Ka-Shing is a powerful figure in the business world. His companies control 70% of Hong Kong's port traffic and most of the telecommunications and utilities. But Li did not become a magnate through comfort and ease. Rather, he tirelessly worked from the ground up at the age of 12 after quitting school, since his father died of tuberculosis when their family fled from Guangdong to Hong Kong to escape war.
Li found a job as an apprentice in a watch-strap factory, where he worked 16 hours a day, 7 days a week and he would send 90% of his pay to his mother. Soon, he became a salesman, and then the general manager of the factory when he was just 19. Due to his appetite for learning, he taught himself business and skills through reading books. He even completed his company's accounting books by himself in the first year even though he didn't have accounting experience.
At the age of 22, he opened his first factory, Cheung Kong Industries, and manufactured plastic, which he foresaw as a booming industry and he was right. In 1958, he ventured into real estate and steadily built his empire. By 1979, Li bought Hutchison Whampoa and sealed his status of being a business tycoon. He attributed his wealth to good knowledge and a no-debt policy. Indeed, the qualities he developed in childhood earned him the title: “Superman” of the Asian business world.
