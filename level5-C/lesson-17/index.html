Everyone knows Michael Jordan, but not many know about him not being accepted in his high school basketball team. After high school, he went to the University of North Carolina under a basketball scholarship and was named Rookie of the Year for 1982. He was also a member of the US Olympic basketball team which won gold in California. Jordan left after his junior year and was selected as the third pick by the Chicago Bulls of the National Basketball Association (NBA) in the 1984 draft.
The Chicago Bulls were a losing team when Jordan got drafted, but he was able to turn it around with his style of play and competitiveness. The whole world became a fan of his hang time and incredible ability to leap. In his first season, he was selected for the All-Star team and NBA Rookie of the Year. During training, Michael forced himself to shoot under pressure and challenged himself to beat the difficult players. He never accepted defeat. For everyone, “Michael never loses.”
Chicago Bulls became a force to be reckoned with in the '80s and Jordan was its star, but he retired shortly and tried his luck at baseball. He later came back and helped the Bulls win the championship again, allowing them to dominate the basketball world. Now retired, Michael is the most decorated NBA player with six NBA championships, five regular-season MVP Awards, and three All-Star MVPs. He proved that the ball had always been in his court; he just had to learn to throw it the right way.
