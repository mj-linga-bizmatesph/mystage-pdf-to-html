Jim Kwik was “the boy with the broken brain.” After suffering from a head trauma at the age of 5, he struggled with learning and reading. Because of his condition, he was bullied and he became extremely shy and withdrawn. It took Jim an extra 3 years to learn reading and he taught himself how to do so through comic books. He loved the X-Men because he felt that they were like him—an outcast—and because of this, he went out every weekend to search for Professor X's school.
College wasn’t any different for Jim. He tried his best to catch up but still couldn't. One time, he went on a trip with a friend and his family. When asked how school was, Jim broke down and narrated about his condition and how hard it was for him to keep up. His friend's dad asked him, “Why are you in school? What do you want to share to the world?” and told him that he was close to achieving them all. Those words guided him to overcome his depression and help others out.
Jim became more confident and started having a lot of friends. He figured that school taught what to learn but never how to learn. He discovered that brains can be rebuilt and that his was not broken—it just needed a better owner's manual. Jim went beyond his own limiting beliefs and made it his passion to help others do the same. He was able to become a brain performance coach to notable figures such as Elon Musk and Will Smith, and even got to meet the X-Men cast in one of his seminars.
