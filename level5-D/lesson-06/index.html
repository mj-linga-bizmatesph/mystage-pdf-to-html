Apple is undoubtedly one of the world’s most powerful companies. It is known for its great products, innovative culture, and the man behind the vision, Steve Jobs. Yet for all his success, Steve Jobs’ life was dotted with tragedies and hardships. As a child born into a poor family, Steve was given up for adoption as a baby. However, every cloud has a silver lining as his foster father introduced his adopted son to the world of electronics - a passion that would eventually help Steve change the world.
In 1976, Steve founded Apple Computers with his business partner Steve Wozniak. Although things went well initially, Steve would eventually be removed from the company he helped found due to poor business results and clashing personalities. Refusing to go down, Steve went out to start his next venture, NeXT Computer. Unfortunately, NeXT also struggled similarly with sales and ended up being acquired by the very company he first established – Apple.
As tumultuous as all of this may have been, Steve took this as a chance to continue to revolutionize the industry and the company. In 2003, Steve was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer and after battling his illness for almost a decade, passed away in October 2011 at 56 years old though not before he lived to see the fruits of his labours. Steve Jobs may not have been the easiest person to work with, but it was precisely his determination that helped Apple become the giant it is today.
