SEE

Read the article.

Imagine this: You’re a senior executive at one of the largest advertising firms in the world. You have a great salary, a loving family, and a life of luxury. Suddenly, it’s all gone. Your job, your money, your marriage, and on top of that, you’re diagnosed with a brain tumor. With nowhere to go and in your fifties, what would you do? For many, it would be so easy to just give up on life, but for Michael Gill, he would find a whole new start and fulfillment at a place he least expected. Starbucks.
As a self-described “son of privilege”, Michael had always lived at the top of white American society. Now, he found himself working at Starbucks serving those that used to serve him while working under an ethnic minority. Karma it seemed, was certainly having its way. Yet, for all the difficulties, Michael began to see life in a different light. By letting go of his pride, embracing a different philosophy on life, and opening himself to new possibilities Michael found himself with “the best job he’s ever had”
In order to spread what he has learned, Michael came up with the three L’s: Leap (with faith): In situations where you are truly trapped, you’ve got to be willing to take chances. Look (with respect): We tend to only see what we want and ignore everything else. However, by opening our eyes to new people and things, we may discover new possibilities. Listen (to your heart): Growing up in a world where others determine your success will lead to a life of insecurity. Live life on your own terms.
