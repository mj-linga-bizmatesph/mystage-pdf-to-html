SEE

Read the article.

Amazon founder Jeff Bezos is now the richest person in the world with an estimated 75 billion-dollar fortune. Although he is an active philanthropist, Jeff has recently made a bold move that upends the way the super-rich tend to approach giving. While his peers tend to focus on creating positive change through long-term solutions and advancing causes they are most knowledgeable about, Jeff is thinking about taking the completely opposite approach through short-term focused “philanthropopulism”.
As otherworldly as some of the super-rich are, the fact remains that they are still a part of our society. As a result, some believe that the public should have a say as to where donations should go. Thus, the concept of philanthropopulism was born. By combining democracy with philanthropy, Jeff hopes to be more impactful by leaving a certain amount of decision making to the people. With this in mind, he defied expectations and took to Twitter to openly make a “request for ideas”.
Shortly after his open call for ideas on June 16, 2017, Jeff Bezos received 40,000 replies and ideas on how to put his fortune to good use to make the most impact in the shortest amount of time. By focusing on short-term targets, listening to the public, and “helping the people in the here and now”, Jeff feels that his efforts will yield significant impact in the long run as well. Although this strategy has baffled experts, any ideas coming from the richest man on the planet might be worth considering.
