GROWTH & DEBT
4
WORDS &
PHRASES
adage – a proverb or saying
play catch-up – to continually try to recover lost progress due to falling behind
sensibilities – one’s feelings, emotions, or morals
greener pastures – a newer, better, more successful alternative
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

For many businesses, debt is the last thing they’d want to see in a quarterly statement. Being in the red has never been a positive thing. However, these days more and more companies are acting on the adage that, “In order to make money, you have to spend money,” even if it’s money you don’t have.
Nikki:
If we have it, let’s spend it. If we don’t have it, let’s borrow it. We need to jump on this opportunity and take advantage of these negative interest rates. All the companies that we’ve looked at have shown growth through aggressive borrowing. It’s time we stopped playing catch-up. Money sitting in the bank at this point is just being wasted.
Emma:
Call me a traditionalist but how can debt mean anything but bad news? No one wants the insecurity of not having enough money. We have to be responsible to our shareholders and to our employees by creating stable, sustainable growth through measured, proven, and dependable means. Piling on debt seems counter to my sensibilities.
Regardless of what the approach is, growth is essential to the longevity of any business. Though many may debate the stability and risk of strategies incorporating debt, the potential for growth and the lure of greener pastures is, for some, worth the risk.
