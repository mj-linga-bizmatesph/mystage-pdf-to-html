WORDS & PHRASES
tried-and-true – proven to be effective, dependable, and trustworthy
game-changing – new or unique, as an element or a factor that changes an existing situation
resonate with – to affect or appeal to someone in a deep or meaningful manner
notoriously – famously, but for a negative quality
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

Following only the clients’ wants and needs may actually be detrimental to the success of a business. Although some professionals believe in tried-and-true methods of product development through market evaluation and data analysis, others prefer a less traditional approach.
Stephen:
Look, it’s simple. If we keep basing what we create on consumer demands and market research, we won’t be able to come up with anything game-changing. People don’t know what they want. It’s up to us as specialists to tell them. If we play it safe, we’ll never be number one.
William:
We’re a business and don’t need to take any unnecessary risks. The numbers don’t lie. Let’s stick with what we know. There’s no need to rock the boat. Pouring a bunch of money, effort, and time into a new concept that may not resonate with our target just doesn’t seem like a risk I’m willing to take.
Striking a balance between innovation and meeting market demand is easier said than done. The public can be notoriously resistant to change, yet fickle and always on the lookout for the next big thing. Whatever path companies choose, most can agree that risk and reward often go hand in hand.
