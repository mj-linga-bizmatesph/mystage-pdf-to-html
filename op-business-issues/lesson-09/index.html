YES-MEN
9
WORDS & PHRASES
conformity – compliance with established rules, standards, and expectations
discord – lack of peace and harmony
go against the grain – to be opposite of popular belief or expectations
disrupt– to disturb, interrupt, or drastically alter or destroy something’s natural state
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

In certain business cultures, conformity and unity are valued above all else. Although a certain level of efficiency, harmony, and progress may be achieved through such means, the resulting lack of varying opinions and viewpoints may end up doing a company more harm than good.
Meg:
The last thing we need is discord among the staff. If everyone had an opinion about something, it would take forever for anything to get done. We need to streamline our processes and focus on efficiency. It’s our job to make the decisions and everyone else’s job to follow orders. It’s the yes-men that’ll get things done.
Henri:
We shouldn’t be looking at our staff like army ants. People want to be heard. If we want to improve employee morale, loyalty, and dedication to the company, we need to let them know that their opinions are valued. Even if their ideas go against the grain, this is a good chance for us to get some fresh perspectives.
How to best utilize one’s workforce is a problem that many companies face. Though employees are expected to follow orders and finish tasks without disrupting processes, many would agree that success depends on more than the ability to do as one is told.
