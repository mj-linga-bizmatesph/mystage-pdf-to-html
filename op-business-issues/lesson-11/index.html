WORKPLACE RIVALRIES
11
WORDS &
PHRASES
rift – a crack, split, or deep divide
spill blood – to severely wound or kill someone
lone wolf – a person who prefers to act and be alone
foe – an enemy or opponent; a rival
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

In high-pressure work environments where performance and results are valued, it is not uncommon for rivalries to form. Although friendly competition can be very motivating, too much can cause deep, irreparable rifts that only result in hard feelings and a fractured team.
Christine:
I’m a competitive person. I’m not afraid to say that. I want to be the best and I’ll do whatever it takes. Joe is good at what he does, but I know I’m better. If getting to the top means having to spill blood, I’ll do it. I know people think that it’s better to have friends than enemies, but there’s nothing more motivating than a great rival.
Rob:
A little friendly competition never hurt anyone, but there’s one thing we can’t forget; we’re a team. We pull together, help each other, and motivate one another. I have little respect for those that get to the top at the expense of others. If you want a rival, look outwards, not inwards. The last thing we need on the team is a lone wolf.
Many would agree that ambition is necessary for success. For those at the top, being second best was never an option. Yet, no one gets very far in life without support from others. As a result, knowing when to push, when to pull, and the difference between friend and foe is perhaps more important.
