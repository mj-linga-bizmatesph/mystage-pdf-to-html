POWER HARASSMENT
12
WORDS & PHRASES
addictive – a substance or activity that causes physical and/or mental dependency
gopher – a small rodent, synonymous with a low-ranking employee who is made to run errands
berate – to scold or to criticize angrily
crack the whip – to use one’s authority to make someone work harder
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

Power can be an addictive and dangerous thing. The feeling of control and authority can lead managers and executives down a dark path that results in low employee morale, reduced productivity, and even legal consequences.
Clark:
My “boss” often orders me to do the most mundane things that just end up wasting my time. “Make me a reservation,” “Take this to the post office,” “Get me a coffee.” When I ask why, she responds with, “I’m the boss, so just do it.” It’s incredibly frustrating. Yeah, I get it. She’s the boss, but I’m not a gopher.
Mia:
My supervisor keeps berating the staff in front of others, and it makes me incredibly uncomfortable. I want to say something, but I’m not sure who I should talk to. No one wants to be treated like a child, and that’s no way to motivate your staff. He’s not a bad guy, but I don’t think he realizes how demoralizing it is. He wasn’t this way before…
Managing a group of people is no easy task. There are times when one may be expected to crack the whip when things are not going to plan. Although the abuse of power may be an easy trap to fall into, some would say that one’s true nature, good or bad, is revealed when given a position of power.
