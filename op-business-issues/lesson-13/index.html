PERFORMANCE VERSUS SENIORITY
13
WORDS &
PHRASES
remuneration – money paid for work or service
comprehensive – including all aspects of something
indebted – owing gratitude, respect, and appreciation
appease – to bring to a state of peace; to satisfy
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

More and more companies are switching from seniority-based incentives to more aggressive, performance-based evaluations. Though both have their merits and demerits, many feel that hard work demands equal remuneration and recognition, regardless of age, seniority, or tradition.
Ken:
I’ve been in this company for more years than I can count, and I expect to be fairly compensated. Although I understand the need for a more comprehensive evaluation system and performance targets, I feel that my many years of hard work should be honored and recognized – not disrespected by working under someone 10 years my junior.
Maya:
I’m indebted to those who have come before me. It’s because of them that we are such a large and respected organization. However, I feel we still need to hold them to certain performance standards. Position as a result of seniority has never been efficient. Respectfully, we need to prioritize progress and efficiency over seniority.
Many have dedicated the majority of their lives to their companies and expect loyalty in return for their many years of long service. Though demands for fair recognition are on the rise, appeasing both parties is essential nonetheless in maintaining harmony throughout the company.
