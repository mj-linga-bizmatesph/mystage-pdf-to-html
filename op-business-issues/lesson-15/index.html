AFTER-HOURS SOCIALIZING
15
WORDS &
PHRASES
obliged – expected to or morally bound to do something
camaraderie – mutual trust and friendship
to let (someone’s) hair down – to relax and behave without inhibition
hassle – an irritating inconvenience
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

There are times when one’s professional duties extend outside the office and past regular working hours. In order to develop closer working relationships, staff may be obliged to have the occasional drink or dinner after work. Although some enjoy such outings, others see it as just more work.
Hyun:
I burn so much of my energy at the office already. I can’t be bothered to spend my evenings with the people I already see for 10 hours a day, 5 days a week. I get the importance of building working relationships, but I can just do that at the office. I don’t need beer and food to build teamwork and camaraderie.
Rie:
We had such a great time last night! We’ve got such a great group, and it’s good to let our hair down once in a while. Did you know that Emma is an amazing singer? You’d never know it by looking at her, but she’s got a great voice. It’s always so good to see what people are like outside the office. It really gives me a fresh perspective on the team.
Though some may view socializing with colleagues as a hassle, teams that value their co-workers as more than just 9 to 5 relationships may perform better than those that don’t. After all, many would agree that business success is more than professional ability; it’s about successful relationships.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  • Is socializing a good way to boost teamwork and effectiveness? Why or why not?
  • How would you describe your team dynamics? Why is it this way?
  • What did you learn about your co-workers from going out with them that was impressive or surprising, or that changed your opinion of them?
  • Can you think of any activities that will help promote teamwork and communication?
