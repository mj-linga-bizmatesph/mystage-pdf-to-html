WORDS & PHRASES
chief – most important, crucial, or essential
have what it takes – to possess the necessary skills or qualities for success
to cut it – to be sufficient
fancy – impressive or elaborate
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

In an ever-expanding globalized world, it is essential for companies to compete effectively overseas. Though there are many hurdles to overcome, chief among them are communication and cultural barriers. Without facing these issues, any overseas business venture may be a dream too far to reach.
Mia:
I have to transfer overseas for an assignment, but to be honest, I don’t think I have what it takes. I just don’t have any interest in anything outside my own country. I’ve lived here my whole life. I don’t know the language, the culture, or its people. I’ll receive some language training before I go, but I just don’t know if I can handle it…or if I want to…
Haylee:
If we want our staff to be more effective, we need to create a more comprehensive training program. Setting arbitrary test score goals and 3-month language training programs isn’t going to cut it anymore. We’ve been doing the same thing for years on end, but I just don’t see any big improvements. We need to take a different approach.
It can be said that being successful in global business means more than just knowing the language. It’s about cultural adaptability, sensitivity, and a receptive mind. Though language fluency may be essential, it takes more than a few fancy words to be effective in the international business scene.
