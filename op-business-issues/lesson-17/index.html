Stressed employees, long working hours, and a myriad of other distractions has led to noticeable decreases in workplace productivity. Although there are many ways to boost and measure productivity, behavioral psychology and economics dictate that what works for one, may not work for all.
Adi:
In my day, we could sit at our desks for 12 hours straight and plough through our tasks without a single complaint. It was work fast, work hard, sink or swim. If you weren’t meeting the quota, you were cut loose. No excuses. Nowadays, people worry if their chairs are comfortable enough or if the wall colors are stimulating enough. People got soft.
Edu:
Give me some loud music, a pair of fuzzy slippers, and a hot cup of cocoa, and I’ll get things done. On the other hand, if you chain me to a desk in a room without windows and start logging my keystrokes, my output is going to tank. They say knowing yourself is half the battle, and I believe it. Now, if only my boss could understand this...
Though there are no easy answers to increasing productivity, focusing less on generic, corporate-mandated KPIs and more on individual needs may be a step in the right direction. Whatever the case, employee performance is often an indicator of a company’s ability to motivate, inspire, and support.
