OUTSOURCING OR INTERNALIZING
18
WORDS & PHRASES
inevitable – unavoidable or certain to happen
growing pains – emotional difficulties and challenges associated with growth
shortcomings – faults or failures to meet certain standards
stunted – hindered, blocked, or restricted in growth or development
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

As businesses grow and processes become more and more complex, the inevitable question of whether to outsource or find a way to internalize certain tasks comes up. Though there are benefits to increasing company know-how, some things are best left to other industry professionals.
Cally:
I’m all for getting things done on our own. Yes, it might take time and there will be some growing pains, but in the end, we’ll be better off. There are dangers to relying too much on others, and I think we should develop a strong internal culture of self-reliance and improvement. It’ll benefit us in the long run.
Bill:
As talented and as smart as we are, there are some things that we just are not equipped to handle. The humility to recognize our shortcomings is going to save us a lot of headaches. Plus, this is a really great opportunity for us to build relationships with other companies and businesses. Sometimes we all need a little help.
When it comes to business, nothing survives in isolation, yet an overdependence on outsourcing may result in stunted internal growth. Though there are solid arguments on both sides, many would agree that the best approach is often a balanced one.
