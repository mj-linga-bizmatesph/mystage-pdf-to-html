to be consumed by (something) – to be obsessed or very concerned with (something)
to come back and bite (someone) – to cause (someone) problems in the future
to take precedence over (something) – to be more important than (something)
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

As companies grow, many are expected to give back to their communities. Although this sense of responsibility and philanthropy is often well-received and admired, some may view it as an unnecessary drain of company resources and time.
Kai:
If we’re in a position to do something good for others, we should do it. Not because it’s good for company image, or good for branding, or increasing influence, but because it’s the right thing to do. In a world consumed by money and profits, it’s nice to know that there are still companies that are able to value some things more than the bottom line.
Nadia:
We need to remove our emotions from the equation and talk about CSR in terms of its merits and demerits. I’m sure everyone “feels” it’s a good idea, but what about our shareholders? We need to stay on point and make sure this doesn’t come back to bite us. My most important responsibilities are to the business, our investors, and you guys.
As members of a community, it is natural to want to contribute to society in meaningful ways, but as a business, more pressing concerns often take precedence over others. Yet, as conflicting as the two may initially appear, many believe that true success cannot be measured by profits alone.
