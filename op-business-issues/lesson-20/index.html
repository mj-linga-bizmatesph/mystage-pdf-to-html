EMPLOYEE RETENTION
20
WORDS &
PHRASES
thorn in (someone’s) side – a nuisance, annoyance, or problem
pay top dollar – to pay much money
down the drain – wasted, useless or worthless
paddle upstream – to make little progress in achieving a goal
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases

With lifetime employment becoming less and less attractive for young talent, employee retention can be a serious thorn in management’s side. Although there are many issues that impact retention, it’s always important to remember that, “brains, like hearts, go where they are appreciated.”
Nina:
I used to think that finding a big company to work in and staying there my whole life was a good thing. Now, I’m not so sure. A lot of companies are willing to pay top dollar for good people, and staying here to play the long game just seems like I’m wasting my time. Considering my age and experience, I should consider looking elsewhere.
Kris:
We need to make sure we protect our assets. By that, I mean our people. We lost three excellent employees over the last two months alone, citing better opportunities. All that training and development, down the drain. Offering standard market rates and progression isn’t enough. Loyalty doesn’t come cheap. We get what we pay for.
Employees look at many things when considering how long to remain with any particular organization. Pay, benefits, and job satisfaction are but just a few. Although many companies concentrate on recruiting, failure to focus on retention will make any expansion efforts a lot like paddling upstream.
