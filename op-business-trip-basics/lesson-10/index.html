PART 1

Review the Biz tips with your trainer and make sure you are comfortable with the expressions and concepts.

When touring the factory, comment on the facilities to show interest. Use these examples to help you:
This is a very nice building. How big is it?
It looks well-maintained. How old is it?
I don’t see too many workers though. Is everything automated?
When meeting someone from a different culture, make sure you introduce yourself properly. Use the following phrases to help you:
Nice to meet you,…
I’m [complete name].
And I’m [position] at [company].
When asking about important information, make sure you confirm your understanding then make a follow-up question. Here are some examples to help you:
How do you ensure the quality of...?
So, you mean...?
And what steps do you take when...?
When asking questions in English, use tag questions to sound more natural. Use these examples to help you:
The factory has strict safety regulations, doesn't it?
You haven't had any accidents in the plant, have you?
The plant isn't breaking any environmental laws, is it?

PART 2

Imagine your business partner is taking you on a short tour of the factory. Apply the expressions you learned in Lessons 6 to 9.

  1. Comment about the building and ask your business partner some questions.
  2. Ask about quality and efficiency in the factory and make follow-up questions.
  3. Ask about safety regulations in the factory.
