PROPOSING A SOLUTION
13
BIZ TIP
When proposing a solution, make sure you identify the problem and explain the possible results of your suggestion. Here are some helpful phrases:
So one issue we're facing is...
I suggest we...
If we do this, we can be sure that…
SEE

Taka is bringing up some concerns.

Taka:
So one issue we’re facing is that the factory doesn’t have a way to measure the actual performance of the batteries under full load.
Van:
That’s right.
Taka:
I can send you the devices to test the batteries with. And, I suggest we test the batteries before and after production.
Van:
I see. You mean test the prototypes before production?
Taka:
That’s right. And then test some samples from the final product.
Jake:
I think that’s a good idea.
Taka:
If we do this, we can be sure that the batteries meet quality standards and avoid any complaints due to performance and compatibility issues.
