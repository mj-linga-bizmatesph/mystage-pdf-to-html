PART 1
Review the Biz tips with your trainer and make sure you are comfortable with the expressions and concepts.

When starting a meeting, clearly explain the purpose, the agenda, and the duration of the meeting. Here are some phrases to help you:
Yes: Sure, that sounds good.
No: I'd love to, but...
Maybe: Can I get back to you? I might…
To start a conversation at the dinner table, show interest in the local dishes by talking about the menu. Here are some examples to help you:
What do you recommend?
What’s in it?
What does it taste like?
When you want to get to know the other person, make small talk and try to find something in common. Use the examples to help you:
So, what do you do on weekends?
Same here.
If you don’t mind my asking…
When sending a follow-up e-mail, add a personal touch and confirm the action plan you agreed to. Here are some phrases to help you:
Thank you for…
Based on our meeting, we agreed to…
Please let me know if you have any questions.
