BIZ TIP
Another important part of the preparation process is to research the day-to-day life of your destination, such as the laws, safety hazards, transportation, general prices, and emergency contacts. Doing your research beforehand will save you a lot of stress and ensure a safe return.

Kwintessential: http://www.kwintessential.co.uk/resources/country-profiles.html
SEE
Sam:
Ryo, are you all prepared for your first trip to the U.S.?
Ryo:
Yeah, I think so. Thanks to you, I’ve learned quite a bit about the business culture.
Sam:
Good.
Ryo:
How much cash do you think I should take?
Sam:
Well, you can use a credit card just about anywhere in the U.S., but I would be careful of using a credit card in small shops. There’s a risk of it being copied. At the same time, carrying a lot of cash could make you a target for muggers.
Ryo:
Well, I don’t plan to go anywhere dangerous, maybe just sightseeing to Midtown.
Sam:
Tourist areas, such as Midtown, are the most common places for theft because there are so many tourists. So, just be careful.
Ryo:
I see. Is there anything I should do to protect myself?
Sam:
It would be a good idea to make photocopies of credit cards, your passport, driver’s license, and any other ID. Also, you should have an emergency contact number, just in case you need help. I don’t mean to scare you, but Japan is just so safe compared to other parts of the world.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions about your destination:

  1. What is the current exchange rate?
  2. How much does an average meal cost?
  3. What are some safety hazards? Dangerous areas? Clean water? Etc.
  4. What’s the tipping culture there?
  5. Do you need immunization shots or vaccinations before you go?
  6. What are some laws that are different from your country?
  7. Who can you contact in case of an emergency?
  8. What are some famous tourists sites in the area?
  9. What’s the weather like?
  10. What’s the history of the country?
  11. How is their relationship with your country?
  12. Is there any political instability?
▼▼▼
ACT

Final Activity:

Imagine that you are scheduled to take a business trip overseas. However, your boss is worried that you are unprepared for the trip. Have a meeting with your boss to explain what you know about the day-to-day life of the country you will be visiting.
