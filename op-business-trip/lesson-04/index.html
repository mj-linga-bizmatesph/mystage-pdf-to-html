OTHER PROGRAMS BUSINESS TRIPILESSON 4
SETTING UP AN APPOINTMENT
4
BIZ TIP
When setting up an appointment, make sure you:
  • Give details of your trip
  • Request an appointment
  • Further build the relationship
SEE

Example:

(Ryo sends an e-mail to set up an appointment.)

To:
Subject:
Bob_Cornell@bettersales.com
U.S. Business Trip approved
Dear Mr. Cornell,

I’m pleased to inform you that my 3-day business trip has been approved.

I’ll be in New York from April 26 to 29. I’ll be attending a tradeshow on the 28th, but I’ll be free all day on the 27th.

Could we meet for a couple of hours on April 27, between 10am-5pm NY time to discuss the possibility of our partnership? I hope you can find the time to squeeze the appointment in your busy schedule.

I look forward to meeting you in person and taking that first step towards a good business relationship with you and your company.

Regards,
Ryo Watanabe
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions about your destination:

  1. How do you usually schedule an appointment with your contact person?
  2. How long are your usual business trips?
  3. Do you often have more than one purpose for going on a business trip?
  4. What would be the main reason?
  5. What could be the other reasons?
  6. Do you know their local time zone?
  7. Have you encountered any confusion because of date and time differences?
  8. What other information would you provide when setting up an appointment?
▼▼▼
ACT

Final activity:

Imagine you’re scheduling an appointment via e-mail with your contact overseas. Don’t forget to include the purpose of your business trip and other relevant information.
