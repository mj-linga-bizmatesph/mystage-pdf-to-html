OTHER PROGRAMS BUSINESS TRIPILESSON 7
STAYING IN THE HOTEL
7
BIZ TIP
Apart from the usual room service, grab the opportunity to ask the hotel concierge about some information that might help you get by during your stay. For example:
  • Asking for a wireless connection or other hotel amenities
  • Asking for advice about the best places in town
  • Asking for information about transportation
SEE

(Ryo is speaking to the concierge.)

Ryo:
Excuse me. How do I access the hotel’s WiFi connection?
Joe:
The password is eastcoast1, all in one word and lower cases.
Ryo:
Thank you. Also I’d like to know a good restaurant nearby and places where I could go sightseeing. Do you have any suggestions?
Joe:
There’s a fine-dining restaurant just 3 blocks away from here called Manhattan Gourmet. The ambiance is just magnificent. As for sightseeing, the Statue of Liberty is a good place to start.
Ryo:
Excellent! One more thing, if you don’t mind?
Joe:
Not at all.
Ryo:
How do I go about getting a taxi cab tomorrow morning, and how much is the taxi fare?
Joe:
You may call the front desk tomorrow as soon as you’re ready to leave as the taxi will arrive within a couple of minutes. The fare starts at $2.50, and $1.24 for every succeeding kilometer.
Ryo:
I see. You’ve been a great help. Here you go. (gives tip)
Joe:
My pleasure. Enjoy your stay.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions about your destination:

  1. What hotel amenities do you ask for when staying at a hotel?
  2. What other information do you usually ask the hotel concierge about?
  3. Are you picky when it comes to restaurants during your business trip?
  4. What kind of food/cuisine do you prefer?
  5. What tourist spots do you think would the locals advise you to see?
  6. What kind of tourist spots are you interested in seeing?
  7. What’s the most common mode of transportation?
  8. Do you know how much the fare is?
▼▼▼
ACT

Final Activity:

Imagine you are calling room service. Ask for some information from the hotel concierge that you think would be helpful during your stay overseas.
