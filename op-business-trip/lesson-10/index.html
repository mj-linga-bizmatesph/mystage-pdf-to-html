OTHER PROGRAMS BUSINESS TRIPILESSON 10
GETTING TO THE CLIENT'S OFFICE
10
BIZ TIP
To avoid being late, make sure you take the following into consideration when going to an appointment:
  • Prepare and organize everything you need to take with you
  • Be aware of the traffic in the area at any given time
  • Ask how long it will take and how much it will cost before getting into the taxi
SEE

Example:

(Ryo hails a taxi cab through the hotel front desk.)

Driver:
Where to, sir?
Ryo:
Yes, could you take me to 53rd Street, please? High Tower building.
Driver:
Sure.
Ryo:
Could you step on it, please. I’ll be running late.
Driver:
Sir, this is New York. Traffic is always heavy around here.
Ryo:
Ah... Can we turn around please, I think I forgot something back in the hotel.

Bizmates style:

Driver:
Where to, sir?
Ryo
Yes, could you take me to 53rd Street, please? High Tower building.
Driver:
Sure.
Ryo
Do you know a faster route to beat the traffic? I’d like to get there as early as possible.

Ryo
Great. How much will it cost?

It should cost about 10 dollars.
Ryo
OK, that’s fine. Thank you.

TRY

Answer these questions about your destination:

  1. What are the things you need to take with you for the meeting?
  2. How much travel time allowance do you give yourself?
  3. When is rush hour?
  4. How do you usually get to your contact’s office?
  5. How easy is it to take a cab in the area?
  6. How long does it take to get there by taxi?
  7. Do you know how long the line is on the bus stop?
  8. How much does it cost?
  9. Do you know other means to get to your destination?
  10. What else would you consider to avoid being late for the appointment?

ACT

Final Activity:

Imagine you’re on your way to an important meeting. Get relevant information by asking the taxi driver before getting in.
