OTHER PROGRAMS BUSINESS TRIPILESSON 12
MEETING THE CLIENTS FOR THE FIRST TIME
12
BIZ TIP
At this point you may be introduced to key personnel of their company. Try to make some kind of connection with them by:
  • Repeating the other person’s name
  • Adding a brief comment about the other person
  • Showing sincere appreciation for their time
SEE

Example:

Bob:
Ryo, let me introduce you to Tom Clark, the Head of Operations.
Ryo:
It’s nice to meet you.
Tom:
It’s a pleasure to meet you too.
Bob:
And this is Liz Perry. Liz oversees Sales and Marketing.
Ryo:
Nice to meet you.
Liz:
It’s nice to meet you too.

Bizmates style:

Bob:
Ryo, let me introduce you to Tom Clark, the Head of Operations.
Ryo:
It’s truly a pleasure to meet you Mr. Clark. I’ve heard a lot of good things about you from Bob. Bob says that you could be a professional golfer.
Tom:
Ha ha. Well, he’s right. Anyway, it’s nice to meet you too, Mr. Watanabe.
Bob:
And this is Liz Perry. Liz oversees Sales and Marketing.
Ryo:
It’s nice to meet you, Ms. Perry. Thank you for making time to join this meeting today.
Liz:
Not at all. It’s nice to meet you too, Mr. Watanabe.
Ryo:
If there’s any information you need regarding sales or marketing, please let me know and I’ll be able to translate it into English for you.
Liz:
That would be very helpful. Thank you.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions about your destination:

  1. Who are you likely to be introduced to during your business trip?
  2. What are their names or positions?
  3. What brief comment about them could you add when introduced?
  4. How else can you show that you mean business or you aren’t wasting their time?
▼▼▼
ACT

Final Activity:

Imagine you’re being introduced to several key people by your contact. Be sure to repeat the other person’s name, add a brief comment, and show appreciation for their time.
