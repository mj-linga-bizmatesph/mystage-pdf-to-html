PRESENTING THE PRODUCT
13
BIZ TIP
To keep your audience interested in your presentation, you can follow these steps:
  • Pause every now and then to involve your audience
  • Present factual information based on graphs and charts
  • Use the Feature-Advantage-Benefit technique
SEE

Example:

Ryo:
Our distributors in Japan have seen their sales significantly increase since they’ve partnered with us. The reason is our products’ reliability is top class. Furthermore, the defective rate of our products is very low. In addition, we have a long list of satisfied customers. Now, I would like to talk about pricing…

Bizmates style:

Ryo:
Ms. Perry, apart from price, what factors are important to your clients when selecting semiconductors?
Liz:
Well, top of the list, other than price, would be reliability. In other words, our clients want products that can be trusted.
Ryo:
That is the same with our Japanese clients. One feature of our product is the high-grade materials we use. So, our products are highly reliable with a near zero defective rate. So you can confidently tell your customers that they are getting the highest quality product in this industry.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions:

  1. What techniques would you use to involve your listeners?
  2. What kind of chart/graph would you use in your next presentation overseas?
  3. What are the features of your products/services?
  4. What could be the benefits of using your products/services?
  5. How would you bridge these features with the benefits?
▼▼▼
ACT

Final Activity:

Imagine you are delivering a presentation about something related to your work. Involve your audience, present factual information, and use the Feature-Advantage-Benefit technique.
