OTHER PROGRAMS BUSINESS TRIPILESSON 15
MAKING A PROPOSAL
15
BIZ TIP
Structure your proposal so that it provides a solution to the client’s problem by:
  • Focusing how your product can benefit THEIR company
  • Stating the financial and non-financial gains
  • Explaining the possible short-term and long-term results
SEE

Example:

Ryo:
OK, what do you think?
Bob:
We’re still unsure that this is a viable business for us.
Ryo:
I understand. OK, please e-mail me if you have other questions. Thank you for your time.

Bizmates style:

Ryo:
OK, what do you think?
Bob:
We’re still unsure that this is a viable business for us.
Ryo:
Do you mean to say that you are not sure how profitable it would be for your company?
Bob:
Yes, that’s right.
Ryo:
My proposal is for BT Distributors to be the sole distributor for our products in the US. This would give your company a competitive advantage.
Bob:
Yes, that’s true.
Ryo:
I understand it may take time for your clients to see our products as a viable option to other major brands. However, in the long run, BT Distributors would be the only seller in the US and that could lead to a very large payoff.
Bob:
OK, Ryo. Let’s look at pricing and see if we can work something out.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions:

  1. How would you structure your next proposal?
  2. What financial benefits would you focus on?
  3. What non-financial benefits would you emphasize?
  4. What could be the short-term results?
  5. What could be the long-term results?
▼▼▼
ACT

Final Activity:

Structure a proposal to your business partner on something related to your work.
