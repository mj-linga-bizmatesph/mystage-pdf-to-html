OTHER PROGRAMS BUSINESS TRIPILESSON 16
BARGAINING AND NEGOTIATING
16
BIZ TIP
The trend in negotiations has moved from a hostile positional approach to one that is more collaborative. Aim for a win-win deal by:
  • Separating the people from the problem
  • Focusing on interests not positions
  • Inventing options for mutual gain
SEE

Example:

Bob:
So, the pricing outlined in this document still doesn’t cover shipping?
Ryo:
I’m afraid it doesn’t.
Bob:
That’s too high. We need to consider other things like our operating expenses, marketing, and warehousing.
Ryo:
But we also need to consider our expenses in the manufacturing and sales.
Bob:
Could you go down another 10% so we could cover shipping?
Ryo:
No, I can’t do that. The best I can do is 2%.
Bob:
OK, we’ll have to give this more thought.

Bizmates style:

Bob:
So, the pricing outlined in this document still doesn’t cover shipping?
Ryo
I’m afraid it doesn’t.
Bob:
That’s too high. We need to consider other things like our operating expenses, marketing, and warehousing.
Ryo
I completely understand your concern1 . However, your expenses including shipping, and our expenses in the manufacturing process2   would be less relevant once we’ve established a niche market. And I think we can do this in a short span of time.
Bob:
It’s still too risky for us.
Ryo
OK, maybe we can ship free samples and offer discounts3   to first-time users to give that initial push to increase volume. This way, we would take on some of the initial risk. This would allow us to test the market without too much risk for you.
Bob:
Hmmm... Yes, I think that could work. Let’s get into the details.

TRY

Answer these questions about your destination:

  1. What problem do you foresee in your next negotiation overseas?
  2. How would you separate the problem from what both parties like to achieve?
  3. What common interests could you focus on?
  4. What options could you come up with that both of you could agree on?

Final Activity:
