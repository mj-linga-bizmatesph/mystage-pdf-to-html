OTHER PROGRAMS BUSINESS TRIPILESSON 19
SENDING A FOLLOW UP E-MAIL
19
BIZ TIP
Add a personal touch to your follow up message instead of the usual “thank you” note. Below are some tips to help you write a good follow up e-mail:
  • Talk about how much you’ve enjoyed your visit as an introduction
  • Confirm the key points you have agreed upon
  • State the action steps that you’ll be taking at this point
SEE

Example:

To:
Subject:
Bob_Cornell@bettersales.com
Thank you
Hi Bob,

Thank you for giving me the opportunity to deliver my presentation and introduce our company and our products.

Please give my regards to Mr. Clark and the rest of the managers.

Regards,
Ryo

Bizmates style:

To:
Subject:
Bob_Cornell@bettersales.com
A New Business Relationship
Dear Bob,

I would like to thank you and everyone for making my U.S. visit fun, meaningful and productive. It was really a pleasure meeting all of you.

As per our agreement, we will start sending the first batch early next month. We are all excited about our newfound business relationship with your company.

Please don’t hesitate to contact me should you have any questions. I look forward to seeing all of you again next time.

Regards,
Ryo
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions:

  1. Who would you send your follow up e-mail to?
  2. How would you show appreciation in your message?
  3. What else would you write to give your message a personal touch?
  4. What key points would you reiterate?
  5. What action plans would you outline in your message?
▼▼▼
ACT

Final activity:

Write a follow up e-mail that outlines the key points and actions plans. Make sure you give it a personal touch.
