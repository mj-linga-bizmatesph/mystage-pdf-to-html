  1. Lesson 1: Responding to an inquiry
    Write an e-mail response to an inquiry based on your situation at work.
  2. Lesson 2: Knowing their business culture
    Describe what you know about the business culture of the country you will be visiting.
  3. Lesson 3: Preparing for a business trip
    Explain what you know about the day-to-day life of the country you will be visiting.
  4. Lesson 4: Setting up an appointment
    Schedule an appointment via e-mail with your contact overseas.
  5. Lesson 5: Answering a call from the contact person
    Have a telephone conversation with your contact and provide the necessary flight details.
  6. Lesson 6: Meeting at the airport
    Engage in small talk with a contact you’ve just met for the first time at the airport.
  7. Lesson 7: Staying in the hotel
    Call room service to order dinner and ask some questions about the amenities.
  8. Lesson 8: Attending a tradeshow
    Make small talk until you have introduced yourself and your company with someone at a tradeshow.
  9. Lesson 9: Calling the contact person
    Call your contact person to confirm your meeting.
  10. Lesson 10: Getting to the client’s office
    Get relevant information from the taxi driver before getting in.
  11. Lesson 11: Taking a tour of the facilities
    Take a tour of your contacts office and ask meaningful questions.
  12. Lesson 12: Meeting the client’s for the first time
    Introduce yourself to your contact’s colleagues.
  13. Lesson 13: Presenting the product
    Make a short presentation on a relevant topic related to your work.
  14. Lesson 14: Answering questions
    Answer questions related to your business.
  15. Lesson 15: Making a proposal
    Structure a proposal to your business partner on something related to your work.
  16. Lesson 16: Bargaining and negotiating
    Negotiate with your business partner on a situation related to your work.
  17. Lesson 17: Having dinner with your clients
    Carry a conversation appropriate at the dinner table with your contact.
  18. Lesson 18: Sightseeing
    Discuss what you can do to avoid being scammed, robbed or swindled during your business trips and the things you need to do if you got into trouble with offenders.
  19. Lesson 19: Sending a follow-up e-mail
    Write a follow up e-mail that outlines the key points and actions plans. Make sure you give it a personal touch.
