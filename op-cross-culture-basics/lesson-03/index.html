TIP
Culture plays a very important role in family life. Here are some phrases to talk about
family life:
we (used to)… all the time.
… is/are/was/were a really big thing for us. 
… is/are/was/were not unusual.
SEE

Ken is discussing family life with his Chilean colleague, Luis.

Ken: So how was your trip to your hometown?
Luis: Oh, it was wonderful. I was able to see my parents, uncles, aunts and several cousins.
Ken: Wow, that’s a big reunion.
Luis: Well, actually, I used to see my extended family all the time. Family gatherings were a really big thing for us. We all gathered at my uncle’s house almost every month.
Ken: That’s nice. I only see my relatives during summer vacation and New Year’s.
Luis : Really? Well, maybe we see each other often because we live very close. In fact, many of my relatives work at my uncle’s company. In Chile, that’s not unusual.

TRY

Answer the following questions about family life of people in your country:

  • Typically, how big are families in your country?
  • How are responsibilities shared in a typical family?
  • What are some common family traditions in your culture?
  • How do families spend time together?
  • How does your country’s culture affect family life?
  • Ask your trainer questions about his or her family life. ​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.
