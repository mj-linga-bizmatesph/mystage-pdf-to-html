BIZ TIP
Food is an important part of a country’s culture. Here are some phrases to talk about food:
my personal favorite is…
… is made from…
… is considered…
SEE

Ken is asking Kishan about Sri Lankan cuisine.

Ken: What kind of food do you eat in Sri Lanka?

Kishan: Well, we have rice and curry almost every day. But my personal favorite is the hopper.​ It’s made from rice flour and coconut milk. It’s like a pancake.

Ken: That sounds tasty. Is it a breakfast dish?
Kishan: We eat it for breakfast and dinner. On special days like birthdays and weddings, we eat kiribath. Kiribath is rice cooked in coconut milk. It’s considered a symbol of luck and happiness.

TRY

Answer the following questions about your country’s food culture:

  • What are common dishes for breakfast, lunch, and dinner in your country?
  • What are some traditional dishes in your country?
  • Is it more common to eat at home or eat out?
  • Is there any special food prepared during your national holidays?
  • Does your country’s food culture affect the way people live?
  • Ask your trainer questions about his or her country’s food. ​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.

ACT

Describe your country’s food culture and how it is connected with the overall culture.
