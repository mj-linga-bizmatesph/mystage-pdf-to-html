WORK LIFE
5
BIZ TIP
You can clearly see a country’s culture in the work life. Here are some phrases to talk about work life:
it’s important for us to…
in some cases, …
I tend to…
SEE

Ken is explaining company rules to Sophie, a new French employee.

Ken: So our regular working hours are from 9:00AM to 6:00PM. You can have a one-hour lunch break any time you’d like.
Sophie: OK, I need to make sure I remember that. In some companies in France, lunch breaks are two hours long.
Ken: Really? 
Sophie: It’s nice to have enough time to eat or socialize. It’s important for us to share food and spend time with colleagues and friends. In some cases, people go home to eat lunch with their families.
Ken: That’s really nice. I tend to eat lunch at my desk and answer e-mails.
Sophie: Well, actually, that’s also the trend in big cities in France nowadays. Everyone’s so busy recently.
Ken: That’s too bad. But let’s go out to eat together today. I know a good restaurant.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer the following questions about work life of people in your country:

  • In your country, do people work long hours? Why?
  • In your country, how many vacation days do people get and actually use? Why?
  • How has your country tried to improve work-life balance?
  • Is it common to have close relationships with colleagues in your country?
  • How has your country’s culture affected its people’s work life?
  • Ask your trainer questions about the work life in his or her country. ​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.
▼▼▼
ACT

Explain how your country’s culture affects its people’s work life and yours.
