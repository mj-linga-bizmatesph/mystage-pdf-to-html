SPORTS

BIZ TIP
Sports and how they are enjoyed reflect a country’s culture. Here are some phrases to talk
about sports of your country:
it’s so… that…
it shows…
in short, …
SEE

Ken is discussing sports with his Argentinian colleague, Morena.

Morena: I didn‘t know football was so popular in Japan, too. In Argentina, it‘s so big that streets become empty when our national team plays because everyone is watching the game.​ It shows how much we love football.
Ken: That’s unbelievable. It’s great that the whole country shares a hobby.
Morena: But it’s actually not our national sport.
Ken: It isn’t?
Morena: A sport called Pato is. In short, it’s like basketball, but players ride horses.​ However, most people have never played or seen it.

TRY

Answer the following questions about sports in your country:

  • Which sports are most widely played or watched in your country?
  • What is the national sport in your country? 
  • What are some traditional sports in your country? 
  • Who are some famous athletes from your country?
  • How has the culture of your country affected its sports (or the other way around)?
  • Ask your trainer questions about sports in his or her country. ​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.

ACT

Explain how sports are enjoyed in your country and their connection with culture.
