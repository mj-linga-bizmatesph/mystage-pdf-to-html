SEE

Ken sees Lin, his Chinese colleague, hang up her phone after a long, angry conversation.

Ken: Who was that?
Lin: It was my mom. I was complaining because she went to the marriage market.
Ken: What’s a marriage market?
Lin: Parents can look for their children’s future partners at a park in Shanghai. My mom came home with five profiles of rich men. She asked me to meet some of them.
Ken: So do parents play a big role in matchmaking?
Lin: They really try to. In many parts of China, parents want to find the best future spouses for their kids. I think it’s because parents of my generation could only have one child.​ They really want their children to live happy lives.

TRY

Answer the following questions about marriage in your country:

  • What are some trends in marriages in your country?
  • What are weddings like in your country? 
  • How tolerant is your country with age differences? International marriage? Same-sex marriage? 
  • Are arranged marriages common in your country? What do you think about them?
  • How are the culture of your country and the idea of marriage connected?
  • Ask your trainer questions about the idea of marriage in his or her country. ​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.

ACT

Explain the idea of marriage in your country and how it is connected with culture.
