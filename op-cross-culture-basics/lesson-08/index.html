Culture can be found in the forms of entertainment of that country. Here are some phrases
to talk about entertainment in your country:
one in three families…
…, or what we call…
it’s mostly popular among…
SEE

Ken is talking with his Kiwi colleague, Emily, about entertainment.

Ken: I‘m planning to go on vacation to New Zealand this year. What do people do for fun there?
Emily: Oh, we do all kinds of outdoor activities. I surf when it’s warm and snowboard when it’s cold. Many of us live near the water, and it’s said that about one in three families own a boat in Auckland.
Ken: Wow, it sounds like people spend a lot of time outdoors.
Emily: Yes. And there are so many things you can do for free. Hiking, or what we call tramping, is a great way to experience our natural environment.
Ken: I‘d like to try that. What else do people do?
Emily: A popular hobby is gardening. It‘s mostly popular among people who are over 40. ​ Another is DIY. I guess we like to do things ourselves.

TRY

Answer the following questions about entertainment in your country:

  • Are there any forms of entertainment that are unique to your country?
  • How widely are they enjoyed? 
  • What forms of entertainment are enjoyed among different age groups?
  • What forms of entertainment do you personally enjoy? 
  • How are your country’s culture and entertainment connected?
  • Ask your trainer questions about entertainment in his or her country. ​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.
