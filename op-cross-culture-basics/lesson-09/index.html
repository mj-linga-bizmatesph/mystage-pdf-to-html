Celebrations are often a showcase of culture. Here are some phrases to talk about
celebrations of your country:
it was originally…
nowadays…
you should… at least once in your life
SEE

Ken is talking with his German colleague, Hannah, about celebrations.

Ken: I can‘t believe it‘s almost October. Are people in Munich getting ready for Oktoberfest?
Hannah: It’s already started. Oktoberfest starts in mid-September.
Ken: It does?
Hannah: Yes. It was originally held in October but it was moved forward because it‘s warmer in September.
Ken: I see. So what happens during the festival?
Hannah: Well, there are many huge tents that serve beer and food. You can see parades, brass bands, and a horse race. Everybody dresses in traditional costumes. Also nowadays, you can get on amusement rides like rollercoasters.
Ken: That sounds like a fun day.
Hannah: It’s not one day. It’s 16 or 17 days long. You should experience it at least once in your life. You’ll understand how much German people love beer.

TRY

Answer the following questions about your country’s celebrations:

  • Which traditional celebrations would you recommend foreigners to participate in?
  • What are the origins of those celebrations? 
  • How are they celebrated nowadays?
  • Are there any cultural items or costumes used in those celebrations? 
  • What can you say about your country’s culture, based on the celebrations you mentioned?
  • Ask your trainer questions about his or her country’s celebrations. ​ Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.
