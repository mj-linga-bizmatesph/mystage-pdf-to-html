HISTORY
11
BIZ TIP
We can understand a country’s culture by learning about its history. Here are some phrases
to talk about the history of your country:
there was a time when…
… to this day.
everyone learns about… in school.
SEE

Ken is asking Min about the history of Taiwan.

Ken: Can people in Taiwan speak Japanese?
Min: Yes, many people choose to learn Japanese as a second or third language. As you may know, there was a time when Taiwan was part of Japan. People remember how Japan helped Taiwan modernize to this day.
Ken: That’s great, but I shouldn’t feel proud about the time Japan occupied Taiwan.
Min: Well, to make you feel better, back where I grew up near Tainan City, everyone learns about a Japanese engineer called Yoichi Hatta in school.
Ken: Who’s that?
Min: He built a dam and helped to improve farming dramatically. He treated Japanese and Taiwanese workers equally, so everyone’s motivation was high. There’s even a street named Hatta Road.

TRY

Answer the following questions about your country:

  • How old is your country’s history?
  • What’s an event in modern history that has helped to shape your country today?
  • What’s an event in early history that has helped to shape your country today?
  • Who is an important historical figure in your country?
  • What other countries have had an impact on the history of your country?
  • Ask your trainer questions about the history of his or her country. ​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.
