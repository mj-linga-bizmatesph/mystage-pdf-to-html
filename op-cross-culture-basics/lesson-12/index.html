BIZ TIP
A country’s culture can even affect the laws of a country. Here are some phrases to talk about laws:
​ you’re (not) allowed to…
I personally feel… 
the law helps… 
SEE

Ken is talking about laws with his Singaporean colleague, Eric.

Ken: I read that Singapore is a “fine“ city. Does it mean that it‘s a beautiful city?
Eric: Yes, but it has another meaning. It‘s a city with many laws that fine you, if you break them. For example, you‘re not allowed to litter anywhere. If you do, you will be fined up to $1,000.​
Ken: Wow, that’s very strict.
Eric: But it doesn’t mean that the police are everywhere all the time. I personally feel the law is good because it helps Singapore to stay super clean. There are also fines for spitting in public and jaywalking.
Ken: Why does Singapore have so many laws like that?
Eric: I think it’s because Singapore became developed so quickly. The government wanted people’s social manners to develop quickly, too.

TRY

Answer the following questions about laws in your country:

  • Is there a law that is unique to your country?
  • Why do you think the law was made?
  • Have there been any big changes in any laws recently? Why?  
  • Are laws strictly followed?
  • Are there any current or past laws that symbolize your country’s culture?
  • Ask your trainer questions about any unique laws in his or her country. ​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.

ACT

Explain some unique laws in your country and how they are connected with culture.
