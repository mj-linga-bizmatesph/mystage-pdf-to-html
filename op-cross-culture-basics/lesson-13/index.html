TIP
A country’s culture can be seen in its industry as well. Here are some phrases to talk about
your country’s industry:
​… is one of the leading countries in …
the key to our success is/was/has been…
these days, …
SEE

Ken sees Levi, his Dutch colleague, taking out paper from a garbage bin.

Ken: Hi, Levi. What are you doing?
Levi: Oh, hi, Ken. I‘m just putting things in the right place. Paper should be recycled, not thrown away with other garbage. Everybody separates waste in the Netherlands.
Ken: didn’t know that. Is recycling very important in your country?
Levi: The Netherlands is one of the leading countries in the waste management industry. We recycle over 64% of our waste.
Ken: Really? How is that possible?
Levi: The key to our success is cooperation. We are a small country, and there’s not much land to dump waste. So the government, companies, and people all cooperate to reduce waste. These days, we export our knowledge overseas.

TRY

Answer the following questions about industry in your country:

  • What are the biggest industries in your country? 
  • Why is your country strong in those industries?
  • What industries represent the uniqueness of your country?
  • What are the most popular industries to work in? Why?
  • How has your country’s culture affected its industries (or the other way around)?
  • Ask your trainer questions about his or her country’s industry.​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.

ACT

Explain some of your country’s industries and how they are connected with culture.
