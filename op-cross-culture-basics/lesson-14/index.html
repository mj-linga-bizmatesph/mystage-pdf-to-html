TIP
How people socialize is a good example of the country’s culture. Here are some phrases to
talk about socializing in your country:
​keep in mind that…
the rule of thumb is…
you need to learn…
SEE

Ken is talking with his Mexican colleague, Antonio, after their visit to a Mexican client.

Ken: The client gave you a hug and a few pats on the back. He only gave me a handshake.
Antonio: Well, I’ve known him for a year. You’ll get your hug after a few more visits.
Ken: That’s good to know. We know each other quite well already. Didn’t we spend about 30 minutes discussing our families and hobbies?
Antonio: Tomorrow, we’ll spend three or four hours doing that over lunch.
Ken: Really?
Antonio: Keep in mind that in Mexico, deals are only closed between friends. The rule of thumb is that no one starts negotiating in the first meeting. If you want to succeed in business here, you need to learn how to build personal relationships.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer the following questions about socializing in your country:

  • In your country, how do people greet strangers? Close friends?
  • How do people address each other? Does it depend on the situation?
  • How do people expand their social or business network?
  • How do people get to know each other better? Is it a quick process?
  • What can you say about your culture based on these social behaviors? 
  • Ask your trainer questions about how people socialize in his or her country.​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.

ACT

​ Explain the way people socialize in your country and how it is connected with culture.
