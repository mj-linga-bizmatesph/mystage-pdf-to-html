HAPPINESS
15
BIZ
TIP
The definition of happiness may depend on the culture. Here are some phrases to talk
about your country’s idea of happiness:
​… has to do with…
we value…
… is not necessarily important.
SEE

Ken and Alan, a colleague from Iceland, are discussing their countries’ ideas of happiness.

Ken: Is it true that Icelanders are the fourth happiest people in the world?
Alan: Yes. Iceland is a great place to live in. We’re happy because there is always someone to help you in bad times. We think happiness has to do with having people you can trust around you.
Ken: That’s very interesting. 
Alan: We also value books. We love reading. We like it so much that one out of eight people publish a book in their lifetime!
Ken: Really? So do many people make money from writing books?
Alan: Not really. We really read and write because we like to. For us, becoming rich is not necessarily important.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer the following questions about your country’s idea of happiness:

  • In your country, what are three typical things that people associate with happiness?
  • What is an ideal life of a man like? How about of a woman?
  • Do you feel that people are generally happy in your country now?
  • What are three things YOU need to live a happy life?
  • How would you prioritize them?
  • Ask your trainer questions about his or her country’s idea of happiness.​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.​

ACT
