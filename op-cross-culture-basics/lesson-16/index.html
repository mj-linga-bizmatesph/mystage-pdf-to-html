NEIGHBORING COUNTRIES
16
BIZ TIP
Looking at neighboring countries can help to understand your own country’s culture. Here
are some phrases to compare neighboring countries with yours:​
​​ … and… are often compared
have… in common
bitter moments
SEE

Ken is talking with his Kenyan colleague, Elim, about Ethiopia.

Ken: I read that Ethiopia‘s GDP may surpass Kenya‘s. They are neighboring countries, right?
Elim: Yes, they are. Kenya and Ethiopia are often compared. We have several things in common. We both have the world’s fastest runners, similar GDPs and similar problems.
Ken: What kind of problems do you both have, for example?
Elim: Just to name a few: diseases, poverty, and security.
Ken: So do Kenya and Ethiopia cooperate to solve those problems?
Elim: Yes. We do have some bitter moments like soldiers crossing the border, but basically, we try to be leaders of East Africa together. We are strengthening our relationship through joint projects.

TRY

Answer the following questions about your country’s neighbors:

  • What neighboring countries are often featured in the news in your country? Why?
  • What can you say about your country’s culture, comparing it with neighboring countries? 
  • How is your country’s culture influenced by neighboring countries?
  • What can your country learn from those other countries? What can they learn from yours?
  • What are some ways your country and the countries you have mentioned collaborate?
  • Ask your trainer questions about his or her country’s neighbors.​
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.
▼▼▼
ACT

​Explain your country’s neighbors and how their cultures compare to your own country’s culture.
