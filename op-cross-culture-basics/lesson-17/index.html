OTHER PROGRAMS CROSS CULTURE BASICS LESSON 17
SCHOOLING
17
BIZ
TIP
A person’s individual culture and the schooling he or she goes through are closely
connected. Here are some phrases to talk about schooling:
back in school, …
extracurricular activities
my favorite subjects were…
SEE

Ken is talking with his colleague, Mosi, after playing a game of football.

Ken: You make great passes, Mosi. It‘s thanks to you that I could score so many goals.
Mosi: I’m glad I could help. Back in school, I was on the football team. We spent a lot of time learning how to pass. Team spirit was an important concept at our school.
Ken: I see. Did you do any other extracurricular activities?
Mosi: I was also in the debate club. Again, working as a team was very important. My favorite subjects were history and literature, but I wasn’t good at math or science. So I learned a lot from working with my classmates who loved those subjects.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer the following questions about the schooling you went through:

  • What was unique about the school(s) you went to?
  • Did your school(s) encourage individual or team performance? Why?
  • Were students actively discussing or quietly listening in class? Why?
  • What part of school did you enjoy the most?
  • What part of school benefited you the most?
  • Ask your trainer questions about the schooling he or she went through.
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.
▼▼▼
ACT

Explain the schooling you went through and how it has affected you.
