YOUTH
18
BIZ TIP
A person’s youth affects his or her individual culture. Here are some phrases to talk about your youth:
I grew up…
I was fascinated by…
I’ll never forget…
SEE

Ken is talking with his colleague, Alex, during a coffee break.

Ken: Alex, thank you for facilitating the anniversary party. And the ice breaker activities you planned were all so fun!
Alex: No problem. I grew up watching people entertain others. My father was really good at organizing parties.
Ken: So I guess your talent comes from your father.
Alex: Yes. As a child, I was fascinated by his skill to help everyone have a good time. I’ll never forget seeing him hand-crafting Bingo cards the night before a party. Now we can order some on the Internet for a few dollars. But enough about me. How was your childhood?
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer the following questions about your youth:

  • What did you do for fun when you were a child?
  • What role did you often play in your class when you were a student?
  • Which people or events helped you develop as a person?
  • What part of your personality has changed compared to the time you were a child? Why did you change?
  • What part of your personality has not changed?
  • Ask your trainer questions about the youth he or she went through.
    Check your understanding by asking follow-up questions.
▼▼▼
ACT

Describe your youth and how it has affected your individual culture.
