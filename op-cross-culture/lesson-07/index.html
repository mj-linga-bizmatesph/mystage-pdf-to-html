7
BIZ TIP
By focusing too much on our short-term goals, we run the risk of missing out on our goals for the long term. Long-term goals include:
Building relationships with key people
Not only sharing but also gaining new knowledge
Developing your global leadership skills
SEE
Ron: So what exactly is this assignment?
Ken: I’m leading a global team to implement a new database system.
Ron: I see. So you need to manage the workflow of your team, meet deadlines, and stay within budget.
Ken: That’s right.
Ron: What about your objectives for the long term?
Ken: Long term? What do you mean? I’ll be there for only one year.
Ron: I mean things like making connections with key people.
Ken: Oh yes. Of course I’ll do that, too.
Ron: That’s good. The reason I mention this is many expats focus so much on their immediate goals, and miss out on the objectives that could have a much bigger impact on the company.
Ken: Like what?
Ron: Like building relationships with key people, exchanging information with local staff, and developing global leadership skills. By acknowledging the importance of these goals while aiming to achieve your project goals, you’re more likely to have a valuable experience.
▼▼▼
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  • What are your immediate goals for working overseas? How will your performance be measured?
  • What are some goals that could have a positive, long-term effect on your career?
  • What could you do to build relationships during your assignment?
  • What could you do to develop your global leadership skills?
▼▼▼
ACT

Explain both your immediate goals and your goals that are likely to have a long-term effect on you and your company.
