OTHER PROGRAMS CROSS CULTUREILESSON 20
REVIEW
20

Review the topics from Lessons 11-19 by answering the questions below.

  • What are the three approaches that you can use to ask about another person’s culture? Give examples of each.
  • What are the three steps that you could take to ask for clarification? What phrases would you use for the first two steps?
  • What advice would you give a junior colleague who is uncomfortable about using the style switching technique?
  • What ground rules would you set if you were to chair your first global meeting? What phrases would you use?
  • Downgrade the following sentence: “You can’t do that.” Upgrade the following sentence: “I don’t think I can finish it by Monday.”
  • Give an overview of the process before giving feedback on performance to a foreign staff.
  • Imagine that your colleague makes the following comment: “All meetings should be less than an hour long.” Follow the three steps to playing devil’s advocate.
  • Imagine that you are having a meeting with your foreign project member to agree on a decision-making process. Be sure to get agreement from your project member rather than just pushing for your suggestion.
  • Imagine that you are with a foreign colleague. Get to know your colleague and build the relationship.
