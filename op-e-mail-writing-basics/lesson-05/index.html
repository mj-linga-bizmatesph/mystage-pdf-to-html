PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

A good business e-mail should follow a standard structure. Below is the basic format:
1. Subject line
2. Greeting
3. Opening
4. Main message
5. Closing

Your subject line should tell your reader what your message is about. Make it clear from the beginning by using the examples below:

Formal:
  • Project proposal: Request for feedback
  • Appreciation for your assistance
  • Request for time off
Casual:
  • Feedback on the project proposal
  • Thanks for your help
  • 2-day time off

Set the tone of your e-mail message right from the beginning by using the key phrases below:

Formal:
  • Dear Mr. Jones:
  • Dear Sir,
  • Hi, Ms. Atkins.
Casual:
  • Hey, Jim
  • Hi, Bob.
  • Tommy,

The tone should be consistent throughout the e-mail. Here are some expressions to help you to close your e-mail:

Formal:
  • Thank you for your patience.
  • I look forward to doing business with you.
  • I hope we can meet again soon.
Casual:
  • Thanks.
  • I can't wait to start our new business venture.
  • See you again soon.
