7
EXPLAINING YOUR PURPOSE FOR WRITING
BIZ TIP

Your reason for writing should be very clear from the beginning. Use the examples below to help you:

Formal:
  • I am writing to let you know that...
  • I'd like to request your feedback on...
  • I am writing to inquire about...
Casual:
  • Just a note to say...
  • I'd like to get your feedback on...
  • I would like to ask about...
SEE
Formal
Dear Mr. Lee,

I hope all is well with you.

I am writing to let you know that your request for leave has been approved.

I hope you enjoy your 3-day vacation.

Best regards,
Casual
Hey, Brandon.

How are things going?

Just a note to say that the boss has approved your vacation leave.

Stay safe and have fun.

Cheers,
TRY

Practice explaining your reason for writing for the following situations:

SITUATION FORMAL CASUAL
Update your business partner on the deal you're trying to close.
Ask your boss for some feedback on your project proposal.
Inquire with HR about how to report your change of address.
ACT

Write a short e-mail and explain your purpose for writing for the following situations:

  1. Ask for more information about a company's particular product.
  2. If time permits, write a similar work-related e-mail.
