WRITING CLEAR INSTRUCTIONS
BIZ TIP

Giving instructions through e-mail can be tricky. Make your instructions clear by following the structure below:

Formal:
  • Please see the procedure below:
  • Step 1: …, Step 2: …, Step 3: …
  • Please let me know if you’d like to clarify anything.
Casual:
  • Here are the steps:
  • 1. …, 2. …, 3. …
  • Shoot me a message if you have any questions.
SEE
Formal
Dear Patrick,

Here’s how you can access our customer database. Please see the procedure below:

Step 1: Open CRM.exe
Step 2: Log in using your credentials
Step 3: Enter customer’s name or ID

Please let me know if you’d like to clarify anything.
Casual
Hi, Patrick.

You can check customer details in the database. Here are the steps:

1. Open CRM.exe
2. Log in using your username
3. Type the customer’s name or ID

Shoot me a message if you have any questions .
TRY

Practice writing clear instructions for the following situations:

SITUATION FORMAL CASUAL
Ask your staff member to gather more information about a competitor.
Tell a foreign visitor how to get to your office from the airport.
Tell a colleague how to lock up the office before leaving.
ACT

Write a short e-mail with clear instructions for the following situations:

  1. Instruct a new colleague on how to access your office system.
  2. If time permits, write a similar work-related e-mail.
