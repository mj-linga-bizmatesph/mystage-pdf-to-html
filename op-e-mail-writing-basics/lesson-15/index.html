PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

A good update should be clear and concise. Go straight to the point by using these sample expressions:

Formal:
  • I'd like to inform everyone that...
  • I’d like to give you some updates on…
  • I'm pleased to say that...
Casual:
  • Just an update.
  • Just a quick heads up.
  • I’m glad to tell you that…

Giving instructions through e-mail can be tricky. Make your instructions clear by following the structure below:

Formal:
  • Please see the procedure below:
  • Step 1: …, Step 2: …, Step 3: …
  • Please let me know if you’d like to clarify anything.
Casual:
  • Here are the steps:
  • 1. …, 2. …, 3. …
  • Shoot me a message if you have any questions.

It’s always a good idea to get other people’s opinions. Ask for feedback and suggestions by using these phrases:

Formal:
  • I'd like to get your opinion on...
  • I look forward to hearing your feedback on…
  • Your feedback is highly appreciated.
Casual:
  • What are your thoughts on...?
  • Please let me know what you think.
  • Any thoughts or suggestions?

Confirming a decision in writing is very important in business. Confirm the details with your reader by using these phrases:

Formal:
  • To confirm what we decided,…
  • This is to confirm that…
  • To make sure everyone understands,…
Casual:
  • To make it official,…
  • Just to confirm,…
  • So we agreed to…
