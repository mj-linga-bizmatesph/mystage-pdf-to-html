PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

One way to connect with your reader is to write with feeling. Here are some expressions you can use:

Formal:
  • It was an honor to have met you.
  • I certainly had a wonderful time during...
  • I couldn’t help but feel sad when…
Casual:
  • It was a pleasure meeting you.
  • I really had a great time during…
  • I felt really blue when…

Another way to establish connection in writing is to offer further assistance. Show your willingness to help by using the expressions below:

Formal:
  • Don’t hesitate to contact me if...
  • Let me know if I can assist you further.
  • I'd be happy to assist you in...
Casual:
  • Feel free to get in touch with me if...
  • Let me know if you need anything.
  • I’d be happy to give you a hand in…

Sometimes it’s not enough to just apologize. We also need to explain what happened and take some action. Follow the steps below:

Formal:
  • I would like to apologize for…
  • Allow me to explain what happened.
  • Rest assured that…
Casual:
  • I’m so sorry about…
  • What happened was…
  • I’d like you to know that…

Expressing sympathy in writing can be very tricky. Make sure you communicate the right tone by using these sample expressions:

Formal:
  • My heart goes out to…
  • I can only imagine what you're going through.
  • I just want you to know that...
Casual:
  • I'm sorry to hear about…
  • I know you’re going through a lot…
  • Hang in there.
PART 2

Write a short e-mail in each of the following situations. Apply the concepts and expressions you learned in Lessons 16 to 19.

  1. Write a letter to a colleague who lost his job because the department downsized. Write how you feel, express sympathy, and offer assistance.
  2. Use a situation you have at work.
