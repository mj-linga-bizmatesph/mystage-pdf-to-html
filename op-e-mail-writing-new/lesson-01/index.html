INTRODUCTION TO BUSINESS E-MAIL WRITING
1
BIZ TIP
Writing and speaking in English are two very different skills. Before you send an e-mail, ask yourself three questions.
  • Does it have a clear purpose?
  • Is the content clear?
  • Is it reader-oriented?
SEE

Example

Dave:
Hi Taro. How are you enjoying the overseas department?
Taro:
Honestly, I’ve lost confidence in my English after transferring there.
Dave:
What do you mean? You’re such a good English speaker. You should have no problems communicating with the people there.
Taro:
No, it’s not my speaking. It’s my writing skills. I get so many e-mails in English now. And the last few e-mails that I’ve written have led to misunderstandings. I didn’t realize that writing is so different from speaking.
Dave:
Yes, that’s true. Speaking and writing are different skills. You can hide your mistakes when you speak, but it’s not so easy with writing.
Taro:
So, what can I do to write better e-mails?
Dave:
Well, I’d be happy to go over the basics with you, but it requires a lot of practice before you become a good writer.
Taro:
I understand.
Dave:
I always ask myself three basic questions before I send an e-mail. Does it have a clear purpose? Is the content clear? Is it reader-oriented?
Taro:
I see. So I should know why I’m writing this e-mail, make sure that my thoughts are organized, and make it as easy as possible for the reader to understand it.
Dave:
Exactly. And if you can’t say yes to these questions, don’t send that e-mail.

TRY

Answer the questions below.

  1. What are some reasons that you have for writing e-mails in English?
  2. How can you make the content of your e-mail as clear as possible?
  3. What do you do to make your e-mails reader-oriented?
