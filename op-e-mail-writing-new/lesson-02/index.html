HAVING PROPER E-MAIL STRUCTURE
2
BIZ TIP
A professionally written e-mail should have proper structure including:
  • A clear subject line
  • An appropriate greeting and closing
  • An opening and closing paragraph to set the mood
  • A detailed body
  • A proper signature block
SEE

Example:

To:
Subject:
jsmith@abc.com
Main topic – Additional information
The subject line should be meaningful with the main topic followed by additional key information.
Greeting,
Your greeting should depend on your relationship with the reader.
Opening paragraph
The opening paragraph sets the tone of the e-mail. You can start with a short greeting, show appreciation, acknowledge receipt, or get right to the point in some cases.
Body
The body should include all relevant details organized clearly for the reader.
Closing paragraph
You can close the e-mail by building the relationship, offering support, showing appreciation, etc.
Closing,
Your closing should match the formality of your greeting.

TRY

Come up with an appropriate expression for each situation.

Situation Expressions
The subject line for a project meeting that’s been rescheduled
Greetings/closings when you know the reader well
Greetings/closings when you don’t know the reader well
Greetings/closings when you don’t know the reader’s name
The opening paragraph to someone you visited the other day, to someone you ‘ve just received an e-mail from, to someone you haven’t seen for a while
The closing paragraph to build the relationship, to offer support, to show appreciation

ACT
Part 1:
Think of a situation at work where you would write a formal and informal e-mail. Write the subject line, greeting, opening paragraph, body, closing paragraphs, and signature block that you would use.

Part 2:
For further practice, write a work-related e-mail. Do it for homework if you run out of time.
