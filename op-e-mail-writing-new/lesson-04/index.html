OTHER PROGRAMS E-MAIL WRITINGILESSON 4
KEEPING IT POSITIVE
4
BIZ TIP
A good writer gives the email a positive tone by considering how the recipient would feel when reading the e-mail. To create a positive tone:

  • Avoid placing blame on the reader
  • Use words, such as seem, think, wondering, etc. to soften the tone
  • Give reasons and background information to help with understanding
  • Avoid using negative words (problem, poor, bad, etc.)
SEE

Negative Example:

Hi, Jon.

Why didn’t you call me at 3pm like you promised? I waited 3 hours for your call.

I need to know whether you can deliver the presentation or not. Give me an answer by tomorrow morning. This is causing so much trouble.

Regards,

Positive Example:

Hi, Jon.

I thought that you would be calling at 3pm today. I hope everything is OK.

Could you let me know whether you can deliver the presentation or not? I’d appreciate it if you could let me know by tomorrow morning, so I can send out the agenda in the afternoon.

Hope to hear from you soon.

Cheers,
▼▼▼
TRY

Change the negative expressions to positive expressions.

Negative Positive
You forgot to attach the file.
The quality is too poor.
You can’t do that.
Can you come to the event or not?
Why didn’t you send me the file?
I won’t attend the meeting.
I don’t know the answer.
▼▼▼
ACT
Part 1:
Come up with a reason to reply to the positive e-mail above. Then, write the e-mail by keeping it positive.

Part 2:
For further practice, write a work-related e-mail. Do it for homework if you run out of time.
