OTHER PROGRAMS E-MAIL WRITINGILESSON 7
ORIENTING THE READER
7
BIZ TIP
A good e-mail guides the reader throughout the e-mail. To orient the reader:

  • State your reason for writing
  • Provide appropriate background information
  • Use signposts
  • Use lists and headers
SEE

Example:

Hi, Jon.

Thank you for giving us this opportunity to present our company’s products. This message will outline two things: First, I am writing to give you an overview of our products; and second, I would like to invite you to attend our product demonstration. Here are the details:

XYZ Product Overview:
  1. PictureMod – a great tool to digitally edit or enhance photos
  2. TriSculpt – a user-friendly 3D modelling software
  3. LiveImage – a software to create slideshows, animations, and short movies

Product Demo:
  1. Date : April 25
  2. Time: 3pm
  3. Venue: 2nd floor, Tokyo branch office

I’d appreciate it if you could get back to me by April 3. I look forward to…

TRY

Come up with e-mail expressions you can use for the following purposes.

Purpose Expression
To give the purpose of the e-mail
To refer to an attached document
To refer to the details
To explain the number of steps in a process
To explain each step
To refer to their request
To refer to past e-mail exchanges
To give a reminder

ACT
Part 1:
Come up with a reason to reply to the sample e-mail above. Then, write the e-mail by orienting the reader.

Part 2:
For further practice, write a work-related e-mail. Do it for homework if you run out of time.
