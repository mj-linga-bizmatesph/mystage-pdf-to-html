COMMON TYPES OF E-MAILS
11
BIZ TIP
Different types of e-mails call for different structures. However, no matter what type of e-mail it is, good e-mail writing in business should focus on:

  • Conveying your purpose through the subject line
  • Having a clear and tactful message
  • Composing the message as quickly as possible
SEE

Example

Dave:
Now that we've gone over the basics of e-mail writing, how do you feel?
Taro:
Much better, but I'm still not sure how I should format my e-mails. Is there a specific template that I can use for e-mails?
Dave:
Well, there's no one template that can be used for all e-mails. It depends on what the purpose of the e-mail is.
Taro:
What do you mean?
Dave:
Well, imagine an e-mail about making a request. The way it is structured is different from that of an e-mail responding to a request.
Taro:
I see. Sounds a bit tricky.
Dave:
Maybe so. Different e-mails call for different structures. However, the common types of e-mails you send in business should focus on 3 key points.
Taro:
So, the first is to have a clear purpose in mind. Right?
Dave:
Yes, that’s right. A meaningful subject line can give your reader an overview of what your purpose for writing is.
Taro:
I see. But what about the message itself?
Dave:
Of course you need to convey professionalism in your e-mails , so write your message as clearly and as tactfully as possible.
Taro:
I understand. So, what’s the third key point?
Dave:
Many people spend too much time in composing their e-mails. Sometimes, valuable time is wasted, so try to write your e-mail as quickly as you can.
Taro:
I agree. So, how can I compose my messages quickly?
Dave:
Having a good idea of how each type of e-mail is structured should help you with that.
