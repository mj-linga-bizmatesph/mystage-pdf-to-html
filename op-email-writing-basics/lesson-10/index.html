PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

Your main message should start with a good opening. Connect with your reader right away by using the examples below:

Formal:
  • I hope all is well with you.
  • How are things in Bangkok?
  • I hope you had a great time at the office.
Casual:
  • I hope everything's OK.
  • How's it going?
  • I hope you had a blast at the office.

Your reason for writing should be very clear from the beginning. Use the examples below to help you:

Formal:
  • I am writing to let you know that...
  • I'd like to request for your feedback on...
  • I am writing to inquire about...
Casual:
  • Just a note to say...
  • I'd like to get your feedback on...
  • I would like to ask about...

Arrange the details of your message in bullet points to make them super clear. Here are some expressions to help you get started:

Formal:
  • The following is an overview of...
  • For your reference, here are the details:
  • The agenda is as follows:
Casual:
  • Here are the details:
  • Here's the data:
  • Here's the agenda:

Inform your reader what needs to be done at the end of your message. State the action plan clearly by using the phrases below:

Formal:
  • So our next course of action is...
  • What we need to do now is to...
  • It would br great if you could...
Casual:
  • Our next step now is...
  • So, I want us to...
  • Can you...?
