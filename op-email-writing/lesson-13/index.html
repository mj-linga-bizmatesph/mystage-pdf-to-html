OTHER PROGRAMS E-MAIL WRITINGILESSON 13
Responding to requests
13
BIZ TIP
Responding to a request means you are acknowledging what people ask of you. When responding to any request you should:
  • Refer to their specific request
  • Clearly state the status of their request
  • Provide them a deadline for unfinished tasks
  • Offer further assistance, if possible
SEE

Example:

  1. Referring to their request
    This is in response to…
    As you requested,…

  2. Stating the status
    I have already…
    I am currently working on…
    I’m afraid we’ve encountered a delay in…

  3. Providing a deadline
    I should be able to send it by…
    You can expect these to be accomplished on…
    We’re looking at getting things done by…

  4. Offering further assistance
    If you have any questions, please let me know.
    Please feel free to e-mail me if…
    Please let me know how else I could help.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What sort of requests do you get from your main office?
  2. How do you acknowledge these requests?
  3. How do you inform them of the status of their request?
  4. Do you sometimes feel the need to offer further assistance?
▼▼▼
ACT
Part 1:
Respond to an e-mail request from the main office asking for the list of people in your office who would attend the company event next month.

Part 2:
For further practice, write a similar, work-related e-mail. Do it for homework if you run out of time.
