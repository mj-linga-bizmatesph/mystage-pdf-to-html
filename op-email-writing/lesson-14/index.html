OTHER PROGRAMS E-MAIL WRITINGILESSON 14
Giving updates and FYIs
14
BIZ TIP
Giving updates or FYIs means you are informing your recipient about something. To get your message across effectively, you should:

  • Open with a clear statement
  • Give the details
  • Ask for confirmation, if necessary
  • Offer to answer any questions
SEE

Example:

  1. Opening with a clear statement
    Please be informed that…
    I am pleased to inform you that…
    Just a quick update on…

  2. Giving the details
    As of today, we’ve already…
    The following information outlines…
    For your reference, I’ve included…

  3. Asking for confirmation
    Could you confirm after reading this message.
    I look forward to getting your confirmation by…
    It would be great if I could get your confirmation, so that…

  4. Offering to answer questions
    Please contact me if you have any questions.
    Should you have any questions, please let us know.
    For further inquiries, please contact…
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What sort of updates do you receive from your boss?
  2. How do you usually give updates/FYIs to your colleagues or staff?
  3. In what situations do you need to get their confirmation?
  4. Do you ask them to contact you if they have any questions?
▼▼▼
ACT
Part 1:
Write an update/FYI to let your staff know that the new system will come into effect next week.

Part 2:
For further practice, write a similar, work-related e-mail. Do it for homework if you run out of time.
