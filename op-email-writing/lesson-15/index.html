OTHER PROGRAMS E-MAIL WRITINGILESSON 15
Writing a complaint
15
BIZ TIP
Writing a complaint should NOT focus on venting your frustration. Instead, aim for a positive outcome by asking your recipient to take action. Achieve this by:

  • Stating the complaint
  • Giving details
  • Requesting action
SEE

Example:

  1. Stating the complaint
    I am writing because…
    I would like to draw your attention to…
    I am writing to notify you about…

  2. Giving details
    The issue happened last…
    We encountered the problem on…
    Here’s what happened…

  3. Requesting action
    I would greatly appreciate if you could look into this right away.
    We kindly ask for your immediate action towards…
    We are willing to cooperate in order to resolve this issue as soon as possible.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What sort of complaints do you write your vendors about?
  2. What information do you use to support your complaint?
  3. What do you tell them to take immediate action?
  4. Would you be willing to cooperate just to resolve the issue?
▼▼▼
ACT
Part 1:
Write a complaint about some bug issues found in the new system that a vendor has set up for your office.

Part 2:
For further practice, write a similar, work-related e-mail. Do it for homework if you run out of time.
