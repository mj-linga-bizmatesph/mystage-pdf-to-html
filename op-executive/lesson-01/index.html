YOUR ROLE: WHAT DO YOU DO?
BIZ TIP

One thing that the reporter will want to do is to get to know you better. It’s important to build some kind of rapport with the reporter. If the reporter likes you, he or she will be more likely to write about you in a positive light. Build rapport by:

  • Showing appreciation for the opportunity
  • Being professional yet friendly
  • Being yourself
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about his role in the company.

Reporter:
Thank you for making time for this interview. I know you are very busy, so I’d like to get started. First, I’d like to know a little more about your role at Synchron Corp.
Ken:
First of all, let me just say it’s my pleasure to have the opportunity to be interviewed. I don’t get many chances like this, so I’m very excited. As for my role, I’m currently the Executive Quality Director. This means that my responsibility is to ensure that the quality of all our products meet our highest standards.
Reporter:
So what is a typical day for you?
Ken:
Well, there is no typical day for this position, which is a good thing because I like variety. However, I spend most of my time in meetings with my staff reviewing quality reports and always coming up with ways to minimize…
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. What is your role in your company?
  2. What is a typical day for you?
  3. What would you say is the most difficult thing about your job?
  4. What do you enjoy the most about your job?
  5. What do you think it takes to be successful in your position?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about your role in your company.
