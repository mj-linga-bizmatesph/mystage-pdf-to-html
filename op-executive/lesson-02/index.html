YOUR BUSINESS: WHAT IS IT?
BIZ TIP

During an interview with the media, you may find an opportunity to emphasize an important but often-misunderstood concept in your business. This is a great opportunity to reinforce your message. You can do this by:

  • Mentioning what the misunderstanding is at the beginning
  • Explaining the important business concept
  • Making sure the reporter believes that it is indeed a misunderstanding
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about his company’s business.

Reporter:
Can you give me a brief description of your business?
Ken:
Sure. Well, many people misunderstand that we are just a manufacturer of men’s watches. You see, we believe that a major problem is that we live in a time-controlled society. And because many of us are so poor at managing time, we have constant pressure and stress in our lives. So, our goal is to give you back control in your life.
Reporter:
And how do you do that?
Ken:
Well, our products do much more than just tell time. They are multi-functional devices that give you reminders and have a built-in calendar. So, it would be inaccurate to say that we are simply a watch manufacturer.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. What is your business?
  2. What makes your products special?
  3. Who are the main users of your products?
  4. How is your company’s industry different from other industries?
  5. How has your industry changed over the years?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about your business.
