A GLOBAL MIND: DOES EVERYONE NEED IT?
BIZ TIP

Sometimes it’s difficult to really understand the reporter’s question. The reporter may be speaking too fast or using a difficult idiom you don’t know. Whatever the reason, it’s important that you clarify the question before you answer. You can do this by:

  • Asking to repeat the question more slowly
  • Asking to rephrase the question
  • Confirming the meaning of the question
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about global-mindedness.

Reporter:
As your company makes efforts to expand its business overseas, do you see a lack of global-mindedness within your company as a barrier?
Ken:
I’m sorry. Could you speak a little more slowly?
Reporter:
Of course. I mean, would you say that your employees have the right mindset for overseas expansion?
Ken:
I see. So you mean, are we mentally prepared for our aggressive plans to expand overseas? We all realize that globalization is inevitable. Not just to survive, but in order to thrive. Top management has done its best to communicate this company-wide. Therefore, many of our employees have made learning English a priority. I can’t say that everyone in our company will be using English in their work, but I can say that everyone will need to have a global mind.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. How has globalization affected your company over the last several years?
  2. What has your company done to adjust to these changes?
  3. What barriers does your company have from becoming truly global?
  4. What can you do to persuade your staff to become more globally-minded?
  5. Share your experiences of working globally.
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about the impact of globalization on your company.
