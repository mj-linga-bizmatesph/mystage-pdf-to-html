ENTREPRENEURIAL SPIRIT: IS IT ONLY FOR START-UPS?
BIZ TIP

An effective technique to get your point across is to start with a general statement which sums up your opinion and then move into specifics to support it. You can do this by:

  • Starting with a general statement to sum up your opinion
  • Going into specifics about your company
  • Once again making a general statement to summarize your opinion at the end
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about entrepreneurial spirit.

Reporter:
I know that your company has a long history and has been very innovative in the past. However, with all these start-ups coming into your industry, how important is keeping the entrepreneurial spirit alive within your company?
Ken:
“Entrepreneurial spirit” is not a term used only by start-ups. It’s a mindset that needs to be embraced by all employees no matter how big or small the company is. As our business environment changes so rapidly, opportunities for new business ideas are all around us. So, at Synchron, we encourage our employees to take calculated risks, and make them into feasible businesses. So, although we are a large corporation with a long history, our employees understand that entrepreneurship equals business growth. And this is something all businesses need.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. How important is entrepreneurial spirit in your company?
  2. Would you say that the entrepreneurial spirit among your staff is strong or weak?
  3. What can your company do to encourage an entrepreneurial culture?
  4. What are the pros and cons of hiring aspiring entrepreneurs in your company?
  5. What are some examples where you gave your staff autonomy and ownership?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about the importance of entrepreneurial spirit in your company.
