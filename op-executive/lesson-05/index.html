FUTURE LEADERS: WHO ARE THEY?
BIZ TIP

During an interview with a reporter, you may get an unexpected question, which you need some time to think about. In this case, you can buy time by:

  • Being honest that you didn’t expect such a question
  • Asking for a bit of time to think about it
  • Letting them know that you may provide more information after the interview
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about future leaders.

Reporter:
The old saying goes, Leaders aren’t born; they are made”. How do you feel about that? And what is Synchron’s approach to developing its future leaders?
Ken:
Hmm… honestly, I didn’t expect to talk about our global leadership program. However, I do have some information on this. Please give me a minute to think about this.
Reporter:
Sure, take your time.
Ken:
OK. Well, at Synchron, we look for employees who show strong potential to become effective leaders and do our best to develop them. And based on my experience, they’re not always the ones who perform the best on their own. We look for people skills, ambition, and focus, as these are traits that are difficult to teach. Then, we give them every opportunity to experience various areas and positions in our business. Anyway, I’ll have our HR director give you more information on our program.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. Do you believe in the saying, “Leaders aren’t born; they are made”? Why or why not?
  2. What is your company’s global leadership development program?
  3. What qualities do you look for in future leaders?
  4. What area of expertise do you think is needed to become a leader in your company?
  5. Do you believe in promoting from within or bringing in someone from outside to be leaders?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about the future leaders of your company.
