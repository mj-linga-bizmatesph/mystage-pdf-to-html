BUSINESS ETHICS: WHAT REALLY MATTERS?
BIZ TIP

In order to answer some questions, it helps to build credibility to show the reporter that you are indeed qualified to answer the question. You can build credibility by:

  • Explaining your expertise in a specific area
  • Mentioning your experience in a specific area
  • Showing confidence in your answer
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about business ethics.

Reporter:
As you know, in recent weeks, there have been a couple of companies in your industry involved in unethical business practices. What is your opinion on this?
Ken:
I’ve seen ethical standards in business change so much compared to my days when I studied business law in university. Many of the common practices then, such as gift-giving, and reprimanding in public are now considered unethical. However, I think a lot of business executives are having trouble adjusting to the progression of ethics. I’m very lucky to have worked in our Legal Department at Synchron; that experience taught me how important it is for companies to clearly communicate values based on good business practices to employees.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. How have the standards of business ethics changed since your younger days?
  2. Why do you think they’ve changed so much? Are these changes good?
  3. What are examples of companies that had a difficult time keeping up with changes in business ethics?
  4. Does your company have clear values based on good business practices?
  5. How are these values communicated and implemented in the company?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about the business ethics in your company.
