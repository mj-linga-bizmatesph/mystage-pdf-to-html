PERSONAL HEALTH: HOW DO YOU MAINTAIN IT?
BIZ TIP

Although your interview with a reporter should be professional, it helps to become a bit personable sometimes to build a closer bond. You can become friendlier by:

  • Using the reporter’s name
  • Making a comment about the reporter
  • Asking the reporter a question
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about personal health.

Reporter:
You seem to be in great shape. Is there anything that you do to stay healthy?
Ken:
Thank you for saying so, Jim. Actually, you look like you go to the gym every day. What do you do to stay in shape? But all kidding aside, health is a major concern for the company and all our executives. Many of us are in our 50s and up, and if any of us got a sudden illness, it could severely impact our business. So over the last few years, our company has tried to create a culture of healthy living. Simple changes, such as adding fruit or vegetable snacks in the office, closing the office early on Wednesdays, and holding monthly outdoor events have greatly raised the awareness of wellness in our company.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. What are some physical and mental health issues that companies need to deal with?
  2. How important is good health to you as an executive? What do you do to maintain it?
  3. How have your views on healthy living changed since your younger days?
  4. How important is your employees’ good health to your company? What does your company do to keep its employees healthy?
  5. Do you think there is more that your company can do to promote healthy living in your company?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about your personal health and how you maintain it.
