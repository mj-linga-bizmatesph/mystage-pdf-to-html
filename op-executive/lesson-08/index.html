YOUR SOCIAL NETWORK: IS IT YOUR SAFETY NET?
BIZ TIP

An effective way to make your answer interactive and interesting is to tell it in a story. This makes it easier for you to add a dramatic effect and to connect better with the reporter. You can do this by:

  • Giving a brief background of the situation
  • Speaking in the narrative form
  • Relating the conclusion of the story to the original question
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about the importance of having a strong social network.

Reporter:
I know you’ve worked for various companies in various industries in your extensive business career. Do you have a wide social network and does it help you in your business?
Ken:
Honestly speaking, I didn’t always pay much attention to building a social network. Until about 10 years ago, I would turn down dinner invitations from business partners. Then, one day, a sudden downturn in our business led to major restructuring that left me jobless for 6 months until an old business contact, Tom, offered to introduce me to the HR Director at Synchron. And so, I said, “Sure, why not?”. And that’s how I got in Synchron. So, now, I spend some time each week keeping in touch with my contacts. My network has become a great source of information and a safety net during times of uncertainty.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. How wide is your social network?
  2. What do you do to build your network?
  3. Have you ever relied on your network for anything or helped people within your network?
  4. What are some tips that you can give in building a strong network?
  5. Would you consider your social network as a safety net?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about your social network.
