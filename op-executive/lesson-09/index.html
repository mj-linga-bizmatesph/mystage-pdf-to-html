RECRUITMENT: WHAT DO YOU LOOK FOR?
BIZ TIP

Sometimes, your answers can get a bit long and it may be unclear to the reporter what your point is. So, you can wrap up your response at the end to ensure there is no misunderstanding. You can do this by:

  • Answering the question in detail
  • Repeating the question
  • Summarizing your points
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about recruitment for his company.

Reporter:
What qualities does your company look for when recruiting?
Ken:
What we look for has definitely changed over the years. We don’t see the point of hiring people based on technical skills as we did before. We place more emphasis on personality traits. One is adaptability. We are constantly changing our processes and our people need to adjust. So willingness to learn is another essential quality. Due to the speed of technological advances, many of the technical skills that we need today will become irrelevant tomorrow. And finally, creativity. New ideas don’t come from robots. They come from people. So that’s an important quality. So what qualities do we look for? Three key qualities would be adaptability, willingness to learn, and creativity.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. What do you think are the merits and demerits of mid-career hires and fresh grads?
  2. What personal traits do you look for in applicants? What are some red flags when hiring people?
  3. Have you ever made a bad hiring decision? How so?
  4. How has the job market changed since you were job hunting?
  5. If you were to give some advice to people interested applying to your company, what would you say?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about recruitment at your company.
