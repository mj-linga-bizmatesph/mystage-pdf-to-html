PROGRAMMERS: WHERE DO YOU FIND THEM?
BIZ TIP

Your opinion is only an opinion until you can back it up with proper evidence. The accuracy of the evidence you use is extremely important as you may be quoted by the reporter. You can do this by:

  • Highlighting your point
  • Providing hard data to support it
  • Reinforcing your point
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about programmers.

Reporter:
With the digital boom, it seems like every company’s IT Department is understaffed. Every executive I speak to says that they need more IT people. Is this an issue at Synchron?
Ken:
Yes, for sure. We’re definitely in need of IT people. Out of the 25 mid-career employees we’ve hired this year, 70% has been for programmers, developers, and other IT-related posts. In the past, we relied on marketing and quality to grow our business. However, now we see IT as the third main pillar to grow our business.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. Is finding IT people an issue for your company?
  2. How much has your IT Department grown over the last few years?
  3. Where do you find IT people?
  4. Do you think your company will continue to need more IT people in the future?
  5. What can you do to attract more IT people?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about the need for IT people.
