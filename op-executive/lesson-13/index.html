WORK-LIFE BALANCE: IS IT A BARRIER?
BIZ TIP

Sometimes, it’s difficult to provide data to support your views. A common way to support your opinion is to provide examples. You can do this by:

  • Highlighting your point
  • Giving examples to support it
  • Reinforcing your point
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about work-life balance.

Reporter:
Some companies see the concept of work-life balance as a barrier to business growth. How does Synchron view work-life balance for its employees?
Ken:
At Synchron, we believe that employee happiness and business growth go hand-in-hand. As a company, we have the responsibility to not only show profits for our shareholders, but to also create happiness for our employees. That’s why we have a variety of benefits. For example: flex time, no overtime Wednesdays, and birthday holidays, just to name a few. All these were introduced so that our employees could balance their work and private life.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. How important is work-life balance in your company?
  2. What does your company do to support work-life balance for its employees?
  3. Do you feel you have control to live the life you want in your company?
  4. Was there a time in your work-life, where your family was a lower priority than your work?
  5. If you were to give some advice to your staff on work-life balance, what would you say?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about the importance of work-life balance for employees.
