DIVERSITY: IS IT THE WAY TO GO?
BIZ TIP

At times, you may be faced with a question that is intended to make you or your company look bad. These are called loaded questions. Avoid falling into the reporter’s trap by:

  • Confirming the source of the reporter’s stats
  • Stating your opinion clearly
  • Supporting your opinion
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about diversity in the workplace.

Reporter:
Less than 2% of your workforce is non-Japanese and less than 10% of your managers are women. Does this show a lack of diversity at Synchron?
Ken:
May I ask where you got those figures?
Reporter:
I got these from your HR Dept.
Ken:
OK, they probably are correct. However, your assumption isn’t. You see, at Synchron, diversity is more than just a mix of nationalities and gender. It’s a wide mix of people with different values and backgrounds. At Synchron, we recruit many mid-career hires from different industries. We also have a wide range of age groups in our workforce. Finally, over the last three years, we’ve tripled the number of non-Japanese hires and plan to increase this over the next five years.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. How diverse is your workforce?
  2. Do you see your workforce becoming more diverse in the future? If yes, how so?
  3. What are the advantages and disadvantages of a diverse workforce?
  4. What can your company do to create a more diverse environment?
  5. What is your experience working with non-Japanese?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about the diversity of your company’s workforce.
