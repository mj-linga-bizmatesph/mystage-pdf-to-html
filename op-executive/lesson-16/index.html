CUSTOMER VOICE: ARE THEY ALWAYS RIGHT?
BIZ TIP

A technique commonly used by reporters during an interview is hypothetical questions. Reporters might ask you a question based on an imaginary situation. It’s very risky to answer these hypothetical questions, so avoid them by:

  • Insisting that you can’t answer a hypothetical situation
  • Giving your general view on the topic
  • Explaining that every situation is different
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about customer needs.

Reporter:
So if a customer requested to add a new feature to your product that your competitors’ products had, would you add the feature?
Ken:
Well, I can’t answer a hypothetical question like that. However, I can say that we do listen very carefully to our customers. We regularly collect feedback from our customers and hold focus groups to see if there are any areas that could be improved.
Reporter:
Right. So, if a customer makes a suggestion, it could become a new feature to your product, right?
Ken:
Well, like I said, that’s a hypothetical question, which makes it very difficult to answer. Every situation is different. But as I said, we listen carefully to our customers.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. Does your company believe that the customer is always right?
  2. How important is customer voice to your company?
  3. How do you collect feedback from your customers?
  4. What has your company changed based on feedback from your customers?
  5. What are the risks of focusing too much on customer needs?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about how important customer needs are to your company.
