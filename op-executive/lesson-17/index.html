17
CHANGE: HOW DO YOU MAKE IT WORK?
BIZ TIP

You certainly can’t be expected to have the answer to every question. The last thing you want to do is give out false information to a reporter. So, don’t be pressured to answer a question if you don’t know the answer. You can always get back to the reporter later. You can do this by:

  • Telling the reporter you don’t know the answer
  • Asking if it’s OK to get back to him or her later
  • Making sure that you actually get back to the reporter
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter about change.

Reporter:
As an executive, I’m sure you are in a position to make major changes. Can you tell me what you do to effectively implement change in your company?
Ken:
Many people say that it’s human nature to resist change. However, I don’t think this is true. I think it’s not knowing the impact of the change that causes resistance rather than the change itself. So as an executive, it’s my job to communicate the reason and impact as clearly as I can.
Reporter:
I see. Back in 2013, your company implemented major reforms. How many people were laid off during that time?
Ken:
Actually, I was with a different company at that time. So, I really don’t know. Would it be all right if I get back to you on that?
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. What’s a major change that you had to implement in your company?
  2. How was the change received?
  3. What did you do to ensure that the change would be successfully implemented?
  4. What other major changes do you foresee in your company?
  5. What major change in your personal life have you experienced? How did you deal with it?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about implementing change in your company.
