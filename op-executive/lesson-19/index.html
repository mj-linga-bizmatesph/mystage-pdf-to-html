19
YOUR FUTURE: WHAT IS YOUR NEXT STEP?
BIZ TIP

There are some things that you are unable to disclose to the media. However, a reporter may still push you for information that is unavailable to the public. Avoid answering their question by:

  • Sharing only what you can
  • Brushing off the question as a sign that you will not answer
  • Stating that the information is confidential
SEE

Ken, an executive director, is being interviewed by a reporter on what his next step in the company is.

Reporter:
I really enjoyed this interview with you. I can tell you are extremely passionate about your job and your products. Could you tell me what your next steps are in your career?
Ken:
Well, for now, my heart is with Synchron. I’m very happy with the opportunities that I’ve received and I just want to do the best that I can to help the company grow.
Reporter:
But what about in terms of your career? Do you see yourself staying with Synchron for the rest of your career?
Ken:
I don’t think anyone can say that he or she will stay with a company forever.
Reporter:
Are there plans for you to be transferred overseas or to another department?
Ken:
I’m sorry, but I’m unable to disclose that information.
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. What is your next step in your company?
  2. Do you see yourself working for your company for the rest of your career?
  3. What opportunities are available to you inside and outside of your company?
  4. What would you like to do next?
  5. Have you ever considered changing companies?
ACT

Answer the question. Imagine you are having an interview with a reporter and you are being asked about your next step at your company.
