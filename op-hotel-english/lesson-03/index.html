Taking your guest
to the room
3
BIZ TIP
Continue to provide a pleasant customer experience as you take them to their room by:
Initiating a conversation
Building on the information they tell you
Showing interest by adding details to your answer
SEE

Example:

Staff: Let me carry your luggage for you. This way please.
Guest: Oh, thank you.

(On the way to the room)

Staff: …
Guest: This is a very nice hotel.
Staff: Thank you.
Guest: Do you have sushi in the hotel restaurant?
Staff: Yes, we do.

Bizmates style:

Staff: Let me carry your luggage for you. This way please.
Guest: Oh, thank you.
Staff: How was your flight, Mr. Wong?
Guest: It was a really nice flight. It was on time and they served sushi on the plane.
Staff: I see. How was it?
Guest: It was delicious. I really like Japanese food. Do they serve sushi in the hotel restaurant?
Staff: Yes they do. They serve all kinds of Japanese food, including sushi. Personally, I think the sushi at the restaurant is much better than most sushi shops around here.
▼▼▼
TRY

Practice starting a conversation using the sample questions below. Build on the information and show interest by adding details.

  • How was your trip?
  • Is this your first time to visit Japan?
  • How do you find the weather here?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play

Imagine you’re taking your guest to his/her room. Start a conversation and try to keep it going.
