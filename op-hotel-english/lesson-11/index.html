Recommending places
to see
11
BIZ TIP
Whether on vacation or on a business trip, guests will try to find time to do some sight-seeing. Recommend places to see by:
Asking what they're interested in
Making a relevant suggestion
Giving some advice on the place
SEE

Example:

Guest: We’d like to go sightseeing this afternoon. Are there places you could recommend?
Staff: Hi, Mrs. Bonham. Sure. I recommend the Tokyo Imperial Palace.
Guest: Well, I’d like my kids to enjoy their vacation further before we leave. So, a place where they could have fun would be great.
Staff: Let’s see. Harajuku is a good place with many toy stores.
Guest: Hmmm… I don’t know. Thank you for your suggestions.

Bizmates style:

Guest: We’d like to go sightseeing this afternoon. Are there places you could recommend?
Staff: Hi, Mrs. Bonham. Sure. There are a lot of places to see in Tokyo. Do you have anything specific in mind?
Guest: Well, I’d like my kids to enjoy their vacation further before we leave. So, a place where they could have fun would be great.
Staff: Do your children like amusement parks?
Guest: Well, we went to Disneyland a couple of days ago. I’d like to see something a little more traditional but not boring.
Staff: I see. In that case, Hanayashiki Amusement Park might be a fun place to visit. It’s Tokyo’s oldest amusement park. So you can experience traditional rides and games that kids enjoyed in Tokyo 100 years ago.
Guest: Sounds fantastic! I think my kids will love it there.
Staff: Also, I would go there early to beat the crowds.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What are some famous tourist spots near your hotel?
  • How would you describe these tourist spots?
  • What important things could you remind your guests about these places?
