Recommending places
to eat
12
BIZ TIP
Food can make anyone's holiday more pleasant, so your guest will likely ask you about other restaurants nearby. Recommend places to eat by:
Asking for specifics (cuisine, type of restaurant, etc.)
Offering an appropriate restaurant
Giving some details and directions
SEE

Example:

Guest: Excuse me. Do you know of any good restaurants nearby?
Staff: Good evening, Mr. Chu. Yes, there are many fine dining restaurants near the hotel.
Guest: I see, but I’m not looking for anything fancy tonight.
Staff: Not a problem. There’re also a few fast food chains nearby.
Guest: OK. I’ll just go out for a walk and see what else are out there. Thanks.

Bizmates style:

Guest: Excuse me. Do you know of any good restaurants nearby?
Staff: Good evening, Mr. Chu. Yes, there are many restaurants near the hotel. Are you looking for some fine dining experience or just casual dining?
Guest: Casual dining. I’m not looking for anything fancy tonight.
Staff: I see. Just for yourself?
Guest: No, with two of my friends. We’d like to eat and drink.
Staff: I see. What kind of cuisine do you have in mind?
Guest: I’d like to try some Japanese dishes.
Staff: In that case, you may want to try Warabi. It’s an izakaya, which is a casual Japanese-style restaurant with lots of drinks and many kinds of dishes. The menu has lots of photos and it’s inexpensive. It’s just 5 minutes away on foot. First, head towards the post office, then…

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What are some fine dining and casual dining restaurants near your hotel?
  • Which restaurants do you often recommend?
  • What details could you tell your guests about these restaurants?
