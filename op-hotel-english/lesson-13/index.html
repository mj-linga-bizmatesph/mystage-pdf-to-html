Recommending a place
to shop
13
BIZ TIP
Your guests might be interested in buying souvenirs. When recommending a place to shop, it’s a good idea to:
Ask what they are looking to buy
Ask if they've already been to a place
Give a description and highlight the good points
SEE

Example:

Guest: Good morning. Is there a place we can go shopping?
Staff: Good morning, Mr. Hyun. Yes. I recommend Ginza.
Guest: Oh, what’s in Ginza?
Staff: There are many expensive shops.
Guest: Hmm, well we’re not looking to spend that much money.
Staff: Oh, I see. But not all the shops are expensive.
Guest: Well, OK we’ll check it out. Thanks.
Staff: You’re welcome.

Bizmates style:

Staff: Good morning, Mr. Hyun. Did you have a good night’s sleep?
Guest: Yes, I did. Thank you. Is there a place we can go shopping?
Staff: Yes, there’re several nice shopping areas around here. Are you looking for something in particular?
Guest: Not really. Just window shopping.
Staff: Have you already been to Ginza?
Guest: No, we haven’t. But we’ve just heard of it.
Staff: Well, in that case, I would recommend Ginza. Ginza is more than just a shopping district. It’s a unique place where you can see both the traditional and trendy sides of Tokyo. Also, the famous Tsukiji fish market is within walking distance from there.
Guest: That’s sounds exciting. OK, so…

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What are some famous places to shop near your hotel?
  • What can you buy in these shopping areas?
  • How would you describe each of these areas?
  • What would you highlight for each of these areas?
