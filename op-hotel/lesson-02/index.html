Checking in
2
BIZ TIP
Make your guest feel important. Welcome your guest by:
Making small talk
Giving details of the room
Asking if there's anything else they need
SEE

Example:

Guest: Hi. I’d like to check in.
Staff: Hi! Welcome to La Grandia.

(Clerk performs check-in procedures)

Staff: Your room is now ready.
Guest: OK, thank you.

Bizmates style:

Guest: Hi. I’d like to check in.
Staff: Good afternoon! Welcome to La Grandia. Is this your first time to stay at our hotel, Mr. Wong?
Guest: Yes, it’s my first time here.
Staff: Thank you for choosing La Grandia. Was our hotel easy to find?
Guest: Yes. I got clear directions from the hotel staff over the phone.
Staff: I’m glad to hear that. Please wait a minute as I find your reservation. So, a double room for two people for 3 nights?
Guest: Yes, that’s right.
Staff: I’ve booked you in a room overlooking the garden. I’m sure you’ll enjoy the view. May I help you with anything else?
Guest: That would be all for now. Thanks again.
Staff: Enjoy your stay, Mr. Wong.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How comfortable are you in making small talk with your guests when they check in?
  • How would you make small talk if you were at the Front Desk?
  • What details could you tell them about their room?
