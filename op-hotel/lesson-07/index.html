Introducing the hotel’s
restaurant
7
BIZ TIP
It's always a good idea to introduce the hotel's restaurant. Make their visit more meaningful by:
Providing an overview of the dishes served
Emphasizing the restaurant's selling points
Making a recommendation
SEE

Example:

Staff: Good evening, Ms. Cruz. Going out for dinner?
Guest: Yes. I’m on my way to the hotel’s restaurant.
Staff: I see.
Guest: Do they serve ramen?
Staff: No, they don’t.

Bizmates style:

Staff: Good evening, Ms. Cruz. Going out for dinner?
Guest: Yes. I’m on my way to the hotel’s restaurant. Do they serve ramen?
Staff: Ramen is considered to be fast food in Japan. So, they don’t have ramen. However, they have a wide range of traditional Japanese dishes, like seafood, meat, and vegetarian dishes.
Guest: Oh, sounds delicious.
Staff: The seafood is brought in directly from the famous Tsukiji market each day, so the fish is very fresh. Also, the atmosphere is very nice with traditional Japanese music.
Guest: Wonderful! I wonder what to order.
Staff: The shrimp tempura is very popular. I highly recommend it. Enjoy your dinner, Ms. Cruz.
Guest: Thank you for the information.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What kind of dishes does your hotel restaurant serve?
  • Can you name some of your hotel’s restaurant’s selling points?
  • What dish would you recommend?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Introduce your hotel’s restaurant to a guest. Emphasize a few selling points and make a suggestion.
