OTHER PROGRAMS HOTEL ENGLISHILESSON 19
Informing your guests
about special events
19
BIZ TIP
Your guest may be interested in local events. Inform them of special events and make their stay more memorable by:
Starting the conversation
Providing details like date, time and venue of the event
Reminding them of other things they need to know
SEE

Example:

Staff: Good morning, Mr. Stevens.
Guest: Good morning. I noticed a lot of people are excited about something. What is going on?
Staff: Oh, it’s just the Fireworks Festival tonight.
Guest: I see. Well, you don’t seem excited.
Staff: Not really. I’ve seen it many times. It happens every summer.
Guest: Well, it doesn’t sound like a spectacular event. I guess I’ll just stay in my room tonight.

Bizmates style:

Staff: Good morning, Mr. Stevens. Will you go see the Fireworks Festival tonight?
Guest: Oh, so that’s what the excitement is about!
Staff: Yes, it’s held every summer and a lot of tourists travel to Japan just to see it.
Guest: Wow! Sounds like a spectacular event.
Staff: Yes, it is. Three countries including Japan compete in a fireworks competition. The fireworks are timed with music in the background. The show runs from 6PM to 8PM tonight.
Guest: Sounds very exciting!
Staff: The best viewing spot would be at the park across the street. I would recommend going there early before the crowd gets thicker. It’s also a good idea to bring your camera with you.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What will be the next special event near your hotel?
  • What details could you give to your guest?
  • What other useful information could you give your guest about the event?
