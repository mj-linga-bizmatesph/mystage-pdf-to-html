LET ME GIVE YOU SOME BACKGROUND INFORMATION.
BIZ TIP

Set the stage by giving the applicant as much information as you're allowed to. Be careful not to give too much information that can lead them to change their answers just to fit in. You can do so by:

  • Introducing the company
  • Explaining the reason for the job opening
  • Explaining the position needed
SEE

Examples

  • Introducing the company
  • We are a start-up company and have just started operating last June 2019.
  • So our company was founded in 1973 by Shin Fujita.
  • We are a manufacturing company with offices in…
  • Explaining the reason for the job opening
  • We are expanding overseas.
  • Our current IT Engineer will be retiring next month.
  • We have created a new department.
  • Explaining the position needed
  • We need someone who can communicate in English with our foreign colleagues.
  • We’re looking for someone who has mastery in the following skills…
  • The new department is looking into adding people to the team to work on the project.
TRY

Your trainer will be your applicant. Give him/her some background information by using the details below

  INTRODUCE YOUR COMPANY EXPLAIN THE REASONS FOR THE JOB OPENING EXPLAIN THE POSITION NEEDED
TRY 1 Your company name and its mission Your company is creating a new dept. Someone who can lead the new corporate sales dept.
TRY 2 Your company name and its history You need to replace someone who is leaving Someone who can coordinate with overseas offices
TRY 3 Your company name and its products Your company’s business is expanding Someone who can start a new business overseas
ACT

Imagine your trainer is your applicant. Give the applicant some background information by introducing your company, explaining the reason for the job opening, and explaining the position needed.
