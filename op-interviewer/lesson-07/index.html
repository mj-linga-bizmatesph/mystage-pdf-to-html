WHY DID YOU LEAVE YOUR PREVIOUS COMPANY?
BIZ TIP

Asking the applicant why they left their previous job or why they’re looking for a new one can help identify trends in the applicant's employment history. Knowing these can be a good basis if he/she would be a good fit in your company. You can use the following expressions to ask:

  • Why do you want to leave your current company?
  • What is your relationship like with your boss?
  • What would you change about your company?
SEE

Examples

  • Why do you want to leave your current company?
  • Why are you leaving your current company?
  • Why did you quit your last job?
  • What made you quit your job?
  • What is your relationship like with your boss?
  • Have you ever made your boss angry? What did you do?
  • Was there anyone you had difficulty working with?
  • Are you satisfied with the relationship you have with your colleagues?
  • What would you change about your company?
  • What would you do differently?
  • What is something that you don’t like in your company?
  • Is there anything that you’d like to improve in your company?
TRY

Your trainer is your applicant. Come up with follow-up questions for the applicant’s responses.

  INTERVIEWER’S QUESTION APPLICANT’S RESPONSE INTERVIEWER’S FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS
TRY 1 Why do you want to leave your company? I’m looking for a bigger challenge in my next job.  
TRY 2 What is your relationship with your boss? I think we have a good working relationship.  
TRY 3 What would you change about your company? I’d like a more open system to recognize top performers.  
ACT

Imagine your trainer is your applicant. Know why your applicant left his/her last company or is looking for a new job. Be sure to follow up on each of the applicant’s responses.
