TELL ME ABOUT WHEN YOU HAD TO…
BIZ TIP

Situational questions help bring out the applicant's personality and outlook. Questions can be about their achievements, failures, or decision-making, to name a few. Here is an example:

  • Tell me about a time when you had to make a big decision in your job.
  • What process did you follow?
  • What was the result of your decision?
SEE

Examples

  • Tell me about a time when you had to make a big decision in your job.
  • Tell me about a time when you had to work with a difficult colleague.
  • Tell me about time when you went above and beyond your work.
  • Tell me about a tough challenge you faced at work.
  • What process did you follow?
  • What actions did you take?
  • What did you do to solve the problem?
  • What were the steps you took?
  • What was the result of your decision?
  • What was the outcome of your actions?
  • Would you say that the problem was completely solved?
  • If you faced the same challenge, would you make the same decisions again?
TRY

Your trainer is your applicant. Come up with follow-up questions for the applicant’s responses.

  INTERVIEWER’S QUESTION APPLICANT’S RESPONSE INTERVIEWER’S FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS
TRY 1 Tell me about a time when you had to make a big decision. Leaving my first company was a tough decision for me.  
TRY 2 What process did you follow? Well, I consulted my family and friends.  
TRY 3 What was the result of your decision? I found a higher-paying job.  
ACT

Imagine your trainer is your applicant. Learn more about your applicant’s personality and outlook by asking about when he/she had to make a big decision and how he/she handled it. Be sure to follow up on each of the applicant’s responses.
