WHAT MOTIVATES YOU AT WORK?
BIZ TIP

Every company would want to have motivated employees on their team. So, it's important to know what motivates your applicant and if s/he can create a motivating work environment for the rest of the team. You can evaluate your applicant's motivation by asking these:

  • What motivates you in your work?
  • How has your motivation changed over the years?
  • What is the most important thing in your next job?
SEE

Examples

  • What motivates you in your job?
  • What drives you?
  • What makes you happy at work?
  • How do you stay eager to work?
  • How has your motivation changed over the years?
  • Was there a time when you felt demotivated?
  • Is it easy to stay motivated? Why or why not?
  • Was there a time when your motivation was challenged?
  • What is the most important thing in your next job?
  • What would be your deciding factor in your next job?
  • What are you looking for in your next job?
  • What are your expectations in your next job? In your next boss?
TRY

Your trainer is your applicant. Come up with follow-up questions for the applicant’s responses.

  INTERVIEWER’S QUESTION APPLICANT’S RESPONSE INTERVIEWER’S FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS
TRY 1 What motivates you in your job? Well, it’s motivating for me to be in an environment where I can learn new skills.  
TRY 2 How has your motivation changed over the years? It used to be about getting promoted, but now the work environment is more important.  
TRY 3 What is the most important thing in your next job? The most important thing is the relationship with my boss and colleagues.  
ACT

Imagine your trainer is your applicant. Learn more about your applicant’s motivation by asking what his/her motivation is, how it has changed, and what’s most important now. Be sure to follow up on each of the applicant’s responses.
