WHAT ARE YOUR CAREER GOALS?
BIZ TIP

It’s important to know your applicant’s long-term career goals. By knowing this, you can check if the applicant’s goals are consistent with your company’s goals.

  • What are your career goals?
  • How do you plan on achieving them?
  • What steps have you taken to achieve them?
SEE

Examples

  • What are your career goals?
  • Where would you like to be 3 years from now?
  • What are your long-term goals?
  • What do you hope to achieve someday?
  • How do you plan on achieving them?
  • What is your strategy?
  • How do you plan to get there?
  • What are your next steps to achieving your goals?
  • What steps have you taken to achieve them?
  • How much progress have you made towards your goals?
  • How close are you to achieving your goals?
  • What have you done so far?
TRY

Your trainer will be your applicant. Come up with follow-up questions for responses.

  INTERVIEWER’S QUESTION APPLICANT’S RESPONSE INTERVIEWER’S FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS
TRY 1 What are your career goals? My career goals are to lead my own development team and be an expert manager.  
TRY 2 How do you plan on achieving them? First, I want to learn by working under a very good manager and with good team members.  
TRY 3 What steps have you taken to achieve them? I took on more responsibility by mentoring new staff at my previous company.  
ACT

Imagine your trainer is your applicant. Learn more about your applicant’s career goals by asking what his/her goals are, how he/she plans to achieve them, and what steps he/she has taken. Be sure to follow up on each of the applicant’s responses.
