TELL ME ABOUT YOUR CURRENT WORK ENVIRONMENT.
BIZ TIP

Each company has its own culture. As an interviewer, you can check if the applicant is a good fit for your culture. You can do so by asking the following questions:

  • Tell me about your current work environment.
  • What type of work environment do you prefer?
  • What type of work environment do you dislike?
SEE

Examples

  • Tell me about your current work environment.
  • What is your current office environment like?
  • How would you describe your company culture now?
  • How is the atmosphere in your current company?
  • What type of work environment do you prefer?
  • What do you like about your current office environment?
  • Do you prefer to work alone or on a team?
  • Tell me your ideal work environment.
  • What type of work environment do you dislike?
  • What would you change about your current work environment?
  • Is there anything you don’t like about your current company culture?
  • What would you say is a bad work environment?
TRY

Your trainer will be your applicant. Come up with follow-up questions for responses.

  INTERVIEWER’S QUESTION APPLICANT’S RESPONSE INTERVIEWER’S FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS
TRY 1 Tell me about your current work environment. Everyone is young and friendly, so the office is loud and busy.  
TRY 2 What type of work environment do you prefer? Well, I’m getting older, so I prefer to work in a professional environment.  
TRY 3 What type of work environment do you dislike? I think I can adjust to most environments, but I dislike unclear rules.  
ACT

Imagine your trainer is your applicant. Learn more about the kind of work environment your applicant likes by asking about his/her current work environment, the kind of environment he/she prefers, and the type of environment he/she dislikes. Be sure to follow up on each of the applicant’s responses.
