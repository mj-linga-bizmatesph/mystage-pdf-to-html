WHAT IS YOUR EXPECTED SALARY?
BIZ TIP

Talking about salary is a sensitive topic, but as the interviewer, it’s important for you to know what the applicant’s expectations are. Ask your applicant his/her expected salary using these questions below:

  • How much are you earning now?
  • What is your expected salary?
  • The salary range for this position is from ___ to ___. Is this acceptable for you?
SEE

Examples

  • How much are you earning now?
  • May I ask what you earn now?
  • What is your current salary?
  • How much do you make now?
  • What is your expected salary?
  • How much do you hope to make?
  • What would you need to earn to take this job?
  • How much do you expect to get?
  • The salary range for this position is from ___ to ___. Is this acceptable for you?
  • The pay we were thinking for this position is between ___ and ___. Does that sound fair?
  • This post pays ____. Is that something you would consider?
  • The annual salary we have in mind is around ___. Would you still be interested in the post?
TRY

Your trainer will be your applicant. Come up with follow-up questions for the responses.

  INTERVIEWER’S QUESTION APPLICANT’S RESPONSE INTERVIEWER’S FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS
TRY 1 How much are you earning now? My last job paid me a monthly salary of xx.  
TRY 2 What is your expected salary? Well, I’d like to make 20% more than my last salary.  
TRY 3 The salary range for this position is from ___ to ___. Is this acceptable for you? Yes, I think that is fair for this position.  
ACT

Imagine your trainer is your applicant. Learn more about what your applicant’s expected salary is. Be sure to follow up on each of the applicant’s responses.
