DO YOU HAVE ANY QUESTIONS?
BIZ TIP

It’s always a good idea to give the applicant an opportunity to ask you questions at the end of the interview. This gives the applicant a chance to address any concerns and avoid any misunderstandings.

  • Do you have any questions?
  • That’s a good question. We…
  • Does that answer your question?
SEE

Example

  • Do you have any questions?
  • Please feel free to ask me anything.
  • I’d be happy to answer any questions now.
  • Is there anything you’d like to ask me?
  • That’s a good question. We…
  • Good question. We…
  • Oh yes. Let me explain that.
  • Right. OK, let me answer that for you.
  • Does that answer your question?
  • Does that make sense?
  • Are you OK with this?
  • Is this OK?
TRY

Your trainer will be your applicant. Come up with follow-up questions for the responses.

  INTERVIEWER’S QUESTION APPLICANT’S RESPONSE INTERVIEWER’S ANSWER
TRY 1 Do you have any questions? What is the office environment like in your company?  
TRY 2 Please feel free to ask me anything. Where is the company headed in the next 5 years?  
TRY 3 Is there anything you’d like to ask me? What do you like most about working for this company?  
ACT

Imagine your trainer is your applicant. Invite questions from the applicant and do your best to address any concerns.
