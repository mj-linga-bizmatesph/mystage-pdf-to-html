BREAKING THE ICE
BIZ TIP

It is important to create a good working relationship with your team to ensure a smooth project. You can do this right from the start by:

  • Spending adequate time for introductions
  • Setting the right mood with some small talk
  • Being friendly and professional
      • (Ken is having a teleconference with his foreign counterparts.)
      • Hello. I’m Ken from IMAYA Corp., Japan. It’s nice to meet you.
      • Hi, Ken. Nice to meet you too.
      • Pleased to meet you too, Ken.
      • Shall we now discuss the agenda?
      • (Ken is having a teleconference with his foreign counterparts.)
      • Thank you all for joining me today. I’m Ken, the manager of the IT division at IMAYA Corp., Japan. As this is our first meeting, let’s spend a few minutes getting to know each other. Let’s start with Joe.
      • Hi, I’m Joe, the IT Manager of IMAYA Corp., Thailand. Nice to meet you.
      • Thank you, Joe. So how’s the weather in Thailand?
      • Very hot as always!
      • I see. I hope you’re in an air conditioned room. All right, Eric?
      • Yes. I’m Eric, the head of IT at IMAYA Taiwan. It’s nice to meet you.
      • Thank you, Eric. How are things going in the Taiwan office?
      • So far, so good, although we’ve had issues with our server lately.
      • Right. Sorry to hear that. Anyway, let’s discuss our agenda for today.
    • Answer these questions.
      • What would you say at the beginning if you were to lead a teleconference?
      • What information would you ask each participant to give about themselves?
      • What small talk topics would you bring up to break the ice?
ACT

Role play.

Imagine a foreign customer is visiting your store. Welcome them in a friendly way, make small talk, and offer your help.
