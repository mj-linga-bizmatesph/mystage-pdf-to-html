INTRODUCING THE OBJECT AND AGENDA
BIZ TIP

A successful meeting starts with a clear objective and an agenda. To make sure your participants can follow, you should:

  • Prepare handouts that they can refer to
  • Get involvement as early as possible
  • Ask for confirmation after discussing the items on the agenda
      • The goal of this project is to select and implement a central Finance and Accounting system for all of IMAYA Corp’s global offices. For this meeting, we will discuss some of the local needs of our regional offices.
      • Oh, I wish you had let us known beforehand.
      • Yeah, I thought we were just going to have a kick-off meeting.
      • Sorry. Let me read the agenda now.
      • Before we begin, has everyone received the handouts I e-mailed last Friday?
      • Yes. Thanks, Ken.
      • As you know, the goal of this project is to select and implement a central F&A system for all of IMAYA Corp’s global offices. Joe, could you please read the meeting objective?
      • Sure. Our goal is to identify and address the local needs of our regional offices with regard to the F&A system.
      • Thanks, Joe. So for the next half an hour, we will list some of the local needs in each regional office. I hope everyone has gotten feedback from the other regions. Then we’ll have 15 minutes to prioritize each item, and finally we’ll wrap things up. Would anyone like to add anything to the agenda?
      • Ken. Can we first identify the F&A systems being used by each region?
      • Good point, Eric! OK, let’s add that to the agenda.
    • Answer these questions.
      • What are the typical objectives and agenda you have for your meetings?
      • What could you say to get involvement from participants?
      • What could you say to ask participants to add anything to the agenda?
ACT

Role play.

Go over your usual meeting objectives and agenda. Be sure to refer to the handout, get involvement, and ask for confirmation of the agenda.
