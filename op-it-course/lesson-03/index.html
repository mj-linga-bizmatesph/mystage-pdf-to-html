KEEPING THINGS UNDER CONTROL
BIZ TIP

Being the chairperson means you need to be in control of the meeting, but it doesn’t mean you have to dominate the discussion. You can do this by:

  • Being assertive when it’s time to move forward
  • Getting input from the participants by asking a direct question
  • Sticking to the agenda as the discussion progresses
      • OK, our first item is to identify the local needs of the regional offices. Some regions are using different F&A systems. Why is this happening?
      • Well, our current system is just too complex. The system has too many unnecessary features. I found an open-source system which is…
      • Joe, just a minute. Please let Eric speak too. What do you think, Eric?
      • About what?
      • OK, our first item is to identify the local needs of the regional offices. Some regions are using different F&A systems. Why is this happening?
      • Well, our current system is just too complex. The system has too many unnecessary features. I found an open-source system which is…
      • Joe, I think we’re steering off topic. Let’s get back to the agenda.
      • Oh sorry. Right, we should identify local needs first.
      • That’s all right. Eric, I know your region uses the same system as China. What are some of the local needs in those areas?
      • Well, our accounting standards in Taiwan and China are quite different from Japan. These differences forced us to adopt a different F&A system from a local vendor.
      • I see. Joe, I know that you use a different system in Thailand. Is that the same reason you moved to that system?
    • Answer these questions.
      • What can you say to get back in control when someone goes off topic during a meeting?
      • What can you say when the discussion has gone over the allotted time?
      • What can you say to encourage other participants to speak up?
ACT

Role play.

Come up with a typical agenda for your teleconference. Get some input from your participants and keep the meeting under control.
