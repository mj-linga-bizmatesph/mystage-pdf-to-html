MEETING A FOREIGN VENDOR
BIZ TIP

Good business starts with a good first impression. Make your foreign vendor feel comfortable by:

  • Introducing yourself in a professional yet friendly way
  • Showing appreciation for visiting your office
  • Making small talk
    • (Adam, an IT vendor from the US, is visiting Ken at IMAYA Corp.)
      • Good morning. I am Ken Yamada. I am the IT Manager at IMAYA Corporation. Nice to meet you.
      • Yes, we spoke over the phone. It’s nice to meet you, Mr. Yamada. I’m Adam Smith with TechSol.
      • This way please.
      • Good morning. You must be Mr. Adam Smith. I am Ken Yamada. It’s nice to finally meet you in person.
      • Yes, nice to finally meet you too, Mr. Yamada.
      • Please call me Ken. Thanks for making it all the way here. How was your flight?
      • It was very smooth, although it was about a 10-hour flight from LA. Good thing I got a window seat.
      • I see. I love sitting by the window too. Anyway, let’s go to the meeting room. We will have two other people from the IT Department joining us. We’re all very excited to learn more about this ERP system of yours.
      • Great. The pleasure is all mine.
    • Practice greeting your guest with these topics and answer the questions:
      • Appreciation:
        stormy weather, long trip, short notice, etc.
      • Compliment:
        suit, tie, glasses, appearance, etc.
      • Small talk:
        weather, news, business, etc.
      • Who might visit you at your office?
      • How would you introduce yourself to this person?
      • What topics for small talk would you talk about?
ACT

Role play.

Greet your foreign guest and make them feel comfortable by introducing yourself, showing appreciation, and making small talk.
