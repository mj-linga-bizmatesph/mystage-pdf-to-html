UNDERSTANDING THE OTHER SIDE
BIZ TIP

Active listening skills are important to understand the other side. Be an active listener by:

  • Clarifying if you don’t understand something
  • Rephrasing your questions when checking your understanding
  • Using relevant examples, if necessary
      • This system will give different access rights to concurrent users depending on their position within the company.
      • Ah… I see.
      • And this can all be controlled in real-time by a central administrator portal. Are you following me so far, Ken?
      • Sorry, what do you mean by concurrent users?
      • This system will give different access rights to concurrent users…
      • Sorry, Adam. Could you speak a little more slowly?
      • Sorry, Ken. This system will give different access rights to concurrent users depending on…
      • What do you mean by concurrent users?
      • These are people who have access to the ERP system.
      • So, do you mean the employees in our accounting department who would access the ERP?
      • Exactly. Your staff shouldn’t be able to access information intended for managers.
      • Right. Let me see if I understand you correctly. For example, in the F&A system, we can allow some people to access all information while limiting access to others. Is this correct?
      • Exactly.
    • Answer these questions.
      • What would you say to someone speaking too fast?
      • What would you say if you couldn’t understand something?
      • What would you say to confirm what the other person is saying?
ACT

Role play.

Imagine you are getting some tips on how to improve your English. If you can’t follow what your trainer is saying, ask to speak more slowly, ask to explain the meaning of a word, and confirm your understanding.
