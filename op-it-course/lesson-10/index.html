GETTING YOUR POINT ACROSS
BIZ TIP

As vendors try to wow you with their sales pitches, you need to be sure to explain what’s important to your company clearly. Get your point across by:

  • Explaining your company’s priorities
  • Being open and honest about how you feel
  • Explaining what is or isn’t negotiable
      • So, what do you think of our ERP system?
      • Hmm, I understand. Thank you.
      • If you have any concerns, I’d be happy to address them now.
      • I will contact you if I have any questions. Thank you.
      • So, what do you think of our ERP system?
      • Our priority is to deploy an ERP system across our global regions within a certain budget and timeframe. We have a pilot launch scheduled in 6 months.
      • I understand. And I’m confident that we can meet those requirements.
      • Honestly, I have two main concerns. One is that your ERP system is not specific to our industry. And two, the training schedule is just too long.
      • Well, our system is highly customizable, so we can make changes to meet your company’s needs.
      • I understand. However, we are looking for a system that matches our business.
      • Yes, but most of the customization is very simple.
      • Please understand this is something that is non-negotiable.
    • Answer these questions.
      • In what situations would you need to negotiate at work?
      • What would your priorities be?
      • What are some terms that are negotiable or non-negotiable?
ACT

Role play.

Imagine you are negotiating terms with a foreign vendor. Explain your company’s priorities, express how you feel, and explain what is negotiable or non-negotiable.
