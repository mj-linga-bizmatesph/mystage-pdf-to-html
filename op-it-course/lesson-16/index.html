16
VISITING AN OVERSEAS OFFICE
BIZ TIP

It’s a good idea to understand the business culture when visiting an overseas office the first time. Make a good impression by:

  • Greeting your foreign colleagues according to their culture
  • Making small talk about their country
  • Refraining from getting down to business right away
      • (At the Kuala Lumpur office.)
      • Ken, it’s nice to finally meet you.
      • Hello. Nice to meet you.
      • Welcome to Malaysia! I hope you had a nice flight.
      • Thank you. By the way, here are the documents containing the project details.
      • (At the Kuala Lumpur office)
      • Ken, it’s nice to finally meet you.
      • Amir. Yes, I’m so happy to finally meet you in person. (Ken gives a slight bow).
      • Welcome to Malaysia! I hope you had a nice flight.
      • Yes, I did. Thank you, Amir… Kuala Lumpur is such a beautiful city.
      • Is this your first time visit Malaysia?
      • Yes, it is. I’m looking forward to trying Malaysian food.
      • We’ll go to a nice local restaurant for lunch.
      • That’s great. Amir, I want to say thank you for piloting the new ERP system.
      • No problem. We’re all excited about it.
    • Answer these questions.
      • Which overseas office would you likely visit in the future?
      • How would you greet them?
      • What do you know about their local business culture?
ACT

Role play.

Imagine you’re visiting an overseas office. Greet your foreign counterpart and make small talk.
