17
SHOWING INTEREST AND COURTESY
BIZ TIP

Your foreign colleague might take you on a short tour of their office and introduce you to other people. Make a good impression by:

  • Greeting them according to their culture
  • Complimenting the good things you see around the office
  • Asking relevant and meaningful questions
      • Let’s go to the meeting room on the 7th floor.
      • OK.
      • Here we are. Down the hallway is the meeting room. But first, let me introduce you to Ms. Nadia Bahar, our finance manager.
      • Hi, I’m Ken Yamada (extends his hands for a handshake). Nice to meet you, Ms. Bahar.
      • (Brief pause)…Apologies, Mr. Yamada but in our culture, we’re not supposed to shake hands with the opposite gender.
      • Really? Sorry about that.
      • I noticed how the interior of the building has that touch of Malaysian culture. I think it’s impressive. How tall is this building, Amir?
      • It’s 40 floors. Yes it does reflect the local culture but also has the look of any IMAYA office.
      • I agree.
      • Here we are. Down the hallway is the meeting room. But first, let me introduce you to Ms. Nadia Bahar, our finance manager.
      • Hi, I’m Ken Yamada (bows slightly), IT manager of IMAYA Corp, Japan. Nice to meet you, Ms. Bahar.
      • Pleased to meet you too, Mr. Yamada. Please call me Nadia.
      • Thank you, Nadia. Please call me Ken.
    • Answer these questions.
      • Who might you be introduced to if you visit an overseas office?
      • What could you compliment if you visited an overseas office?
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your foreign counterpart is giving you a short tour of the office. Continue making small talk and ask relevant questions.
