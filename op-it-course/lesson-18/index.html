DEALING WITH RESISTANCE
BIZ TIP

Handling resistance can be tricky, especially in business. Make them understand where you’re coming from by:

  • Confirming their concerns
  • Showing you understand their situation
  • Developing a plan to address those concerns
      • As we’ve discussed, the Kuala Lumpur office shall be piloting IMAYA’s new ERP system.
      • Yes, but I don’t think it will make things easier for us. The system is complicated. And learning this new system requires training. I’m afraid time is something we don’t have for that.
      • I understand.
      • As we’ve discussed, the Kuala Lumpur office shall be piloting IMAYA’s new ERP system.
      • Yes, but I don’t think it will make things easier for us.
      • Do you mean that the new system will be more difficult to use than the current one?
      • Absolutely. We don’t have time for proper training.
      • Are you saying that you don’t have enough qualified trainers?
      • That’s right. Amir is the only one who can use this system right now. He tried training me last week, but I still can’t understand it.
      • I understand. You only have one person who can use the system, and with the launch date approaching, Amir won’t have enough time to properly train everyone in your department. Is this your main concern?
      • Exactly.
      • In that case, we will send an expert trainer from Japan. Could you…
    • Answer these questions.
      • What’s a big change that might happen in your company someday?
      • What are some reasons that people might resist to this change?
      • What could be done to address these concerns?
