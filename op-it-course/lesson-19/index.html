LAYING OUT AN ACTION PLAN
BIZ TIP

A good action plan is essential in project management. Before implementing a project, you should:

  • Set your goals clearly (S.M.A.R.T.)
  • Take responsibility
  • Check for further questions or concerns
      • OK, so once the software has been developed, training will start immediately.
      • How long is the pilot program going to last?
      • It’ll be for six months. Then, we’ll begin implementing the ERP system at other global offices.
      • Do we have any goals as far as the Kuala Lumpur office is concerned?
      • Let’s just do our best to familiarize ourselves with the new system.
      • OK, so once the software has been developed, our first step is to conduct training for a month in several groups so as not to completely halt operations. Next, we’ll officially launch the ERP here in the Kuala Lumpur office. It will run for six months.
      • What will happen during those six months?
      • We will monitor the efficiency of the ERP system and compare the result with last year’s performance. I would like to coordinate with you and Amir on this, so we’ll have a bi-monthly meeting to check our progress.
      • Sounds good. Will you be visiting us again?
      • Of course. I can arrange that. Is everyone clear with our upcoming actions?
    • Practice going over the sample requirements below with your vendor:
      • What kind of project might you be implementing soon?
      • How would you set the goals for that project?
      • What courses of action would you take to implement it?
ACT

Role play.

Discuss your action plan on a work-related project that will be implemented soon.
