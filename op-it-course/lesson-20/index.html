20
MAKING A GOOD FOLLOW-UP
BIZ TIP

Your follow-up e-mail shouldn’t just contain the usual “thank you” message. Add a personal touch by:

  • Briefly greeting your business partner
  • Going over the key points in bullet points
  • Adding a personal touch
      • To:
        Subject:
        A.Aziz@imaya.com.my; N.Bahar@imaya.com.my
        ERP system: Kuala Lumpur office
        Dear, Mr. Aziz and Ms. Bahar.

        Thank you for taking the time to go over the project details with me. It was a pleasure meeting
        both of you. As agreed, we will be doing a soft launch in 2 weeks.

        Please let me know if you have any questions during the pilot program.


        Regards,
        Ken Yamada


      • To:
        Subject:
        A.Aziz@imaya.com.my; N.Bahar@imaya.com.my
        ERP system: Kuala Lumpur office
        Hi, Amir and Nadia.

        really had a wonderful time during my visit to your office. Here’s a summary of what we’ve agreed on:
        • We will send 2 trainers from Japan to train your staff in April
        • We will meet every 2 months to monitor progress
        • Nadia will give me weekly updates by e-mail

        I’ve learned so much about the Malaysian office during my short visit and I hope that I can see
        everyone at the Kuala Lumpur office again.


        Regards,
        Ken
    • Answer these questions.
      • Who did you last send a follow-up e-mail to?
      • What key points did you mention?
      • How did you add a personal touch to your message?
