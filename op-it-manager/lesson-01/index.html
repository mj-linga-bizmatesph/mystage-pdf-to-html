OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 1
WELCOMING YOUR NEW STAFF
1
BIZ TIP
The first day at a new company for a foreign worker can be very stressful. He/she might feel nervous, confused, and just not know what to say or do. As the manager, you can provide comfort and guidance by orienting your staff. You can do this by:
Making your staff feel comfortable
Giving an overview of the schedule
Asking if there are any questions
SEE

The IT Manager, Yuki Tanaka, welcomes his new Software Developer, Sarah Anand.

Yuki: Hello, Sarah. Welcome to day one! How was your weekend?
Sarah: It was great. I couldn’t stop thinking about my first day. I’m very nervous.
Yuki: Well, don’t worry. We’re all excited to have you with us.
Sarah: OK, thank you.
Yuki: Let’s go over our schedule for today. In the morning, we’ll first set up your workstation and introduce you to our team. Then, we can take a quick tour of the office. And then, I’ll go over the company rules and the workflow of our department. After lunch, we can work on setting some goals together and answer any questions you have.
Sarah: OK, that sounds good.
Yuki: Great. Do you have any questions so far?
Sarah: No, everything is clear. I’ll start working on setting up my PC.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Do you have a special way of making your new team member feel relaxed?
  • What do you usually discuss with a new staff member on his/her first day of work?
  • Do you make small talk with new staff members? Why or why not?
  • As a manager, what else can you do to break any cultural barriers on Day 1?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Make him/her feel comfortable, give an overview of the schedule, and ask if he/she has any questions.
