INTRODUCING KEY PEOPLE
2
BIZ TIP
One reason that foreigners have difficulty working in a Japanese company is because they are unable to make personal connections with their colleagues. So, it is important to give your new staff member a chance to meet other members on the first day. Introduce your team by:
Giving the name and position
Explaining their responsibilities
Adding a personal touch
SEE
Yuki introduces Sarah to her team.
Yuki: Everyone, let me introduce our new Software Developer, Ms. Sarah Anand. She arrived from India last week and she’ll be joining us starting today.
Sarah: Hi! It’s nice to meet you all. Please call me Sarah.
Yuki: So, Sarah, here’s our IT team. This is Hiro, our Senior Developer. He’s mainly responsible for leading the development team for projects for our main service. He’s currently working on a new product launch. He’s also very good at shogi, which is like Japanese chess.
Sarah: Hello, Hiro-san. I’ve heard of shogi, but I’ve never tried. I hope I can learn to play.
Yuki: And this is Ayako, our Junior Developer. Ayako is on the same team as Hiro. She’s a Front-end Engineer, so she is mainly responsible for the UI and communicates directly with Sales and Marketing. Ayako joined our company only 3 months ago and speaks English very well. So maybe she can help you with any basic questions about setting up your workstation.
Sarah: Oh, great. I’m happy to work with you, Ayako.
Yuki: OK now, let me show you around the office. That room over there is…
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

1. Who are the members in your department? In your company? What are their roles?
2. What are their responsibilities?
3. Is there any personal information that you can share about them?
4. Do you think it’s necessary to introduce key people to a new employee? Why or why not?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Introduce members of your team by giving the name and position, explaining their responsibilities, and adding a personal touch.
