EXPLAINING THE JOB
4
BIZ TIP
How the role and job description are explained at the very beginning is extremely important to avoid any misunderstandings later on. As the manager, you can minimize misunderstandings in the future by:
Explaining his/her main responsibility
Discussing other roles
Giving an example of his/her task
SEE

Yuki discusses certain details about Sarah’s job.

Yuki: OK, let’s discuss your role as a Software Developer, Sarah. Your main responsibility is to work with your project team to develop software based on the specifications you get from the Project Manager. And your goal is to meet each milestone so that we can meet the launch date while keeping the highest level of quality.
Sarah: Yes, I understand that.
Yuki: However, you also have other roles. You should keep in mind what is best for the company, not just our department. For example, if you notice something missing in the specifications, it’s your job to report it to the PM.
Sarah: Got it.
Yuki: Also, as part of the IT team, your role is to support the other departments with IT-related issues. For instance, you might receive requests for data or get basic questions about our system. And it’s your job to assist them efficiently as possible. Is that clear?
Sarah: Yes, Yuki-san. I need to think about how to help the whole company, not just the work in front of me.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What’s the main responsibility of developers in your company?
  • What other roles are assigned to them?
  • What are some specific examples of their tasks?
  • What are some common misunderstandings that might occur with a foreign member on your team?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Explain his/her job by explaining his/her main responsibility, discussing other roles, and giving an example of his/her task.
