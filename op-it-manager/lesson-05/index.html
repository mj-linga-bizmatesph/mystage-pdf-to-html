OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 5
EXPLAINING HOUSE RULES
5
BIZ TIP
To some foreign workers, the office rules may be very different from what they are used to due to cultural differences. You can help them understand and follow the rules by:
Stating the rule
Explaining the reason for the rule
Explaining what could happen if the rule isn’t followed
SEE

Yuki explains some of the rules to their team.

Yuki: OK, Sarah. Now, I’d like to go over some house rules.
Sarah: Sure, Yuki-san.
Yuki: One important rule is “Never miss meetings.” We have daily stand-up meetings in our department and I want everyone to attend. Did you also have these meetings in your previous company in India?
Sarah: No, not on a daily basis.
Yuki: The reason we have daily meetings is because we want to make sure that everyone on the team is aware of the progress of each task. The progress of one task could affect progress of other tasks. So, if you miss one meeting, it could affect the progress of the entire project.
Sarah: I understand. OK, I’ll do my best to attend all our daily meetings.
Yuki: Great! Now, let’s move on to some other rules.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

1. What are some important rules in your department?
2. What rule are you very strict about as a manager?
3. Why do you have this rule?
4. What could happen if this rule isn’t followed?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Discuss one important rule in your company by stating the rule, explaining the reason, and explaining what could happen if the rule isn’t followed.
