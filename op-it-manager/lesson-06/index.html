INVITING YOUR STAFF OUT TO LUNCH
6
BIZ TIP
It’s very difficult for foreign workers to establish a personal connection with their colleagues right away. Inviting your staff out to lunch could help build this connection. Invite them casually by:
Checking if he/she has other plans
Giving some background information
Telling him/her what time you plan on leaving
SEE
Yuki and Sarah take a break from their orientation.
Yuki: OK, so before we take a break, do you have any questions, Sarah?
Sarah: None, Yuki-san. Everything is clear so far. Thanks.
Yuki: Alright, let’s take a break for now. Where are you having lunch?
Sarah: Actually, I’m not sure yet. Do you have any recommendations, Yuki-san?
Yuki: Well, there’s this great Thai restaurant nearby. Some of the IT members are going there for lunch. Would you like to join us?
Sarah: I’d love that. Thank you, Yuki-san.
Yuki: Not at all, Sarah. OK, let me just grab my things and call the team. We’ll meet you at the lobby in 5 minutes.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

1. Where do you usually have lunch?
2. Who do you usually go out with during lunch breaks?
3. What can you talk about with your new staff member during lunch?
4. Do you think it’s important to have lunch with your colleagues?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Invite him/her out to lunch by checking if he/she has other plans, giving some background information, and telling him/her what time you plan on leaving.
