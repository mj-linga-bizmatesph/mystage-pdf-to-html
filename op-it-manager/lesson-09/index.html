OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 9
HANDLING QUESTIONS
9
BIZ TIP
Giving your foreign staff the opportunity to ask questions allows you to know how well he/she understands everything. However, it’s very easy to misunderstand the meaning of the question. So, when your foreign staff asks you something, make sure you understand it clearly by:
Rephrasing the question
Providing the answer
Confirming if you've answered the question
SEE
Yuki is now having the Q&A session with Sarah.
Yuki: Do you have any questions for me, Sarah?
Sarah: Yes, Yuki-san. What’s our policy on overtime work?
Yuki: Do you mean will you be required to work after hours?
Sarah: Yes, that’s right.
Yuki: Well, as a general rule, we don’t want anyone to work overtime. We want everyone to finish their work within office hours. However, if we are behind schedule and we need to meet a deadline, yes you would be expected to work overtime. Does that answer your question?
Sarah: Yes, it does. Thank you. I would also like to know if I’m qualified to file leaves during my probationary period.
Yuki: Are you asking if you could take a day off during your probationary period?
Sarah: That’s right. I might need 1 day to go to process my visa renewal.
Yuki: If it’s something important like your visa, of course, you can take time to do that. However, if it’s a vacation, please wait until your probationary period ends. Is that clear?
Sarah: Oh yes. Thank you, Yuki-san.

TRY

Answer these questions.

1. What are some typical questions you might get after an orientation?
2. How would you rephrase these questions?
3. How would you answer these questions?
4. What would you say if you got a question you couldn’t answer?
