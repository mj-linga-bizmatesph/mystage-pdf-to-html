OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 12
ASSIGNING A TASK
12
BIZ TIP
When assigning tasks to your foreign staff, the instructions need to be very clear to make sure that there is no misunderstanding. You can do this by:
Sharing background information
Assigning the task
Explaining the next steps
SEE

Yuki assigns a new task to Sarah.

Yuki: Hi, Sarah. Let’s start our meeting. I’d like to talk to you about a new assignment.
Sarah: Sure, Yuki-san.
Yuki: Let me give you some background information on this project. We’ll be launching a new service at the end of September. And we need to put together the website, the payment site, and the operation system for the administrator and the client.
Sarah: Wow, so it’s a completely new service. This is very exciting.
Yuki: So, your task is to develop the administrator site with Taka.
Sarah: OK, that’s no problem.
Yuki: So, the first step is for you and Taka to meet with the admin team to help them put together a wireframe for the system. This may require several meetings to understand the specs. The admin people are not IT specialists, so you may need to ask questions to really understand what they need.
Sarah: I understand, Yuki-san. I’ll talk to Taka about this right away.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What assignment would you give to a new member of your team?
  • What background information would you need to share for this assignment?
  • What are the next steps for this assignment?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Assign a new task by sharing background information, assigning the task, and explaining the next steps.
