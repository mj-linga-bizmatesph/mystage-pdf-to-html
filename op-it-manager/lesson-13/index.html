OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 13
FOLLOWING UP ON A TASK
13
BIZ TIP
Foreign workers may not know what to expect in a progress meeting, so it’s a good idea to be open about the objective and process from the very start. You can do this by:
Explaining the objective and flow of the progress meeting
Asking for details about the progress
Making recommendations
SEE

Yuki has a follow-up meeting with Sarah.

Yuki: Hey, Sarah. OK, let’s start our meeting. The purpose of this progress meeting is just to get updates from you and to discuss any issues.
Sarah: I see.
Yuki: Let me go over the flow of our meeting. First, let’s go over your tasks and the status of each one. Then, we can talk about any issues. And finally, come up with action plans to resolve these issues.
Sarah: Sounds good. So, here are the list of tasks that I have for this project. The ones in green have been completed. The ones in red are still in progress.
Yuki: I see. One of your tasks seems to be behind schedule. Is everything OK?
Sarah: Well, there was some miscommunication with the PM, so I had to redo one of the codes.
Yuki: OK, let’s look at what we can do to avoid miscommunication with the PM.
Sarah: Yes, I’ve started...
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How would you start a progress meeting with your foreign staff?
  • What would you say to your foreign staff to explain the purpose and the flow of the meeting?
  • What are some possible issues that might occur for your foreign staff?
  • What solutions could you recommend to your foreign staff to deal with these issues?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Casually follow up on a task by explaining the objective and flow of the follow-up meeting, asking for details about the task, and then recommending some solutions.
