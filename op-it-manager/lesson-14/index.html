GIVING FEEDBACK
14
BIZ TIP
Feedback can be a very sensitive topic, especially to foreign workers. So, it needs to be communicated so that it will be well-received. You can give your foreign staff feedback effectively by:
Highlighting good points
Discussing areas for improvement with specific actions for improvement
Ending on a positive note
SEE

Yuki gives Sarah some feedback.

Sarah: Hi, Yuki-san. Do you have a minute? I’d like to get your feedback on the wireframe I created before I submit it to the team.
(Yuki spends a few minutes looking at the wireframe)
Yuki: Wow, well done on finishing it so quickly. Speed is important in this work, so good job.
Sarah: Thank you, Yuki-san.
Yuki: When you put this together, did you communicate directly with the end users on the admin team?
Sarah: No, I just spoke to the PM who communicated with the admin team.
Yuki: I see. I notice two missing features on page 6, such as the “NEXT” button and “Confirmation” page.
Sarah: I noticed that too, but they weren’t in the specifications, so I didn’t include them.
Yuki: OK. Here’s what I want you to do. First, tell the PM that you noticed some missing features and would like to confirm with the admin team. Then, confirm with the admin team manager. And finally, report back to the PM on the changes. Then, everyone will be clear about the changes. OK, so keep up the good speed.
Sarah: I understand. Thanks for the feedback, Yuki-san.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What’s a task that you might assign to a foreign staff?
  • What are some good points you could highlight about their performance?
  • What are some areas for improvement and specific action plans you could have for this assignment?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Give your staff some feedback by highlighting some good points, discussing areas for improvement and specific actions, and ending on a positive note.
