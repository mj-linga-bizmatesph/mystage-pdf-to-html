OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 15
BRINGING UP A CONCERN
15
BIZ TIP
Some foreign workers need time to get accustomed to the local work culture. So, when bringing up a concern, show them you understand their situation. You can do this by:
Starting with some background information
Asking for details
Coming up with a solution together
SEE
Yuki sits down with Sarah to talk about a concern.
Yuki: So, Sarah, how’s the project?
Sarah: So far so good, Yuki-san.
Yuki: I see. Actually, the reason I scheduled a meeting is because I’d like to discuss a concern raised by Hiro about your absence last week. Can you tell me what happened?
Sarah: Sure. I had the flu for a couple of days, so I missed a few meetings. But it’s not my fault. I have no control over these things.
Yuki: I understand. But Hiro needs to make sure that each member reports progress and communicates it to the team. Do you have any back up plans when you're absent?
Sarah: Well, I guess I could email him my progress, and he could update me when I get back.
Yuki: Right. So, that would allow the team to move forward without waiting for you to return.
Sarah: OK, that makes sense, Yuki-san. I’ll do that next time. Thanks for letting me know.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

1. What’s a concern that you might need to bring up with a foreign staff member? (missing deadlines, quality issues, etc.)
2. How would you get further details from your staff?
3. How would you come up with a solution together with your staff?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Bring up a concern by starting with some background information, gathering further details, and coming up with a solution together.
