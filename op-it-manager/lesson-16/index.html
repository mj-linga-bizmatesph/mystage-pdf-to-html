OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 16
MAKING A SUGGESTION
16
BIZ TIP
It might be difficult for some foreign workers to keep up with the efficiency or working style of their Japanese colleagues. As the manager, you could help them become more efficient. You can do this by:
Identifying the problem
Making your suggestion
Explaining the possible results
SEE

Yuki suggests an idea that could help Sarah perform better.

Yuki: Hey, Sarah! I noticed that some of your tasks are behind schedule. Is everything OK?
Sarah: Sorry about that, Yuki-san. I just don’t have enough time.
Yuki: I see. Sometimes it’s impossible to get everything done. So maybe the problem is that we just need to prioritize these tasks.
Sarah: So, I should do the important tasks first right?
Yuki: Well, you need to do the tasks that are important and urgent. So out of the important tasks, which one needs to be done first?
Sarah: I think it’s the report for Hiro.
Yuki: OK, let’s work on that now. So, by completing the important and urgent tasks first, your work will still be impactful. As for the less important and less urgent tasks, you can do them when you have time or maybe have others to help you with them.
Sarah: I see. So, I just need to learn to better prioritize. That makes sense. Thanks for your help.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

1. What is a common problem that a new foreign staff member might have?
2. What suggestion could you make to solve the problem?
3. What possible result could you get with this solution?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Offer some suggestions that could help him/her improve his/her working habits by identifying the problem, making a suggestion, then explaining the possible results.
