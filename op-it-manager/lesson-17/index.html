OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 17
GIVING A VERBAL WARNING
17
BIZ TIP
In order for your foreign staff to understand the seriousness of an issue, official warnings may need to be used to change their behavior. You can do this by:
Giving a detailed record of the incidents
Clearly communicating that this is an official warning
Explaining what will happen if improvements aren’t made
SEE

Yuki gives Sarah her first verbal warning.

Yuki: Sarah, I’d like to talk to you about your being late yesterday.
Sarah: Sorry about that, Yuki-san.
Yuki: Sarah, this is my second time to talk to you about punctuality in the last 2 months. You have been late to work 3 times on the following days. We talked about this on June 23rd and again today.
Sarah: It won’t happen again.
Yuki: I understand that being late for work sometimes can’t be helped. However, this has been a recurring issue. Your punctuality affects not only your performance but the team’s progress as well. So, this time, I’m issuing you an official verbal warning.
Sarah: …
Yuki: Another incident of lateness will lead to a written warning, which could lead to contract termination as you are in your probationary period.
Sarah: Oh, I’m so sorry, Yuki-san. I won’t let you down.
Yuki: I know that you can do this, Sarah. I’m counting on you.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What are some reasons that a staff member might be given a warning for?
  • What details would you need to communicate to the staff when issuing an official warning?
  • What would happen if improvements aren’t made?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member and he/she has been arriving late for work in the past few weeks. Issue a verbal warning by giving a detailed record of the incidents, clearly communicating that this is an official warning, and explaining what will happen if improvements aren’t made.
