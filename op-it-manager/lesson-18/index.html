OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 18
COUNSELING YOUR STAFF
18
BIZ TIP
Working in a different country can be pretty stressful due to cultural differences, language barrier, and having no one to talk to. Give your staff support by:
Showing that you understand their situation
Getting more details from them
Offering some advice
SEE

Yuki calls on Sarah as she looks a bit down.

Yuki: Sarah, I noticed you don’t look so happy lately. Is everything OK?
Sarah: Not really. I feel like I’m slowing down the project.
Yuki: Why do you think that?
Sarah: Well, I can’t understand our Japanese meetings, and I work slower than the others.
Yuki: I know it’s hard to adjust to a new culture, a different language, and different rules and processes. But no one expects you to understand the Japanese meetings. What have you been doing to improve in these areas?
Sarah: Well, I used to ask questions during the meeting, but I stopped doing that because the PM seemed a bit annoyed. Also, Kenji told me I had to work faster.
Yuki: Well, have you been communicating with Ayako? She speaks English so she can help you.
Sarah: Not really. I know she’s busy too, so I don’t want to bother her.
Yuki: OK, let me speak to the PM about this. Maybe we can assign Ayako to be your official buddy. So, rather than asking her for help each time, you can set aside some time each day to understand what’s happening.
Sarah: Thanks for understanding my situation, Yuki-san. It means a lot.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What do you think may be causes of stress for a foreign worker in your company?
  • What questions could you ask to get more information about their situation?
  • What advice could you offer to support them?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Listen to your staff’s concerns and then give him/her support by showing understanding, getting more details, and giving advice.
