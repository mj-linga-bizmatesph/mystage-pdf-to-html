OTHER PROGRAMS IT MANAGERILESSON 19
GIVING RECOGNITION
19
BIZ TIP
One common complaint of foreigners working in Japan is the lack of recognition they receive for their work. So, when you have the opportunity, give recognition by:
Openly congratulating your staff's efforts
Pointing out what he/she did well
Explaining the impact of his/her efforts to the business
SEE

Yuki congratulates the team for successfully completing their projects.

Yuki: OK, team! Huddle up! I am pleased to say that we have successfully launched our new system on time. Congratulations, everyone!
(Everyone celebrates with a loud applause)
Yuki: I also would like to take this opportunity to commend Sarah for her work with the front-end development. Although we were behind schedule at one point, Sarah’s quick work allowed us to catch up to still meet the deadline. Because of everyone’s efforts, we can maximize sales of our new service.
Sarah: Thank you, Yuki-san. I couldn’t have done it without everyone’s support.
Yuki: I’m glad you were able to sort out the recurring issue in the past. Your new attitude towards your job will greatly help us complete more projects next year and inspire the team even more. Please keep up the good work, Sarah.
Sarah: Will do. Thank you, Yuki-san.
Yuki: All right! Now, let’s also congratulate Taka for being…

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What is a typical reason to congratulate your staff?
  • What would you say to congratulate your staff?
  • What would you say to point out details of what he/she did well?
  • What would you say to explain the impact of his/her efforts on the business?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your trainer is a new team member. Congratulate your staff for a job well done by openly congratulating him/her, pointing out what he/she did well, and explaining the impact of his/her efforts on the business.
