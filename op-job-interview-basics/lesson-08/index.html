QUESTIONS ABOUT YOUR EXPERIENCE

BIZ TIP
When talking about your experience, highlight your strengths and the positive impact it has had on your company by using the SAR (Situation, Action, Result) approach:
I was expected to…
It was established in…by…
In order to…I…
In the end, I was able to…
SEE

Angela Ortiz wants to know more about Ken’s professional background…

Angela: What would you say is your biggest accomplishment?
Ken: At my previous job, I was expected to increase sales. In order to achieve my targets I created custom proposals, developed client relationships, and spent a lot of time researching the market. In the end, I was able to increase sales by 20% and win the President’s award for performance excellence.
Angela: That’s excellent. Thank you. I’d like to hear more about…

Ex. I was expected to transfer to our New York office. In order to prepare myself I studied English everyday and made opportunities to meet New Yorkers to practice. In the end, I was able to adapt quickly to life in America and be successful at my post.

Answer the following additional questions:

  1. How did you improve a bad situation?
  2. Tell me about a time when you had to make a split-second decision.
  3. I’d like to hear about a time you made a mistake.
