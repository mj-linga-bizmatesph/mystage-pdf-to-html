BIZ TIP
When answering behavioral questions, use the PAR (problem, action, result) format to add details to your answer and make the most impact.
Problem: I was/We were facing a problem with…
Action: To solve this issue I…
Result: As a result…
SEE

Angela Ortiz, the HR Director, is asking Ken about how he handled past situations…

Angela: Can you tell me about a time when you were under pressure?
Ken: A few years ago we were facing a problem with one of our project managers. Everything was behind schedule and he quit. To solve this issue I took over his position, met with the employees and created a new plan. As a result, we finished the project successfully and on schedule.
Angela: Excellent. Thank you.
Ken: My pleasure.

TRY

Answer the following question using the PAR format:
Can you tell me about a time you improved a difficult situation?

PROBLEM ACTION RESULT
Team work and communication Organized weekly meetings and monthly social gatherings Team atmosphere and communication improved

  2. Tell me about a time when you had to adapt to a tough environment.
