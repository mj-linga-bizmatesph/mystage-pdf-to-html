MAKING A GOOD FIRST IMPRESSION
BIZ TIP

Making a good first impression is essential in a job interview. Statistics show that people tend to make up their minds about the other person in the first couple of minutes and spend the rest of the time justifying their assumptions. And it takes more than just arriving on time and dressing professionally to make a good first impression.

SEE

A Typical Response:

Interviewer Hello. Thank you for waiting.
Interviewee Not at all.

Bizmates Style:

Interviewer Hello. Thank you for waiting.
  • Don’t wait for the interviewer; offer a firm handshake and make eye contact.
  • Give a compliment and initiate small talk by asking questions.
  • Find a connection and build up rapport.
Interviewee

Not at all. It’s a pleasure to meet you. My name’s Ken Tanaka.

Thank you for taking the time to meet with me. And thank you for e-mailing the map to the office. It was very helpful.

Your office is in a very convenient location. I saw many nice restaurants along the way. Do you usually go for lunch around here?

Oh yes, I saw that restaurant on my way here. I love American-style hamburgers, too. Have you tried the burger shop in…?

TRY

Practice making a good impression with the topics below. Introduce yourself, give a compliment, and initiate small talk. Then try to find a connection and build rapport.

TOPIC GIVE A COMPLIMENT INITIATE SMALL TALK
The location of the office and its surroundings
The décor of the office
The interviewer
