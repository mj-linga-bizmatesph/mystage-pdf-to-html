UNDERSTANDING THE MOTIVE OF THE QUESTIONS
BIZ TIP

The first step to answering a question well is to understand it. However, oftentimes, the motive of the questions may not be so clear. Therefore, it’s important to understand the motive of the questions before answering them.

SEE

A Typical Response:

Interviewer Why did you apply for this job?
Interviewee Well, my current job requires me to work lots of overtime. And the office is very far, so I’m looking for a place that’s closer to work. Also, the salary you are offering is a lot higher.

Bizmates Style:

Interviewer Why did you apply for this job?
Motive: How serious are you about working for our company?
How to Answer: Show you are serious by highlighting your knowledge of the industry and company and by showing that this position fits in with your long-term career plans.
Interviewee

Well, your company is the leader in the industry, always innovating new products, such as the XE2. I would take pride in working for the best. Up to now, I’ve worked for mostly small-to-medium sized companies where I oversaw the entire marketing department. Now, I feel I’m ready to apply my marketing skills on a much bigger scale.

TRY

Study the questions, motives, and advice on how to answer. Then prepare your own answers.

QUESTION MOTIVE HOW TO ANSWER
Tell me about yourself. How do your work experience and ambitions relate to this job? Show how your experience and personality make you the right person for this job.
What did you like or dislike about your former boss? How well will you get along with your new boss? Show you are adaptable and easy to work with.
What are your plans in the next 5 years in this company? Is this a long-term or short-term job for you? Show how this job fits in with your long-term career plan.
