8
HIGHLIGHTING YOUR SELLING POINTS
BIZ TIP

One of the most effective ways to make a positive impact on the interviewer is to maintain consistency in your answers by focusing on three selling points that you have to offer. Keep these three selling points in mind and look for opportunities to reinforce them.

SEE

A Typical Response:

Interviewer : What motivates you in your work?
Interviewee : I’m motivated by a company’s vision. A company with a strong vision that I believe in is a strong motivation for me.

Bizmates Style:

Interviewer : What motivates you in your work?
Selling points: communication skills, marketing know-how, results-driven
How to Answer: Show how the work itself is a motivator and how your selling points make you right for the position.
Interviewee :

I’m motivated by results and the people around me. That’s why I like my job as a marketer. I get to communicate with other departments, and I can apply my marketing know-how and see the actual results of my efforts.

TRY

Identify your three selling points and show how you can reinforce them in your answers to these questions.

QUESTION MOTIVE HOW TO ANSWER
Why do you want to change jobs? What might make you leave our company? Show you want more opportunities to utilize your 3 selling points and how you can do this in this job.
Are you a team player? Can you work alone, and will you be able to collaborate with other colleagues? Show how your 3 selling points give you the flexibility to work both independently and on a team.
Do you handle pressure well? Will you be able to handle the pressure in this job? Share a high-pressure situation you faced and how you overcame it because of your 3 selling points.
A question you might be asked
ACT

Role play

Imagine you are having an interview. Practice answering these questions in your mock interview.
