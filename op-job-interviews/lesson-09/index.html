9
USING OPENING SENTENCES
BIZ TIP

Sometimes it’s difficult to come up with a good answer right away. So you may need a few seconds to put your thoughts together. One way to do this is to use an opening sentence when giving your answer.

SEE

A Typical Response:

Interviewer If you could be any animal, what would you be and why?
Interviewee Huh? Animal? Hmm…

Bizmates Style:

Interviewer If you could be any animal, what would you be and why?

Sometimes a curve ball question is asked to see how you react under pressure. Don’t panic. Give yourself a bit of time with an opening sentence to buy some time and give an honest answer.

Interviewee

Hmm… Well, to be honest, I’ve never thought of that before. However, I think it would be nice to be a…

TRY

Practice answering these questions by using opening sentences to buy time.

QUESTION OPENING SENTENCES YOUR ANSWER
What do you think our company could do differently?
  • Wow, that’s a tough question. I’ll have to think about this for a while.
  • That’s an interesting question. Let me think about the best way to answer this for you.
  • I have a few good examples, but maybe the one that is most relevant is…
  • I’m not sure if this is the answer you are looking for, but let me share my experience.
How would your boss describe you?
Can you tell me about a time you disagreed with a decision made by your company?
ACT

Role play

Imagine you are having an interview. Practice answering these questions in your mock interview. Use the opening sentences if you need to buy time.
