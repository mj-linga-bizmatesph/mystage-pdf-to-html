BIZ TIP
A welcome speech is your chance to make a good impression on your team. You can answer three questions for your staff:
Who are you?
What do you think of them?
What are your expectations?
SEE
Example
Taka:
Good morning, everyone. My name is Taka Suzuki, and I am your new manager. I have lots of experience in MMB Japan, so I can teach you about many things. My expectation is to be number 1. So I hope you can help me achieve my dream. Thank you.
Bizmates style
Taka:
Good morning, everyone. I’d like just a few minutes to introduce myself. My name is Taka Suzuki, but please call me Taka. I joined MMB Japan in 1998 and have worked in both QA and Sales. I’m married and have two children. I love golf, so I hope I have a chance to play some rounds with you.

This is my first time to work overseas, so I’m very excited to have this opportunity. I’ve heard that we have an excellent team with several experienced salespeople with lots of knowledge and new reps with great ideas.

For the first week, I’d like to have one-on-one meetings with everyone to hear your ideas. And then, I hope together we can develop a strategy to help us become the best sales team in the country. I look forward to doing great work with everyone. Thank you.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What are some good habits for making a speech?
  2. What work experience can you mention about yourself?
  3. What personal information can you share about yourself?
  4. What can you say about your team?
  5. What can you say about your expectations?
  6. What could you say to close your presentation?
▼▼▼
ACT

Give a short welcome speech to your new team.
