GETTING TO KNOW YOUR TEAM
2
BIZ TIP
Once you get to know your staff, it’ll be much easier for you to carry out your responsibility as a manager. Get to know them by:
Starting a personal conversation
Sharing information about yourself
Asking a question to continue the conversation
SEE
Example
Taka: How are you today?
Mick: I’m fine, thanks. How about you?
Taka: Good thanks.
Bizmates style
Taka: Hi, Mick. Do you have any plans for the weekend?
Mick: Well, I was thinking of taking my son to the park.
Taka: How old is your son?
Mick: He just turned five last month. Do you have any kids?
Taka: Yes, I have a 10-year old son and a 12-year old daughter. They don’t want to play with their dad anymore. Is there a park near your home?
Mick: Yeah, it’s like a 5-minute walk.
Taka: Oh, you’re lucky to have a park close by. There’re very few parks in Tokyo. Is it a big park?
Mick: Not really, but it has a playground, which my son loves.
Taka: That’s great. I hope you have a great time with your son.
Mick: Thanks, Taka.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. How close is your relationship with your staff?
  2. What do you do to get to know them better?
  3. What are the merits and demerits of having a close relationship with your staff?
  4. Think of questions you could ask to start a conversation on these topics: current news, a hobby, a book, hometown, work experience.
  5. What information can you share about yourself?
▼▼▼
ACT

Imagine that you are with one of your staff at the office. Get to know your staff by starting a personal conversation, sharing information about yourself, and asking a question to continue the conversation.
