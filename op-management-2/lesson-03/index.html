Lesson 3: Getting their input
3
BIZ TIP
A manager’s greatest resource of information is his or her staff. Get valuable input from them by:
Casually asking about their tasks
Pressing for details to better understand their situation
Offering assistance if applicable
SEE
Example
Taka: Hi, Mick. What are you doing?
Mick: Ah, well,.. I’m creating a proposal for a client.
Taka: I see. You should include sample feedback from our satisfied customers.
Mick: Yes, I know.
Bizmates style
Taka: Hi, Mick. What’s that you’re working on?
Mick: I’m creating a proposal for a client.
Taka: I see. So who’s the client?
Mick: It’s a major automaker. It could become a big contract.
Taka: Great. So what’s the proposal?
Mick: Well, I’ve just made a few changes to our standard package, but I want to make it a little more special.
Taka: And how are you going to do that?
Mick: I’d like to add in some client reviews from other automakers, but this is the first time for us to work with one.
Taka: Well, in Japan we have contracts with several major automakers. I could ask our Japan sales office for reviews if you’d like.
Mick: Yeah, that’ll be great. Thanks a lot, Taka.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. How well do you know your staff’s work?
  2. What valuable information can you get from your staff?
  3. Do you sometimes casually ask your staff about their work?
  4. What are some questions you can casually ask about your staff’s tasks?
  5. What type of questions could you ask to press for details?
  6. What assistance could you offer?
▼▼▼
ACT

Imagine that you are with one of your staff at the office. Casually ask about his or her work, press for details, and then offer assistance if applicable.
