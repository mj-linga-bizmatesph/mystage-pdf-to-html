SCHEDULING A ONE-ON-ONE BY E-MAIL
BIZ TIP

As there is no body language in e-mails, it’s very easy for the tone to be misinterpreted. So be sure to show appreciation and understanding by:

  • Saying thank you
  • Saying why you are thanking them
  • Showing understanding of their situation
SEE

Example

Dear everyone,

I will have one-on-one meetings with you. Please tell me your schedule.

Best regards,
Taka

Bizmates Style

Hi everyone,

Thank you for the warm welcome you’ve given me so far. I’d like to schedule our one-on-one meetings starting next Monday.

Here are the details:

  • Select a 15-minute slot between 9AM-6PM
  • Prepare a short update of your current projects and your week’s to-do list

I know it’s a busy time, but could you please send me your preferred schedule by Apr. 21?

Looking forward to seeing everyone.

Regards,
Taka

TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. Do you carefully consider the tone of your e-mail before you send it?
  2. Has the tone of your e-mail ever been misunderstood by someone?
  3. What are some reasons you can thank your staff for?
  4. What are some things you can write to show you understand their situation?
ACT

Imagine that you are writing an e-mail to your staff to schedule a one-on-one meeting.
