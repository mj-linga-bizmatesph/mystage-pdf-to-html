Setting expectations
5
BIZ TIP
Meeting with a new manager in a one-on-one meeting could be intimidating to many people. Let them know what to expect right from the beginning by:
Creating a positive atmosphere
Making the purpose clear
Going over the agenda
SEE
Example
Taka: OK, Mick. Please come in. So tell me about your work.
Mick: Ah, did I do anything wrong?
Taka: No, no.
Bizmates style
Taka: Hi, Mick. Please come in. Please make yourself comfortable. How’s everything going?
Mick: Good, thanks.
Taka: Good. Since this is our first time to have a one-on-one, let me go over the purpose and the flow of these meetings.
Mick: Yes, that’ll be great.
Taka: Every Monday, I’d like to have a 15-minute meeting to know your workweek and to see if I can give any support and feedback.
Mick: I see.
Taka: So, just 5 minutes for updates on your current projects, 5 minutes for your week’s to-do list, and 5 minutes for comments and questions.
Mick: Great.
Taka: And please use these meetings to bring up any concerns. Let’s make it our culture to deal with issues before they become serious.
Mick: Sounds great.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What do you think are the merits and demerits of having short one-on-one meetings with your staff?
  2. If you were to have one-on-one meetings, what would the purpose be?
  3. If you were to have one-on-one meetings, what would the agenda be?
  4. How could you create a positive atmosphere?
▼▼▼
ACT

Imagine that you are having your first one-on-one meeting with your staff who is very nervous. Create a positive atmosphere, make the purpose clear, and go over agenda.
