MAKING A WELCOME
BIZ TIP

Active listening is more than listening carefully. It’s a two-way process. Be an active listener by:

  • Acknowledging that you understand
  • Pressing for details to get the real meaning
  • Clarifying to avoid misunderstandings
SEE

Example

Taka:
So, tell me about your projects.
Mick:
My biggest project now is organizing the Global Management event.
Taka:
…
Mick:
We’re expecting over 100 HR people to attend. My team’s responsible for coordinating the event… Is this OK?
Taka:
Yes, I’m listening.

Bizmates style

Taka:
So, tell me about your projects.
Mick:
My biggest project now is organizing the Global Management event.
Taka:
Yes, I’ve heard about that.
Mick:
We’re expecting over 100 HR people to attend. My team’s responsible for coordinating the event.
Taka:
What exactly is your team doing?
Mick:
My team’s creating invitations, assigning guest speakers, and gathering attendees.
Taka:
You mean this is all done in-house by your team?
Mick:
Yes, that’s right. We print all the invitations and even send them out.
Taka:
Wow, so how much of your time does this take?
Mick:
A lot. We have weekly meetings to discuss…
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What can you do to acknowledge that you are listening?
  2. Come up with several questions you can ask to press for details for these responses:
    1. “This task is taking up a lot of my time.”
    2. “We’re so understaffed right now.”
    3. “We’re so understaffed right now.”
  3. What can you say to clarify, to avoid misunderstandings?
ACT

Imagine that you are asking your staff about his or her main tasks. Be an active listener by acknowledging, pressing for details, and clarifying.
