7
SETTING GOALS
BIZ TIP

Effective goals give your team direction and motivation in their work. Set meaningful goals with your staff by:

  • Connecting individual goals to company goals
  • Setting goals that are S.M.A.R.T. (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
  • Involving the employee
SEE

Example

Taka:
So, I have a goal for you. Please get appointments with new clients.
Mick:
OK, and how do I do that?
Taka:
Please make more cold calls.
Mick:
OK, I guess I can do that.
Taka:
Great. I think this is a good goal.

Bizmates style

Taka:
OK, so let’s set goals for the rest of the year. One area our company is focusing on now is getting new clients.
Mick:
I see. OK, I’ll do my best to get more appointments.
Taka:
And how will you do this? (SPECIFIC)
Mick:
Well, I’ll spend an extra 30 minutes each day making cold calls.
Taka:
How many appointments can you get? (MEASURABLE)
Mick:
Currently, I’m averaging 7.3 appointments a week. I’ll aim for 8 appointments a week.
Taka:
How about 9 a week? Is that still achievable? (ACHIEVABLE)
Mick:
9? Yeah, I think I can do that.
Taka:
Excellent. And are you sure this will impact sales? (RELEVANT)
Mick:
Oh yes. More appointments should lead to more contacts.
Taka:
Great. And when’s the deadline? (TIME-BOUND)
Mick:
I guess by the end of next quarter.
Taka:
OK, so let’s put all that together. So to increase sales, you’ll spend 30 minutes each day making cold calls to average 9 appointments each week for the next three months.
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. Change the following goals to make them SMART.
    1. I will improve my English.
    2. I will save money.
    3. I will improve my health.
  2. What are your SMART goals as a manager?
