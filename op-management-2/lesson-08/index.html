OTHER PROGRAMS MANAGEMENT 2ILESSON 8
Getting commitment
8
BIZ TIP
The manager could have the biggest impact on an employee’s level of commitment. Get commitment from your team by:
Confirming the goals
Giving some motivating words
Asking for commitment
SEE
Example
Taka: OK, that’s all for our meeting.
Mick: That’s it?
Taka: Yes. Thank you for your time.
Bizmates style
Taka: OK, so before we end our meeting, let’s confirm the goals we’ve set.
Mick: Sure, so to increase appointments, I’ll spend 30 minutes each day making cold calls to average 9 appointments each week for the next three months. To increase sales, I’ll…
Taka: Perfect. Do you have any questions about them?
Mick: No, everything is clear.
Taka: Great. I just want to say thank you for being so open and honest with me. You’ve really helped me get a better understanding of how things are done here.
Mick: It’s my pleasure, Taka.
Taka: I know we have really high targets this year, but I think if we all work together, we will be able to achieve them. Can I count on you to help us do this?
Mick: Of course, Taka. I’ll do my best.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What do you do to motivate your staff?
  2. Do you think all employees have the same level of commitment to their goals and company? Why or why not?
  3. Describe three of your staff and explain their level of commitment to the company.
  4. Would your words of motivation differ depending on which employee you were speaking to?
▼▼▼
ACT

Imagine that you are ending a meeting with your staff. Confirm the goals you’ve set together, give some motivating words, and then ask for commitment.
