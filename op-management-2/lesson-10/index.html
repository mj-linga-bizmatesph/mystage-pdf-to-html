PART 1

Talk about the following topics.

  1. Lesson 1: MAKING A WELCOME SPEECH

    Give a short welcome speech to your new team.

  2. Lesson 2: GETTING TO KNOW YOUR STAFF

    Imagine that you are with one of your staff at the office. Get to know your staff by starting a personal conversation, sharing information about yourself, and asking a question to continue the conversation.

  3. Lesson 3: GETTING THEIR INPUT

    Imagine that you are with one of your staff at the office. Casually ask about his or her work, press for details, and then offer assistance if applicable.

  4. Lesson 4: SCHEDULING A ONE-ON-ONE BY E-MAIL

    Imagine that you are writing an e-mail to your staff to schedule a one-on-one meeting.

  5. Lesson 5: SETTING EXPECTATIONS

    Imagine that you are having your first one-on-one meeting with your staff who is very nervous. Create a positive atmosphere, make the purpose clear, and go over agenda.

  6. Lesson 6: LISTENING ACTIVELY

    Imagine that you are asking your staff about his or her main tasks. Be an active listener by acknowledging, pressing for details, and clarifying.

  7. Lesson 7: SETTING GOALS

    Imagine that you are meeting with your staff to set goals.

  8. Lesson 8: GETTING COMMITMENT

    Imagine that you are ending a meeting with your staff. Confirm the goals youâ€™ve set together, give some motivating words, and then ask for commitment.

  9. Lesson 9: FOLLOWING UP BY E-MAIL

    Imagine that you are writing an e-mail to follow up on your staff after a meeting.
