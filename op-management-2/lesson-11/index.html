Getting your meeting started
11
BIZ TIP
An important step to having effective meetings is for all attendees to know what is expected from them. Make this clear from the start by:
Going over any ground rules
Assigning roles such as a minute taker and timekeeper
Clarifying the purpose and agenda
SEE
Example
Taka: OK, let’s start our meeting. Mick, can you start?
Mick: Ah, OK. So, shall I talk about the project schedule?
Taka: No, please talk about the tasks for the project first.
Mick: Ah, sorry. I wasn’t prepared for that.
Bizmates style
Taka: Hi, everyone. Before we start, I’d like to go over three simple ground rules. First, let’s start and finish on time. Second, no electronic devices during meetings please. Third, please stick to the agenda.
Mick: Sure, Taka.
Rob: Sure.
Taka: Steve, could you be the timekeeper?
Steve: No problem.
Taka: Great, thanks. The objective for this meeting is to set up a project plan for our new system. And let me go over the agenda. Steve, you have 20 minutes to help us list all the tasks. Then, Rob will spend 15 minutes for role assignment. And then Mick, you…
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What ground rules would you set for meetings with your team?
  2. What would you say to assign a minute taker and timekeeper?
  3. State the purpose and go over the agenda for the following situation:
Objective: to identify new sales channels to increase sales
1. Review current sales channels – 15 min. (Sophia)
2. Brainstorm other possible channels – 20 min. (James)
3. Prioritize potential channels – 15 min. (Amber)
4. Summary and questions – 10 min. (You)
▼▼▼
ACT

Imagine that you are having your first meeting with your new team. Go over the ground rules, assign roles, and clarify the purpose and agenda.
