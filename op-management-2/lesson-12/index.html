GETTING EVERYONE TO PARTICIPATE
BIZ TIP

An effective meeting is when all attendees participate. Encourage everyone to speak up by:

  • Directing a question at one person rather than everyone
  • Mentioning the person’s area of expertise or experience
  • Keeping the question specific rather than too general
SEE

Example

Taka:
So what are some issues we might face during implementation?
All:
…
Taka:
Anyone? Mick? What do you think?
Mick:
Hmm, I’m not sure.

Bizmates style

Taka:
So, I want to make sure that we have a back-up plan for any possible issues that we might face during implementation. Mick, you worked with the IT department on the last project. What technical problems did you encounter?
Mick:
Yes, we had many bugs after the system was implemented. So it would be a good idea to have extra time for testing and retesting.
Taka:
That’s a good point. Steve, you work closely with the operators. What is their biggest concern with the new system?
Steve:
Well, many of them are concerned that the new system will be difficult to use.
Taka:
I see.
TRY

Answer these questions.

Think of three people you work with in your company and list their area of expertise or unique experience and a few relevant questions that you could ask them.

STAFF EXPERTISE or EXPERIENCE QUESTION
Taka
  • Had a recent business trip to Taiwan
  • How do the people at the Taiwan office feel about the new…?
     
     
     
ACT

Imagine that you are leading a meeting with your team. Get everyone to participate.
