13
FACILITATING
BIZ TIP

Being a good facilitator allows others to speak while you keep the discussion moving forward. Facilitate your meetings by:

  • Showing that you understand what’s been said
  • Leading the staff to a solution
  • Putting what your staff said into perspective
SEE

Example

Taka:
So Mick, please tell us more about the concerns from the operators.
Mick:
Well, the operators are afraid that the new system will be difficult to use like the current system.
Taka:
OK, no problem. We’ll do our best to make it user-friendly.
Mick:
Right.

Bizmates style

Taka:
So Mick, please tell us more about the concerns from the operators.
Mick:
Well, the operators are afraid that the new system will be difficult to use like the current system.
Taka:
So, the operators want a simple and easy-to-use system?
Mick:
Right.
Taka:
So, what can we do to address their concerns?
Mick:
Well, we need to make user-friendliness a top psriority when developing the system.
Taka:
And how can we do that?
Mick:
Well, we can get some of the operators involved in the development of the system.
Taka:
So, by having key operators help us design the user interface, we can ensure that the system will be more user-friendly.
Mick:
Exactly.
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What are common workplace problems that your staff has brought to your attention?
  2. What did you do to solve these issues?
  3. Did the solution come from the staff or from someone else?
ACT

Imagine that your staff has brought up the concerns below to you. Use facilitation to lead the discussion to reach a solution.

  1. A colleague is always late with his work and it slows down my work.
  2. There are too many changes in the workplace.
  3. I have too much work.
