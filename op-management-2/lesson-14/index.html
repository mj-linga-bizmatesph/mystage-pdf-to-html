QUESTIONING
BIZ TIP

Open-ended questions can be used to get lots of information while closed questions can be used to get specific information. Here are some examples:

  • Open-ended questions
  • Could you give us some background…?
  • Can you tell us about…?
  • Closed questions
  • What exactly did you announce?
  • Was the announcement…?
  • Who did the e-mail come from?
SEE

Example

Taka:
So Mick, in your last project, did you make an announcement about the system?
Mick:
Announcement? To whom?
Taka:
To everyone… when you implemented the system.
Mick:
Oh… Yes, we did.

Bizmates style

Taka:
Could you give us some background information on the last project?
Mick:
Yes, the project was very similar to this one. We had to implement a new system for the accounting department.
Taka:
Can you tell us about the implementation plan?
Mick:
Sure, we followed a 4-step process of announcing, training, following up, and revising.
Taka:
What exactly did you announce?
Mick:
We gave background information of the project and the training schedule.
Taka:
Was the announcement made by e-mail or in-person?
Mick:
Both. First, we announced it in-person to our managers. And then, we sent out an e-mail to everyone.
Taka:
Who did the e-mail come…?
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. Come up with three open-ended questions relevant to your work.
  2. Come up with three closed questions for each of the open-ended questions.
  3. Come up with some open-ended questions about studying English that you can ask your trainer. Use these phrases to help you:
    1. Could you tell me about…?
    2. What are some ways to…?
    3. What steps did you take to…?
ACT

Have a discussion with your trainer using the questions above. Mix open-ended and closed questions.
