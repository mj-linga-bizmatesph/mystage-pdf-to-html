Sending the minutes by e-mail
15
BIZ TIP
The KISS rule or Keep It Short and Simple allows the reader to spend as little time as possible to read and respond to e-mails. To keep it short and simple:
Use bullet points
Use lists rather than long text
Avoid unnecessary pair words (discuss and decide)
SEE
Example

Hi, everyone.

I would like to thank you for your active participation in the project meeting on May 25. We have discussed and decided on three meeting ground rules which we will follow in all our meetings from now on. The first rule is to start and to finish the meeting on time. The second rule is that we cannot use electronic devices in the meeting. And the third rule is that we should always follow the agenda. We also decided the main concerns which we will focus on. The first one is…

Bizmates style

Hi, everyone.

Thank you for your active participation in the project meeting on May 25. Here are the key points:

Decisions:

  • 3 meeting rules: 1. start/finish on time, 2. no electronic devices, 3. follow agenda
  • Main concerns: UI, tight schedule, training for operators

Actions:

  • Select 2 operators to join project team by Jun. 1 by Mick
  • Revise schedule by Jun. 2 by Sam

See you in our meeting on Jun. 3 at 2:00pm.

Regards,

TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. Give an example of a purpose of one of your meetings at work.
  2. Give examples of decisions you might make in a meeting with your team.
  3. Give examples of actions you might set in a meeting with your team.
▼▼▼
ACT

Using the above information, write an e-mail with the meeting minutes to your team.
