DELEGATING WORK TO YOUR STAFF
BIZ TIP

When delegating work to your staff, explain the goal, not the task. And let your staff figure it out. However, ensure there is no confusion by:

  • Explaining the “real” objective of the assignment
  • Explaining any guidelines (deadline, budget, limitations, rules, etc.)
  • Asking your staff what actions he or she will take
SEE

Example

Taka:
Mick, I’d like you to make a product comparison report on TechSoft’s new product.
Mick:
Sure. OK, so what do you want me to do?
Taka:
First go to their website and get information on pricing, features, etc. And then, please speak to some of your existing clients to…

Bizmates style

Taka:
Mick, I’d like you to make a product comparison report on TechSoft’s new product.
Mick:
Sure. What do you want me to do?
Taka:
You see, TechSoft’s new product is very similar to our model X34. I want to see how our customers view TechSoft’s product compared to ours and what actions we should take.
Mick:
I see.
Taka:
I need the report by next Wednesday. Also please do not contact TechSoft directly. I’d like you to be discreet.
Mick:
No problem.
Taka:
So how do you plan to do your research?
Mick:
Well, first I’ll check TechSoft’s website for product information, pricing, and customer feedback. I’ll also check some blogs…
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What’s a typical assignment that you might give to your staff?
  2. What’s the real objective of the assignment?
  3. What background information would you need to give your staff when giving the assignment?
  4. What actions would you expect your staff to take to complete the tasks?
ACT

Imagine that you are assigning a task to your staff. State the objective clearly, explain any guidelines, and ask your staff what actions he or she will take.
