BIZ TIP

Feedback is only valuable if the recipient is willing to listen and to change his or her ways. Have your staff be open to constructive criticism by:

  • Highlighting something positive about their work
  • Giving specific feedback for improvement
  • Closing with something positive:
SEE

Example

Taka:
Mick, I had a chance to review your report.
Mick:
Great. What do you think?
Taka:
This is not enough information. And this chart is too confusing.
Mick:
Oh, I’m sorry.

Bizmates style

Taka:
Mick, I had a chance to review your report.
Mick:
Great. What do you think?
Taka:
You did a good job in describing TechSoft’s product.
Mick:
Thanks for saying so.
Taka:
However, I need to determine what action we can take.
Mick:
Right.
Taka:
Can you find out more about their discounts? How much discount do they give? How many orders are needed for it? And so on.
Mick:
I see. I think I can speak to one of my contacts about this.
Taka:
Great. Also, about this chart. If you place the headings at the top and use bullet points for the details, it’ll be easier to read.
Mick:
OK, let me change that.
Taka:
Thank you. I can tell you worked very hard on this. There’s a lot of good information in the report. Thanks for your update.
Mick:
No problem. Thank you for the feedback.
TRY

Come up with tasks you might delegate to your staff. And then, list up positive feedback and constructive criticism you might give about their work.

Task Positive Constructive
     
     
     
ACT

Imagine that you are giving constructive criticism to your staff about a task you’ve assigned to him or her.
