TAKING A STEP BACK
1
SEE

Read the article.

Oftentimes we get so busy trying to run our day-to-day business. In a fast-paced business setting, people around us aren’t slowing down, so why should we? But sooner or later, we’ll realize we’re no longer moving forward

Too much work can take its toll. Take a step back from your work once in a while. It could give you the opportunity to re-assess the issues you are facing and even come up with new ideas. Sure, you have lots of work to do. But when you’re mentally drained, you can’t really accomplish that much, can you? Pause for a moment to unwind or to get a broader perspective of the situation. Spend some time alone or just chat with someone. The idea is to condition your mind and start out fresh again by taking a step back.

There are many ways to momentarily take your mind off work, but you may start with the tips below:

  • Drop what you’re doing – take a break and try to clear your mind off your work
  • Find someone to talk to – talk to someone about your problem, or just have a friendly chitchat. Ideas sometimes come from unlikely topics
  • Compose yourself – focus on the issue once more and draw your solution from the information you got during your short break

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Is taking a step back a common concept in your country?
  • Do you prefer to be alone or talk with another person when taking a step back?
  • Who would you talk to when taking a step back?
  • How much time do you need to take a step back?
  • Can you add any other tips on what to do when taking a step back?
  • Can you think of a situation at work where you felt you weren’t making progress in something? What did you do to overcome it?

ACT

Role play

Talk to your business partner about a huge challenge you’re currently facing. Then review the information you got during the conversation to come up with a possible solution.
