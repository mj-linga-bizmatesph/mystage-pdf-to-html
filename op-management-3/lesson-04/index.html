SETTING GOALS THAT PEOPLE UNDERSTAND
4
SEE

Read the article.

Goals give us a sense of direction of who or what we want to be. When goals involve other people, it becomes vital for these goals to be comprehensible to everyone involved. And when this happens, great things can be accomplished.

However, it isn’t enough to just know what your goals are. A truly effective goal includes what, when, how, and by whom. For example, let’s compare two managers: Manager A wants to increase his team’s efficiency. On the other hand, Manager B wants to increase his team’s efficiency by 10% in the next three months by completing the easy tasks first to make room for the difficult ones later. Both have the same goal, but Manager B has set the team’s objectives clearly.

Whether it’s personal or business, the “SMART” way of structuring a clear goal should be:

  • Specific – what exactly will we do?
  • Measurable – how will we track it?
  • Attainable – is this really possible?
  • Relevant – is this really important?
  • Time-bound – when is the deadline?
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Are you familiar with the SMART approach in setting goals?
  • What’s your main goal as a manager?
  • What sort of goals do you have for your team?
  • What sort of personal goals are you aiming for?
  • How would you use the SMART approach to make these goals clear?
  • How would you convey your goals at work to your staff?

Use the SMART approach to set a goal for your job, career development, and personal life.
