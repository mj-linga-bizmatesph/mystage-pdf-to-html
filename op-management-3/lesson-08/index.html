HONING YOUR SKILLS

SEE

Read the article.

Any carpenter would tell you that having a well-sharpened tool is essential to successful woodworking. He needs to keep his saw or chisel sharp. Otherwise, his work would be nothing more than just glorified firewood.

Honing your skills is a continual process. The only way to do your job effectively and efficiently is by making sure your skills can keep up with the tasks. However, it’s not just about perfecting your competencies but also acquiring new ones relevant to what you need to do. You can learn every skill out there all you want but the question is: Do you really need to learn all of them? Sure, they may come in handy in the future, but you would’ve already spent a considerable time learning things that are trivial. So prioritize shaping the skills that your job requires. And you can start by knowing your duties as a manager like the back of your hand.

Here are some tips to guide you in honing your skills as a manager:

  • Identify your responsibilities – focus on your main responsibilities first
  • Understand the tools needed – know the skills needed to perform the job effectively
  • Gauge your skill level – evaluate your strengths and weaknesses
  • Take action – do something about the areas you need to improve on
