RESOLVING CONFLICT QUICKLY

SEE

Read the article.

Your team is composed of people with different skills, experiences and viewpoints. When managed effectively, this kind of diversity can help you surpass all expectations.

However, such diversity can also lead to conflicts that could break the harmony within the team and impair performance. Sure, some are just petty quarrels that can be ignored, but keep in mind that these could end up in a dispute. When this happens, you need to extinguish the flame before it even breaks out. But conflict isn’t all that bad. In fact, it’s a good opportunity for your team members to understand and appreciate one another’s viewpoints or actions. It just depends on how you mediate the conflict.

The steps below can help you and your team resolve conflict:

  • Agree to resolve the conflict – everyone involved should acknowledge the conflict and agree to cooperate in resolving it
  • Analyze the situation – understand what caused the conflict and separate facts from beliefs
  • Reach an agreement – decide a course of action as a team
  • Prevention over cure – use the experience to avoid similar conflicts from happening in the future
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How diverse is your team? (skills, experiences, culture, etc.)
  • Do you consider this diversity their strength or weakness? Explain.
  • When is conflict good? When is it bad?
  • What do you usually do whenever small conflicts arise in your team?
  • Tell me about a time you had to resolve a team conflict.
  • What could be the worst-case scenario if you ignore a potential team conflict?

ACT

Role play.

Imagine your most senior employee walked out of the meeting because your smartest worker commented on the former’s idea. What would you do?
