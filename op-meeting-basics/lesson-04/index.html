4
E-MAILING THE PARTICIPANTS
BIZ TIP

Keep your e-mails short, simple, and easy to understand by using the 5-sentence rule:

  • A pleasantry: I hope everyone is having a ...
  • Reason for writing: I'm writing to ...
  • Summary of main points or bullet points: Date...Time...Place...
  • Call to action: Please...by...
  • Closing: have a great day...
SEE

Ken is e-mailing all participants the details of the meeting.

Hi, everyone.

I hope you're having a great week.

I am writing to give the details of our next meeting.

  • Date: Thursday, July 7th
  • Time: 10:00am - 12:30pm
  • Place: Meeting Room 15
  • Topic: Decide next project's concept and timeline
  • Agenda: See attached file


If you'd like to add to the agenda, please let me know by tomorrow morning. Have a great day, everyone.

Cheers,
Ken
TRY

Edit and improve the following e-mail using the 5-sentence rule.

Hi, everyone.

Hello everyone! hope you had a nice weekend! The weather was really beautiful! I went out with my family to the park that you suggested, Ken. My kids really enjoyed the petting zoo nearby. Did everyone get my last message? I sent it last Tuesday. I want to confirm the number of people that will be attending our company's seminar. It's this Friday for about an hour from 1:00pm. It's about improving writing skills so if you have the time, I recommend you join! We can't decide the place yet because I don't have a solid number. So, let me know if you can come or not ASAP. Anyway, have a great day, everyone! See you on Friday, hopefully!

Cheers,
Joanne

ACT

Write an e-mail using the 5-sentence rule to the participants of your next meeting in your company.
