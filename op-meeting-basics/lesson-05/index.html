PART 1

Review the Biz tips with your trainer and make sure you are comfortable with the expressions and concepts.

Telling your partner why you'd like to meet is the first step to scheduling your meeting. Do so effectively by using the following expressions:

  • Sorry to bother you. Do you have a minute?
  • I'd like to set up a meeting to discuss...
  • We need to think of some...

Matching your schedule with your counterpart can be challenging. Use the following expressions to help you:

  • Would you be available...from...to...?
  • Are you free...at...?
  • Does...at...work for you?

Prepare for your meeting by creating the agenda. Use these expressions below:

  • Is there anything you'd like to add to the agenda?
  • How much time do you need?
  • Do you need anyone to prepare anything?

Keep your e-mails short, simple, and easy to understand by using the 5-sentence rule:

  • A pleasantry: I hope everyone is having a ...
  • Reason for writing: I'm writing to ...
  • Summary of main points or bullet points: Date...Time...Place...
  • Call to action: Please...by...
  • Closing: Have a great day...
