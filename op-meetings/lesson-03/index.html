BIZ TIP
To make sure that everything flows smoothly, follow these steps when starting a meeting:
  • Explain the purpose
  • Assign roles (it’s a good idea to get the individual’s approval first)
  • Go over the agenda
SEE
Makoto:
All right, let’s get started. Does everyone have a copy of the agenda?
Liz:
Sorry, I didn’t print out a copy.
Makoto:
I’ve got an extra copy. Here you go. OK, the purpose of this meeting is to kick off our new global project. Mia has agreed to take the minutes for us. Thank you, Mia.
Mia:
No problem.
Makoto:
And Liz, could you be the timekeeper?
Liz:
Sure.
Makoto:
Great. We’ve got 6 items on the agenda. First, we have 5 minutes for introductions. Then, I’ll spend 10 minutes explaining the project objective.After that, Mia will explain our role assignments for the project for 15 minutes and Liz will talk about our schedule for 15 minutes. Then, Taro wants 10 minutes to finalize our budget. That’ll leave us with 5 minutes for questions.If everyone is OK with this agenda, I’d like to get started.
Everyone
Yes, that’s fine.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What did Makoto say to get everyone’s attention?
  2. What did Makoto say to check if everyone had a copy of the agenda?
  3. What is the purpose of the meeting?
  4. What roles did Makoto assign?
  5. What did he do after assigning the roles?
  6. How do you start meetings in your company?
