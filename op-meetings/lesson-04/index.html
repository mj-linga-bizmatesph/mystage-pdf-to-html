GETTING INVOLVEMENT
4
BIZ TIP
Don’t let one or two people dominate the meeting. Get involvement from the quiet participants by following the steps below:
  • Call their attention
  • Mention their area of expertise or experience
  • Direct a specific, rather than general, question at them
SEE
Makoto:
As you know, our marketing department has identified a potential market in the area of customized wiring. Our goal is to research its potential and then to create standard procedures to handle requests for customization.
Mia:
But we already offer customized wiring.
Makoto:
Yes, but up to now, our customization options have been limited because we haven’t had a standard pricing scheme or ordering procedures. Taro, you work in the sales department. How often do you get requests for customization from your clients?
Taro:
Ah, recently it’s become more common. I think over 20% of the requests are for customized wiring.
Makoto:
Wow, that’s quite a lot. Yoshi, as an engineer, what difficulties do you have when you get these requests?
Yoshi:
Well, we often don’t get all the specs from the sales reps, so we have to confirm with them several times. This takes a lot of time and work.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What did Makoto do to make it easier for Taro and Yoshi to give their opinions?
  2. Why do you think this approach made them feel comfortable in sharing their opinions?
  3. Are there people who aren’t comfortable expressing their opinions in meetings in your company?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play

Ask your trainer some questions by noting his/her area of expertise and then asking an open question.
