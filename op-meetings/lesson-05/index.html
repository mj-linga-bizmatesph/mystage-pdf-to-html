STATING YOUR OPINION CLEARLY
5
BIZ TIP
Sometimes it’s necessary to interrupt a discussion so you can state your opinion. You can do this naturally by:
  • Showing agreement or disagreement
  • Stating your opinion and the reasons behind it
  • Providing evidence to support your opinion
SEE
Mia:
OK, now that we’ve identified that requests for customization are becoming more common, we need to figure out a way to coordinate these requests to the engineers.
Yoshi:
Well,…
Liz:
Yeah, there should be a system that would flawlessly relay those requests.
Yoshi:
But I…
Liz:
Maybe we could come up with a customization request form that lists all possible kinds of customization.
Yoshi:
That’s a good idea, but in my opinion, it would be best for an engineer to accompany the sales rep. This would reduce customer complaints about incorrect specifications. According to my research, we received over 30 complaints last month alone.
Mia:
It’ll be too costly to send an engineer to the client.
Yoshi:
I don’t see it that way. In my view, it’ll actually save money. Mainly because engineers spend so much time in internal meetings trying to understand the clients’ requests. In fact, engineers spend an average of 12.8 hours just to understand the requests.
Mia:
Wow, I didn’t realize that.
▼▼▼
TRY

Practice expressing your opinion, using these expressions.

Interrupting:

That’s a good point, but… | I see what you mean, but…

Opinion:

In my opinion,… | In my view,… | From my perspective….

Reasons:

This would | The reason is… | Mainly because…

Evidence:

In fact,… | According to my research,… | Based on my knowledge,…

▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Have a debate with your trainer and state your opinion on one of the following topics. Be sure to interrupt, state your opinion, give reasons, and provide evidence.
  1. Living in the city vs. living in the countryside
  2. Working in your country vs. working overseas
  3. Working for a start-up vs. working for a multi-national company
