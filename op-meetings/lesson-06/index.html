OTHER PROGRAMS MEETINGSILESSON 6
MAKING A DECISION
6
BIZ TIP
Before making a decision, decide on the decision-making process. Here are some factors to consider:
  • Initial costs and recurring costs
  • Short-term and long-term impact
  • Short-term and long-term risks
SEE
Liz:
All right. We’ve looked at all our options. Shall we vote? I’m for the idea of offering semi-customized options.
Mia:
You got my vote. What about you, Yoshi?
Yoshi:
Yes, you can count me in.
Makoto:
Just a minute. Maybe we should look a little more carefully at how we make our decision.
Liz:
OK, what do you suggest?
Makoto:
Well, let’s first decide the factors that we’re going to base our decision on.
Liz:
Do you mean like costs?
Makoto:
Yes, what are the initial costs and are there any recurring costs for each option?
Liz:
Right. Let’s see, for option 1…
Makoto:
And do we have forecasts for short-term and long-term impact for each option?
Liz:
OK, we can put together forecasts for those.
Makoto:
And let’s not forget the risks involved.
Liz:
OK, we can collect these data first, and then make our final decision.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. Do you think voting is a risky method of making an important decision?
  2. Why is it important to consider other factors first?
  3. How are final decisions made at your company?

ACT

Role play

Imagine you’re trying to decide whether to enter a new foreign market or not. What factors would you need to consider? How much weight would you place on each factor?
