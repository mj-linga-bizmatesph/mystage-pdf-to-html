OTHER PROGRAMS MEETINGSILESSON 7
TAKING CONTROL
7
BIZ TIP
As chairperson, it is your responsibility to keep the meeting on track. Take control of the meeting by:
  • Being assertive
  • Immediately stopping a potential irrelevant topic
  • Referring back to the agenda
SEE
Makoto:
So we all agree that a semi-customized solution is the best option?
Liz:
You bet. I’m exhausted. So Mia, where are you going for your vacation?
Makoto:
Liz, we’re on a really tight schedule. Could we all please focus on the meeting for now?
Liz:
Yes, sorry. I’m listening.
Makoto:
OK, so we all agree with a semi-customized solution?
Mia:
Yes. I wonder if HR has released the new office seating assignment?
Makoto:
I think we’re steering off topic. Let’s stick to the agenda, OK?
Mia:
Sure.
Yoshi:
(Yoshi is looking at his smart phone.)
Makoto:
Yoshi, what’s your opinion?
Yoshi:
Oh sorry. Yes, I agree. So, shall we discuss the implementation plan?
Makoto:
No, we're running short on time, so let's move on to the next topic. We can add that to the agenda for the next meeting.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. How often do meetings in your company steer off topic?
  2. What do the participants usually talk about?
  3. How does the chairperson handle this kind of situation?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play

Imagine that you are having an important meeting with your colleagues. How would you react to the following situations:
  1. Two of the attendees are chatting with each other.
  2. You’ve run out of time allotted for a topic on the agenda.
  3. An attendee is writing an e-mail on her PC.
  4. An attendee begins to talk about something not on the agenda.
  5. An attendee is criticizing another attendee’s idea.
  6. The minute taker doesn’t seem to be writing something important that was said during the meeting.
  7. The timekeeper doesn’t seem to be keeping an eye on the time.
  8. An attendee keeps interrupting another attendee who is trying to speak.
