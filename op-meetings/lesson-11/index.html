DIFFERENT TYPES OF MEETINGS
11
BIZ TIP
Different types of meetings call for different structures. Your approach will have to change depending on factors such as:
  • The purpose or goal of the meeting
  • The number and type of participants
  • The amount of time for the meeting
  • The frequency of the meeting (regular vs. irregular)
SEE
Chris:
Congratulations on your promotion, Rei! Are you excited about holding your first meeting with your team?
Rei:
Yes, I’m preparing for it now, but I’m not sure how I should structure the meeting. What’s the proper flow of a meeting?
Chris:
Well, that depends on what you want to achieve.
Rei:
Achieve? Nothing really. It’s just a meeting.
Chris:
Every time you hold a meeting, you take up valuable time of a lot of people including yours. So, it’s important to have a clear purpose or goal and to structure it accordingly.
Rei:
I see. I never thought of it like that.
Chris:
And the type of meeting you hold should depend on what you want to achieve or in other words, the goal.
Rei:
So, the goal determines the type of meeting I’ll hold.
Chris:
Exactly. You could have a brainstorming session, a project meeting, a problem-solving meeting, and many more.
Rei:
Is there anything else I need to consider?
Chris:
Sure, you need to consider the number of and type of participants you have. You also need to think about the amount of time you have for the meeting and whether this will be a recurring meeting or not. All these will determine the structure of your meeting.
Rei:
Wow, I still have a lot to learn.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. What type of meeting is the most common in your office?
  2. What’s the purpose of this type of meeting?
  3. Who are the usual participants?
  4. What goals do you wish to accomplish at the end?
