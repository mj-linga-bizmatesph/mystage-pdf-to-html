PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

The first step in the negotiation process is preparation. To make sure you are prepared, answer these 5 questions:

Who are they?
Where will we meet?
When is the deadline to decide?
Why are we negotiating?
How do we present?

Gathering information is only the first step of the preparation process. Also, make sure you understand your situation by:

Knowing your objective
Knowing your bottom line
Knowing your options

The tone of your business e-mail should be professional and friendly. Use the following phrases to invite your counterpart to your office for a meeting:

I hope you had a wonderful weekend.
I’d like to set up a meeting to discuss…
Could we meet at our office...?

Just like your e-mail, your tone should sound professional and friendly on the phone. You may use the expressions below to confirm details and add extra information.

I just wanted to confirm our schedule for Monday.
I've booked a room at our office. Would you be able to come to our office?
I can e-mail you a map to our office.
