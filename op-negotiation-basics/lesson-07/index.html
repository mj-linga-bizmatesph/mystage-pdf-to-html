SETTING GOALS
7
BIZ TIP
Make sure your counterpart understands the purpose of your meeting and the agenda. Use the following expressions to help you set your goals:
I'm hoping that we could decide on...
I've prepared a simple agenda for today.
Is there anything you'd like to add?
SEE
Ken OK, so at the end of this meeting, I’m hoping we could decide on the start date of the project. I’ve prepared a simple agenda for today.
---Mira starts reading the agenda---
Ken First, I would like to hear about your company needs. Next, I’d like to present what we need. Finally, I’d like to finalize the date to kick-start the project.
Mira Sure, Ken. That sounds good.
Ken Is there anything you'd like to add, Mira?
Mira No, thank you. I’m OK with the agenda.
▼▼▼
TRY

Practice setting the goal of each meeting and going over the agenda.

PURPOSE AGENDA
To renegotiate my salary
  1. Discuss the criteria for a pay raise
  2. Present my achievements
  3. Review my performance
To negotiate a budget increase
  1. Present our original project plan
  2. Review changes to our plan
  3. Propose a budget increase
To propose a new product
  1. Current market needs
  2. Issues with our current product
  3. Present our new product concept
▼▼▼
ACT

Imagine you are meeting with your counterpart to negotiate something. Set the goal of your meeting, go over the agenda, and ask if there is anything your counterpart would like to add.
