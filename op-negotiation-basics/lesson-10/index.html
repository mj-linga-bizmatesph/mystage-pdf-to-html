PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

The next step in the negotiation process is to build a relationship with your counterpart. Make small talk and connect on a personal level by saying:

Please call me Ken.
Did you have a hard time finding the building?
Okay, let me introduce myself first.

Make sure your counterpart understands the purpose of your meeting and the agenda. Use the following expressions to help you set your goals:

I'm hoping that we could decide on...
I've prepared a simple agenda for today.
Is there anything you'd like to add?

The more questions you ask, the more information you can get from your counterpart. Understand their needs by asking various types of questions:

For a general understanding: Can you tell me about...? / Can you fill me in on…?
For more details: Why…? How…? What…? Where…? When…? Etc.
For specific details: Do you…? Did you…? Etc.

It’s always a good idea to check your understanding during the negotiation process. Use the following to clarify information:

So, you‘d like to... Is that correct?
Let me check my understanding.
So, just to confirm, ...

PART 2

Apply what you’ve learned in the second phase (building relationships) of the negotiation process.

  1. Make small talk and get to know your counterpart on a personal level.
  2. Discuss the goal of your meeting and go over the agenda.
  3. Ask the needs of your counterpart and confirm your understanding.
