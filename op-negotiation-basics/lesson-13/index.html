DISAGREEING
13
BIZ TIP
Sometimes we are afraid to show disagreement because we might offend other people. Here are some ways to say “NO” politely and professionally:
That's interesting. I'm just concerned about...
I have a different view on this.
Our priorities might be a bit different. We're thinking of...
SEE
Ken Do you have special personnel that does all the product testing?
Mira Well, we usually invite a few people outside the company to do it.
Ken That's interesting. I'm just concerned about the information leaking out.
Mira I understand. However, the feedback we’ll get from these users would be helpful.
Ken Sure, but I have a different view on this, Mira. It’s risky because one of the testers could be a competitor or a tech blogger. We don’t want this leaking out.
Mira I understand, Ken, but it’s the valuable feedback we’re after.
Ken Our priorities might be a bit different. We're thinking of forming a special team in our company to make sure the information remains confidential during the test.

TRY

Read the ideas below and practice showing disagreement using different expressions.

ACT

Imagine that you are having a discussion with your trainer about why English should or shouldn’t be the corporate language in your company. Be sure to give reasons each time you disagree with your trainer.
