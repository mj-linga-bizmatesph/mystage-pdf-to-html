FINDING COMMON GROUND
14
BIZ TIP
Finding common ground is a good way to reach an agreement. Offer some options that the other person might agree to. Here are some phrases that could help:
OK, would you agree to...?
I'm willing to accept this, if you could consider...
What if I..., would you...?
SEE
Mira By the way, not all the testers are outsiders, Ken. Most of them are our employees.
Ken I see. OK, would you agree to having our special team run the tests with your employees?
Mira Fair enough. But please note that the 6-month timeframe is only for the development. The tests would run for another 6 months.
Ken I see. I’m willing to accept this, if you could consider lowering the price of each unit upon production.
Mira I’d love to Ken but this technology isn’t cheap.
Ken Right. What if I invite a friend of mine to feature your company in a tech magazine, would you give us a volume price instead?
Mira Hmm, I think that sounds great. Sure, let me ask my boss.

TRY

Find common ground by offering options that the other person can agree to.

SITUATION YOUR OPTIONS
Your colleague wants to go to a Chinese restaurant for lunch, but you want to go to an Italian café.
  • You want to go to an Italian café.
  • You accept the Chinese restaurant if you go to an Italian café for coffee later.
  • You will pay for lunch if you go to an Italian café.
An old friend would like to meet with you on Friday afternoon but you’re so busy.
  • You’re free on Saturday.
  • You accept if you can meet Friday night.
  • You can meet Friday afternoon if you can meet by your office.
