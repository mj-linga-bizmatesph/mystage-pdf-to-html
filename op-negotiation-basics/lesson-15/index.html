PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

When presenting your idea, you can follow a simple structure to make it easy to understand. You can use phrases like:

Introduce the topic and background: I’d like to talk about...
Make your proposal: So I propose that...
Highlight the merits: So, this would give your company... (merit)

Expressing agreement is easy. However, saying “yes” all the time doesn’t sound natural. Here are some expressions that show agreement:

Yes, I totally agree with you.
Yes, I'm fine with that.
That makes sense.

Sometimes we are afraid to show disagreement because we might offend other people. Here are some ways to say “NO” politely and professionally:

That's interesting. I'm just concerned about...
I have a different view on this.
Our priorities might be a bit different. We're thinking of...

Finding common ground is a good way to reach an agreement. Offer some options that the other person might agree to. Here are some phrases that could help:

OK, would you agree to...?
I'm willing to accept this, if you could consider...
What if I..., would you...?
