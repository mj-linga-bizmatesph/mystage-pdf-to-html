PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

Make sure you go over what you agreed on before ending the meeting. Summarize by using the phrases below:

So we agreed to… (settled items)
However, you still need to… (unsettled items)
Could you get back to me by…? (action plans)

Make sure you end the meeting on a positive note. Show your counterpart your appreciation, give some positive feedback, and mention the next steps by using the phrases below:

Thank you for your time today, [name].
I’m so glad that we had a chance to share our ideas today.
So, I’ll e-mail you the final version by next Monday.

It’s always a good idea to summarize the terms of your agreement in writing. Send a follow-up e-mail by using the following expressions:

Thank you for visiting our office yesterday.
Just to confirm what we decided,...
Here are the next steps...

You can continue to keep ties with your counterpart even after the negotiation by e-mail. Here are some expressions to keep in touch with your counterpart in an e-mail:

I hope you had a nice long weekend.
I just wanted to…
Please let me know if you have any questions or concerns.
