SELECTING YOUR NEGOTIATION APPROACH

BIZ TIP
The approach we take in a negotiation is often influenced by our personality and background.
However, in theory, our approach should depend less on who we are and more on how important the other side and the outcome are. Select an appropriate approach by:
Determining how important the relationship with the other side is
Determining how important the outcome of the negotiation is
Deciding your approach based on the importance of the relationship and the outcome
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about an upcoming negotiation.
Ken I now understand what a negotiation is, but I’m still not sure what approach to take with my counterpart. Should I be a tough or soft negotiator?
Ron Well, that depends on two things: how important the relationship is with your counterpart and how important the outcome of the negotiation really is.
Ken What if both aren’t important?
Ron Then, you can just AVOID the negotiation. No need to negotiate.
Ken What if keeping the relationship isn’t important, but the outcome is?
Ron Then, you should COMPETE. Take what you can. And if the relationship is important, but the outcome isn’t, you should ACCOMMODATE. Basically, keep them happy.
Ken Right. What if both the relationship and the outcome are important? Should I COMPROMISE?
Ron Well, COMPROMISING is a kind of middle ground. Many people confuse COMPROMISING with COLLABORATING. They are very different. Giving up something is not the same as working together. So if both are important, COLLABORATE rather than COMPROMISE.
