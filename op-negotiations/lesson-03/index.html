TAKING A WIN-WIN APPROACH
3
BIZ TIP
A win-win negotiation is more than just thinking about the other side’s needs. It means aiming to reach an agreement where both sides have the best possible solution. Aim for win-win by:
Creating a positive environment
Focusing on the WHYs and not just the WHATs
Exploring options
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about an upcoming negotiation.
Ken So Ron, I often hear the term “win-win negotiation,” but what does that really mean?
Ron In simple terms, it means that you want to reach an agreement where both sides have the best possible solution.
Ken I understand this concept, but in reality, that’s very difficult to do.
Ron Of course. Sometimes it’s impossible. However, by having this mindset, you’re more likely to have less conflict, less stress, and a better outcome.
Ken I see. So how do I take a win-win approach?
Ron First, you need to create a positive environment. We need to remember that we are all people, and people want to collaborate with people they like. Don’t make the negotiation a people problem. It’s about understanding and solving a problem.
Ken Right. A positive environment.
Ron And focus on the WHYs and not just the WHATs. We all have different reasons for our decisions. So find out the reasons and the importance of each.
Ken You mean I should find out not only WHY but also WHICH factors are most important?
Ron Exactly. And finally, use your creativity and flexibility to explore options for both sides.
