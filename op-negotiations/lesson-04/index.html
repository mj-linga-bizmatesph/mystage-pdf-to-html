AVOIDING PEOPLE ISSUES
4
BIZ TIP
The most common reason for negotiations to fail is people issues. In effective communication, what’s important is not WHAT is said or even HOW it is said, but HOW it is RECEIVED. Avoid people issues by:
Understanding the common problems between people
Making relationship building one of your goals
Searching for opportunities
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about an upcoming negotiation.
Ken So, I’ve determined that both the relationship with my counterpart and the outcome of this negotiation are important. So, I will take a collaborative approach.
Ron That’s good. So, we need to make sure that people issues don’t become a barrier.
Ken Why would people issues become a barrier?
Ron That’s the most common reason for negotiations to fail. It’s human nature to have ill feelings toward someone who disagrees with you. We begin to distrust them, alter our tone of voice, and even use aggressive words without realizing it.
Ken I see. OK, I’ll be careful.
Ron Well, if you make relationship building one of your goals, it’ll come naturally.
Ken OK. I’ll make sure that is my goal.
Ron Great. And always look for opportunities for both sides.
Ken Opportunities? Shouldn’t we be trying to solve problems so we can move forward?
Ron Yes, but the opportunities should come first. Once both sides understand the magnitude of the opportunities of working together, you’ll both be more willing to solve any issues that come up.

TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. Try to recall a negotiation you had where you encountered people problems.
  2. Which approach did you take? (avoiding, competing, accommodating, collaborating, compromising)
  3. What did the other person do that might have caused people problems?
  4. What did you do that might have caused people problems?
  5. What do you think you could have done to avoid people problems?

ACT

Explain the importance of avoiding people problems in a negotiation and how to do it.
