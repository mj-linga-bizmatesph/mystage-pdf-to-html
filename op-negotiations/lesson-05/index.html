OTHER PROGRAMS NEGOTIATIONSILESSON 5
FOCUSING ON INTERESTS
5
BIZ TIP
Oftentimes in negotiations, we focus too much on what the other side is saying and not enough on why they are saying it. However, to find a solution which satisfies both sides, we need to know the real reasons or their interests. Focus on interests by:
Understanding why you want something
Trying to really understand why they want something
Moving the discussion forward to meet the WHYs
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about an upcoming negotiation.
Ken I’m now preparing for my negotiation. I’m listing up everything we want and they want.
Ron That’s good, but I would go a step further, and ask yourself WHY you want them.
Ken Hmm, I see.
Ron By asking yourself WHY several times, you’ll begin to see your true interests. And rather than communicating WHAT you want to the other side, sometimes it’s more effective to communicate the reason behind it. Then, the other side can understand your real interests.
Ken But I don’t want to give them too much information.
Ron Well, by being open about your interests, they’ll more likely be open about theirs.
Ken What if they don’t disclose their interests?
Ron I would ask them why they are making such a request or why another request wouldn’t be OK. That’s the essence of win-win negotiations. To really satisfy both sides, you need to share each other’s real interests.
Ken I agree with that.
Ron However, in some cultures, they may be less willing to share that information. But remember that WHY they want something is more valuable than WHAT they want.
