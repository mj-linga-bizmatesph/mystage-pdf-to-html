BEING PERSUASIVE

BIZ TIP
It often takes different approaches to persuade someone. One basic principle of persuasion is Aristotle’s “modes of persuasion,” which are ethos, pathos, and logos. By taking a 3-pronged approach, you are more likely to persuade others. Be persuasive by:
Demonstrating credibility
Appealing to emotions
Using logic
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about an upcoming negotiation.
Ken Ron, I’m putting together the logic to persuade my counterpart in our negotiation, but I’m not sure it’s enough to persuade him.
Ron That’s good. Using logic is a very effective approach, but it may not be enough.
Ken What do you mean?
Ron Well, Aristotle’s research on the subject of persuasion revealed three key approaches, which are ethos, pathos, and logos.
Ken What do these words mean?
Ron Well, ethos refers to persuading the other side using credibility. You have 15 years’ experience in this field. Our company is known as the market leader. Our senior executive with over 30 years’ experience agrees with you. All these add to your credibility.
Ken I see. So I can mention these to support my proposal.
Ron That’s right. But sometimes that’s not enough. You also need to appeal to emotions or pathos. So you can communicate passion, fear, love, or some other emotion as well.
Ken Right. And the last must be using logic or logos. So by taking all three approaches, I am more likely to persuade the other side.
