RESOLVING CONFLICT
7
BIZ TIP
In almost every negotiation, we’re likely to encounter some form of conflict. It may be a minor disagreement on an issue or complete difference in values. Whatever the case may be, we can improve our chances of coming to an agreement by keeping three things in mind. Resolve conflict by:
Not getting personal
Starting with areas you can agree on
Using time to your advantage
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron.
Ken Ron, I’m worried about having conflict in my negotiation.
Ron I’m sure you’ll disagree on some things. That’s part of negotiating.
Ken I guess so, but I’d rather not have an argument.
Ron Nothing’s wrong with arguing relevant points as long as you don’t get personal. Don’t take comments personally and don’t make personal attacks. As long as both sides want to reach an agreement, conflict is not a bad thing. It’s a chance to reach an agreement.
Ken I understand, but sometimes it seems impossible.
Ron It might seem impossible at first. However, if you start with areas you can agree on, you’ll slowly build trust. And this will encourage both sides to look for ways to make it work.
Ken Right. If we both believe that a solution is possible, we’ll be more flexible.
Ron That’s right. And remember that you can always use time to your advantage.
Ken Do you mean I can ask for more time?
Ron Sure. The worst thing you can do is rush into a bad decision. Sometimes taking a break from the negotiation will open up new possibilities.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  1. Have you ever received a comment that felt like a personal attack from someone at work?
  2. Have you ever made a comment that the other person felt like it was a personal attack from you?
  3. Have you been in a situation where you just found it impossible to agree on anything with someone? What did you do? What could you have done?
  4. Have you ever rushed into a bad decision?

Explain how conflicts are a part of negotiations and what you can do to improve your chances of reaching an agreement.
