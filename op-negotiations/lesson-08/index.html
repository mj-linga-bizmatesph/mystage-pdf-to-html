KNOWING YOUR BATNA
8
BIZ TIP
Sometimes it’s just not possible to reach an agreement. And your only option is to walk away. However, you can only walk away if you know what’s waiting for you, which is your plan B or your BATNA. Knowing your BATNA allows you to know when to walk away from a negotiation. Know your BATNA by:
Clearly defining your objective
Brainstorming options in case you can’t reach an agreement
Selecting the best option
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron.
Ken I’m still worried that I might not be able to reach an agreement with our vendor.
Ron Sure, that happens. So what will you do?
Ken I don’t know. So, that’s why I’m worried.
Ron You’re worried because you don’t know your BATNA.
Ken What’s a BATNA?
Ron Your BATNA is your Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement. It’s your plan B. It’s what you will do if you can’t reach an agreement.
Ken Well, I haven’t thought of that. How do I create my BATNA?
Ron First, you should clearly define your objective. What do you want to achieve?
Ken I want to have a contract with a reliable vendor with affordable rates.
Ron OK, but there are many other vendors. You can even look overseas. So the next step is to brainstorm options in case you can’t reach an agreement.
Ken I see. And I should select the best option which is my BATNA.
Ron Exactly. Not reaching an agreement shouldn’t be a concern. If you know your BATNA, you have nothing to worry about. And it’s helpful to know the other side’s BATNA, too.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer the following questions to come up with your BATNA.

  1. What’s a situation that you might negotiate in the near future? (a salary raise, a promotion, a contract, a budget, a project deadline, etc.)
  2. What is the objective of the negotiation?
  3. What are all the possible options that you could take in case you can’t reach an agreement?
  4. What is the best possible option from your list; in other words, what is your BATNA?
