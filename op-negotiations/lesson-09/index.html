OTHER PROGRAMS NEGOTIATIONSILESSON 9
KNOWING YOUR INNER SELF
9
BIZ TIP
You may not realize it, but the person you negotiate with the most often is yourself. Whether it’s trying to decide to quit smoking or to finally change jobs, we are constantly fighting with our inner selves. Know your inner self by:
Differentiating how you are supposed to feel from how you really feel
Understanding your purpose
Aligning yourself with your purpose
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron.
Ken Ron, I feel more confident with myself about taking part in a negotiation.
Ron That’s great, but the person you really need to negotiate with is yourself.
Ken What do you mean?
Ron One reason negotiating is difficult is because we’re unsure of who we are. We need to differentiate how we are supposed to feel from how we really feel. Have you ever followed orders from your boss without thinking or taken your parents’ advice without knowing why?
Ken Yeah, I’m sure I have.
Ron Well, a true negotiator operates on his or her own principles. And the first step to knowing your principles is to know your inner self.
Ken And how do I do that?
Ron Well, you need to understand your purpose in life. What motivates you? What do you want to achieve? How do you want to be treated?
Ken I never really thought about that.
Ron Once you understand your purpose, you can look at where there is a mismatch between who you are and what you do.
Ken Yes, there are some things that I do in my job that I don’t agree with.
Ron Sure, and there are many things that we can’t change in a company. However, some things we can align with our purpose. And in the end, this will make you a better negotiator.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer the following questions to know your inner self.

  1. What’s your life’s purpose? What motivates you? What do you want to achieve? How do you want to be treated?
  2. Where is there a mismatch between who you are and what you do?
  3. What can you do to align what you do with your purpose?

ACT

Explain your inner self and how it will affect you as a negotiator.
