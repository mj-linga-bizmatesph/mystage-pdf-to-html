TAKING THE LEAD
11
BIZ TIP
According to research, taking the lead from the start of the negotiation allows you to control the entire negotiation process. By taking the lead, you are showing confidence and are more likely to be persuasive. Take the lead by:
Making the first contact
Deciding the venue
Suggesting an agenda
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about starting his negotiation.
Ron So, when is your first meeting with your counterpart?
Ken We haven’t decided yet. I’m waiting for the other side to make the first move. I don’t want to show that we are desperate.
Ron If I were you, I would e-mail your counterpart and show that you are eager to renegotiate your contract. You can say, “I’m writing to set up a meeting to review our contract. After looking over our current arrangement, I feel there are some revisions we can make to better satisfy both sides.”
Ken Wow. That’s pretty direct.
Ron Yes, this shows you are confident and serious. Also, you could say, “I’d be happy to visit your office on any of the following days.”
Ken I see. So I decide the venue and just give a few dates and times.
Ron Exactly. And I would suggest an agenda like this: “I’ve put together a draft agenda that we could follow. Please feel free to add any other items.” By taking the lead from the start, you’ll be able to control the negotiation. And that’s a big advantage.
