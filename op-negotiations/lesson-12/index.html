SETTING A POSITIVE ENVIRONMENT
12
BIZ TIP
During the first 5 minutes of the negotiation, your counterpart is making all kinds of judgment about you. In this time, you want to create a positive environment so that the other side opens up and shows willingness to listen to you. Set a positive tone by:
Being positive rather than nice
Making small talk
Mirroring and matching your counterpart
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about starting his negotiation.
Ken I’m meeting with my counterpart tomorrow. I hope he creates a positive environment.
Ron It’s up to you to create a positive environment. Start by giving a friendly greeting like this, “Jim, it’s nice to see you again. Thank you for making time to meet with me.”
Ken OK, I can be nice to him.
Ron Being positive is not the same as being nice. If you make effort to be nice to him, you’ll expect him to do the same. Instead, focus on creating a positive environment. Make small talk by asking, “How’s everyone on your team? How’s business these days? When did you change your company’s website?” Your responses should be positive, too. Avoid saying things like, “I’m so busy,” or, “Business is slowing down.”
Ken OK, I can do that.
Ron And try to mirror and match your counterpart.
Ken What do you mean?
Ron Well, if you watch two close friends communicating, you’ll notice that their tone, reactions, and body language are very similar. By copying the other person’s behavior, you’ll notice it’ll be easier to connect with them.

TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. What could you say to give a friendly greeting to your counterpart in a negotiation?
  2. What are some small talk questions you can ask your counterpart in a negotiation?
  3. What questions might your counterpart ask you during small talk?
  4. What could you do to mirror and match your counterpart in a negotiation?
▼▼▼
ACT

Imagine you are having your first meeting with your counterpart in a negotiation. Create a positive environment by giving a friendly greeting, making small talk, and mirroring and matching your counterpart.
