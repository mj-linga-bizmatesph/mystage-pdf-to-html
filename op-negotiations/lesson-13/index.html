SETTING EXPECTATIONS
13
BIZ TIP
Now, you’ve created a positive environment with your counterpart through small talk. The next step is to transition to business talk. A natural flow is to set expectations. You can do this by:
Giving a brief explanation of the background and purpose of the meeting
Going over the items on the agenda
Asking if there’s anything your counterpart would like to add
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about starting his negotiation.
Ken OK, so after I’ve created a positive environment, what do I do next?
Ron Well, it’s a good idea to set expectations to make sure that your counterpart is comfortable before moving forward. You can say, “Thank you for making time for me. Our companies have partnered together for 2 years now, and although we’re satisfied with the partnership, we feel there may be some areas that we could still improve. So, I hope that we can share ideas and agree on some revisions to benefit both sides.”
Ken I see. Then, do I go over the agenda?
Ron Yes, you can say, “I’ve prepared an agenda for today. Let me go over the items. First, we can discuss…”
Ken That’s good. And then, we can start our discussions?
Ron Not yet. It’s a good idea to get their input first. You can say, “Is there anything you’d like to add to the agenda before we begin?”
Ken Oh right. I think that’s a polite thing to do.
▼▼▼
TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. Describe a negotiation you might have with someone.
  2. What would the background and purpose of the negotiation be in this case?
  3. What could you have on the agenda for your meeting?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play a meeting with your counterpart for a negotiation. Start by creating a positive environment, and then give your counterpart the background and purpose of the negotiation. And then, go over the items on the agenda. And finally, ask your counterpart for input.
