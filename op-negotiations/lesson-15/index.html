OTHER PROGRAMS NEGOTIATION BASICSILESSON 15
FRAMING YOUR PROPOSAL
15
BIZ TIP
How you frame your proposal could have a drastic impact on the outcome of the negotiation. You can effectively frame your proposal by:
Identifying what’s most important to the other side
Showing the value your proposal brings to the other side
Showing the loss of opportunity to them if the proposal isn’t accepted
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about starting his negotiation.
Ken I’m trying to come up with an effective way to present my proposal to my counterpart.
Ron How you frame your proposal could have a significant impact on whether they accept it or not. I know a simple flow that many negotiators use.
Ken Oh great. What is it?
Ron First, you need to identify what’s most important to them. So you can say, “I now understand that what’s most important to your company is.... Is my understanding correct?”
Ken I see. So, I let them know that I understand their needs.
Ron That’s right. Then, you highlight the unique value that your proposal will bring to them. You can say, “My proposal is to.... This will give your company...”
Ken Right. Then, I can talk about the costs to them and the other details?
Ron No, not yet. Then, you can explain the loss of opportunity if they don’t accept. You can say, “If we don’t do this, we could face...”
Ken Isn’t it bad to talk about something negative like that?
Ron No, because your proposal is to avoid the negative situation for them. And people are more motivated to act to avoid a negative situation than to gain a positive one.

TRY

Discuss these questions with your trainer.

  1. Describe a negotiation you might have with someone.
  2. In this case, what might be the most important issue to the other side?
  3. In this case, what value would your idea or proposal bring to them?
  4. In this case, what opportunity loss would they face if they didn’t accept?
