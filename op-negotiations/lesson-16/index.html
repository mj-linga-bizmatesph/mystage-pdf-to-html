OTHER PROGRAMS NEGOTIATIONSILESSON 16
DEALING WITH RESISTANCE
16
BIZ TIP
It wouldn’t be a negotiation if there was no disagreement. So expect to experience resistance from your counterpart somewhere in the process. You can deal with it by:
Clarifying the reason for resistance or disagreement
Looking for possible options
Asking for a possible solution
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about starting his negotiation.
Ken I understand that conflict is part of the negotiation process, but how do I deal with it?
Ron Well, first you need to clarify the reason for the conflict. Learn more from them. For example, if your counterpart says the price is too high, you can ask, “May I ask what you are comparing the price to?” This will help you understand his reasoning.
Ken That makes sense.
Ron And then, you can look for possible options by asking, “Are there any items in the package that you don’t really need?” or, “What are the must-haves in this package?”
Ken Right. So we might be able to change the package so that it’s more appropriate to his needs.
Ron Exactly. And you could ask him to come up with a possible solution by asking, “What could we do to make this an acceptable package for you?”
Ken I see. That way my counterpart gets involved in coming up with a solution as well.
