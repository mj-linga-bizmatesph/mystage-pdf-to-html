FOLLOWING UP
19
BIZ TIP
Never assume that the deal is done just because you reached an agreement in a meeting. Be sure to follow up in writing within a couple of days of the meeting to ensure there is no misunderstanding. Write a good follow-up e-mail by:
Confirming points of agreement
Summarizing unsettled issues
Suggesting next steps
SEE
Ken is speaking to his mentor, Ron, about his negotiation.
Ken Ron, my meeting with my counterpart went really well. We formed a very good relationship and agreed on a lot of points. I couldn’t have done it without you.
Ron Aren’t we celebrating a little too early? Has he already signed the contract?
Ken No, not officially.
Ron Then, the negotiation is ongoing. To make sure both sides stay on focus, I would write a follow up e-mail like this:

Thank you for your time.

Hi, Dave.

Thank you for a great meeting. I’m glad we could openly exchange ideas and progress towards a closer partnership. Just to check my understanding, I’d like to highlight the key points from our meeting on June 1, 2017.

Points of Agreement:

  • (list points of agreement)

Unsettled Issues

  • (list points of issues)

Next Steps:

  • (list of next steps)

Please let me know if there is anything I missed. I look forward to seeing you in our next meeting.
