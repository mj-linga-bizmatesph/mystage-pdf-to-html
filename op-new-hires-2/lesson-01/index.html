ANSWERING THE PHONE
BIZ TIP

You are representing your entire company when you answer the phone. Show professionalism by:


  • Memorizing your opening for English calls
  • Asking for the name and company
  • Using the caller’s name to make a connection
SEE

Example

(Telephone rings and Yuji answers)

Yuji:
Odenwa arigatou gozaimasu. CrossNex no Suzuki de gozaimasu.
Caller:
Do you speak English?
Yuji:
Ah…, a little.
Caller:
Could I speak to Mr. Yasuda in the Overseas Department?
Yuji:
Wait please.

Bizmates style

(Telephone rings and Yuji answers)

Yuji:
Odenwa arigatou gozaimasu. CrossNex no Suzuki de gozaimasu.
Caller:
Do you speak English?
Yuji:
Yes. Thank you for calling CrossNex. May I help you?
Caller:
Could I speak to Mr. Yasuda in the Overseas Department?
Yuji:
Yes, may I have your name and company please?
Caller:
Yes, this is Mike Lawson with CyberTec.
Yuji:
Thank you, Mr. Lawson. Could you please hold for just a minute?
Caller:
Sure.
TRY

Discuss these questions.

  1. What could you say to answer the phone from an English caller?
  2. What could you say to ask for the caller’s name and company?
  3. What could you say to ask for the spelling of the caller’s name?
  4. What could you say to have the caller wait?
  5. What could you say to take a message from the caller?
ACT

Role play.

Situation 1: Imagine that you are at your office and you receive a call from an English speaker who wants to speak to your boss. Answer the phone and transfer the call.

Situation 2: Imagine that your boss is out of the office. Take a message for your boss.
