3
MAKING SMALL TALK
BIZ TIP

Engage in small talk and make your guest feel comfortable by:


  • Being a good listener, not a good talker
  • Showing interest
  • Looking for something in common
SEE

Example

(Yuji and Mr. Lawson are in the lobby at CrossNex.)

Mike:
So, how’s everything at CrossNex?
Yuji:
Oh, I’m very busy. I’m a new employee so there are many things I don’t know. I have to learn more about our business. We have many manuals. So, I must read them every day.
Mike:
I see.

Bizmates style

(Yuji and Mr. Lawson are in the lobby at CrossNex.)

Yuji:
So, how was your flight?
Mike:
It was long, but it was a smooth flight.
Yuji:
That’s good. Will you go sightseeing while you are here?
Mike:
Yes, do you have any recommendations?
Yuji:
Well, what are you interested in?
Mike:
I’d like to see some of the latest Japanese gadgets.
Yuji:
Oh, I love new gadgets too. You should visit Akihabara. It’s Tokyo’s Electric Town. It’s only about 20 minutes from here by train.
Mike:
Great. Do they have computer equipment too?
Yuji:
Oh yes. They have all kinds of…
TRY

Discuss these questions.

  1. What could you ask about your guest’s flight?
  2. What could you ask about your guest’s country?
  3. What could you ask about his schedule while in Japan?
  4. What could you say to ask about your guest’s interests?
  5. What places in Japan could you recommend to visit?
ACT

Role play.

Situation 1: Imagine that you are with a foreign guest in the lobby. Make small talk, get to know your guest, and recommend a place to visit.

Situation 2: Try it again, but this time, your guest is interested in something else.
