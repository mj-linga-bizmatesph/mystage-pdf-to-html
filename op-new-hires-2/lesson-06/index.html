6
HANDLING QUESTIONS
BIZ TIP

Handle questions professionally by:


  • Inviting questions professionally
  • Checking for understanding
  • Answering fully
  • Confirming if the other person is satisfied with your answer
SEE

Example

(Yuji has just finished his presentation.)

Yuji:
OK, that’s all for my presentation. Do you have any questions? Go ahead.
Mike:
What’s the function of the Nagoya office?
Yuji:
Yes, we have an office in Nagoya.
Mike:
No, I mean what does the Nagoya office do?
Yuji:
Ah, it’s a sales office.

Bizmates style

(Yuji has just finished his presentation.)

Yuji:
OK, that’s all for my presentation. Now, I’d be happy to answer questions. Yes, Mike?
Mike:
What’s the function of the Nagoya office?
Yuji:
Are you asking what the Nagoya office’s role is?
Mike:
Yes.
Yuji:
It’s our sales office. We have 5 people responsible for sales in the entire Tokai area. Does that answer your question?
Mike:
Yes, it does. Thank you.
TRY

Discuss these questions.

  1. What can you say to invite questions?
  2. What can you say to check for understanding of the question?
  3. What can you say to confirm if the other person is satisfied with your answer?
  4. Think of a list of questions that people might ask you about your company.
ACT

Role play.

Situation 1: Imagine that you are answering questions about your company after a presentation. Answer them by following the Biz tips.

Situation 2: Imagine that you are answering questions about a topic of your choice after a presentation. Answer them by following the Biz tips.
