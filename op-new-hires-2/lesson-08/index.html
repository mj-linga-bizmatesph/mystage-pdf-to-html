8
EXPLAINING CHARTS AND GRAPHS
BIZ TIP

Describe charts and graphs effectively by:


  • Introducing the graph or chart first
  • Describing changes in figures
  • Giving reasons for changes
SEE

Example

(Yuji is explaining the figures on a graph to Mr. Lawson.)

Mike:
OK, can you explain the sales figures of each product?
Yuji:
Yes. Sales for Product A was 2.3 million in the first quarter. It was 3.5 in the second quarter. And it was 1.7 in the third quarter. And finally, it was 1.7 again in the fourth quarter.

Bizmates style

(Yuji is explaining the figures on a graph to Mr. Lawson.)

Mike:
OK, can you explain the sales figures of each product?
Yuji:
Yes, this chart shows the sales amount of Product A for each quarter of last year. The vertical axis shows sales and the horizontal axis shows each quarter. In the 1st quarter, Product A started high at 2.3 million dollars. In the 2nd quarter, sales peaked at 3.5 million due to a successful sales campaign. However, in the 3rd quarter, we saw sales drop to 1.7 million due to the strong yen. Finally, sales remained stable at 1.7 million in the fourth quarter.
TRY

Discuss these questions.

  1. What can you say to introduce a chart or graph?
  2. What can you say to explain the increase of a number?
  3. What can you say to explain the decrease of a number?
  4. What can you say to explain no change in a number?
