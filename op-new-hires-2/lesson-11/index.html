THE BUSINESS MINDSET

SEE

Read the story aloud.

Many new hires experience culture shock in their first few months of starting their jobs. Scholastic vocabulary, such as lectures, semesters, and grades, are replaced with meetings, quarters, and performance evaluations. Jeans and T-shirts are replaced with business attire. Instead of paying to study, we are paid to work. Welcome to the world of business. And to survive in this new world, you must have a business mindset. Here are some basic concepts of having a business mindset.

Having a business mindset means:

  • Knowing that the purpose of a business is to make money
  • Understanding your corporate structure and how decisions are made
  • Supporting your ideas with facts and data
  • Knowing what’s important to the company
  • Learning from your mistakes
  • Setting goals, tracking them, and getting results
  • Looking for ways to streamline and automate processes
  • Putting your resources to best use
  • Taking pride in your work
TRY

Discuss these questions.

  • Have you experienced culture shock since joining your company?
  • What’s the biggest shock that you experienced since joining your company?
  • Which from the above list is the biggest surprise to you?
  • Do you think some of the points in the above list are relevant to university students?
  • Do you agree that one must have a business mindset to survive in the business world?
ACT

Present your ideas.

What areas do you feel you need to develop in order to have a business mindset? How do you plan to develop a business mindset?
