BUSINESS MANNERS
BIZ TIP

Closed questions encourage Yes/No or short answers. Ask closed questions when you want to confirm or know a specific detail.

  • Is this your bag?
  • When did you buy it?
  • What brand is it?
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Every time you meet someone outside of your company, by telephone, e-mail, or face-to-face, you are representing your company. And your actions and your behavior, will have a direct impact on how they will see your company. Good business etiquette is more than just being professional. It’s a strategy for you and your company to help get results. Good business manners lead to better business.

Common business manners:

  • Be well-groomed and appropriately dressed.
  • Say your name slowly and clearly when introducing yourself.
  • Smile and make eye contact.
  • Put away your cellphone during a conversation.
  • Call early if you are going to be late.
  • Cover your mouth when you cough.
  • Reply to e-mails within 3 business days.
  • Follow through promises you make.
  • Avoid technical language.
  • Remember people’s names.
TRY

Discuss these questions.

  • How do you feel about the statement that you are representing your company every time you interact with someone outside of your company?
  • Have you ever formed a positive or negative impression of a company based on an interaction with someone from that company?
  • Do you agree that good business etiquette is a strategy to help get results?
  • Can you add to the above list of manners?
ACT

Present your ideas.

Explain the rules of good manners that you plan to follow in your company?
