BUSINESS MANNERS
BIZ TIP

Open-ended questions elicit a more detailed answer. Use open-ended questions when you want to get more information from another person. Here are some examples of open-ended questions:

  • What was your experience in…
  • What do you think of…?
  • What steps did you take to…?
SEE

Read the story aloud.

Any successful businessperson will tell you the importance of connections in business. Regardless of position, status, age, or field of business, business contacts can help you in your work. This is especially true when your contacts are based overseas. The more people you know, the more value you add to yourself and to your company. Therefore, it’s important to see every encounter that you have with people as an opportunity to expand your network.
However, it takes more than an exchange of business cards and a handshake for someone to become your contact. Keeping in touch through social media and e-mail will help develop the relationship over time. Also note that the rule of “give and take” should apply. Look for ways to help your contacts and they will be more wiling to help you when the time comes. So, there is truth in the saying that “It’s not WHAT you know, but WHO you know”.
TRY

Discuss these questions.

  • What do you think the author means by the more people you know, the more value you add to yourself and your company?
  • Do you see every encounter with people as an opportunity to expand your network?
  • Why do you think it’s not enough to just exchange business cards to make contacts in business?
  • What is the rule of “give and take”?
  • What does the saying, “It’s not WHAT you know, but WHO you know” mean?
ACT

Present your ideas.

Explain how business contacts can help you in your business and how you plan to expand your network.
