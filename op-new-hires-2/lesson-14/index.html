TEAMWORK
BIZ TIP

Avoid “one-liners” when responding to questions. To keep a healthy conversation going, don’t just answer the question, but add some details too.


  • How was your weekend?
  • Good, thanks.
  • Good, thanks. I went to a park with my children. We were there for…
SEE

Read the story aloud.

A team can be defined as a group of people gathered together working towards a common objective. This means that the company you work for is one big team. Your department is a smaller team within a big team. And your section within your department is even a smaller team. The point is that you need to be a team player if you work for a company.
Teamwork means understanding the team objectives and your role in order to achieve the objectives. A mistake on your part affects the success of the entire team. And a team member facing trouble is your problem as well. With these points in mind, it makes sense to not only ask for but to offer help when needed.
TRY

Discuss these questions.

  • What’s the definition of a team?
  • What’s the definition of teamwork?
  • How would you compare a sports team to a company?
  • Do you agree that you need to be a team player if you work for a company?
  • What does the author mean by a team member facing trouble is your problem as well?
  • Tell me your experience working on a team?
ACT

Present your ideas.

How is teamwork important to you in your new job?
