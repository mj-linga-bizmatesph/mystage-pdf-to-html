THE PDCA CYCLE
BIZ TIP

Active listening means you are making a conscious effort to hear not just the words in the message but to understand the meaning of the whole message.


  • Pay attention: Yes, OK, I see, uh huh, etc...
  • Provide feedback: So what you’re saying is..., I think that’s a good idea, etc...
  • Respond appropriately: Wow, that’s great! , I’m sorry to hear that, etc...
SEE

Read the story aloud.

It’s not very often that we get something right the first time. The first time you try ice skating, you’re bound to fall. The first time you swing a bat, you’re bound to miss. This is the same with business. Therefore, a simple tool that can help you get back on your feet and eventually hit a homerun is the PDCA cycle, which stands for Plan, Do, Check, and Act.
Here's how it works:
Plan: Find out what the problem is, the real problem. There may be several factors. The better the analysis, the better the solution. So, spend appropriate time planning it out.
Do: Come up with possible solutions and select the best one. Then, carry it out, but only on a small scale. You don’t want to take a big risk. Remember it’s your first time.
Check: Measure the effectiveness of your solution. Is there anything you need to change? If so, plan it out and do it again. Keep doing it until you are satisfied.
Act: Now you are ready for a full-scale launch. However, the PDCA cycle continues on. Keep Checking, Planning, and Doing to reach perfection.
