INVITING SOMEONE OUT
BIZ TIP

When inviting someone out, set the right tone by using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Hi, _____. Have you had lunch?”
  • “Would you like to join me / us for…?”
  • “If you’re free, do you want to…”
Respond to chat:
  • “Sounds good!”
  • “Count me in!”
  • “Sorry, I’d love to, but… Thanks, though.”
SEE

Chie: Hi, Mitch. Have you had lunch?

Mitch: Hey Chie. No, I haven’t.

Chie: If you’re free, do you want to have lunch?

Mitch: Sure, count me in! Who else is going?

Chie: It’s just me and Tom. Anyway, I was thinking about having sushi or ramen. What do you think?

Mitch: Sushi sounds good. What time are we leaving?

TRY

Use the phrases from the Biz Tip for the following situations:

SITUATIONS
Invite a new colleague to go drinking
Invite a colleague to have lunch at a new restaurant across the street
Invite a colleague to join the company soccer game
ACT

Invite your boss to a farewell party for your team member. Be ready to answer some questions about the farewell party.
