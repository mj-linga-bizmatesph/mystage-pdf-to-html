LESSON 1: STARTING AND ENDING CHATS

When communicating with colleagues through chat, it is good to know how to start and end the conversation effectively. Here are some useful expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Morning! Do you have a minute?”
  • “Hi, ___. Mind if I ask a few questions?”
  • “Sorry to bother you. Can I ask you something?”
End chat:
  • “Thanks. Talk to you later!”
  • “All right, just wanted to know. Thanks!”
  • “Great! Cheers."

Start a chat with your new foreign colleague and ask about their country. Be ready to answer questions about yourself.

LESSON 2: ASKING FOR A FAVOR

Ask for a favor and make requests politely using the following expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Can I ask you a favor?”
  • “Could you please/ Would you please…?”
  • “Can you give me a hand with…”
Respond to chat:
  • “I’d be glad to help.”
  • “Sure, how can I help?”
  • “Not at all. It’d be my pleasure.”

Ask a colleague to help you put together a PowerPoint presentation in English. Be ready to answer some questions about your PowerPoint presentation.

LESSON 3: INVITING SOMEONE OUT

When inviting someone out, set the right tone by using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Hi, _____. Have you had lunch?”
  • “Would you like to join me / us for…?”
  • “If you’re free, do you want to…”
Respond to chat:
  • “Sounds good!”
  • “Count me in!”
  • “Sorry, I’d love to, but… Thanks, though.”

Invite your boss to a farewell party for your team member. Be ready to answer some questions about the farewell party.

LESSON 4: GIVING REMINDERS

When giving reminders, keep it friendly by using the expressions below:

Start chat:
  • “Just a gentle reminder…”
  • “Please remember to…”
  • “By the way, did you…?”
Respond to chat:
  • “Thanks for reminding me.”
  • “I’m so sorry. It slipped my mind! I’ll…”
  • “Actually, I already…”

Your colleague is going on a business trip to visit your client. Remind your colleague to bring a souvenir. Be ready to answer some questions about what to bring.
