9
INVITING QUESTIONS
BIZ TIP

To avoid miscommunication, invite questions from your counterpart and confirm their understanding using the examples below:

Start chat:
  • “Do you have any questions about…?”
  • “Is everything clear with…?”
  • “Are you having any issues with…?”
Respond to chat:
  • “I’m good! I’ll let you know if I have any questions.”
  • “Can you give me more details about…?”
  • “I might need help with…”
SEE

Jun: Hi, Mark. Thanks for attending the meeting. Is everything clear with your new assignment?

Mark: Hey, Jun. Actually, can you give me more details about the sales report?

Jun: Sure. Please compare last month’s numbers to this month’s numbers. Can you finish it this week? Do you have any questions about this?

Mark: Actually, I might need help analyzing the data first, but I’ll try my best to finish within the week.

TRY

Use the phrases from the Biz Tip for the following situations:

SITUATIONS
Ask your colleague if they have questions about how to request a day off
Ask your colleague if they have any questions about how to use Google Calendar
Ask your colleague if they have any questions about the company’s overtime rules
ACT

Ask your new colleague if they have any questions about the company’s products or services. Be prepared to answer questions about them.
