LESSON 6: GIVING INSTRUCTIONS

When giving instructions, use steps to make them easy to understand. Use the expressions below:

Start chat:
  • “The first thing to do when… is…”
  • “First…then…next…lastly…”
  • “Be sure to…” / “Watch out for…”
Respond to chat:
  • “Got it! / I’m on it!”
  • “Let me confirm. You mean…?”
  • “What happens when / if… ?”

Give your colleague instructions on how to get to your office from Tokyo station. Be ready to answer questions about the route.

LESSON 7: ASKING FOR UPDATES

Be clear and specific when asking for updates by using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “How’s the … going?”
  • “I just wanted to check on the status of …”
  • “How soon can you…?”
Respond to chat:
  • “Sorry to keep you waiting.”
  • “So far, so good.”
  • “Unfortunately, I don’t think I can…”

Ask your colleague for an update on the English translation of your company’s website. Be ready to answer some questions about the website.

LESSON 8: MAKING CORRECTIONS

Correct misunderstandings and other mistakes over chat using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Sorry, I accidentally…”
  • “I realized I...Can you…?”
  • “My mistake. …should be…,not…”
Respond to chat:
  • “No worries. Thanks for letting me know.”
  • “No problem. I’ll be sure to…”
  • “That’s okay. Don’t worry about it!”

You sent your colleague the wrong customer information. Correct your mistake and be prepared to answer questions about the customer.

LESSON 9: INVITING QUESTIONS

To avoid miscommunication, invite questions from your counterpart and confirm their understanding using the examples below:

Start chat:
  • “Do you have any questions about…?”
  • “Is everything clear with…?”
  • “Are you having any issues with…?”
Respond to chat:
  • “I’m good! I’ll let you know if I have any questions.”
  • “Can you give me more details about…?”
  • “I might need help with…”

Ask your new colleague if they have any questions about the company’s products or services. Be prepared to answer questions about them.
