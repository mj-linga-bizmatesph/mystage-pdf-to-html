GIVING ADVICE
BIZ TIP

Keep the conversation positive when giving advice by using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Great...However, I noticed…”
  • “Nice work on…but in order to…I recommend…”
  • “Just to…I would suggest…”
Respond to chat:
  • “That’s a good point! I had better…”
  • “I was trying to…Is that okay?”
  • “That’s great advice, I’ll be sure to…”
SEE

Yumi: Hi, Paul! Great presentation! However, I noticed you didn’t have Q&A at the end.

Paul: Thanks, Yumi! Sorry, that’s a good point! I had better be more careful.

Yumi: No problem, but just to make sure there’s no confusion, I would suggest at least 10 minutes of Q&A.

Paul: That’s great advice. I’ll be sure to do that.

TRY

Use the phrases from the Biz Tip for the following situations:

SITUATIONS
Your colleague wears shorts to the office. Give your colleague advice.
Your trainee completed their project. However, it is late. Give your trainee advice.
Your colleague’s proposal is great! However, it is too long. Give your colleague advice.
ACT

Give your subordinate advice on how to be more professional during client visits in Japan. Be ready to answer questions about being professional in Japan.
