Practice responding to various chat situations from your trainer using the expressions you reviewed.

LESSON 11: GIVING UPDATES

Report important information quickly using the following expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Sorry, I’ll be late… due to...Thanks for your understanding.”
  • “I’ll be off...please feel free to…”
  • “I’ll be out of the office for...from…to…”
Respond to chat:
  • “Sorry / Glad to hear that.”
  • “Noted. Thanks for letting us know.”
  • “Copy that.”

Tell your team that you will be travelling overseas. Be prepared to answer some questions about your trip.

LESSON 12: RESCHEDULING MEETINGS

Use the following expressions to help you reschedule meetings in a more professional way:

Start chat:
  • “Sorry, I need to/can we reschedule…because…”
  • “Will you be available at / on…?”
  • “I’m sorry for the trouble / inconvenience.”
Respond to chat:
  • “No worries.”
  • “That works for me.” / “Sorry, I can’t. How about…?”
  • “Noted. See you at…”

Reschedule a meeting with your boss because your train stopped due to an accident. Be ready to answer some questions about your availability.

LESSON 13: MAKING A TEAM ANNOUNCEMENT

Make your team announcements more professional using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Good morning, everyone. This is to inform you that…”
  • “Announcement: …”
  • “Hi, team. I regret / am pleased to announce that…”
Respond to chat:
  • “Way to go, team. Cheers!”
  • “That’s great news! I look forward to...”
  • “That’s terrible news. That being said, I’m sure…”

Announce to the team that you are transferring. Be prepared to answer some questions about your new post.

LESSON 14: GIVING ADVICE

Keep the conversation positive when giving advice by using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Great...However, I noticed…”
  • “Nice work on…but in order to…I recommend…”
  • “Just to…I would suggest…”
Respond to chat:
  • “That’s a good point! I had better…”
  • “I was trying to…Is that okay?”
  • “That’s great advice. I’ll be sure to…”

Give your subordinate advice on how to be more professional during client visits in Japan.
Be ready to answer questions about being professional in Japan.
