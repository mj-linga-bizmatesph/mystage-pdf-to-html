19
GIVING ENCOURAGEMENT
BIZ TIP

Create a motivating office environment when things are difficult by using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “You’re almost there. Don’t give up!”
  • “I know it can be hard to…, but you hang in there!”
  • “Sorry to hear…didn’t work out. Things will bounce back, though!”
Respond to chat:
  • “Thanks. I really appreciate that.”
  • “Thank you for the words of encouragement. That means a lot to me.”
  • “Thanks for the support! I couldn’t have done it alone.”
SEE

Kazu: Hey, Emma. Sorry to hear our deal with ARC didn’t work out. Things will bounce back though! Don’t worry!

Emma: Yes. It was a big shock for our team. Thank you for the words of encouragement. That means a lot to me.

Kazu: Don’t mention it! We’re a team! Let me know if we can help.

Emma: Thanks for the support, Kazu! I couldn’t have done this alone.

TRY

Use the phrases from the Biz Tip for the following situations:

SITUATIONS
Encourage a colleague who is stressed with too much work
Encourage a colleague who failed a promotion test
Encourage a colleague who is unhappy because of a failing project
ACT

Encourage a team member who’s having trouble managing his team. Encourage them and be ready to answer some questions about the situation.
