LESSON 16: EXPRESSING OPINIONS

Express your opinion in the right manner by using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Honestly, I think…”
  • “Regarding…I feel we should / shouldn’t…”
  • “I think it would be best to/not to…”
Respond to chat:
  • “Thank you for your thoughts. I’ll consider…”
  • “I appreciate your insight. I’ll…”
  • “That’s a good point. Let’s…”

Your colleague wants to outsource customer service, but you disagree. Express your opinion and be ready to answer questions.

LESSON 17: RAISING CONCERNS

Speak your mind while maintaining a respectful tone when addressing concerns by using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “Sorry to bother you, but I noticed…”
  • “There have been some concerns about you… Would you mind…?”
  • “...brought up an issue about… Do you know anything about this / is everything okay?”
Respond to chat:
  • “I didn’t realize…I will be sure to…”
  • “I see where…are/is coming from. I’ll try to…”
  • “Thank you for bringing this up… I will…”

Raise a concern to your boss about always working until 11 PM because of constant teleconferences with the US. Be ready to answer questions about your concern.

LESSON 18: SHOWING APPRECIATION

Be specific when showing appreciation to spread positivity in the workplace. Use the expressions below:

Start chat:
  • “ I really appreciate you/ your…”
  • “I just wanted to take a moment to say thank you for…”
  • “Thank you so much again for… It really helped me out.”
Respond to chat:
  • “Not at all! / Don’t mention it! / My pleasure!”
  • “I’m always happy to help.”
  • “No, thank YOU for…”

You just got promoted. Show appreciation to your boss for mentoring you and be ready to answer some questions about your new post.

LESSON 19: GIVING ENCOURAGEMENT

Create a motivating office environment when things are difficult by using these expressions:

BIZ TIP

Create a motivating office environment when things are difficult by using these expressions:

Start chat:
  • “You’re almost there. Don’t give up!”
  • “I know it can be hard to…, but you hang in there!”
  • “Sorry to hear…didn’t work out. Things will bounce back, though!”
Respond to chat:
  • “Thanks. I really appreciate that.”
  • “Thank you for the words of encouragement. That means a lot to me.”
  • “Thanks for the support! I couldn’t have done it alone.”

Encourage a team member who’s having trouble managing his team. Encourage them and be ready to answer some questions about the situation.
