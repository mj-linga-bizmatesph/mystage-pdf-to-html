PART 1

Good preparation is the key to a good presentation. Keep the following in mind when preparing for your presentation:

Time: How much time do I have?
Goal: What is the goal of my presentation?
Style: Is this a formal or casual presentation?

Your opening will set the mood for your presentation. Use the expressions below to help you create the atmosphere you want:

Formal:
Good morning. Thank you for …. My name is [name]. I am a [job title] at [company]. Today, I’d like to take this opportunity to present [main topic].
Casual:
Good morning. Thank you for …. My name is [name]. I am a [job title] at [company]. Today, I’d like to take this opportunity to present [main topic].

Give your audience some background information on the topic before starting. Talk about what was, what is, and what will be, using the following expressions to help you:

Formal:
The last…have been…
Currently…is…
In the future, we expect…to…
Casual:
…years ago…
Now…is…
In the future, …will…

Make sure your audience knows your presentation outline before you start. Use the following expressions to help you:

First, I’ll talk about [topic] for [total time]
Next, I’ll explain [topic] for [total time]
Finally, I’ll go through [topic] for [total time]
We’ll have [total time] for Q&A at the end
My presentation will take about [total time]
