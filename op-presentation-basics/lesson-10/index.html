PART 1

Review the Biz Tips with your trainer and make sure you are comfortable with the expressions and concepts.

Express and support your opinions in presentations by using the expressions and flow below:

Formal:
I strongly believe that…
This is due to the fact that…
Therefore, we had better… if we hope to…
Casual:
I think…
Because…
So we should… if we want to…

Charts and graphs can be important tools to support your presentation. Use them effectively with the expressions below:

This chart shows [topic]
You’ll notice that [topic] [increased/decreased] from [#] to [#] in [year] because of [reason].
[topic] remained stable at [#]

Providing details to your comments will make your presentation more convincing. Use the following expressions to help you:

Moreover,…
In addition,…
Furthermore,…
However,…
Unfortunately/Fortunately…
In contrast,…
Therefore,…
So,…
As a result,…

The way something is presented is just as important as the content. To make your presentation impactful, consider these points:

Gestures: Use your hands and body to make certain points more impactful.
Voice: Vary your speed, tone, and stress to keep the audience interested.
Involving the audience: Ask the audience simple questions or have them give you a show of hands to get them engaged.

PART 2

Use everything you’ve learned to make your presentation impactful.

Deliver a brief presentation that includes opinions and support, a chart, and additional information. Remember to use good body gestures, rhythm, intonation, and audience involvement.
