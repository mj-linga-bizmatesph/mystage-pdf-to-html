OTHER PROGRAMS PRESENTATION BASICSILESSON 17
CLARIFYING THE QUESTION
17
BIZ TIP
Some questions may be difficult to understand. Use the following expressions to help you clarify the question:
Formal: Casual:
My apologies. I don’t quite follow.
Sorry, I didn’t catch that.
Would you mind rephrasing the question?
Could you say that again more simply?
Allow me to clarify. You’d like to ask…is that right?
Let me check what you mean. You want to know…
SEE
Formal Casual
Jim:

What do you do when you’re in a rut?
Ken:

My apologies. I don’t quite follow. Would you mind rephrasing the question?

Jim:

Sure, if you are in a bad situation, how do you recover?

Ken:

I see. Allow me to clarify. You’d like to ask what my strategy is to stay positive in hard times. Is that right?

Jim:

Yes, that’s right.
Ken:

Well…
Jim:

Ken, what do you do when you’re in a rut?
Ken:

Sorry, I didn’t catch that. Could you say that again more simply?

Jim:

Sure, if you are in a bad situation, how do you recover?

Ken:

I see. Let me check what you mean. You want to know how I stay positive in hard times?

Jim:

Yes, that’s right.
Ken:

Well…
