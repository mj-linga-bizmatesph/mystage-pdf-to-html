PART 1

Review the Biz Tips with your trainer and make sure you are comfortable with the expressions and concepts.

Asking the audience for questions is an essential part of the presentation process. Use the following expressions and flow to help you:

Formal:
I’d be happy to answer any questions.
Please don’t hesitate to speak up.
I’ve often been asked…
Casual:
Questions, anyone?
Don’t be shy.
People often ask me…

Some questions may be difficult to understand. Use the following expressions to help you clarify the question:

Formal:
My apologies. I don’t quite follow.
Would you mind rephrasing the question?
Allow me to clarify. You’d like to ask…is that right?
Casual:
Sorry, I didn’t catch that.
Could you say that again more simply?
Let me check what you mean. You want to know…

Answering questions can be the most challenging part of the presentation. Use the flow below to help you answer simple questions:

Formal:
Thank you for your interest in….
To answer your question…
Does that answer your question?
Casual:
Good question.
Let’s see…
Is that okay?

Use set phrases to handle difficult questions during Q&A:

The answer is confidential: I'm sorry, but that information is confidential. What I can tell you is…
You don’t know the answer: Unfortunately, I don’t have the answer, but, if you give me your contact information…
The person is being inappropriate or rude: Thank you for your comment/question. Does anyone else have any questions?
