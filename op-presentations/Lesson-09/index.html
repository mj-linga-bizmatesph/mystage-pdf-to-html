OTHER PROGRAMS PRESENTATIONS I LESSON 9
HANDLING DIFFICULT QUESTIONS
9
BIZ
TIP
Poorly handling difficult questions could ruin one’s presentation and damage the presenter’s confidence. Protect yourself by:
Being prepared for difficult questions
Having set phrases for difficult questions
Not being pressured to have an answer to every question
SEE
Example
Presenter: Are there any questions? Yes?
Attendee: Which departments at MMB are currently most in need of employees?
Presenter: Ah… Sorry, I have no idea.
Bizmates style
Presenter: Now, I’d be happy to answer questions. Yes, the gentleman wearing the navy suit. Please go ahead.
Attendee: Which departments at MMB are currently most in need of employees?
Presenter: Do you mean which departments at our company are looking for new employees?
Attendee: Yes.
Presenter: That’s a good question, but I don’t have that answer with me. I’d be happy to get back to you after the presentation.
Attendee: Yes, thank you.
▼▼▼
TRY
Answer these questions.
  • What could you say if you don’t know the answer to a question?
  • What could you say if you don’t have enough time to answer the question?
  • What could you say if the answer is confidential?
  • What could you say if you couldn’t catch the question?
▼▼▼
ACT
Role play.
Deliver a short presentation on a topic you’re very familiar with and answer some difficult questions from the audience.
