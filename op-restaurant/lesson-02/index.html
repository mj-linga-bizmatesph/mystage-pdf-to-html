WELCOMING YOUR CUSTOMERS
2
BIZ TIP
Welcoming your customers is another opportunity to make a good impression. Make them feel they’ve come to the right restaurant by:
Giving a warm, professional welcome
Getting necessary information
Courteously asking them to wait
SEE
Example:
(At the restaurant reception)

Staff:

Do you have a reservation?

Patron:

No, I don’t. Do I need one?

Staff:

Please wait. I will check. How many people?

Patron:

Oh, it’s just the two of us.

Staff:

OK, please wait.

Patron:

Sure.

Bizmates style:

(At the restaurant reception)

Staff:

Welcome to Shochikuen. How are you doing this evening?

Patron:

Pretty good. Thank you.

Staff:

Do you have a reservation?

Patron:

No, I’m afraid I don’t.

Staff:

Not a problem. How many people are in your party?

Patron:

Oh, it’s just the two of us.

Staff:

Please have a seat while we set up your table.

Patron:

Great. Thank you.

▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What else could you say to welcome your customers?
  • How would you ask for necessary information?
  • What else could you say to ask them to wait?

ACT
Role play.
Imagine you’re at the Front Desk. Greet your customers and make a good impression.
