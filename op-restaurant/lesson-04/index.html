OTHER PROGRAMS RESTAURANT ENGLISHILESSON 4
TAKING THEIR DRINK ORDER
4
BIZ TIP
Something as simple as taking a drink order can create a great customer experience. You can do this by:
Asking for their drink order professionally
Answering questions
Providing additional information
SEE
Example:

Staff:

What do you want to drink?

Patron:

Well, I’m not sure, maybe I’ll try a Japanese drink.

Staff:

Okay. I’ll come back later.

Patron:

Ah, OK.

Bizmates style:

Staff:

May I take your drink order?

Patron:

Well, I’m not sure, maybe I’ll try a Japanese drink.

Staff:

We have a wide range of sake on the next page.

Patron:

I see. What exactly is sake?

Staff:

Sake is an alcoholic drink made from fermented rice. The brewing process is similar to beer, but the alcohol content is about 20%.

Patron:

Sounds nice.

Staff:

There’re different types of sake. But I recommend Honjozo. It has a nice fragrance. And it’s a little sweet and easy to drink.

Patron:

Sure. We’ll get a bottle of that.

▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What type of questions might your customer ask about your drink menu and how would you answer them?
  • What additional information could you tell them about drinks served in your restaurant?
▼▼▼
ACT
Role play.
Imagine your customer is now ready to order their drink. Take the order and provide a list of options or some information if necessary.
