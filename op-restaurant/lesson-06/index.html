EXPLAINING DISHES
6
BIZ TIP
Your customers may be unfamiliar with many dishes on the menu. Your explanation can raise the value of each dish. You can do this by:
Highlighting the ingredients
Explaining the preparation method
Describing the taste
SEE
Example:

Patron:

So what is tako sunomono?

Staff:

It is Japanese octopus salad.

Patron:

Hmm, I see. I think I’ll pass on that.

Bizmates style:

Patron:

What is tako sunomono?

Staff:

Sunomono is a common Japanese salad with cucumber, ginger, and seaweed. We use the best seaweed from Northern Japan.

Patron:

Sounds delicious.

Staff:

The cucumber and ginger are thinly-sliced and mixed with seafood. Tako sunomono or tako-su for short is octopus. Kani-su is crab. And Ebi-su is shrimp.

Patron:

And how does it taste?

Staff:

It’s covered with a sweet vinegar dressing. The combination between the vinegar, seafood, and cucumber is just perfect.

Patron:

OK, I’ll try it.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What are the most popular dishes in your restaurant?
  • What ingredients do they use?
  • How are they prepared?
  • What do they taste like?
