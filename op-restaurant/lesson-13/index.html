OTHER PROGRAMS RESTAURANT ENGLISHILESSON 13
EXPLAINING CUSTOMS AND RULES
13
BIZ TIP
Some customers may not be familiar with Japanese etiquette, customs, or your restaurant rules. Inform them by:
Explaining the custom or rule
Explaining the reasoning
Ending on a positive note
SEE
Example:
(A patron enters a Japanese-style room, without taking off his shoes.)

Staff:

Excuse me. No shoes please.

Patron:

Pardon me?

Staff:

Please take off your shoes.

Patron:

Oh, I’m sorry.

Staff:

OK.

Bizmates style:

Staff:

Pardon me, Mr. Richards.

Patron:

Yes?

Staff:

I’m sorry to trouble you, but we ask our customers to remove their shoes at the entrance.

Patron:

Oh really?

Staff:

Yes, we have traditional, Japanese style booths, called zashiki. The floor has tatami mats which are made from straw.

Patron:

Oh that’s interesting.

Staff:

The zashiki booths give our customers an authentic Japanese experience.

Patron:

Sounds wonderful.

▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What are some customs or rules that may be unknown to foreign customers in your restaurant? (no tipping, must take off shoes, shouldn’t stand chopsticks in the rice bowl, etc.)
  • What would you say to your customer in each of these situations?
  • How would you explain the reasoning for these rules?
▼▼▼
ACT
Role play.
Imagine a foreign customer is unfamiliar with a custom or rule. Explain it to your customer, explain the reasoning, and end on a positive note.
