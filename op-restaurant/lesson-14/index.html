INTRODUCING JAPANESE PHRASES
14
BIZ TIP
Another way to give your customers a unique experience is to take the time to introduce some useful Japanese phrases. You can do this by:
Describing the situation
Introducing the phrase
Explaining its meaning
SEE
Example:
(A patron enters a Japanese-style room, without taking off his shoes.)

Staff:

Here is your agedashi tofu.

Patron:

Arigato.

Staff:

Hai, arigatougozaimasu.

Patron:

Pardon me?

Staff:

Ah, thank you very much.

Bizmates style:

Staff:

Here is your agedashi tofu.

Patron:

Arigato.

Staff:

Oh, you speak very good Japanese.

Patron:

No, that’s all I can say. Ha, ha, ha.

Staff:

Before we start eating the first dish that is served, we say itadakimasu. I-TA-DA-KI-MAS.

Patron:

I-TA-DA-KI-MAS.

Staff:

Very good. This means I am grateful to receive this food.

Patron:

I see. That’s a useful phrase. Thank you.

▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Explain the situations in which you would use these Japanese phrases: kanpai, oishii desu, gochisousamadeshita, osaki ni douzo, osakini itadaki masu.
  • Explain what each of the phrases means.
  • What other useful phrases could you teach your foreign customers?
