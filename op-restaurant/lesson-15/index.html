OTHER PROGRAMS RESTAURANT ENGLISHILESSON 15
RECOMMENDING A DISH
15
BIZ TIP
Keep in mind that some foreign customers aren’t familiar with most Japanese dishes. Help them out and recommend a dish by:
Understanding their preferences
Suggesting a dish
Describing the dish
SEE
Example:

Staff:

Are you ready to order?

Patron:

Actually, I’m not sure yet. What do you recommend?

Staff:

I recommend gomae.

Patron:

What’s that?

Staff:

It’s spinach salad.

Patron:

No, I don’t like spinach.

Staff:

I’m sorry.

Bizmates style:

Staff:

Are you ready to order?

Patron:

Actually, I’m not sure yet. What do you recommend?

Staff:

Let’s see. Is there anything you can’t eat or don’t like?

Patron:

I’m allergic to shrimp.

Staff:

OK, would you like something light or heavy?

Patron:

Let’s start with something light.

Staff:

Sure, and do you prefer seafood or meat?

Patron:

I’ll go for seafood.

Staff:

OK, I suggest buri kama shiyoyaki.

Patron:

What’s that?

Staff:

This is a very simple dish. It’s salted and grilled yellowtail collar. The collar is the best part of the fish. And this is the best time of year to eat yellowtail.

Patron:

Sure, we’ll try it.

▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What are some questions you can ask to understand your customers’ preferences?
  • What dishes do you often recommend?
  • How would you describe these dishes?
