OTHER PROGRAMS RESTAURANT ENGLISHILESSON 17
RAISING AN ISSUE WITH A CUSTOMER
17
BIZ TIP
There may be times when unexpected problems arise. Inform your customers about the problem by:
Breaking the news gently
Showing empathy
Offering an alternative solution
SEE
Example:

Staff:

Excuse me. We have no more fresh oysters for tonight.

Patron:

Oh no. I was really hoping I could try some Japanese oysters.

Staff:

I’m sorry.

Bizmates style:

Staff:

Excuse me. I have some unfortunate news.

Patron:

No more oysters?

Staff:

That’s right. I’m terribly sorry. We just served our last order of oysters a few minutes ago.

Patron:

Oh no. I was really hoping I could try some Japanese oysters.

Staff:

I understand you were hoping to have fresh seafood while in Japan. However, we still have fresh abalone. This is from the Izu Peninsula which has the best abalone in Japan.

Patron:

OK, we’ll try it. Thank you.

▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What are some common concerns you need to raise to your customers? (a particular dish may take a long time to prepare, the customer wants a non-smoking table but only smoking is available, another table is complaining about the noise from the customer…)
  • How would you break news and show empathy?
  • What alternative solution could you offer?
