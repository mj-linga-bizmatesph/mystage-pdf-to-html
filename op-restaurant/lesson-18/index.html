OTHER PROGRAMS RESTAURANT ENGLISHILESSON 18
DEALING WITH CUSTOMER COMPLAINTS
18
BIZ TIP
Handling complaints is an opportunity to show you care about your customers. Deal with complaints professionally by:
Giving a sincere apology
Explaining what you will do
Getting confirmation
SEE
Example:

Patron:

Excuse me. I asked for no peppers, but there are peppers in this dish.

Staff:

I’m sorry.

Patron:

I’m allergic to peppers. Please be careful.

Staff:

I’m very very sorry.

Bizmates style:

Patron:

Excuse me. I asked for no peppers, but there are peppers in this dish.

Staff:

I’m terribly sorry about this. Please let me take this back to the kitchen and complain directly to the chef. I’ll ask them to prepare a fresh dish with no peppers right away.

Patron:

Yes, no peppers please. How long will this take?

Staff:

Could you please wait just a minute while I check with the kitchen?

Patron:

Yes, that’s fine.

(A few moments later.)

Staff:

Thank you for waiting. Your dish will be ready in about 7 minutes. Will this be OK?

Staff:

OK, I’ll wait.

▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • “I’ve been waiting for my food for over 20 minutes.”
  • “This isn’t what I ordered.”
  • “I made a reservation tonight, not tomorrow.”
  • “This dish is terrible.”
  • “It’s too hot in here.”
  • “The table next to us is too noisy.”
▼▼▼
ACT
Role play.
Imagine a customer is complaining. Handle the situation and deal with the complaint.
