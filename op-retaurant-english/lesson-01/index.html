OTHER PROGRAMS RESTAURANT ENGLISHILESSON 1
TAKING A RESERVATION
1
BIZ TIP
Taking reservations usually is your first opportunity to make a good impression on the customer. You can do this by:
Having an English opening
Asking for details professionally
Confirming the details
SEE

Example:

(On the phone)

Staff:

Odenwa arigatou gozaimasu. Shochikuen de gozaimasu.

Patron:

Do you speak English?

Staff:

A little.

Patron:

I’d like to make a reservation please.

Staff:

Name please?

Patron:

Tim Bradley.

Staff:

How many and what time?

Patron:

For four people. This Saturday at 6PM.

Staff:

OK. Thank you. Goodbye.

Bizmates style:

(On the phone)

Staff:

Odenwa arigatou gozaimasu. Shochikuen de gozaimasu.

Patron:

Do you speak English?

Staff:

Yes. Thank you for calling Shochikuen. How may I help you?

Patron:

I’d like to make a reservation please.

Staff:

Certainly. May I have your name please?

Patron:

Yes, this is Tim Bradley.

Staff:

Thank you Mr. Bradley. And for how many people and what day and time?

Patron:

For four people. For this Saturday at 6PM

Staff:

So a table for four this Saturday at 6PM. Is this correct?

Patron:

Yes. That’s right.

Staff:

Thank you, Mr. Bradley. See you on Saturday evening.
