ASKING THE CUSTOMER'S NEEDS
BIZ TIP

Once you understand your customer’s needs, you can offer the right product. You can do this by:

  • Asking questions about their needs and why
  • Showing you understand their needs
  • Asking what their budget is
    • (At a multimedia store)
      • Hello. May I help you?
      • I’m OK, thanks.
      • Okay…
    • (At a multimedia store)
      • Hello. Are you looking for new headphones?
      • Yes, I am.
      • Are they for listening to music, gaming, or teleconferencing?
      • Oh, mostly for teleconferencing for work.
      • I see. So, you’d like something that has good sound quality and noise-cancelling feature?
      • Yes, that’s right.
      • And do you have a budget in mind?
      • Well, under 5,000 yen would be good.
      • I see. Let me show you our premium headsets that are great for everyday use…
    • Answer these questions.
      • Do you think it’s important to ask if your customers need your help?
      • What do you do when they say they don’t need your help?
      • What other question examples can you use to offer help to customers?
ACT

Role play.

Imagine your customer needs some help. Ask what they need or want, and why. Then ask if they have a budget or specific product in mind.
