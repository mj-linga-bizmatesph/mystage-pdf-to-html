HANDLING CONCERNS
BIZ TIP

A customer’s concern about your product is an opportunity to educate your customer. You can do this by:

  • Confirming the reason for their concern
  • Confirming your understanding
  • Educating your customer
    • (At a sports shop)
      • I like these running shoes – but I think the outsole is too bulky.
      • I see.
      • …
      • We only have this design for that brand.
    • (At a sports shop)
      • I like these running shoes – but I think the outsole is too bulky.
      • I see. Do you mean you don’t like the design?
      • No, I don’t care about the design. They just look difficult to run in.
      • So, do you mean you are worried that they are uncomfortable?
      • Yes, that’s right.
      • This shoe was designed to give maximum comfort and durability. So, it’s very comfortable even for high-impact running and on uneven surfaces. Let me also give you our brochure.
    • Answer these questions.
      • What are some common objections your customers have?
      • Do you agree or disagree with their objections?
      • What information can you provide to give them reassurance?
ACT

Role play.

Handle objections from your customer. Confirm the reason for their objections, confirm your understanding, and provide reassurance about your product or service.
