EXPLAINING SALES AND REWARD PROGRAMS
BIZ TIP

Sales and reward programs can be used to encourage a purchase. You can do this by:

  • Introducing the sale to interest the customer
  • Explaining the details
  • Showing its savings with a clear example
    • (At a souvenir shop)
      • Hello, we have our Summer Sale today.
      • I see.
      • Please let me know if you have any questions.
      • Okay, thanks.
    • (At a souvenir shop)
      • Hello, we have our Summer Sale today. We only have this sale one day each month.
      • I see.
      • Everything in this area is priced at 1,000 yen. And these items are 1,500 yen.
      • 1,000 and 1,500 yen? That’s great.
      • Yes, it’s a very good deal. For example, the original price for this traditional Japanese hand fan is 3,800 yen, but today it’s only 1,000 yen.
      • That is a good deal.
      • Also, if you get our free member’s card, you can get an extra 5% off all items in our store. So, the fan will be only 950 yen.
      • OK, maybe I’ll get a couple for my friends.
    • Answer these questions.
      • What sales or reward programs does your store offer?
      • What are the details of these sales and reward program?
      • What examples can you give to explain the details?
ACT

Role play.

Offer your store’s sale or reward program to a customer. Explain the details and give clear examples.
