DEALING WITH RETURNS
BIZ TIP

When accepting a return, rather than automatically giving a cash refund, keep the customer by:

  • Asking the reason for the return
  • Offering a different item as an exchange
  • Asking for the receipt
    • (At a clothing store)
      • Hi. I bought this T-shirt yesterday, but I’d like to return it.
      • OK, receipt please.
    • (At a clothing store)
      • Hi. I bought this T-shirt yesterday, but I’d like to return it.
      • Oh, was there a problem with the shirt?
      • Well, I just didn’t like the design when I tried it on at home.
      • I’m sorry to hear that. Would you like to exchange it for another item? We have many new designs in the back.
      • Can I exchange it for a different item instead of a T-shirt?
      • Certainly. You may choose any item you’d like in our store.
      • OK, in that case, maybe I’ll get a hat instead.
      • Yes, our hats are right over here. And may I have your receipt please?
      • Sure, here you go.
    • Answer these questions.
      • What is your store’s return policy?
      • What are some items that customers sometimes return to your store?
      • What are their reasons for returning items?
ACT

Role play.

Imagine a customer is returning an item that he or she purchased the day before. Ask the customer for the reason for returning the item, offer another item as an exchange, and ask for the receipt.
