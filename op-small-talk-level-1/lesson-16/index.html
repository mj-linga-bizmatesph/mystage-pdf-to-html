SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to time?

  • It seems we got here early. Let’s kill time at the bookstore until they arrive.
  • They stayed with us for a fortnight before heading back home.
  • Nice pocket watch. Where did you get that timepiece?
  • I need to run an errand, but I’ll be back in a jiffy.
▼▼▼
TRY

Let’s talk about time. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • How do you spend your time at home? At work?
  • Do you always have the time for leisure after work?
  • How do you usually spend your time alone?
  • What’s your quality time with the family like?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • How valuable is another person’s time to you?
  • Do you prefer having a flexible time schedule or a rigid one?
  • Where do you spend most of your time? At home or in the office?
  • How do you feel when someone doesn’t seem to value your time?
