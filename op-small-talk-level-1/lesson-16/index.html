SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to time?

  1. It seems we got here early. Let’s kill time at the bookstore until they arrive.
  2. They stayed with us for a fortnight before heading back home.
  3. Nice pocket watch. Where did you get that timepiece?
  4. I need to run an errand, but I’ll be back in a jiffy.
▼▼▼
TRY

Let’s talk about retirement. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  1. How do you spend your time at home? At work?
  2. Do you always have the time for leisure after work?
  3. How do you usually spend your time alone?
  4. What’s your quality time with the family like?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  1. How valuable is another person’s time to you?
  2. Do you prefer having a flexible time schedule or a rigid one?
  3. Where do you spend most of your time? At home or in the office?
  4. How do you feel when someone doesn’t seem to value your time?
