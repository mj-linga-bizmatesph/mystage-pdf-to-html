BIZ TIP
Rather than focusing on individual words, group words together to sound more natural.
Q: Where are you going with your friends tonight?
A: We | are | going | to | watch | a | movie | at | my | parents’ | house.
A: We’re going | to watch a movie | at my parents’ house.
SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to friendship?

  • We seldom meet at our favorite hang-out. I guess everyone’s really busy.
  • I miss my old chum. We’ve been through a lot since childhood.
  • I don’t mind asking my dad for advice. He’s always been my confidant.
  • We were partners in crime back in high school and did some crazy things.
▼▼▼
TRY

Let’s talk about friends. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • Are most of your friends men or women?
  • How long has it been since you’ve last seen an old friend?
  • Do you consider yourself popular among your friends?
  • Have you ever had a conflict with your best friend?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • Do you like meeting new friends?
  • Name 3 words that best describe your best friend.
  • How often do you go out with your friends?
  • What do you usually do when you go out with them?
