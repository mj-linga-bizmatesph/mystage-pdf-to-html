BIZ TIP
To improve your vocabulary, rather than learning single words, try to memorize the entire phrase with the word being used. amass = to gather or collect
a. You can amass a lot of information by reading the encyclopedia.
b. You can amass a lot of money by... (try to continue the sentence)
SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to books?

  • I hate when film adaptations are very different from the original book.
  • You may not believe this but the bully at school is a bookworm.
  • They’re all throwing the book at me for something I didn’t even do.
  • She’s having a hard time because her new boss always goes by the book.
▼▼▼
TRY

Let’s talk about sports. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • What’s a good book you can recommend?
  • What book has had a significant impact on your life?
  • Who are some famous authors in your country?
  • What’s a book you’ve recently read?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • How long does it usually take you to finish reading a book?
  • What book do you recommend?
  • What book will you read next?
  • Do you have any favorite authors?
