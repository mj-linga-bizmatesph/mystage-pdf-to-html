TOPIC 16: HISTORY
16
BIZ TIP
To continue a conversation, listen well, respond appropriately and ask a question.
Q: Are you interested in history?
A: No, I’m not.
A: Well, not really because I never really studied it. How about you?
SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to time?

  • I didn’t know my grandfather was an adventurer until I read his memoirs.
  • This milestone will be recorded in the annals of our company’s history.
  • I met my wife back in college, and the rest is history.
  • The new manager seems OK. But history repeats itself sometimes.
▼▼▼
TRY

Let’s talk about history. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • Do you know the history of how your country was founded?
  • What are some historical sites you’ve visited in your country?
  • What are the typical things you can see in museums in your country?
  • What do you know about your family history? Company history?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • How well do you know your company’s history?
  • Are workers obliged to know your company’s history?
  • Are you interested in learning the history of someone or something?
  • What historical places have you been to?
▼▼▼
ACT

What are your thoughts about this quote?

"History is a pack of lies about events that never happened told by people who weren't there."

-- George Santayana (poet)
