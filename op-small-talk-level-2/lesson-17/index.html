TOPIC 17: STRESS MANAGEMENT

BIZ TIP
Non-verbal cues like facial expressions or gestures are just as important. Without them, it would be easy to misinterpret what you mean.
Try using non-verbal cues on these sentences:
a: You should have given me a call. (irritated)
b: You should have given me a call. (concerned)
SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to stress management?

Try using non-verbal cues on these sentences:

  • My dad will blow a fuse when he finds out I wrecked his car.
  • Finally, the project’s over. Time to kick back and relax!
  • What’s all the fuss about? We’re just talking about the font size.
  • It isn’t time to unwind yet. There are a lot of things to do around here.

TRY

Let’s talk about stress management. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • What tasks put you under a lot of stress at work? At home?
  • How do men/women manage stress in general in your country?
  • Are there many stress management services in your country?
  • Have you tried any of these services to help you manage stress?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • Do you prefer to be alone when you’re stressed out?
  • Do you know anyone who makes a lot of fuss about everything?
  • Has there been a time when you couldn’t handle stress anymore?
  • How do you usually behave whenever that happens?
