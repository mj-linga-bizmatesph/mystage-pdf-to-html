BIZ TIP
Sometimes you might not want to answer a question. In this case, you can use these phrases.
I'd better not comment on that.
I'm sorry but I'm in no position to answer that.
Hmm, I'll have to get back to you on that one.
SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to cross culture?

  • Canada’s cultural mosaic consists of various colorful cultural identities.
  • America is a melting pot where people learned to adopt other cultures.
  • Not all Americans wear shorts and T-shirts when they travel. That’s such a stereotypical comment.
  • Ken had a culture shock when he went out with his foreign colleagues.
▼▼▼
TRY

Let’s talk about cross culture. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • What are some typical cross culture issues?
  • Have you experienced any culture shock during your trips overseas?
  • Which cultural aspect do you think is far different from yours?
  • Would you adopt a foreign country’s culture?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • How important is cultural preservation to you?
  • Do you know anyone who has adopted a foreign culture?
  • In what ways does your culture differ from Western culture?
  • What Western cultures have been adopted in your country?
▼▼▼
ACT

What are your thoughts about this quote?

"Preservation of one's own culture does not require contempt or disrespect for other cultures."

-- Cesar Chavez (activist)
