BIZ TIP
If the other person goes off on a tangent and you want them to get back to your question, you can use these phrases.
Just to get back to my question.
So, what I really want to know is…
Getting back to what I asked you earlier,…
SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to success?

  • Awards were given to the cream of the crop in the Sales Department.
  • John is a great employee. I respect his winning spirit.
  • As they say: no pain no gain. I really want to win the tournament.
  • She’s pulling an all-nighter to submit an important report tomorrow.
▼▼▼
TRY

Let’s talk about success. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • How is success defined in your culture?
  • How do you measure success? (achievement, position, money, etc…)
  • When do you consider someone to be successful?
  • Do you consider yourself successful in your line of business/work?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • Could you name a few people who are successful in your industry?
  • How important is success to you?
  • Is it right to confine the meaning of success only to business?
  • What kind of people are successful in your company?

ACT
