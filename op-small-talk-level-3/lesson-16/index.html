TOPIC 16: COFFEE
16
BIZ TIP
Some people are hesitant to speak up. You can encourage participation by highlighting something they are familiar with before asking your question.
So you studied medicine. Is caffeine really that addictive?
So you’re from Seattle. Is Starbucks really big there?
SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to coffee?

  • The aroma fills the air with a sweet wonderful scent.
  • The barista asked me a lot of things, when all I wanted was just black coffee.
  • It has full-bodied flavor and delicate bitterness. Just the way I like it.
  • Flights have been cancelled due to a storm brewing in the Pacific.
▼▼▼
TRY

Let’s talk about coffee. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • Are there a lot of coffee lovers in your country?
  • Personally, what do you like and don’t like about coffee?
  • What’s the most popular coffee shop/company in your country?
  • Is coffee more popular than tea in your culture?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • How old were you when you started drinking coffee?
  • What time of day do you have coffee?
  • Have you ever experienced what it feels like when you drink too much coffee?
  • Have you ever tried canned coffee?
▼▼▼
ACT

What are your thoughts about this quote?

“My coffee gets increasingly better the more I drink and the closer I come to the bottom of the cup, where all the sugar is. I wonder if life is the same way as we approach the end.”

-- Jarod Kintz (author)
