GREYING SOCIETIES
BIZ TIP
  • life expectancy – average number of years one is expected to live
  • utmost – greatest or most extreme amount of
  • golden years – the last one third of someone’s life, generally between 65 and 80+
  • inevitable – unavoidable and inescapable
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

In some developed countries, life expectancy is continuing to increase while birth rates are on a decline. Though many support advancements in technology and medicine, some may view an overly aged society as a sign of troubling times ahead.

Ben:
I have nothing but the utmost respect for my elders. However, something has to be said about the kind of impact they’re having on health care and welfare costs. It wouldn’t be so bad if we had more young people to support the aged, but that just isn’t the case. I don’t have any answers, but I’m worried about how the government is going to handle it.
Carl:
There’s one thing in this world that doesn’t discriminate and that’s time. We all age equally. Now that I’m in my golden years, I’d like to believe that the society I contributed so much to has the decency to support an old man. Sure it’s inconvenient and expensive, but when you get to my age, every little bit helps.

Many would agree that the contributions the elderly have made to our communities over the course of their lives is nothing short of extraordinary. Though costs are an inevitable part of the discussion, most would agree that no price is too steep when it comes to the lives of those we care about.
