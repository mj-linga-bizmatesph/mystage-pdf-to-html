WEALTH & STATUS
WORDS & PHRASES
  • capitalist – an economic system driven by profit and free market competition
  • notion – an idea, belief, or opinion about something
  • The be-all and end-all – the most important thing or factor
  • unscalable – something that cannot be climbed
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

In capitalist societies, one’s worth is often associated with material wealth. Although the value of a person cannot be determined by wealth alone, it is an undeniable fact that many view money as power.

Gabriel:
I’ve always been brought up with the notion that I am what I wear. There is truth in what they say about being able to tell a lot about a person by their watch and shoes. In my society, status is everything. If you have money and position, you can do and have anything.
Meg:
I hate that money is the be-all and end-all. The only way to advance as a species is to move past our petty individualistic ambitions and strive towards greater harmony and compassion. The blind pursuit of money divides us, creates conflict, and causes more harm than good.

Status and wealth are powerful influences in modern society. It is a master key for some, and an unscalable wall for others. Although it’s easy to put a price on goods, there are some things in life that can’t be so easily bought.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. Do you think everything has a price?
  2. What are some of the key differences between those who are wealthy and those who are not?
  3. Do large amounts of money change people? Would it change you?
  4. Aside from wealth, how else would you ascribe status to people?
