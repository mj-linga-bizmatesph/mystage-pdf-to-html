IMMIGRATION REFORM
WORDS & PHRASES
  • confront – to face and deal with a problem or difficulty
  • have something against – oppose or dislike something
  • status quo – current social situation or state of affairs
  • irrefutable – something that cannot be denied or opposed
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

In our globalized world, immigration has become the focal point of many debates and political platforms. As a result, many nations are now expected to confront the reality that an open-door policy may be the only way to meaningful growth.

Kat:
I don’t have anything against other peoples and cultures, but I personally prefer to look after our own. There are reasons why we’ve become such a powerful nation, and I don’t think bringing in more and more immigrants and refugees will do us any good. I say keep them out, and let’s protect our own people and interests.
Mina:
Growth has never come from maintaining the status quo. This includes immigration. A community that leverages the strengths of various peoples and cultures is stronger than one that doesn’t. There is strength in diversity, and compassion in making those different from us become our neighbors.

There is no easy answer when it comes to protecting national interests. However, the irrefutable fact remains that the world’s greatest resource isn’t oil, gas, or precious metals, but rather the people that inhabit it and the talent they possess.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. Why are some nations and people opposed to increasing immigration?
  2. What do you think are some of the biggest challenges immigrants face in your country?
  3. How would increased immigration impact your country from economic and cultural standpoints?
  4. Why do people choose to immigrate? Is there anything that would cause you to leave your country?
