THE (DIS)ADVANTAGE OF BEAUTY
WORDS & PHRASES
  • clear-cut – simple or specific
  • detest – strongly dislike
  • ogle (someone) – to stare at (someone) in an intrusive or impolite manner
  • veil – something that conceals or hides something from view
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

One’s appearance is viewed as either an advantage or a disadvantage. In a world obsessed with appearance, one may believe beauty is an advantage worth having. Fortunately or unfortunately, it’s not so clear-cut.

Julia:
I detest beautiful people. They think the world is nicer than it really is. All they need to do is bat their eyelashes or flex their muscles, and the door magically opens. I’ve worked hard to get to where I am today not because of my looks, but in spite of them. Nothing was ever handed to me.
Lee:
Did you see the way all the guys were ogling her? Even our executives had their tongues hanging out. The last thing we need are those kinds of distractions at the office. If it were up to me, I wouldn’t have hired her in the first place. I doubt that she has anything meaningful to offer anyways.

Is beauty a blessing or a curse? Some view it as a key to all the doors, whereas others see it as a veil that keeps people from seeing beneath the surface. Whatever it is, many agree that beauty is more than just skin-deep.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. What is your definition of beauty?
  2. What kind of stereotypes do beautiful people face?
  3. Do current beauty standards do more harm than good?
  4. Can you think of any situations in which beauty can be viewed as a disadvantage?
