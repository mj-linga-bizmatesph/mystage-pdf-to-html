SOCIAL ANXIETY & ISOLATION
WORDS & PHRASES
  • mock – to tease or laugh at in a hurtful manner
  • recluse – someone who lives a solitary life and avoids social contact
  • freak – a person who is viewed as abnormal or very strange
  • dismiss – to treat or view something as unworthy, unimportant, or of no use
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

One may be surprised to know that in a city of millions, there are many that feel like they just don’t belong. As a result, many seek isolation where there is no one to judge, mock, or criticize. Society can be a cold place, and sometimes, stepping out the door requires more courage than some expect.

Kenneth:
I can relate. There are times when I just want to escape, sit in my room, and tune everyone and everything out. The world outside can be so tiring and there are times when I just can’t be bothered to put on a face and deal with it. It’s not like I want to be an outcast or recluse or anything, I’m just saying, we all need a break sometimes.
Jen:
I don’t know why but I just can’t seem to get it right with people. For whatever reason I just can’t get my mouth to say what my brain is thinking. When I’m finally able to form the words, they always come out awkward, strange, and I look like a freak. I’m trying really hard, but I’m just so nervous all the time. I just want people to like me.

Though there are many reasons why active social participation is challenging, one cannot dismiss the fact that the one thing many of us fear is rejection. The need to be liked by others often drives us to do the things we do, the things we don’t, and the things we wish we could.
