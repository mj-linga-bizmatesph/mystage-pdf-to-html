AGE DISCRIMINATION
WORDS & PHRASES
  • arbitrary – something seemingly random or meaningless
  • deemed – to be considered or viewed a certain way
  • creak – a squeaking noise something makes when it moves or is under pressure
  • infinite – limitless and without end
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

Some believe that age is just a number – an arbitrary figure meant to represent experience, knowledge, and potential. However true that may be, we can all agree that age is less about how we appear and more about how we feel.

Mai:
I’m 32, single, and changing careers. So what? Why is everyone so shocked? It isn’t fair that I have to live my life according to other people’s timelines and standards. Why am I deemed a failure or no longer desirable because of a number? My age and experience are assets, not embarrassments.
Ron:
My body aches, my bones creak, and my memory isn’t as good as it used to be, but I sure don’t feel like slowing down. It feels strange that I have more energy in my old age than when I was a young man at the office. I wish people could see that. All they notice now are the grey hairs and wrinkles…but I am more.

There are old souls in young bodies and passionate youth in the aged. Although age reflects the number of years one has been alive, it is a poor measure of the infinite complexities of the human spirit.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. Why do you think people are sometimes ashamed to admit their age?
  2. Do you think your real age matches how old you feel? Why or why not?
  3. Have you or someone you know experienced discrimination because of their age? What happened?
  4. Have you ever been impressed by someone who was either very young or very old? What happened?
