JOB HOPPING
WORDS & PHRASES
  • bygone era – a period of time in the past
  • jump ship – to abandon a group, company, or task especially when there is difficulty faced
  • stay the course – to continue doing something even when it is hard
  • change of pace – a change in one’s life, environment, or routine to add variety
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

For many young people, the idea of a lifetime career is an idea from a bygone era. More and more people are starting to consider job hopping the norm and an effective way of boosting experience, connections, and compensation. However, some still view this behavior as something less than appealing.

Nikolai:
I could never stay in one place for more than a few years, max. It’s not that I didn’t like my jobs, it’s just that I felt I learned all I could and it was time to move on and jump ship. I’m like a shark, I gotta keep moving or I’ll die. I look at people doing the same thing for years and years and years and I wonder to myself, why?
Jorge:
In society there are a few things that really matter. One of them is loyalty. Loyalty to your family, your friends, and to your job. That is a sign of a responsible person. I’m not sure why people are jumping around like rabbits. I’ve always been told to stay the course, and that’s what I’ve done with every aspect of my life. And I’m proud of that.

Many would agree that one’s career plays a huge role in defining who they are as people. Although one may be defined by their career choice, we can all agree that sometimes, we all need a change of pace, and finding oneself often requires a few, if not many, changes.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. Would you say people are changing their careers more often in your country? Others? Why?
  2. Do many people come and go in your company? How long do you plan to stay in your current job?
  3. Are there any benefits to staying with one job or company for more than 5 or 10 years?
  4. What would it take for you to consider a change in career, job, or company?
