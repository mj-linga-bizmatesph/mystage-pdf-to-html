THE WORKING POOR
WORDS & PHRASES
  • make ends meet – to earn the minimum amount needed to survive
  • keep one’s head above water – to survive a difficult financial situation
  • in the black – profitable
  • a dime a dozen – very common and worthy of little attention or value
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

Making ends meet is as challenging as ever. Shifts in corporate practices, government policies, and numerous social problems have made the pursuit of a stable life with dependable income a challenge for many.

Jim:
I grew up in a simple but loving family. My parents worked hard but we were always struggling to keep our heads above water. Now, I’m stuck in the same cycle as my parents. I’m no university graduate or fancy business man - but I’m honest, and I work hard. I wish that was enough.
Kenneth:
We all have our bottom lines. I know people are in difficult times, but it’s my job to protect corporate interests. If I could give everyone full-time positions and raises, I would. The simple fact is we need to watch our risks and make sure we come out the other side in the black. Otherwise, everyone loses.

Although unfortunate circumstances are a dime a dozen, some believe that hard work can overcome any obstacle. However, one might be surprised to discover that some of the hardest workers are often the poorest.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. What societal issues and corporate practices contribute to the proliferation of the working poor?
  2. What is the average income of people in your country? What is considered the poverty line?
  3. Some say the rich get richer while the poor stay poor. Do you think this is correct?
  4. What’s your view on the issue of companies needing to cut costs versus employees needing to earn?
