THE INDIVIDUAL VERSUS SOCIETY
WORDS & PHRASES
  • go against the grain – to be contrary or opposite to the natural state or feeling of someone or something
  • chaos – complete disorder and confusion
  • rigid – inflexible; cannot be bent or adapted
  • suffocate – to have difficulty breathing or to feel trapped and oppressed
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

When our values go against the grain, we face conflict not only within ourselves but also all around us. However, it is within this conflict that we are able to determine who we really are, what matters most, and the strength of our resolve.

Joe:
If everyone ran around doing whatever they wanted, it’d be chaos. It’s because of our laws and cultural norms that we are able to live in such a peaceful society. We don’t always get what we want, but it’s that kind of sacrifice that makes us responsible adults who can contribute to our society and the greater good.
Florence:
It’s not that I feel I don’t belong, it’s just that I want to be more. I look around me and I see…sheep – people bound by customs and mindsets forced upon them by a society too slow and rigid to change. If we want to reach our full potential as individuals, we need to question things, challenge our surroundings, and expand our horizons.

The struggle to define oneself is a journey that can take a lifetime of experience. Though some may feel they are being suffocated by overwhelming societal pressures, one is often defined by their reaction to their environment and their ability or inability to find their voice in the crowd.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. Have you ever felt as if you didn’t belong? Why or why not?
  2. What aspects of your society do you find difficult to accept?
  3. How important is individualistic expression in your country, and is it encouraged? Why or why not?
  4. How have people’s attitudes changed over the past few generations?
