INTERNATIONAL MARRIAGES
WORDS & PHRASES
  • disillusioned – having lost faith or belief in something that was once considered important
  • fall for someone – to develop romantic feelings for someone
  • be dead set against – to oppose or forbid (something) strongly
  • transcend – to go beyond a limit
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

Many consider love to be one of life’s greatest pursuits, yet one of life’s biggest challenges. It’s an uphill battle littered with the disillusioned and broken hearted, yet the pain of failure is a risk many are willing to take regardless of age, gender, or nationality.

Yong:
My daughter said she’s fallen for a foreigner. How can you love someone so different from your own people? Can’t she find someone from the same country? Sure, he might be a nice person, but I am worried for her. It’s important that we keep our family together and she marry into a good, local family. I won’t allow it.
Nathan:
Her mother is dead set against us being together. Why? I know it’s not so simple, but why interfere with and complicate something as pure as love? Shouldn’t her daughter's happiness be the most important factor? I know I’m not perfect, but I know I’d be a wonderful husband. I wish her mother would give me a chance.

Relationships, marriages, family, and acceptance have never been easy things. Though one may hope that love transcends all boundaries, the success of any relationship isn’t solely dependent on the couple’s efforts alone but also on the support of those around them.
