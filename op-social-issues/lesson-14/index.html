THE BYSTANDER EFFECT
WORDS & PHRASES
  • volatile – changing rapidly and unexpectedly for the worse
  • acting up – behaving or performing badly or inappropriately
  • inaction – choosing not to act in a situation where it is expected or necessary
  • intervention – an action resulting in the prevention of something
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

There are moments when one is called to act. There isn’t the luxury of time or the reassurance of safety, but a volatile situation that could quickly worsen if no one does anything. Does one allow this to become someone else’s responsibility, or take matters into their own hands?

Yong:
I’m no hero. I’m not looking to cause or be a part of anyone’s problems. So what? Some weird guy is acting up, big deal...Happens all the time. It just isn’t worth the trouble. I have no intention of being more than I am. If it doesn’t directly involve me, I’ll look the other way. I need to worry about myself.
Nathan:
If there’s a bad situation, it’s up to someone to do something. It’s the inaction of good people that is part of the problem. Sure, it’s scary. Sure, it might be nothing. But I would feel terrible if something did happen. These are the moments that define who we are, and I would rather act than run.

For many, the fear of acting is influenced by many factors: the seriousness of the incident, the consequences of one’s intervention, or other people’s reaction to the situation. No matter how it is perceived, the only question that matters is, what are you going to do about it?

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. Have you ever encountered a situation in which the bystander effect occurred? What happened?
  2. Can you think of any situations in which the bystander effect resulted in tragedy?
  3. In your society or culture, is the bystander effect less or more likely to occur? Why?
  4. What circumstances would prompt you to take action?
