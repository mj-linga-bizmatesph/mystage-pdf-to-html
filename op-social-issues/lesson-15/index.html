DECLINING BIRTH RATES
WORDS & PHRASES
  • sacrifice – something that is given up in order to get or attain something else
  • spontaneity – the occurrence of something quickly or suddenly without the need for careful planning
  • in a heartbeat – instantly or immediately
  • child rearing – the raising or taking care of children
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

More and more people in developed societies are choosing not to have children. The costs, commitment, and changes associated with parenthood are serious barriers that make many wonder if having children is really worth the sacrifice.

Lea:
All my life I’ve been an independent person, doing what I want, when I want. I have a dream of my future and it simply doesn’t involve me being a mother. It’s not that I don’t like children, but I want to be in control of what I do with my own life. I don’t want to lose the ability to…just go. I need spontaneity and spark more than I need kids.
Mel:
I’ve always wanted kids. In today’s world, people want more money, want more time, want more everything. But I’d give it up in a heartbeat so I can watch my children eat ice cream for the first time, teach my son how to talk to girls, or watch my baby grow up to be a strong, beautiful woman. I need my life to be more than just being about me.

Having a family and child rearing have never been easy tasks. It’s a lifetime commitment that requires much more than just time and money. As a result, some may be more suited for parenthood than others, yet whatever choice one makes, we are all responsible for our own happiness.

  2. What do you think are the most important factors to consider before having children?
  3. What can your company, the government, or society do to improve the situation?
  4. How does having children change one’s life?
