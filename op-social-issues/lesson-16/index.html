GROUP MENTALITY & CONFLICT
WORDS & PHRASES
  • vilify – to speak unpleasant things about someone or something in order to create a bad impression
  • rough – very hard or challenging
  • motivator – something that provides a strong purpose or reason
  • relentless – something harsh, severe, or intense
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

As social creatures, we have the need to identify with and belong to social groups. Although this group mentality eventually provides a sense of belonging, it also results in the vilifying of those that are different and the inciting of conflict where there was once just the desire to belong.

Yong:
I moved around a lot, growing up. It was rough always being the new kid, but I learned a lot about people and how to fit in quickly. One thing I can say for sure is that people love to love the same things…but love to hate the same things even more. Strange how people need to feed that part of themselves.
Nathan:
We are a million different people pulling in a million different directions. We need conflict to give us direction and focus. We need enemies and rivals to push and drive us. Whether that be another person, company, or country, conflict has always been humanity’s most effective motivator.

Humans are inherently social, yet relentlessly self-destructive and ironically selective in our inclusiveness. Though there is growth in conflict, so too is there value in realizing that we are far more similar than we are different. We are, after all, human.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. What groups do you associate yourself with? What does it mean to be a part of these groups?
  2. Who are considered your (your group’s) rivals? Why do you consider them as such?
  3. Do you think humanity is more competitive than it is cooperative?
  4. Do you feel society can ever move past the need for conflict? Or is it human nature?
