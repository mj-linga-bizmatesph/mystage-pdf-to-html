MEDIA & TRUTH
WORDS & PHRASES
  • clean-cut – neat and respectable in appearance
  • ignorance – lack of awareness or knowledge
  • Big Brother – a powerful government or organization that monitors its civilians
  • agenda – a masked or undisclosed plan often related to certain ideological beliefs
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

As a society, we look to newspapers, television programs, and clean-cut anchors to deliver news we deem trustworthy. The unfortunate reality is that truth comes at a cost. And sometimes, the cost is more than some are willing to pay.

Thomas:
People believe whatever is being said in the media without a second thought. That is the most dangerous kind of ignorance; the kind that is viewed as informed. I’m not some crazy conspiracy theorist, but I think we can all agree there’s power in information, and more importantly, misinformation.
Hugo:
I’m okay with Big Brother filtering the news that gets out to the masses. There’s a lot of information out there that could do more harm than good. They’re the Government for a reason, and I believe they have our best interests at heart. I live a simple life. I don’t need to know everything.

In a world filled with political, personal, and economic agendas, it’s difficult to know what to believe. However, by staying informed, asking the right questions, and discussing the issues with various people we can ensure that we remain properly informed and truly aware.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. Are there any biases in your news sources?
  2. Have you ever felt misinformed or misled by certain reports or stories?
  3. In what case do you feel censorship or misinformation is appropriate?
  4. Some say that the truth hurts. Can you think of any examples where this is true?
