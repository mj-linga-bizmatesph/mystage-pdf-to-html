WORK-LIFE BALANCE
WORDS & PHRASES
  • daunting – something difficult or intimidating to handle
  • make do – to manage or handle something even with limited resources
  • waste away – to lose energy, power, motivation and become weak
  • detrimental – harmful or damaging
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

It has been said that working professionals spend a third of their lives at the office. It comes as no surprise then that many find balancing their private lives and professional careers challenging, daunting, and near impossible to achieve.

Yolanda:
My biggest responsibility is to my family, and as ironic as it may sound, that is why I work sixty hours a week. True, I don’t get to see them as much as I would like, but we make do. I work hard to provide them with a house, education, food, and all the comforts that I can afford. I wish I could do both, but I can’t be in two places at once.
Pietro:
As soon as that clock hits 6:00PM, I’m out the door. The company pays me to be here for 8 hours and that’s what I’ll do. Life is short. I don’t want to waste away behind a desk while my family is waiting for me, while the sun is out, and while I’m still young enough to enjoy things. We spend so much time at work as it is; no need to be there more.

Balance is important in all aspects of life. Too much of any one thing can be detrimental to one’s health and happiness. Though professional obligations are often prioritized over more personal commitments, productivity has been closely linked with successful work-life balance.
