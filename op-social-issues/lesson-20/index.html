AVOIDING SENSITIVE TOPICS
WORDS & PHRASES
  • worldview – a particular view, philosophy, or conception of the world
  • mum on – to be silent, secretive, or tight-lipped about
  • minefield – an area with planted explosives, or a situation with many unseen dangers
  • complacency – a feeling of uncritical self-satisfaction or self-admiration
SEE

Read the following and take note of the highlighted phrases.

There are many topics that are viewed as being too sensitive, risky, and difficult to discuss openly. However, the most meaningful conversations, the ones that form our worldview, lead to growth and define who we are, are often the most difficult to have.

Jin:
There are so many topics I’m curious about, so many things I don’t understand. I just don’t know how I should bring them up. There are a lot of issues I’m mum on because I’m afraid I’m going to offend someone, but isn’t this going to make things worse? Issues like politics, religion, and gender identity affect millions, and I shouldn’t even talk about it…
Ken:
It’s a minefield out there. There’s a lot more at risk than just one person’s idea of social justice. Being politically correct and knowing how to play it safe are good things. Humans can be emotional, volatile, and easy to offend. Though we hope for the best in people, the truth is, there are no guarantees. Better safe than sorry, right?

Fear of sensitive topics, inopportunity, and complacency result in many misunderstandings, conflicts, and damaged relationships. However, when mutual understanding and respect are valued over individualistic agendas, the path forward doesn’t seem as risky as one may have initially believed.

TRY

Answer the following questions:

  1. What kind of issues have you wanted to discuss but were afraid of broaching?
  2. What did you hope to learn from having those discussions?
  3. Is it always the best to play it safe? Why or why not?
  4. Have you ever encountered a situation in which someone was talking about a controversial topic? What did you learn?
