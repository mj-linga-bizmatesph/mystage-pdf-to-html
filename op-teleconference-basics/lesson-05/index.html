PART 1

Review the Biz tips with your trainer and make sure you are comfortable with the expressions and concepts.

Scheduling your teleconference is the first step. Use the following expressions to help you:

  • I’m writing to schedule our next…
  • The details are as follows…
  • If anyone has anything to add, please…

Responding to invitations is just as important as scheduling. Use the following expressions to help you:

  • I’d be delighted to…
  • Could I have 10 minutes to…?
  • I’m looking forward to speaking with everyone on…

Prepare for your teleconference by creating an agenda. Use the following expressions to help you:

  • Is there anything you'd like to add to the agenda?
  • How much time do you need?
  • Do you need anyone to prepare anything?

Ensure your e-mails are short, simple, and easy to understand by using the 5-sentence rule:

  • A pleasantry: Thank you for making the time to…
  • Reason for writing: Here are the details of…
  • Summary of main points or bullet points: Date, Time, Ext code, Topic, Agenda
  • Call to action: Please e-mail me by…
  • Closing: Have a great day…
PART 2

Use everything you’ve learned to effectively schedule a meeting.

  1. Schedule a meeting with your counterpart and find a day that is best for everyone.
  2. Prepare an agenda and ask your partner if they'd like to add anything.
  3. end a confirmation e-mail to all participants.
