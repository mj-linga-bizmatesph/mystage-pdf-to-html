13
GETTING CLARIFICATION
BIZ TIP

Miscommunication can sometimes occur during teleconferences. Make sure no important information is missed by using the expressions below:

  • Sorry, I don’t follow. Allow me to clarify. You mean…
  • Sorry, I’m a bit lost. Let me confirm…
  • So you mean to say that…
SEE

Ken is trying to clarify the reason why their shipment has been delayed with his counterpart…

Pat
Our current supplier didn’t say that they wouldn’t be able to deliver within the week. They said they wouldn’t be able to guarantee it.
Ken
Sorry, I don’t follow. Allow me to clarify. You mean they can’t guarantee delivery this week?
Pat
Yes, that’s right. They also said they would have been able to if they’d received the order sooner.
Ken
Sorry, I’m a bit lost. Let me confirm. The supplier feels that it’s our problem for ordering the materials too late?
Pat
Yes, that’s right. So they don’t want to accept the blame.
Ken
So you mean to say that the supplier will not guarantee delivery and they feel it’s our fault? That’s unbelievable. Their system caused…
TRY

Read the following sentences below and ask your trainer to clarify the meaning.

SENTENCE MEANING
We need everyone to pitch in. We need everyone’s help and cooperation.
The upgrade will cost an arm and a leg.
The contract details are still up in the air.
Negotiations were a piece of cake.
ACT

Imagine that your trainer is your colleague and he/she wants to set up a meeting with you. Find out the purpose of the meeting, the schedule of the meeting, and the attendees.
