SCHEDULING A TELECONFERENCE
BIZ TIP

When scheduling a teleconference, always keep the following in mind:

  • provide alternative dates to choose from
  • specify a time zone when setting up the time as basis
  • include the objectives and a clear agenda in your email
SEE

Take a look at this BAD example.

To:
Ken@MMB.com.jp; john@MMB.com.ca; james@MMB.com.au; sanjay@MMB.com.in
Subject:
Project Z: Planning stage – Please confirm by April 18
Dear Project Team,

I am writing because I’d like to schedule a teleconference with you so we could talk about a few things. Let’s hold the teleconference on April 28 at 10 am. The details below will outline our agenda:

  1. Introductions
  2. Objectives
  3. Brainstorming
  4. Q&A
  5. Wrap Up

Please confirm. Thank you.

Regards,
Taro Sato
TRY

Refer to the sample email above and answer these questions:

1. What can you do to improve the way Taro scheduled the teleconference?
2. What would you say to provide an alternative date?
3. What would you say to make the objectives and agenda clear?
