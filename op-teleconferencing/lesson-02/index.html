CONFIRMING THE SCHEDULE
BIZ TIP

Inform all the participants as soon as the schedule is finalized along with the following:

  • a timetable shown in different time zones for the participants
  • handouts or visual aids for the teleconference
  • access codes if you plan on using a bridge

World time zone reference: www.timeanddate.com/worldclock/converter.html

SEE

Take a look at this BAD example.

To:
Ken@MMB.com.jp; john@MMB.com.ca; james@MMB.com.au; sanjay@MMB.com.in
Subject:
Project Z: Planning stage – Teleconference on April 28
Dear Project Team,

Thank you for sending in your confirmation. Our teleconference is now set on April 28 at 10:00 am.

Your access codes to the bridge are as follows:

  • Ken – 4545
  • John – 3621
  • James – 9874
  • Sanjay - 5656

Thank you.

Regards,
Taro Sato
TRY

Refer to the sample email above and answer these questions:

1. What could Taro do to avoid confusing the participants with the schedule?
2. What could Taro say to let the participants know that the handouts and visual aids are attached to this e-mail?
3. What could Taro do to avoid sharing the access codes with everyone?
