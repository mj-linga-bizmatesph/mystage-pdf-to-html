4
SETTING THE MOOD
BIZ TIP

To create a mood that will encourage participants to speak up, consider these pointers:

  • use a friendly and positive tone
  • have a brief conversation with each participant
  • make them feel that their input is valued and greatly appreciated
SEE

Example:

Taro:
OK, now that we’re done with the introductions. Let’s get started shall we?
Ken:
Yes, I’m fine with that.
John:
OK.
James:
Sure.
Sanjay:
All right, but I do have one question if you don’t mind, Taro?
Taro:
Hold that question, Sanjay. We’ll have more time for that in the Q&A.

Bizmates Style:

Taro:
I’m really excited to work closely with everyone on this project. By the way, Ken, how are things going in the Yokohama office?
Ken:
Things are great here, Taro.
Taro:
That’s good to hear, Ken. Now, John, I understand it’s around 9PM in Ontario, right?
John:
That’s right. Everyone’s gone home except for our team.
Taro:
We really appreciate that you and your team could join us today. So, James how’s the training coming along in Sydney?
James:
Things are going well. We’re doing lots of training here as usual.
Taro:
Sounds good. Before I forget, Sanjay, how are the first few weeks at the New Delhi office?
Sanjay:
Things are a bit slow, Taro, but we’re slowly picking up the pace.
Taro:
I understand, that’s usually how it is after setting up a new office. Anyway, I’m really glad you all can make it . So, shall we begin?
TRY

Make small talk and set the right mood using these following topics.

1. Weather
2. Business at the local office
3. Participants in different time zones
3. Recent events
ACT

Role play.

Engage in a brief conversation with your participants and set the right mood.
