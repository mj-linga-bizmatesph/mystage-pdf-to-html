5
GOING OVER THE AGENDA
BIZ TIP

When going over the objectives and the agenda, you should:

  • refer the participants to the handouts to make sure they can follow
  • get involvement when possible
  • ask for confirmation after going over the topics
SEE

Example:

Taro:
All right, before we begin. Let me discuss the purpose of this meeting, and then I’ll go over the agenda.
So, our objective is to come up with ideas today on how we can get this global project off the ground.
As for our agenda, our next order of business is brainstorming. That will be for 30 minutes. Afterwards we will be having a Q&A session for 15 minutes.
Finally we’ll wrap things up and that will be for 3 minutes only. OK, let’s begin.
John:
Taro, where is the agenda?

Bizmates Style:

Taro:
Before we start, does everyone have a copy of the handouts?
All:
Yes.
Taro:
Could you please look at page 1. John, could you read the objective for us?
John:
Our objective is to layout action plans for each department in this project, namely: Marketing, Human Resources, Training and Operations, Sales, and IT.
Taro:
Thanks John. We’re still in the developmental stage, so things might still change as we go along, but at least we can all start with something to work with. Let’s go to page 2 of the handout. I’ll quickly go over the items for today.

So for the next 30 minutes, we will brainstorm action plans for each department. Afterwards, we will have a Q&A session for about 15 minutes and finally summarize everything. Is this clear to everyone?
All:
Yes, all clear.
