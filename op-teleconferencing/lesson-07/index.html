7
STAYING IN CONTROL
BIZ TIP

Make sure to do the following as your discussion progresses:

  • keep track of and adhere to the agenda
  • be aware of the time spent on each item
  • be assertive when you know it’s time to move forward
SEE

Example:

Taro:
OK, James, do you think you can start training people and have them ready in 3 months?
James:
Yes, that is possible if we hire people with enough background experience, otherwise the training might take longer.
John:
I agree. I remembered when I hired someone a few years ago who didn’t have marketing experience. It was really difficult because we had to...
Taro:
Yes,…

(After 5 minutes)

Taro:
Yes,…

Bizmates Style:

Taro:
OK, James brings up a good point about hiring people with the right experience.
John:
I agree. I remembered when I hired someone a few years ago who didn’t have marketing experience. It was really difficult because we had to...
Taro:
I think we’re steering off topic. Ken, as an HR Manager, do you think it’s easy to get these kind of people?
Ken:
Yes, if our ads mention exactly what type of people we are looking for, it shouldn’t be a problem.
John:
Is that common in Japan? I thought recruitment in Japan was focused more on hiring generalists. My cousin lives in Tokyo…
Taro:
John, sorry to cut you off, but we’re running short on time, so let’s move on to the next topic on the agenda.
TRY

Engage in a discussion with your trainer using the following topics. Make sure you keep track of each item and move forward when you think it’s time.

1. Importance of learning English as a second language
2. Pros and cons of learning a second language
3. End the discussion
ACT

Role play.

Use a topic in one of your teleconferences as part of the agenda in this exercise. Monitor the progress and assertively inform your trainer that it’s time to move on to the next item.
