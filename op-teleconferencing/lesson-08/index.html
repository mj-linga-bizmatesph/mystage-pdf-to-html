PAUSING FOR QUESTIONS
BIZ TIP

When questions arise during a discussion, make sure that they are dealt with effectively by:

  • clarifying the point of the question
  • asking for a participant’s input or opinions
  • making sure others are following the discussion
SEE

Example:

Sanjay:
Taro, I have a question. How do you imagine the interface of the new software to be like?
Taro:
Hmmm... I imagine it to be globally distributed like our existing software. A license key also needs to be included.
Sanjay:
Ah, what I meant was...
Taro:
We need to start off with major cities around the world because that’s where our target market is.
Ken:
Taro, I think what Sanjay is trying to ask is how would the software look like to make it user-friendly.
Taro:
I see. Well, why didn’t you say so?

Bizmates Style:

Sanjay:
Taro, I have a question. How do you imagine the interface of the new software to be like?
Taro:
Sorry Sanjay. What do you mean by interface?
Sanjay:
I mean the new design as seen by the user.
Taro:
I see. Thank you, Sanjay. I don’t think we plan to change the design unless it’s necessary. But let’s ask the group too. What do you think, Ken?
Ken:
I think you’re right, Taro. Our designers will likely keep the original design and just make minimal changes.
Taro:
OK, thanks Ken. Is everyone following this discussion?
TRY

Engage in a discussion with your trainer using the following topics. Make sure you pause to ask relevant questions.

1. A new movie
2. A sports icon
3. Latest electronic gadgets
ACT

Role play.

Use a topic in one of your teleconferences and pause for questions.
