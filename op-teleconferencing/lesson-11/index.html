11
JOINING THE TELECONFERENCE
BIZ TIP

Whenever you join any teleconference, always make sure to:

  • find a quiet room and be on time
  • check your equipment in advance
  • introduce yourself by stating your name and department/company or geographical location
SEE

Example:

Jason:
OK, let’s start. Are you ready, Hiro?... Hiro?

(A few minutes later)

Hiro:
Hello? I’m here. I’m sorry. I got cut off.
Jason:
Hiro, it’s very noisy in the background.
Hiro:
Sorry, we’re doing renovations in the office, and I don’t have a headset.
Jason:
OK, maybe you can click the mute button for now.
Hiro:
Where is the mute button?
Jason:
It’s on the top right side of…

Bizmates Style:

Jason:
OK, let’s start. Are you ready, Hiro?...
Hiro:
Yes, I’m here. May I just say that it might be a little noisy due to renovations going on in our office? So, I’ll put my mic on mute when I’m not speaking.
Jason:
OK, thanks for letting us know, Hiro. Let’s start with introductions. Hiro, can you go first?
Hiro:
Sure, I’m Hiro Yamada, one of the Sales Specialists at MMB Tokyo, Japan office. It’s nice to meet both of you.
Luke:
Glad you could join us. I’m Luke, Lead Designer of ABC Co. Joining us on the other line is Jason, our Operations Manager.
Jason:
Hi Hiro. Jason here. We’re looking forward to your presentation.
Hiro:
Thank you. By the way, did you get a chance to read through the handouts I emailed you two days ago?
Luke:
Yes, Hiro. Thank you.
TRY

Answer the following questions based on the second dialogue.

1. How did Hiro introduce himself?
2. What did he say to check if the handouts had been received?
3. How was he able to create a good impression at this stage of the conference?
ACT

Role play.

Introduce yourself, check to see if the handouts have been received, and ask permission to begin your presentation.
