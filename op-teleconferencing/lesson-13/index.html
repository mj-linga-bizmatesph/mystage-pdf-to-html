13
GETTING CLARIFICATION
BIZ TIP

Clarifying is so important in teleconferencing. So, to make sure you understand,:

  • don’t assume or pretend that you understand
  • tell them to simplify their English
  • paraphrase to confirm your understanding of their question
SEE

Example:

Luke:
So, you’re saying that even beginners can easily learn the ropes?
Hiro:
Huh? Learn the what?
Luke:
Learn the ropes. I mean, learning how to use this software would be a walk in the park, even for beginners, correct?
Hiro:
Oh yes. They can use the software anywhere – in the park, at home, in the office and so on.
Luke:
No, I mean “walk in the park.” It means easy to use.
Hiro:
I’m sorry?

Bizmates Style:

Luke:
So, you’re saying that even beginners can easily learn the ropes?
Hiro:
I’m sorry could you rephrase that, please?
Luke:
I mean, learning how to use this software would be a walk in the park, even for beginners, right?
Hiro:
Sorry Luke. I’m not so familiar with idioms. Could you please simplify your question?
Luke:
My question is, can beginners really learn to use this software?
Hiro:
I see. So, you mean can someone with very low computer skills learn to use our product?
Luke:
That’s right.
Hiro:
Absolutely. This product was made for beginners.
TRY

Get some clarification on the following statements.

1. “I know that product like the back of my hand.”
2. “I find it hard to digest all this information.”
3. “OK let’s seal the deal and get the ball rolling.”
4. “My laptop’s speakers are acting up again.”
5. “I don’t want my staff to run around like headless chickens.”
ACT

Role play.

Deliver a short presentation using one of the topics below and get some clarification when your trainer poses a question.
  1. Your favorite hobby
  2. Your colleagues
  3. Your plans this weekend
  4. Your product/service
