14
BEING UNDERSTOOD
BIZ TIP

Misunderstandings are so common in teleconferences. So be sure to guide the listeners by:

  • referring to the page number of a graph or chart
  • waiting until they confirm that they can see it
  • regularly confirming if they understand you
  • explaining in a different way
SEE

Example:

Hiro:
Let’s go back to our handouts and look at the line graph again. As I mentioned earlier, the company can double its profitability.
Luke:
...
Jason:
Sorry, Hiro. We didn’t catch that.
Hiro:
Yes, as I said, the company can double its profitability.
Luke:
Sorry. What page are you on?

Bizmates Style:

Hiro:
Let’s go back to our handouts and look at the line graph on page 3. Can you let me know when you’ve found the graph?
Luke:
Yes, I’m there.
Jason:
Sorry, just a minute. OK, found it.
Hiro:
As I have mentioned earlier, the company can double its profitability.
Luke:
...
Hiro:
Is this making sense?
Luke:
Sorry, I’m a bit confused.
Hiro:
Let me explain it in a different way. It takes your employees half the time to do the work with our software.
Jason:
I see. So, that’s what the figures on the graph illustrate.
Hiro:
That’s right. Luke, are you following me?
Luke:
Yes, I understand now.
TRY

Rephrase each statement then ask a question to see if you’re understood.

1. Being busy doesn’t necessarily mean we’re doing things effectively and productively.
2. Building a relationship with a client is more than just apple-polishing.
3. It’s not what you know, but who you know.
4. A hard worker can labor for long hours; a smart worker can accomplish twice as much
ACT

Role play.

Deliver a statement related to your situation at work. Apply what you have learned to see if your trainer understood you.
