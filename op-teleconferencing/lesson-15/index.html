15
HANDLING OBJECTIONS
BIZ TIP

Objections should be seen as an opportunity. It’s the other person’s way of telling you what their needs are. Address them by:

  • listening carefully to really understand the objection
  • showing you understand their point
  • recommending a solution
SEE

Example:

Jason:
I still think it’s expensive compared to the other brands.
Hiro:
Well, it’s not expensive if you consider...
Jason:
Plus, it’s a new product. There aren’t many reviews out there yet testifying that this is a great application.
Hiro:
No, I disagree with you.
Jason:
We’re just concerned that this product will not meet our expectations.
Hiro:
I think you are wrong.

Bizmates Style:

Jason:
I still think it’s expensive compared to the other brands.
Hiro:
I see.
Jason:
Plus, it’s a new product. There aren’t many reviews out there yet testifying that this is a great application.
Hiro:
I understand where you’re coming from.
Jason:
We’re just concerned that this product will not meet our expectations.
Hiro:
Right. So, let me see if I understand your concern correctly. Because our product is still new, you feel that it may be a risk to make such a large investment. Is this right?
Jason:
Yes, exactly.
Hiro:
I understand. In that case, would you be willing to try out our software for a month? That would allow you to…

  2. Working for a big company versus working for a start-up
  3. Having a cat versus having a dog for a pet
