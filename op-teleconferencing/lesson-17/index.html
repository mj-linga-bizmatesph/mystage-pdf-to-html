17
MAKING A PROPOSAL
BIZ TIP

The goal of making a proposal is to have it accepted. To do this, your proposal should:

  • be delivered with confidence
  • solve a problem or concern
  • be easy for the other person to carry out
SEE

Example:

Hiro:
Would it be possible for you to try our free 30-day trial period?
Jason:
I still have some concerns about it.
Hiro:
But if you try it for 30 days, maybe you’ll like it.
Jason:
I think we’ll have to think about it first, Hiro. Is that OK?
Hiro:
Oh OK. Sure, no problem.

Bizmates Style:

Hiro:
OK, here’s my proposal. Why don’t you take our free 30-day trial? If you don’t like it, then I won’t come back. However, if you like it, we can move on to the next step.
Jason:
I still have some concerns about it.
Hiro:
Yes, and that’s why I’m suggesting you try the software for 30 days. This is the only way you can really understand the user-friendliness of this software.
Jason:
Let me think about it.
Hiro:
It takes only 2 minutes to install.
Jason:
OK, then let’s try it.
TRY

Come up with a proposal or suggestion to the following problems.

1. I started smoking again. I can’t seem to break the habit.
2. My new assistant is so slow at picking things up.
3. I’m not happy with my current job. I don’t know if I can continue my work.
