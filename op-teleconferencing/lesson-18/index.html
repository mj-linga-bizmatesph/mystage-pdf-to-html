18
RESPONDING TO QUESTIONS
BIZ TIP

Going through Q&A is a great opportunity to reiterate key points. The following are some tips in answering questions:

  • make sure you know your subject very well
  • it’s OK to not have the answer
  • be sure to offer to follow-up on any questions you can’t answer
SEE

Example:

Hiro:
OK, do you have any questions?
Jason:
When is the next version scheduled to be released?
Hiro:
Hmm, maybe next year?
Jason:
Are you asking me?
Hiro:
Sorry I don’t know.
Jason:
Are any of our competitors using this software?
Hiro:
Maybe, but if I tell you, I might get in trouble.
Jason:
We only have a couple minutes left, but could you tell the steps to implementing the new software company-wide if we were to go with it?
Hiro:
OK, that is a very difficult question, but I will try to answer it for you. First, …

Bizmates Style:

Hiro:
OK, do you have any questions?
Jason:
When is the next version scheduled to be released?
Hiro:
I’m sorry I don’t have the answer with me. Could I get back to you after I’ve confirmed with our programmers?
Jason:
Sure. Are any of our competitors using this software?
Hiro:
I’m sorry, but I’m not in a position to disclose that.
Jason:
OK, that’s fine. We only have a couple minutes left, but could you tell the steps to implementing the new software company-wide if we were to go with it?
Hiro:
I don’t think we have enough time to answer that, but I’ll e-mail you a brief explanation of the steps, including the timeframe.
