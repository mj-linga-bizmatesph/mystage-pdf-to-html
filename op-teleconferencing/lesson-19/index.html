19
CONVEYING YOUR THOUGHTS
BIZ TIP

Conveying your thoughts appropriately sometimes isn’t just about what you say, but also how you say it. Below are some suggested phrases:

  • Could you please...?
  • I’m afraid I/you...
  • May I suggest that we...
  • I’m sorry, but...
  • Would you...?
  • Shall we...?
SEE

Example:

Yoko:
Please stop talking. I will start the teleconference now.
John:
OK.
Yoko:
John, you have to take the minutes this time.
John:
All right.
Yoko:
John, I think your microphone isn’t working.
John:
Really? Is this better now?
Yoko:
Yes, but your background is too noisy.
John:
I’m in a secluded area so there couldn’t be any background noise from my end.
Yoko:
It’s OK now. Let me go over some meeting rules. First, you must come on time. And you must not leave early.

Bizmates Style:

Yoko:
Could I get your attention? Shall we get started?
John:
OK.
Yoko:
John, I think it’s your turn to take the minutes. Is that OK?
John:
All right.
Yoko:
John, I can’t seem to hear you clearly. Could you check your mic?
John:
Really? Is this better now?
Yoko:
Yes, but I hear some background noise from your side.
John:
I’m in a secluded area so there couldn’t be any background noise from my end.
Yoko:
It’s OK now. Let me go over some meeting rules. First, please try to arrive on time and stay until the end of each meeting.
TRY

Rephrase the statements below into more polite expressions.

1. “Wait. I need to find that document you sent me.”
2. “I have no idea what you’re talking about.”
3. “I need to go now. I have another meeting. See you.”
4. “You need to speak louder.”
5. “That’s ridiculous.”
ACT

Role play.

Convey your thoughts appropriately in the following situations.

1. Everyone is talking all at the same time
2. The participant couldn’t be understood because he talks too fast
3. You couldn’t find the page that the presenter is talking about
