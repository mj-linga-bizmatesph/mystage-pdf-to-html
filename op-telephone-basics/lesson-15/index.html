PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

When you’re calling on behalf of someone, say why you’re calling and provide the details. Here are some phrases:
This is _______ and I’m calling on behalf of…
I'm calling in reference to...
He/she would like to inform you that…
Setting up an appointment with a busy person can be difficult. However, you can still set up the meeting by following these steps:
I'd be happy to visit your office on...
I can work around your schedule. What about on…?
Thank you. So just to confirm,...
Provide enough details when leaving a message. Make your request clear by using these sample phrases:
Could you please ask Mr./Ms.______ to call me back?
I’d like to talk to him/her about…
He/she can reach me…
Get enough information when following up on an urgent matter. Get the details you need by using these sample expressions:
I'd like to follow up on...
Would you know when...?
Could you give me a time frame?

PART 2

Handle the following situations effectively and professionally from beginning to end. Apply the expressions you learned in Lessons 11 to 14.

  1. You’re calling on behalf of your boss about his appointment tomorrow. Tell the other person he won’t make it because of an emergency meeting.
  2. Set up an appointment with a prospective client to present your products. Try to work around his schedule.
  3. You bought new office equipment 2 weeks ago but you haven’t received the order confirmation yet. Make a follow-up inquiry.
