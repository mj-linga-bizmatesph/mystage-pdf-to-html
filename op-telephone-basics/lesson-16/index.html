HANDLING AN UPSET CALLER
16
BIZ TIP
It’s very challenging to talk to an upset caller. Calm the person down by listening carefully and by using these expressions:
I'm very sorry to hear that, Mr./Ms._______.
Could you tell me what happened?
Here's what I can do for you...
SEE

A customer is filing a complaint against the company.

Lyn
I’m really dissatisfied with your company.
Miki
I’m very sorry to hear that, Ms. Reynolds. Could you tell me what happened?
Lyn
Well, someone in your company just messed up my address on file. And now, someone else is getting the magazines that I am paying for. On top of that…
---Miki silently listens to the customer’s frustration---
Miki
I perfectly understand how you feel. Here’s what I can do for you, Ms. Reynolds. Let me update your address and file a refund for you.
Lyn
Thank you so much. I would really appreciate that.
Miki
Not at all. May I ask for your complete address again, please?

TRY

Practice handling an upset caller using the details below:

Caller’s name Situation Your solution
Tanya Garfield Her online account was suspended and she wasn’t notified.
Ron Frey Nobody has replied to him since last month about his complaint.

ACT

Role play.

  • Your caller is upset because your new staff member spent half an hour trying to understand her situation. Handle the call and calm the caller down.
  • If time permits, do another role play using a similar work-related situation.
