PART 1

Discuss the key points you learned in the last four lessons and give a few sample expressions.

It’s very challenging to talk to an upset caller. Calm the person down by listening carefully and by using these expressions:
I'm very sorry to hear that, Mr./Ms._______.
Could you tell me what happened?
Here's what I can do for you...
Keep in mind that you still have to sound professional even when filing a complaint. Here are some phrases you can use:
I'd like to report an issue about...
Is there anything you can do to help me out?
It would be great if…
It’s difficult to handle a call when you can’t understand the other person. Use these expressions to understand them:
Sorry, could you rephrase that, please?
I'm really sorry but could you slow down for me, please?
Sorry, I can barely hear you. Could you speak a bit louder?
When a caller gets off topic, you may need to redirect them to the main topic. Use the phrases below to help you:
Anyway, you mentioned that…
Going back to the issue,…
So you said that…

PART 2

Handle the following situations effectively and professionally from beginning to end. Apply the expressions you learned in Lessons 16 to 19.

  • You’re on the phone with your supplier about a mistake in the invoice. File a complaint.
  • Your caller has a general inquiry about your products/services but started talking about different things. Handle the call and get back to the main topic.
  • Your caller complains about her online purchase. You couldn’t understand what she’s saying because she speaks too fast and too soft. Handle the situation.
