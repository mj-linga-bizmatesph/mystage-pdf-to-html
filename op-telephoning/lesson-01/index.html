UNDERSTANDING TELEPHONE ENGLISH
1
BIZ TIP
Telephoning is much more difficult than face-to-face communication as we
need to rely on our words and tone of voice. Consider the following whenever
you make or answer a phone call:
Repeat the other person’s name to build the relationship
Use proper telephone English
Understand the 10-second rule
SEE

Example

Frank:
Hi there, Yuko! So how’s your first week here?
Yuko:
It’s been really wonderful. Everyone is so warm and friendly. However,
I’m a bit worried about using English on the phone.
Frank:
I see. Why is that?
Yuko:
Well, I never had this much opportunity to use telephone English in my
previous job. Do you have any good advice?
Frank:
Sure. Let’s go over some of the basics. One thing is to repeat the other
person’s name to build the relationship.
Yuko:
OK, so telephoning isn’t all that different from face-to-face.
Frank:
Actually, we have to use appropriate expressions. For example, we
would never say, “Who are you?” or “What’s your name?” over the
phone.
Yuko:
Right. So, I should say, “May I have your name, please?”
Frank:
Yes, that’s much better. Also, you have to understand the 10-second
rule, Yuko.
Yuko:
What’s that?
Frank:
Your caller will make assumptions about your education, personality,
background, skills and so on, during the first 10 seconds of the call.
Yuko:
I see. So I have to sound as professional as possible to make a good
impression, don’t I?
Frank:
Exactly.
