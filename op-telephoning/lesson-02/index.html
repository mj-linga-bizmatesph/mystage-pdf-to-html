ANSWERING THE PHONE
2
BIZ TIP
Consider the key points to make a good impression when answering a call:
Memorize your English opening
Smile when you speak
Correctly spell the caller's information by using names, months, cities or
countries (example: A as in Adam…)
SEE

Example

(Try reading the lines without smiling)

Yuko:
Odenwa arigatou gozaimasu. DataComm no Takeda de gozaimasu.
Caller:
Is there an English speaker?
Yuko:
Yes. I speak English.
Caller:
Hi, I’m looking for Mr. Frank Connelly in the overseas
department.
Yuko:
OK.

Bizmates style

(Try reading the lines while smiling)

Yuko:
Odenwa arigatou gozaimasu. DataComm no Takeda de gozaimasu.
Caller:
Is there an English speaker?
Yuko:
Yes. Thank you for calling DataComm Corp. May I help you?
Caller:
Hi, I’m looking for Mr. Frank Connelly in the overseas department.
Yuko:
Certainly. May I have your full name and the company you’re with
please?
Caller:
Yes, this is Carmon Logan with PL Plastics.
Yuko:
I’m sorry, I didn’t catch your last name. Could you spell that for me
please?
Caller:
Sure. It’s L-O-G-A-N.
Yuko:
Is that “N” as in November?
Caller:
Yes.
Yuko:
Thank you Mr. Logan.
