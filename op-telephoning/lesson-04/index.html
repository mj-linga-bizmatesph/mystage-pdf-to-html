TAKING A MESSAGE
4
BIZ TIP
When taking a message, apart from getting the name, company, and phone number of the caller, you should also:
Apologize on behalf of your colleague
Explain where the person is
Say when the person will return
SEE

Example

Yuko:
Thank you for calling DataComm. Yuko speaking. May I help you?
Caller:
Hi, this is Sean Mitchell with ElectroCon. May I speak with Mr. Taro Sato please?
Yuko:
Mr. Sato’s not here. May I take a message?
Caller:
Uh, OK. Could you please tell him that Sean with EletroCon called. I’d like to confirm the details of the wiring specifications that he and I talked about last…
Yuko:
Sorry, please speak more slowly.

Bizmates style

Yuko:
Thank you for calling DataComm. Yuko speaking. May I help you?
Caller:
Hi, this is Sean Mitchell with ElectroCon. May I speak with Mr. Taro Sato please?
Yuko:
I’m sorry, but Mr. Sato is in a meeting right now. He should be back in about half an hour. Shall I have him call you back?
Caller:
Yes, please.
Yuko:
OK, may I have your name, company, and number please?
Caller:
Yes, this is Sean Mitchell with ElectroCon. Taro should have my number, but just in case he doesn’t, it’s 555-2121.
Yuko:
Thank you, Mr. Mitchell. So that’s 555-2121?
Caller:
Yes, that’s right.
Yuko:
I’ll be sure he gets the message. Thank you. Have a nice day, Mr. Mitchell.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Who do you usually take messages for?
  • What are some of the reasons you tell the caller when that person is unavailable?
  • What kind of messages do you often take from your callers?
  • What could you say if your colleague is (in the restroom, on vacation, on the phone, away from his desk, in a meeting, etc.)

ACT

Role play.

Simulate a phone conversation and take a message for your colleague.
