TOPIC 5: PARADISE
5
BIZ TIP
Words aren’t pronounced one by one unless you want to emphasize them. To sound fluent, connect your words in your sentences in one smooth flow.
Try saying these sentences:
a. Bob is in paradise when he is on the phone talking to his girlfriend.
b. Bobizin paradise | when hezon the phone | talking tohis girlfriend.
SEE

Do you know these words and phrases related to paradise?

  • The park was opened as a haven for endangered animals.
  • The countryside offers a tranquil environment that some people look for.
  • The company tries to establish a utopia among its employees.
  • I’ve never known perfect bliss until I saw my firstborn child.
▼▼▼
TRY

Let’s talk about paradise. Let the trainer take the lead in asking you questions. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • How does your culture view the concept of paradise?
  • What place in your country can be considered a paradise?
  • What simple things can make you feel you’re in paradise?
  • What is your idea of a tropical paradise?

Let’s switch roles. Now you take the lead in asking questions and giving your opinion. Here are some questions to get you started.

  • Is paradise more of a concept or a physical place to you?
  • What place at home do you consider your paradise?
  • What was your idea of paradise when you were a child?
  • How do you imagine an ideal, paradise-like place?
