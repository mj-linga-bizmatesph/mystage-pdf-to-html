OTHER PROGRAMS TELEPHONINGILESSON 7
GIVING DIRECTIONS
7
BIZ TIP
There may be instances when you get calls asking for directions to your office.
When this happens, you should:
Ask where the caller is or is coming from
Mention how long the trip would take
Use common and identifiable landmarks
SEE

Example

Caller:
Hi, I have an appointment with Mr. Tanaka at 3pm and I was
wondering if you could give me directions to your office.
Yuko:
Yes, we are next to the Excel Hotel in Shinjuku.
Caller:
I’m sorry, but I don’t know where that is.
Yuko:
Do you know the Ace Records building?
Caller:
No, I don’t.
Yuko:
OK, go out the North Exit and then turn left…
Caller:
I went out the West Exit by mistake.
Yuko:
Oh, I see.

Bizmates style

Caller:
Hi, I have an appointment with Mr. Tanaka at 3pm and I was
wondering if you could give me directions to your office.
Yuko:
Certainly. May I ask where you are now?
Caller:
Yes, I am at the West Exit at Shinjuku station.
Yuko:
I see. It’s only about a 5-minute walk from there. Do you see the Ace
Records sign on your left?
Caller:
Yes, I do.
Yuko:
OK, walk past the building and turn left at the next corner. Then,
you’ll see the Excel Hotel. We’re on the 3rd floor of the building next
to the hotel.
Caller:
Thank you so much for your help.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • How often do you give directions on the phone?
  • Where do the callers usually come from?
  • What is the nearest train station to your office?
  • How long does it take to reach your office from that station?
  • What landmarks can you use in giving directions?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Give directions on the phone to someone who wants to visit your office.
