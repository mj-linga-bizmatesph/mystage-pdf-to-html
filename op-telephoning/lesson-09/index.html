OTHER PROGRAMS TELEPHONINGILESSON 9
Handling complaints
9
BIZ TIP
Being too apologetic when handling complaints could make the caller more
frustrated. Follow the key points below to handle any complaint:
Take responsibility
Find an immediate and reasonable solution
Use the 4-step process – Understand, Apologize, Take Action, Confirm
SEE

Example

(Yuko is handling a complaint from a customer.)

Mr. Wan:
This is the second time we’ve found a mistake in our invoice.
Yuko:
I’m terribly sorry about that.
Mr. Wan:
What is going on in your billing department?
Yuko:
I’m so sorry for the trouble.
Mr. Wan:
Every time there’s a mistake, valuable time is wasted.
Yuko:
I’m very sorry for causing you such inconvenience.

Bizmates style

(Yuko is handling a complaint from a customer.)

Mr. Wan:
This is the second time we’ve found a mistake in our invoice.
Yuko:
Could you please tell me the details of the mistake, Mr. Wan?
Mr. Wan:
Yes, we were overcharged again. Our corporate discount rate
wasn’t included.
Yuko:
I know that mistakes like these take up your valuable time. I’m
terribly sorry about this, Mr. Wan. I’ll call our billing department
and have them e-mail you a revised invoice right away. Will this be
all right?
Mr. Wan:
Yes, could you please do that immediately?
Yuko:
Absolutely. We’re terribly sorry for the inconvenience.
