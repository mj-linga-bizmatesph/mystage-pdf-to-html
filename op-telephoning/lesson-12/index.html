OTHER PROGRAMS TELEPHONINGILESSON 12
Making inquiries
12
BIZ TIP
In business, time is money. So, keep the following key points in mind when making inquiries:
Have all the information you need before you make the call
Identify yourself properly
Make your inquiry as clear and concise as possible
SEE

Example

Clerk:
Thank you for calling PL Plastics. May I help you?
Ken:
This is Ken Sato with DataComm Corp. Our order was supposed to arrive this morning, but it’s still not here.
Clerk:
May I have the order number?
Ken:
Just a second. Let me look for it. I’m sorry. I can’t seem to find it.
Clerk:
Do you remember when you placed your order, Mr. Sato?
Ken:
Maybe last Tuesday or Wednesday.
Clerk:
Do you remember the details of the order?
Ken:
Ah…, I think…

Bizmates style

Clerk:
Thank you for calling PL Plastics. May I help you?
Ken:
Yes, this is Ken Sato with DataComm Corp. Our order was supposed to arrive this morning, but it’s still not here.
Clerk:
May I have the order number?
Ken:
Yes, it’s 000365.
Clerk:
Thank you, Mr. Sato. I’m sorry, but I can’t seem to find your order, Mr. Sato.
Ken:
I placed an order for 50 units of model XT100 on Dec. 15. And I was told by your salesperson, Mr. Carmen Logan, that it would arrive today before noon.
Clerk:
Let me get Mr. Logan for you.
▼▼▼
TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Who do you usually make inquiries to?
  • What do you often inquire about?
  • Do you always have the necessary information ready before making the call?
▼▼▼
ACT

Role play.

Think about the information you should prepare for the following situations and then practice making the inquiries.

  • The price of a certain product/service
  • How soon your order can be delivered
  • Something related to your business
