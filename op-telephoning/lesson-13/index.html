Leaving a message
13
BIZ TIP
Your goal in leaving a message is to make sure the other person gets your
message and calls you back as soon as possible. To do this, you need to:
Leave a clear and brief message
Provide your contact details
Mention the best time to give you a call back
SEE

Example

Clerk:
Thank you for calling PL Plastics. May I help you?
Ken:
Yes, this is Ken Sato with DataComm Corp. May I speak to Mr. Logan?
Clerk:
I’m sorry, but he’s not here right now. May I take a message?
Ken:
Yes, could you please ask him to call me back when he gets in?
Clerk:
Certainly. May I have your number?
Ken:
Yes, it’s 555-2938.
Clerk:
Thank you, Mr. Sato. I’ll give him the message.

Bizmates style

Clerk:
Thank you for calling PL Plastics. May I help you?
Ken:
Yes, this is Ken Sato with DataComm Corp. Our order was supposed to
arrive this morning, but it’s still not here. May I speak to Mr. Carmon
Logan, our contact person?
Clerk:
I’m sorry to hear that, Mr. Sato. I’m afraid Mr. Logan is out of the
office. May I take a message?
Ken:
Can you tell me what time he is scheduled to return?
Clerk:
He’s just out to lunch. So, he should be back by 1PM.
Ken:
OK. Could you please ask him to call me back at my office as soon as
he gets back? He can reach me at 555-2938.
Clerk:
Yes, I’ll give him the message, Mr. Sato.
Ken:
And may I have your name, please?
Clerk:
Yes, it’s Sara Hopkins.
Ken:
Great. Thank you for your help, Sara.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • Who usually takes your messages when the person you’re looking for isn’t
    around?
  • What information do you provide them with?
  • When is a good time for them to call you back?

  • To confirm receipt of an important document
