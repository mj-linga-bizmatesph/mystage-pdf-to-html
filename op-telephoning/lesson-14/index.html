OTHER PROGRAMS TELEPHONINGILESSON 14
Leaving a voice message
14
BIZ TIP
A lot of business calls end up going to voicemails. Be sure to mention who the message is for and follow the key points below when leaving voice messages:
Be prepared with your message
State your phone number twice slowly and clearly
Continue from the last message if you get cut off
SEE

Example

(Ken gets the answering machine.)

A.M.:
Thank you for calling PL Plastics. Our office hours are from Monday to Friday from 9AM to 6PM… Please leave your name and number after the beep.
Ken:
Ah… yes. This message is … ah. This is Ken Sato with DataComm Corp. Ah… I’m calling about ah… my ah.. order. I ah... (click)

(Ken suddenly gets cut off and tries calling again.)

A.M.:
Thank you for calling PL Plastics. Our office hours are from Monday to Friday from 9AM to 6PM…Please leave your name and number after the beep.
Ken:
Yes, this is Ken Sato with DataComm Corp. Ah,… I’m calling about ah… our order. Ah,… our order was supposed to be delivered today, but ah… it hasn’t… (click).

Bizmates style

A.M.:
Thank you for calling PL Plastics. Our office hours are from Monday to Friday from 9AM to 6PM…Please leave your name and number after the beep.
Ken:
Hi, this is Ken Sato with DataComm Corp calling for Mr. Carmen Logan. Our order was supposed to come this morning, but it still hasn’t arrived. Could you please call me back at 555- (click)

(Ken suddenly gets cut off and tries calling again.)

A.M.:
Thank you for calling PL Plastics. Our office hours are from Monday to Friday from 9AM to 6PM…Please leave your name and number after the beep.
Ken:
This is Ken Sato again. Please call me at 555-2938. That’s 555-2938.
