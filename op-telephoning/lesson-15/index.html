Dealing with technical difficulties
15
BIZ TIP
A bad reception can be very frustrating during phone calls, especially if the call
is very important. Deal with technical difficulties by:
Politely asking the person to repeat what he/she said
Telling him/her that the reception isn’t good
Offering an immediate call back, if everything else fails
SEE

Example

Ken:
Hi this is Ken Sato with DataComm. Could I speak to Mr. Logan please?
Clerk:
Hello, Mr. Sat… May I ask what this is in ref… to?
Ken:
What’s that?
Clerk:
May I…. this is…garding?
Ken:
I really can’t understand what you’re saying. May I speak to Mr.
Logan?
Clerk:
Sure, Mr. Sato. Before I transfer… know what… in reference to?
Ken:
Oh! Yes, I’m calling about… Hello? Hello?
Clerk:
Hello?

Bizmates style

Ken:
Hi this is Ken Sato with DataComm. Could I speak to Mr. Logan please?
Clerk:
Hello, Mr. Sat… May I ask what this is in ref… to?
Ken:
Sorry, could you say that again please?
Clerk:
May I…. this is…garding?
Ken:
I’m afraid the line is really choppy. Can you hear me clearly?
Clerk:
Yes, I can.
Ken:
I see. Let me call you back in a few seconds.

(Ken hangs up and calls again.)

Clerk:
Hello, thank you for calling PL Plastics. May I help you?
Ken:
Hello, this is Ken Sato again. OK, I think the line is much better.

TRY

Answer these questions.

  • What else can you say to ask the person to repeat what he/she said?
  • What else can you say to tell the other person about having a bad
    reception?
  • What else can you say to offer an immediate callback?
  • Tell me about the last time you encountered a bad reception on the phone.
