OTHER PROGRAMS TELEPHONINGILESSON 16
Making an appointment
16
BIZ TIP
Misunderstandings often occur when setting up an appointment over the phone. To ensure there are no misunderstandings, you can do the following:
Give the reason for the appointment
Confirm the details before ending the call
Send an e-mail to confirm the details after the call
SEE

Example

(Ken had just been transferred to Carmon Logan.)

Ken:
Hello, Mr. Logan! How are you?
Carmon:
Pretty good. Thanks! What can I help you with, Ken?
Ken:
I’d like to meet with you to discuss another order for our next project. Are you free next Wednesday?
Carmon:
Sure. What time?
Ken:
How about 3PM?
Carmon:
Sure, 3PM on the 14th is perfect.
Ken:
14th? No, I mean the 7th.
Carmon:
The 7th? You mean the day after tomorrow? Sorry, I’m going to be out of town.

Bizmates style

Ken:
Hello, Mr. Logan! How are you?
Carmon:
Pretty good. Thanks! What can I help you with, Ken?
Ken:
I’d like to meet with you to discuss another order for our next project. Are you free on Wednesday, Dec. 7th?
Carmon:
I’m sorry. I’m going to be out of town on Wednesday.
Ken:
How about Friday, the 9th?
Carmon:
Sure. I’m free all morning.
Ken:
Great. Can we meet on Friday, Dec. 9th at 10AM at your office?
Carmon:
That’s perfect.
Ken:
OK, I’ll send you an e-mail to confirm the date and time.
